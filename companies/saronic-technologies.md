# Saronic Technologies

## Overview

Saronic Technologies is an Austin, Texas-based defense technology company that designs and builds autonomous surface vessels (ASVs) for naval and maritime defense missions. Founded in 2022, the company has grown rapidly to become one of the most well-funded defense startups, reaching a $4 billion valuation by early 2025.

Saronic produces a family of autonomous vessels across three size classes:

- **Spyglass** -- 6-foot autonomous surface vessel for reconnaissance and surveillance
- **Cutlass** -- 14-foot autonomous vessel for patrol and interdiction missions
- **Corsair** -- 24-foot autonomous vessel (in development) for larger naval operations

The company operates its own shipyard in Austin, TX, enabling rapid prototyping and production on timescales far shorter than traditional naval shipbuilding.

## Funding story

Saronic has raised $830 million across four rounds in just three years, reaching unicorn status by July 2024 and a $4 billion valuation by February 2025.

## Team

**Dino Mavrookas** (CEO) leads a team of approximately 271 employees (as of late 2024) focused on autonomous maritime systems. The four co-founders bring complementary expertise in autonomous systems, defense technology, and shipbuilding.

## What makes them interesting

1. **Autonomous shipbuilding at startup speed.** Traditional naval shipbuilding takes years to decades. Saronic is compressing this to months by operating its own shipyard with modern manufacturing techniques.

2. **Navy's autonomous surface vessel gap.** The US Navy has identified a critical need for unmanned surface vessels to counter peer adversaries (particularly China) in the Pacific. Saronic is directly addressing this doctrinal need.

3. **Fastest funding trajectory in defense tech.** Going from founding to $4B valuation in under three years is extraordinary, even by defense tech standards.

4. **CNBC Disruptor 50.** Named to the 2025 list, signaling mainstream recognition of the company's potential.

5. **Vertical integration.** Owning their own shipyard gives Saronic control over production timelines and quality, and enables the rapid iteration cycles that software-native defense companies thrive on.

## Risks and open questions

- **Unproven at scale.** The company is still early in delivering production vessels to the Navy. Scaling from prototypes to fleet quantities is a significant manufacturing challenge.
- **Regulatory and operational hurdles.** Autonomous vessels operating in contested maritime environments face complex rules of engagement and international maritime law questions.
- **Competition.** Other companies (e.g., L3Harris, Textron) are also developing autonomous maritime systems with larger existing defense relationships.
- **Revenue maturity.** Despite massive funding, public revenue data is limited. The valuation may be driven by market potential rather than current earnings.
