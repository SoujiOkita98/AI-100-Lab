# Hebbia -- Company Profile

## Overview

Hebbia is an AI platform for knowledge work, purpose-built for finance, legal, and government sectors. Its flagship product, **Matrix**, replaces the traditional chatbot interface with a spreadsheet-like analytical grid where AI agents collaborate to process millions of documents, extract structured data, and deliver exhaustive, auditable answers. Rather than building its own foundation model, Hebbia orchestrates an "agent swarm" architecture on top of frontier models (it is an OpenAI customer) and layers proprietary retrieval, reasoning, and workflow automation on top. The company has become a de facto AI analyst engine for Wall Street and Big Law, claiming that 30% of the top 50 global asset managers by AUM are customers.

## Business Model

Hebbia sells subscription SaaS seats to enterprises:

- **Professional seats:** ~$10,000/seat/year -- unlimited reasoning, agent building, advanced integrations, workflow automation.
- **Lite seats:** ~$3,000--$3,500/seat/year -- for users who consume outputs and run pre-built agents.

The company bundles a **forward-deployed engagement team** (ex-bankers, ex-lawyers) who configure templates and map data sources for each customer, effectively acting as an embedded consulting layer. This services-driven onboarding reduces time-to-value and enables land-and-expand deals, allowing Hebbia to command "Bloomberg-level pricing" in an industry accustomed to expensive data terminals.

Source: [Sacra](https://sacra.com/c/hebbia/), [eesel.ai deep dive](https://www.eesel.ai/blog/hebbia-ai)

## Product: Matrix

Hebbia's core product is **Matrix**, which it describes as "the interface to AGI." Key characteristics:

- **Spreadsheet-like UI** rather than a chatbot -- users define structured questions across rows and columns; AI agents populate cells with answers sourced from uploaded or connected documents.
- **Agent swarm architecture:** Complex tasks are decomposed into sub-tasks handled by coordinated AI agents working in parallel across potentially millions of documents.
- **"Infinite effective context window":** Proprietary retrieval and chunking allows reasoning over practically unlimited document sets -- far beyond any single LLM's native context.
- **Auditable outputs:** Every answer is traceable to source documents with citations, critical for regulated industries.
- **Integrations:** Microsoft Azure, FactSet, Third Bridge, Preqin (added Jan 2026), and others.

Source: [Hebbia product page](https://www.hebbia.com/product), [Hebbia blog - Introducing Matrix](https://www.hebbia.com/blog/introducing-matrix-the-interface-to-agi)

## What Makes Hebbia Interesting

1. **Vertical depth over horizontal breadth.** While many AI startups chase general-purpose copilots, Hebbia has gone deep into finance and legal -- industries with massive document volumes, high willingness to pay, and tolerance for expensive tooling. This lets them charge Bloomberg-level prices.

2. **Profitable at Series B.** Hebbia was reportedly profitable on $13M ARR when it raised $130M in July 2024 -- rare among AI startups burning through GPU costs. This suggests strong unit economics and disciplined spending.

3. **Matrix as a new interaction paradigm.** The spreadsheet-like agent interface is a genuine UX innovation for enterprise AI. It sidesteps the limitations of chatbots (single-threaded, hard to audit) and maps naturally to how analysts already work with structured data.

4. **Remarkable investor roster.** Seed investors include Peter Thiel, Jerry Yang (Yahoo co-founder), Ram Shriram (early Google backer), Stanley Druckenmiller (legendary macro investor), and Alexandr Wang (Scale AI CEO). The Series B brought a16z and GV. This network provides deep distribution into exactly the financial firms Hebbia targets.

5. **Founder pedigree and youth.** Sivulka was ~21 when he founded the company and ~25 when he raised $130M. His Stanford neuroscience research (ML systems inspired by biological computation) informs Hebbia's multi-agent architecture. He is a genuine technical founder running a company that sells to the most demanding enterprise buyers.

6. **Forward-deployed services model.** Embedding ex-bankers and ex-lawyers alongside customers creates deep switching costs and enables Hebbia to capture workflow-level value, not just tool-level value. This is reminiscent of Palantir's forward-deployed engineering model adapted for knowledge work.

7. **Acquisition strategy.** The FlashDocs acquisition (Jun 2025) shows Hebbia building toward an end-to-end workflow: ingest documents, reason over them, and produce client-ready deliverables -- closing the loop from analysis to output.

## Competitive Landscape

Hebbia competes with:
- **Harvey AI** -- deeper in legal, broader professional services; much larger ($11B valuation, ~$195M ARR by end 2025).
- **AlphaSense** -- market intelligence platform; more established but less AI-native.
- **Kira Systems / Luminance** -- legacy legal AI tools.
- **Bloomberg Terminal / FactSet** -- incumbent data terminals; Hebbia positions as a next-gen complement or replacement.
- **General-purpose AI copilots** (ChatGPT Enterprise, Microsoft Copilot) -- broad but lack Hebbia's vertical depth and compliance features.
