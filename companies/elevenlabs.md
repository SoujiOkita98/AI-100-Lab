# ElevenLabs

## Overview

ElevenLabs is an AI voice and audio company founded in April 2022 by two childhood friends from Warsaw, Poland. The company builds generative AI tools for speech synthesis, voice cloning, dubbing, and conversational AI agents. In under four years it has grown from a two-person startup to a ~736-person company valued at $11 billion, making it one of the fastest-scaling AI startups globally and one of the highest-valued AI companies with European roots.

## Founding Story

Mati Staniszewski and Piotr Dabkowski grew up together in Warsaw and attended Copernicus High School. Their shared frustration with the poor quality of Polish dubbing on American films became a formative experience. After divergent careers -- Staniszewski at Palantir (deployment strategy) and Dabkowski at Google (ML engineering) -- they reunited in 2022 to build ElevenLabs. Before founding the company, they had collaborated on side projects including an accent-detection app and a recommendation engine.

Dabkowski brings deep technical credibility: Oxford and Cambridge degrees in AI/ML, with a thesis published at NeurIPS. Staniszewski brings go-to-market and operational experience from Palantir. The combination of a research-grade CTO and a commercially savvy CEO has been central to the company's rapid scaling.

## Products

- **Text-to-Speech (TTS):** Core product. Generates expressive, controllable speech across 70+ languages with 10,000+ available voices. Available via API and web interface.
- **Voice Cloning:** Instant clones from seconds of audio, plus professional-grade cloning that captures fine-grained vocal traits and accents.
- **AI Dubbing:** Translates speech into 20+ languages while preserving the speaker's original voice, emotion, and intonation. Launched October 2023.
- **ElevenLabs Reader App:** iOS and Android app (launched June 2024) for listening to articles, PDFs, and ePubs with AI voices.
- **Voice Library:** Marketplace where users share and monetize voice clones. Creators earn ~$0.03 per 1,000 characters; total payouts have exceeded $1M.
- **ElevenAgents:** Conversational AI voice agent platform for customer service and interactive applications.
- **Eleven v3 / Expressive Mode (Feb 2026):** Latest generation model with emotionally intelligent, context-aware TTS optimized for real-time dialogue, plus improved turn-taking for natural conversations.

## Business Model

ElevenLabs operates a **freemium SaaS + API** model:

- **Free tier:** 10,000 characters/month
- **Paid tiers:** Starting at ~$22/month (Creator plan), scaling with usage volume
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing based on volume; API access for integration into third-party products
- **Revenue split (Dec 2025):** Roughly 50/50 between enterprise and consumer, with enterprise expected to grow to 60% by end of 2026

## Revenue & Traction

- **~$330M ARR** as of 2025, up 175% year-over-year from ~$120M at end of 2024 ([SaaStr](https://www.saastr.com/elevenlabs-from-0-to-300m-arr-in-3-years-why-the-best-ai-b2b-apps-are-exploding/))
- **41% of Fortune 500** companies use the platform
- Notable customers: Washington Post, TIME, HarperCollins, Paradox Interactive, Deutsche Telekom, Deliveroo, Revolut, Square, and the Ukrainian government

## What Makes ElevenLabs Interesting

1. **Speed of scaling:** $0 to ~$330M ARR in roughly three years is among the fastest revenue ramps in SaaS/AI history, rivaling the trajectories of OpenAI and Midjourney.

2. **European-origin AI powerhouse:** Founded by Polish immigrants, headquartered in New York, with offices in London and Warsaw. Poised to become one of the first European-founded AI companies to IPO (targeting readiness within 2-3 years per March 2026 Bloomberg reporting).

3. **Full-stack audio AI:** Unlike point solutions, ElevenLabs spans TTS, cloning, dubbing, conversational agents, and a creator marketplace -- building a platform moat rather than a single-feature product.

4. **Investor quality and conviction:** a16z has invested in every round since Series A (quadrupling its investment at Series D). Sequoia entered at Series B and led the Series D. The angel roster (Mustafa Suleyman, Mike Krieger, Brendan Iribe) reads as a who's-who of tech founders.

5. **Research-to-product pipeline:** Dabkowski's academic pedigree (NeurIPS publications, Oxford/Cambridge) translates into consistently state-of-the-art voice quality, giving ElevenLabs a technical edge that has persisted even as competition from OpenAI, Google, and Amazon has intensified.

6. **Dual-sided marketplace dynamics:** The Voice Library creates network effects -- more voices attract more users, which attracts more voice creators -- adding defensibility beyond pure model quality.

7. **Safety considerations:** The same technology that enables powerful voice cloning also raises deepfake risks. ElevenLabs has faced scrutiny (early misuse incidents in 2023) and has invested in safety measures including voice verification and usage policies. How well they navigate the regulatory environment will be a key factor going forward.

## Key Risks & Uncertainties

- Exact revenue figures are based on press reports and may not be independently audited
- Competitive pressure from OpenAI (which has its own TTS capabilities), Google, Amazon, and emerging startups like Cartesia and Fish Audio
- Regulatory risk around synthetic voice / deepfake legislation, particularly in the EU
- Whether the company can sustain growth rates as it scales into the hundreds of millions in ARR
- Headquarters location is reported variously as New York or London across sources; the company appears to operate across both with no single dominant HQ
