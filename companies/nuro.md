# Nuro

## Overview

Nuro is an autonomous vehicle technology company that began by building purpose-built self-driving delivery robots and has since pivoted to licensing its Level 4 autonomous driving software -- "Nuro Driver" -- to automakers and mobility providers. The company is now at the center of one of the largest announced robotaxi deployments in the world, partnering with Uber and Lucid Motors to field 20,000+ autonomous vehicles over six years.

## Founders

### Jiajun Zhu (JZ)

Jiajun Zhu is Chinese-born, educated first at Fudan University in Shanghai -- one of China's most elite universities -- before earning his MS in Computer Science at the University of Virginia. He was one of the founding engineers of Google's self-driving car project (now Waymo), where he served as a principal software engineer leading the perception and simulation teams. He holds over 100 patents. At Nuro, JZ focuses on technology and people/culture.

### Dave Ferguson

Dave Ferguson grew up in New Zealand, the son of a diplomat. He studied Computer Science and Mathematics at the University of Otago before moving to the US for his PhD at Carnegie Mellon University's Robotics Institute. At CMU, he led the planning group for the team that won the 2007 DARPA Urban Challenge -- a formative experience that cemented his passion for self-driving technology. He then joined Google's self-driving car project as a principal engineer and machine learning lead. He holds over 100 patents and has published 60+ academic papers. At Nuro, Dave handles finance, investment, and partnerships.

The two met at Google's self-driving car project and worked together for several years before co-founding Nuro in September 2016. As of mid-2025, both serve as Co-CEOs.

## Business Model Evolution

### Phase 1: Autonomous Delivery Robots (2016-2023)

Nuro originally designed and built small, purpose-built autonomous delivery vehicles (the R1, R2, and planned R3) with no passenger compartment -- engineered purely for last-mile goods delivery. Key customer pilots included:

- **Kroger** (2018) -- grocery delivery in Scottsdale, AZ
- **Domino's** (2019) -- pizza delivery in Houston, TX
- **CVS** (2020) -- prescription delivery in Texas
- **7-Eleven** (2021) -- pilot deliveries in California
- **Uber Eats** (2022) -- 10-year agreement for vehicle deployment in CA and TX

In 2020, Nuro received the first-ever autonomous vehicle exemption from the US Department of Transportation (NHTSA), allowing its R2 to operate without traditional human-driver controls.

### Crisis and Restructuring (2022-2023)

Facing a tightening funding environment, Nuro laid off ~20% of staff (~300 employees) in November 2022, followed by another ~30% (~340 employees) in May 2023. The company paused commercial operations, delayed volume production of the R3, and shifted resources toward core R&D. Co-founders Zhu and Ferguson stated these cuts would extend the company's capital runway by roughly doubling its operational lifespan.

### Phase 2: Licensing Nuro Driver (2024-present)

In September 2024, Nuro formally pivoted its business model to **licensing its autonomous driving software stack ("Nuro Driver")** to automakers, suppliers, and mobility providers. Rather than building and operating its own fleet, Nuro now offers its road-proven Level 4 autonomy platform as a technology product that OEMs can integrate into their vehicles.

## Key Partnership: Uber x Lucid x Nuro Robotaxi

In July 2025, Nuro announced a landmark partnership with Uber and Lucid Motors:

- **Vehicle:** Lucid Gravity all-electric SUV (up to 450 miles range), outfitted with Nuro Driver
- **Operator:** Uber purchases and operates the vehicles on its ride-hailing network
- **Scale:** 20,000+ robotaxis planned over six years
- **CES 2026:** Production-intent vehicles unveiled at CES in January 2026
- **Testing:** Autonomous on-road testing began December 2025 in the San Francisco Bay Area
- **Launch:** Commercial service expected to launch in the Bay Area later in 2026 (pending validation)

This is one of the largest robotaxi commitments announced globally and positions Nuro as a major player alongside Waymo and Cruise/GM in the autonomous ride-hailing space.

## What Makes Nuro Interesting

1. **Rare successful pivot.** Nuro navigated a near-death experience in 2022-2023 (massive layoffs, paused operations) and emerged with a fundamentally different -- and arguably more scalable -- business model. Licensing software to OEMs avoids the capital-intensive trap of building and operating a hardware fleet.

2. **Deep Waymo DNA without being Waymo.** Both founders were among the earliest and most senior engineers at Google's self-driving project. They bring Waymo-caliber technical depth but operate independently, making Nuro Driver available to any OEM -- a commercially neutral positioning that Waymo (owned by Alphabet) cannot offer.

3. **First NHTSA exemption.** Nuro was the first company to receive a regulatory exemption from NHTSA for a vehicle with no human-driver controls (2020), demonstrating regulatory credibility.

4. **Uber as both investor and customer.** The Uber partnership creates powerful alignment -- Uber has financial skin in the game as a Series E investor and operational skin as the fleet deployer. This is a stronger lock-in than a typical vendor relationship.

5. **Founder backgrounds span continents.** The pairing of Zhu (Chinese-born, Fudan-educated) and Ferguson (New Zealand-born, CMU-trained) reflects the global talent pipeline that defines Silicon Valley's AI/robotics ecosystem. Zhu's path -- from Fudan to UVA to Google to co-founding a multi-billion-dollar AV company -- is a notable example of Chinese-origin technical talent shaping US autonomous driving.

6. **Scale of ambition.** 20,000 robotaxis over six years with Uber would make this one of the largest autonomous fleet deployments ever attempted, rivaling Waymo's own expansion plans.

## Open Questions and Uncertainties

- Whether the Uber/Lucid robotaxi service will launch on schedule in late 2026 and at what scale remains to be seen.
- The $6B valuation is a down-round; future fundraising or IPO prospects are uncertain.
- Employee count (~975) is well below the pre-layoff peak; whether the team is sufficient for the expanded robotaxi mission is unclear.
- Competition from Waymo (which has a head start in ride-hailing) and emerging Chinese AV companies is intense.
- Exact revenue figures are not publicly disclosed; it is unclear how much licensing revenue Nuro currently generates.
