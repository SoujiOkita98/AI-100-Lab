# Helsing

## Overview

Helsing is a European defense AI company headquartered in Munich, Germany, with offices in Paris, London, and Berlin. Founded in 2021, it builds AI software designed to be integrated into existing military platforms across all domains -- air, land, sea, space, and cyber. The company positions itself as the European answer to the question of technological sovereignty in defense AI, operating exclusively for European and NATO-allied nations.

Helsing's core technology is a real-time AI software stack that enables sensor fusion, autonomous decision-making, and multi-domain awareness on legacy and modern military platforms. Rather than building hardware, Helsing focuses on the AI "brain" that makes existing systems significantly more capable.

## Funding story

Helsing has raised approximately EUR 1.37 billion (~$1.5B USD) across four funding rounds, reaching a EUR 12 billion valuation by June 2025.

## Team

**Torsten Reil** (CEO) brings deep AI expertise from NaturalMotion, where he pioneered AI-driven animation and simulation (sold to Zynga for $527M). His Oxford DPhil work in computational biology gives him a strong ML research foundation.

**Gundbert Scherf** brings the defense domain expertise as a former senior official at the German Federal Ministry of Defence, where he led cybersecurity and defense innovation policy.

**Niklas Koehler** brings strategy and operational scaling from his time as a McKinsey partner.

The founding team represents a deliberate combination of AI/ML research capability, European defense institutional knowledge, and operational strategy expertise.

## What makes them interesting

1. **European defense AI champion.** Helsing is the most valuable defense AI startup in Europe and the leading candidate for European technological sovereignty in military AI, filling a gap left by the US-centric defense tech ecosystem.

2. **Software-only model.** By focusing purely on AI software that enhances existing platforms, Helsing avoids the massive capital requirements of hardware development while addressing the most critical capability gap in European defense.

3. **Eurofighter integration.** Helsing's AI is being integrated into the Eurofighter Typhoon program with Airbus Defence, giving it a role in one of Europe's most important combat aircraft platforms.

4. **Daniel Ek backing.** Spotify's founder serving as Chairman and leading multiple funding rounds through Prima Materia signals deep conviction and provides significant tech-sector credibility.

5. **Geopolitical tailwind.** The Russia-Ukraine war has driven a massive increase in European defense spending, and Helsing is positioned as the primary beneficiary of Europe's push for indigenous defense AI capability.

## Risks and open questions

- **Revenue opacity.** Despite a EUR 12B valuation, public revenue figures are scarce. The valuation may be driven more by geopolitical urgency and market potential than current earnings.
- **European procurement complexity.** Selling across multiple European defense ministries, each with different procurement rules and political dynamics, is significantly more complex than selling to a single customer (e.g., US DoD).
- **Competition from US players.** Anduril, Palantir, and other US defense AI companies could seek European contracts, potentially with more mature products.
- **Regulatory and ethical landscape.** European attitudes toward autonomous weapons and AI in defense differ from the US, which could constrain product capabilities.
