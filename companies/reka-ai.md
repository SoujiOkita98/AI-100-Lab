# Reka AI

## Overview

Reka AI is a multimodal AI model builder headquartered in Sunnyvale, California, with a UK subsidiary. Founded in 2022 by five researchers from DeepMind, Google Brain, and Meta FAIR, the company builds foundation models that natively process text, images, audio, and video. Reka reached unicorn status in July 2025 after raising $110M at a $1B valuation from Nvidia and Snowflake.

What makes Reka distinctive in the crowded foundation model landscape is its extreme leanness: the company built models competitive with GPT-4 using roughly 20 core developers and a total team of about 60 people. This is orders of magnitude smaller than the teams at OpenAI, Google DeepMind, or Anthropic.

## Founding story

The five co-founders -- Dani Yogatama, Yi Tay, Cyprien de Masson d'Autume, Mikel Artetxe, and Qi Liu -- came together from DeepMind (London), Google Brain, and Meta FAIR. While working on AI systems including DeepMind's AlphaCode and Google's Bard, they recognized that it was impractical to expect monolithic LLMs from Big Tech to serve all enterprise use cases. They founded Reka to build efficient, deployable multimodal models that enterprises could run anywhere -- cloud, on-premises, or at the edge.

The founding team is remarkably international: Yogatama is Indonesian (CMU PhD, ex-DeepMind London), Yi Tay is Singaporean (ex-Google Brain, co-led PaLM-2), de Masson d'Autume is French (ex-DeepMind), Artetxe is Basque/Spanish (ex-Meta FAIR), and Qi Liu's background is less publicly documented but appears to include DeepMind experience.

## Funding story

**2023 -- Series A ($58M at $300M valuation).** Reka emerged from stealth in June 2023 with backing that included DST Global and angel investor Nat Friedman (former GitHub CEO). The pitch: a small team of elite researchers could build competitive multimodal models at a fraction of Big Tech's cost.

**2024 -- The Snowflake near-acquisition.** In May 2024, Snowflake entered talks to acquire Reka for over $1B. The deal fell apart, with both companies deciding to "move independently." This was a validating signal for Reka's technology even though no transaction closed.

**2025 -- Series B ($110M at $1B valuation).** In July 2025, Reka raised from Nvidia and Snowflake (the same company that had tried to buy them a year earlier). The round tripled the company's valuation and gave Reka unicorn status just three years after founding. Total raised: $168M.

## Team

The founding team's defining characteristic is the combination of elite AI research credentials with extraordinary geographic diversity. This is not a Stanford-to-startup pipeline -- it is a genuinely global team that came together through shared research interests at top labs.

- **Dani Yogatama (CEO):** The operational leader. Indonesian, educated at Institut Teknologi Bandung, then a Monbukagakusho fellow at the University of Tokyo, then a CMU PhD. Spent several years at DeepMind in London working on AlphaCode and Bard before founding Reka. His path -- Indonesia to Japan to Pittsburgh to London to Silicon Valley -- is unusually non-linear for an AI startup CEO.

- **Yi Tay (Co-Founder, former Chief Scientist):** Singaporean. The most publicly prominent researcher on the founding team. At Google Brain, he co-led PaLM-2 and invented UL2 and Differentiable Search Indexes -- work that shipped into Google's core products. At Reka, he drove the technical effort to build competitive multimodal models with a skeleton crew. He departed in November 2024 (after ~1.5 years) to return to Google DeepMind, where he now co-leads a new Singapore lab with Quoc Le focused on reasoning and RL. His departure was significant but Reka continued to execute, raising its Series B seven months later.

- **Cyprien de Masson d'Autume (CTO):** French. Ex-DeepMind researcher. Leads the technical organization. Limited public biographical information.

- **Mikel Artetxe (Co-Founder):** Basque/Spanish. University of the Basque Country. Former research scientist at Meta FAIR.

- **Qi Liu (Co-Founder):** Previously at DeepMind. Limited public biographical detail.

## What makes them interesting

1. **Extreme efficiency.** Reka built GPT-4-class multimodal models with approximately 20 developers. Their Reka Core model -- processing text, images, audio, and video -- was developed at what CEO Yogatama describes as "a fraction" of what larger competitors spend. This efficiency thesis, if sustainable, is a genuine strategic advantage.

2. **True multimodal from the ground up.** Unlike many competitors who bolt image/audio understanding onto text-first models, Reka's models were designed as natively multimodal from inception. The model suite (Core, Flash, Edge) spans the full capability-efficiency spectrum.

3. **The Snowflake saga as validation.** Snowflake's willingness to pay $1B+ for an acquisition (which fell through) and then invest in the Series B anyway signals that Reka's technology has genuine enterprise value, particularly for data platform companies seeking embedded AI capabilities.

4. **International founding team in a homogeneous industry.** Five co-founders from five different countries (Indonesia, Singapore, France, Spain, and possibly China) is unusual in Silicon Valley AI. This diversity of background and research tradition may contribute to their unconventional approach.

5. **Deployability as differentiator.** Reka emphasizes deployment flexibility -- cloud, on-prem, edge -- which appeals to regulated industries (healthcare, finance, government) that cannot send data to third-party APIs. The Carahsoft partnership for US government distribution underscores this.

6. **Agentic pivot with Nexus.** The March 2025 launch of Reka Nexus (AI workforce management) signals a shift from pure model provider to platform company, following the broader industry trend toward agentic AI systems.

## Risks and open questions

- **Yi Tay's departure.** Losing a co-founder and chief scientist of Yi Tay's caliber -- especially one who returned to Google DeepMind -- is a meaningful talent loss. The company's ability to continue innovating at the frontier without him is an open question.
- **Scale challenge.** At $10.9M in revenue (reported September 2025) and a $1B valuation, Reka trades at roughly 90x revenue. The company needs to grow revenue substantially to justify the valuation, and competing for enterprise AI deals against OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and Mistral with a 60-person team is an asymmetric fight.
- **Commoditization risk.** As open-source models (Llama, Mistral, Qwen) improve and major cloud providers embed AI capabilities natively, the market for independent model providers may narrow.
- **Limited public information.** Several co-founders have sparse public profiles. Revenue data comes from a single source. Overall confidence in this profile is medium.
