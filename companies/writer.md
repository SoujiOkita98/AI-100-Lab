# Writer — Enterprise AI Platform

## Overview

Writer is a full-stack enterprise generative AI platform built on its proprietary family of **Palmyra** large language models. Unlike many AI startups that wrap third-party foundation models, Writer develops its own LLMs trained specifically on business and enterprise data, and pairs them with an integrated application and agent-building layer. The company positions itself as the enterprise-native alternative to horizontal AI providers, emphasizing governance, brand consistency, and data privacy.

## Founders & Team Origins

### May Habib — CEO & Co-founder

May Habib's path to enterprise AI began far from Silicon Valley. Born in rural Lebanon, she emigrated to Canada with her family in the 1990s as the eldest of eight children. She became her family's English interpreter — an early, formative experience with the power and friction of language. She studied Economics and Near Eastern Languages and Civilizations at **Harvard University**.

After Harvard, Habib worked in investment banking and venture capital before founding **Qordoba**, a localization and translation startup, around 2015. That company evolved over several years from translation into AI-powered content consistency, eventually rebranding as **Writer** in August 2020 to reflect its broader enterprise AI ambitions.

Habib has been recognized on the **Forbes AI 50** (2023), received **Inc.'s Female Founder Award** (2023), was selected as a **World Economic Forum Young Global Leader** (2024), and spoke at **TEDAI San Francisco** (2025).

### Waseem AlShikh — CTO & Co-founder

Waseem AlShikh grew up in the mountains south of Damascus, Syria, where he discovered computer science at age 18 — a field that was not widely taught in Syria at the time. He moved to Lebanon to study, earning degrees in Electronics from **Beirut Arab University** and **Damascus Polytechnic University**. Courses were taught in English, a language he was still learning; to cope, he built a summarization tool — an early sign of his AI instincts.

AlShikh eventually connected with May Habib through the tech community in Dubai (via Twitter). They co-founded Qordoba and later Writer together. He has led Writer's proprietary model development and full-stack platform architecture.

### Founder Narrative

Both founders are immigrants from the Middle East (Lebanon and Syria) who experienced language barriers firsthand. This shared background — and their prior collaboration at Qordoba — gives Writer an authentic origin story rooted in language, localization, and enterprise communication. They are not ML researchers who pivoted to enterprise; they are enterprise-and-language operators who built their own models to serve their customers better.

## Business Model

Writer operates a **B2B SaaS subscription model** with pricing based on seats and usage. Key revenue characteristics:

- **ARR (estimated):** ~$47M as of November 2024, up from ~$16M in 2023 (~194% YoY growth). Sacra is the source for these estimates; Writer has not publicly disclosed exact revenue figures.
- **Net revenue retention:** Reported at ~160%, indicating strong land-and-expand dynamics.
- **Customer count:** 300-500+ enterprise customers (sources vary; the company reported "over 250" as of mid-2024 and "hundreds" by late 2024).
- **Average contract value:** Exceeds $100K; Fortune 500 deals reportedly reach $1M+.
- **Valuation multiple:** ~40x ARR at the Series C, which is aggressive but reflects the company's growth trajectory.

Writer monetizes across multiple vectors:
1. **Platform subscriptions** — access to the Writer platform, AI Studio, Knowledge Graph, and governance tools.
2. **Model API access** — Palmyra models available via Writer's API and also through **Amazon Bedrock**.
3. **Professional services / deployment** — enterprise onboarding, custom model tuning, and integration support (degree of revenue from this is uncertain).

## Technology & Product

### Palmyra Model Family

Writer's proprietary LLM family, **Palmyra**, is the core differentiator. Key models include:

- **Palmyra X5** — The flagship model (released 2025). Features a 1-million-token context window, adaptive reasoning, and is purpose-built for agentic enterprise workflows. Reported to deliver near GPT-4-level performance at ~75% lower cost.
- **PalmyraMed** — A vertical-specific model for healthcare use cases.
- **Earlier Palmyra variants** — Smaller, faster models optimized for specific enterprise writing and content tasks.

Palmyra models are trained on business writing and enterprise data, explicitly avoiding copyrighted content — a selling point for compliance-sensitive enterprises.

### Platform Components

- **AI Studio** — No-code/low-code environment for building custom AI agents and workflows.
- **Knowledge Graph** — Connects AI to internal company documents and data sources for grounded, citable generation.
- **AI Guardrails** — Automated enforcement of brand voice, style guidelines, and regulatory compliance.
- **Writer Agent** (launched Nov 2025) — A unified interface combining conversational AI ("Ask Writer") with autonomous action agents, running on Palmyra X5.

Writer's **Action Agent** achieved a 61% top score on the GAIA benchmark at its highest difficulty level in mid-2025, demonstrating competitive agentic capabilities.

## What Makes Writer Interesting

1. **Full-stack ownership in a wrapper world.** While most enterprise AI startups build on OpenAI or Anthropic APIs, Writer develops and trains its own Palmyra models end-to-end. This gives them control over cost structure, data privacy guarantees, and model customization — critical for regulated industries.

2. **Immigrant founder story with domain authenticity.** Both founders experienced language barriers as immigrants (Lebanon, Syria). They did not pivot to AI from a research lab; they evolved into it from a decade of solving enterprise language problems (Qordoba). This gives the company unusually deep domain expertise in how large organizations actually use language.

3. **Strategic investor-as-customer flywheel.** The Series C investor list reads like a customer roster: Salesforce Ventures, Adobe Ventures, IBM Ventures, Accenture, Vanguard, Citi Ventures, Workday Ventures. These are not passive financial investors — they are distribution partners and reference accounts.

4. **Enterprise governance as a moat.** Writer's emphasis on brand guardrails, compliance controls, and content governance addresses the #1 concern enterprises have with generative AI: losing control. This positions Writer well against both horizontal AI providers (who lack governance depth) and point solutions (who lack the full platform).

5. **Competitive model economics.** Palmyra X5's claim of near-GPT-4 performance at 75% lower cost is significant if it holds. For enterprises running AI at scale, model cost is a core purchasing criterion, and owning the model gives Writer margin advantages that API wrappers cannot match.

6. **Agentic AI pivot (2025-2026).** Writer's expansion from content generation into autonomous AI agents (Action Agent, Writer Agent) positions the company in the fastest-growing segment of enterprise AI. Their GAIA benchmark results suggest this is not vaporware.

## Risks & Open Questions

- **Model quality at frontier.** Palmyra models are strong for enterprise tasks but have not demonstrated frontier-level capabilities on general reasoning benchmarks compared to GPT-4, Claude, or Gemini. If enterprises increasingly demand frontier intelligence, Writer may face pressure to supplement with third-party models.
- **Revenue scale vs. valuation.** At ~$47M ARR and a $1.9B valuation (~40x multiple), Writer needs to sustain triple-digit growth to justify the price. Any deceleration would compress the multiple significantly.
- **Competition from platform incumbents.** Microsoft (Copilot), Salesforce (Agentforce), and Google (Gemini for Workspace) are embedding AI directly into enterprise workflows. Writer must prove its standalone platform is worth the incremental vendor relationship.
- **Exact 2026 financials are unconfirmed.** ARR figures cited are Sacra estimates from late 2024; Writer has not publicly disclosed 2025 or 2026 revenue. Actual numbers may differ.
