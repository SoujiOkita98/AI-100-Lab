# Humans&

## Overview

Humans& is a frontier AI research lab founded in late 2025 with the philosophy that AI should empower people rather than replace them. The three-month-old startup raised $480 million in seed funding at a $4.48 billion valuation, announced January 20, 2026 -- the second-largest seed round in venture capital history.

The founding team brings together an unusual mix: a former Anthropic post-training researcher (Andi Peng), Google's seventh employee (Georges Harik), two former xAI/Grok developers (Eric Zelikman and Yuchen He), and a Stanford cognitive science professor (Noah Goodman). The blend of frontier model experience, engineering pedigree, and cognitive science suggests an approach to AI development that is both technically ambitious and philosophically distinct.

## Funding story

**2026 -- $480M Seed at $4.48B valuation.** Led by SV Angel and co-founder Georges Harik. The investor list is elite: Nvidia, Jeff Bezos, GV (Google Ventures), and Emerson Collective (Laurene Powell Jobs). This is the second-largest seed round in venture history, trailing only Mira Murati's Thinking Machines Lab ($2B at $12B in July 2025).

## Team

- **Andi Peng:** Former Anthropic. Worked on RLHF and post-training for Claude 3.5 through 4.5. Brings deep knowledge of frontier model alignment.
- **Georges Harik:** Google employee #7. Built Google's early ad systems. Provides operational gravity and investor credibility (he co-led the round himself).
- **Eric Zelikman & Yuchen He:** Both ex-xAI, worked on Grok. Bring experience from Musk's fast-moving AI operation.
- **Noah Goodman:** Stanford professor of psychology and computer science. His expertise in how humans think and learn is presumably central to the "human-centric" thesis.

## What makes them interesting

1. **The "human-centric" thesis is differentiated.** While most frontier labs compete on raw capability (bigger models, more compute), Humans& is explicitly positioning around human empowerment. If they can operationalize this into products, it addresses a growing market concern about AI displacement.

2. **Founding team diversity of origin.** Pulling from Anthropic (safety culture), xAI (speed culture), Google (scale culture), and Stanford (research culture) creates an unusual organizational DNA.

3. **Noah Goodman's cognitive science lens.** Having a professor of psychology and computer science as co-founder signals this is not just another scaling lab. The cognitive science perspective could yield genuinely novel approaches to human-AI interaction.

4. **Valuation for a three-month-old company** at $4.48B on a seed round reflects either extraordinary investor conviction or peak market froth -- or both.

## Notes

- **Very early stage.** No products announced, no revenue, no public technical details. The company is essentially a team and a thesis at this point.
- **Confidence is medium** due to limited public information about technical approach, product roadmap, or business model. The "human-centric" framing is philosophically appealing but commercially vague.
- **The $480M seed at $4.48B valuation** implies investors are paying 2025-2026 frontier lab prices for what is currently a research vision. The bar for execution is extraordinarily high.
