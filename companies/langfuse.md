# Langfuse

## Overview

Langfuse is an open-source LLM engineering platform that provides observability, metrics, evaluations, prompt management, and analytics for LLM applications. Built during Y Combinator's W23 batch -- just as GPT-4 was initially released -- Langfuse has become one of the most popular LLMOps platforms globally, used by tens of thousands of developers. The platform has processed over 2.1 billion requests and 2.6 trillion tokens as of early 2026.

## Products & Technology

Langfuse provides an integrated suite of LLM development tools:

1. **Tracing & Observability:** End-to-end tracing of LLM calls, tool invocations, and chain executions with latency and cost tracking
2. **Prompt Management:** Version control and management for prompts across environments
3. **Evaluations:** Built-in and custom evaluation frameworks for measuring LLM output quality
4. **Analytics:** Dashboards for monitoring usage patterns, costs, and performance metrics
5. **Datasets:** Management tools for few-shot examples and test cases

The platform integrates with OpenTelemetry, LangChain, OpenAI SDK, LiteLLM, and many other frameworks. It is open-source under an MIT-style license with a managed cloud offering for teams that don't want to self-host.

## Business Model

- **Open-source core with managed cloud:** Self-hosted version is free; cloud-hosted SaaS is usage-based
- **Revenue (2024):** ~$1.1M ARR reached in June 2024 with a 7-person team
- **Target customers:** AI/ML engineering teams building production LLM applications

## What Makes Langfuse Interesting

1. **Open-source advantage at scale.** With tens of thousands of developers and billions of requests processed, Langfuse has built significant community momentum as the open-source alternative to proprietary LLMOps platforms.

2. **Framework-agnostic approach.** Unlike LangSmith (tied to LangChain), Langfuse works with any LLM framework, broadening its addressable market.

3. **Capital-efficient growth.** Reaching $1.1M ARR with a 7-person team on $4.5M in funding demonstrates strong product-market fit and efficient operations.

4. **European AI infrastructure.** As one of the few Berlin-based LLM tooling companies, Langfuse benefits from European data residency requirements for regulated industries.
