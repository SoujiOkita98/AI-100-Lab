# Shield AI

## Overview

Shield AI is a San Diego-based defense technology company building autonomous AI pilots for military aircraft. Founded in 2015 by brothers Brandon Tseng (a Navy SEAL) and Ryan Tseng (a serial entrepreneur), along with robotics engineer Andrew Reiter, the company's core thesis is that the future of airpower is autonomous -- and the AI pilot, not the airframe, is the decisive technology.

The company's flagship product is **Hivemind**, an AI autonomy software stack that enables aircraft to fly, navigate, and complete missions without GPS, communications, or human remote control. Hivemind has been operationally validated since 2019 and deployed on over fifteen different platforms.

## Products

- **Hivemind** -- The core AI pilot software. Provides perception, planning, and decision-making for autonomous flight in contested, GPS-denied, and comms-denied environments. Licensed to allies and integrated onto third-party platforms. Selected as mission autonomy provider for the U.S. Air Force CCA program (Feb 2026).

- **Nova** -- Small autonomous quadcopter (sUAS) for indoor reconnaissance and mapping. Uses lidar for GPS-agnostic navigation. Originally designed for special operations forces clearing buildings. Shield AI's first product.

- **V-BAT (MQ-35)** -- Group 3 VTOL unmanned aircraft with a distinctive ducted-fan design. Over 12 hours of endurance. Acquired via the Martin UAV purchase in 2021. Operationally deployed across the Black Sea, Caribbean, Middle East, and Ukraine. Won the U.S. Navy's Mi2 competition in 2021 over 13 competitors. Now powered by Hivemind for autonomous operations.

- **X-BAT** -- AI-piloted VTOL fighter jet announced October 2025. Designed for expeditionary and maritime operations in contested environments. Powered by Hivemind. First VTOL flights planned for fall 2026; full mission capability flights targeted for 2028. Represents Shield AI's push from ISR drones into combat aircraft.

## Team

**Brandon Tseng** (Co-Founder & President) is a former Navy SEAL who graduated from the U.S. Naval Academy and served two combat tours in Afghanistan. His frontline experience -- seeing soldiers enter buildings without intelligence on what was inside -- directly inspired Shield AI's founding mission. After his military career, he earned an MBA at Harvard Business School. Named to TIME's 100 Most Influential People in AI (2025).

**Ryan Tseng** (Co-Founder & President, formerly CEO) is Brandon's brother and a serial entrepreneur. He holds a BSEE from the University of Florida and an MBA from MIT Sloan. Before Shield AI, he founded WiPower Inc., a wireless charging startup acquired by Qualcomm in 2010, where he then served as Technical Lead for Wireless Power. Ryan led Shield AI as CEO for its first decade, stepping aside in May 2025 to focus on government and defense industry relationships.

**Andrew Reiter** (Co-Founder & CTO) is a robotics and computer vision engineer who previously worked at Draper Laboratory (formerly MIT's Instrumentation Lab), where he developed computer vision applications. He is the technical architect behind Hivemind.

**Gary Steele** (CEO, joined May 2025) was brought in to scale Shield AI through its next growth phase. Steele previously served as President, Go-to-Market at Cisco, and before that was CEO of Splunk (grew revenue 58%, led to profitability, orchestrated $28B acquisition by Cisco). He also founded Proofpoint, taking it from zero to IPO and an eventual $12B enterprise value.

**Key acquisitions that brought in talent:**
- **Heron Systems** (acquired July 2021) -- AI company whose AI agent defeated a human F-16 pilot 5-0 in DARPA's AlphaDogfight Trials. Brought AI dogfighting and tactical decision-making expertise.
- **Martin UAV** (acquired July 2021) -- Brought the V-BAT platform and its engineering team.

## What makes them interesting

1. **Software-first in a hardware-dominated industry.** While most defense drone companies compete on airframes, Shield AI's core asset is Hivemind -- a platform-agnostic AI pilot that can be integrated onto any aircraft. This positions them as the "Android of autonomous flight," licensing AI to allies and even competitors' platforms (e.g., Anduril's Fury).

2. **CCA program is a potential generational contract.** Shield AI was selected in February 2026 as mission autonomy provider for the USAF Collaborative Combat Aircraft program -- the military's flagship effort to field AI-piloted fighter wingmen alongside F-35s. The CCA is structured as a multi-decade, multi-billion-dollar production program. Hivemind successfully flew on Anduril's YFQ-44A Fury, and in a March 2026 test, the aircraft switched mid-flight between Shield AI's Hivemind and Anduril's Lattice -- demonstrating interchangeable autonomy software on a combat aircraft for the first time.

3. **Battle-tested in Ukraine.** V-BATs have been operationally deployed in Ukraine and have withstood electronic warfare (EW) attacks that downed competing UAVs. This real-world survivability in a contested EW environment is a powerful differentiator and validation of Hivemind's GPS-denied navigation.

4. **Navy SEAL founder creates authentic customer empathy.** Brandon Tseng's personal combat experience gives Shield AI unusual credibility with military operators. The company's origin story -- "soldiers entering buildings blind" -- resonates deeply with its customer base and drives product design decisions.

5. **X-BAT represents a bold vertical integration play.** By unveiling the X-BAT autonomous VTOL fighter jet (October 2025), Shield AI is moving from being a software/small-drone company to designing its own combat aircraft. If successful, this transforms them from a software licensor into a full-stack autonomous combat aircraft company.

6. **International expansion.** Shield AI is one of the few U.S. defense tech startups actively selling to allied militaries -- India, Singapore, South Korea, Romania, Indonesia, Japan. This diversifies revenue beyond the U.S. DoD and creates a network effect for Hivemind adoption.

## Risks and open questions

- **Revenue scale vs. valuation.** At ~$300M revenue and a potential $12B valuation, the company trades at ~40x revenue -- rich even by defense tech standards. Execution on the 70-100% growth target is essential.
- **CCA program uncertainty.** The Collaborative Combat Aircraft program is subject to Congressional appropriation decisions, potential budget cuts, and shifting Pentagon priorities. A slowdown or cancellation would remove Shield AI's largest growth catalyst.
- **X-BAT execution risk.** Designing and manufacturing a fighter jet is a different order of magnitude from building software and small drones. First flights are not expected until fall 2026 and full capability not until 2028.
- **Leadership transition.** Gary Steele joined as CEO in May 2025, replacing founder Ryan Tseng. Steele is a proven enterprise software executive but has no prior defense industry experience. The transition is still early.
- **IPO timing.** No IPO date has been announced. The company has substantial private capital runway but will eventually face pressure to provide liquidity to investors.
- **Competition.** Anduril (Lattice), General Atomics, Lockheed Martin Skunk Works, and Boeing's MQ-28 Ghost Bat all compete in autonomous military aviation. [Confidence: high on competitive landscape; uncertain on relative positioning.]
