# Artisan AI

## One-liner

AI employee platform that builds autonomous digital workers for sales, starting with Ava, an AI BDR that finds leads, writes personalized emails, runs outbound sequences, and self-optimizes -- positioning itself as a replacement for human sales development reps.

## Founders & Leadership

### Jaspar Carmichael-Jack -- Co-founder & CEO

- Young founder who gained attention for Artisan's provocative "Stop Hiring Humans" marketing campaign, which generated significant controversy and brand awareness.
- Y Combinator alum.

### Sam Stallings -- Co-founder

- Former IBM product manager.
- Brings enterprise product experience to the team.

## Founding Story

Founded in 2023, Artisan emerged from Y Combinator with a bold thesis: AI agents should be treated as digital employees, not tools. The company gained notoriety for its provocative outdoor advertising campaign in San Francisco featuring the tagline "Stop Hiring Humans," which drew both attention and backlash. Despite the controversy, the campaign succeeded in making Artisan one of the most recognized names in the AI SDR space within its first year.

## Product & Business Model

### What Artisan Does

Artisan builds AI "employees" (called Artisans) for business functions, starting with sales:

1. **Ava** (AI BDR) -- The flagship product. Ava automates the full outbound sales workflow: lead discovery and research, personalized email writing in the customer's tone of voice, sequence management, self-optimization based on response data, and email deliverability infrastructure management.
2. **Aaron** (AI Inbound Agent) -- Handles inbound message responses (launched/launching 2025).
3. **Aria** (AI Meeting Manager) -- Meeting scheduling and management assistant (launched/launching 2025).

### Pricing / Monetization

SaaS subscription model. Artisan positions pricing against the fully loaded cost of a human BDR ($70K-$120K/year), offering the AI alternative at a significant discount.

Artisan differentiates through aggressive brand marketing, a multi-product "AI employee" roadmap (Ava, Aaron, Aria), and strong email deliverability infrastructure.

## What Makes Artisan Interesting

1. **Controversial but effective marketing.** The "Stop Hiring Humans" campaign was one of the most talked-about AI startup marketing moves of 2024, generating massive brand awareness at low cost.
2. **Multi-agent product roadmap.** Expanding beyond outbound (Ava) to inbound (Aaron) and meetings (Aria) signals a platform play to own the full sales workflow.
3. **Y Combinator pedigree.** YC backing provides access to a strong network of early-adopter B2B customers.
4. **Speed to scale.** Reaching 250 customers and ~$6M ARR within roughly 18 months of launch is strong traction for an enterprise SaaS product.

## Risks & Open Questions

- **Brand polarization.** The "Stop Hiring Humans" messaging alienates some potential customers and has drawn criticism from the tech community.
- **LinkedIn and platform bans.** Reports of Artisan-related outreach being flagged on LinkedIn could limit distribution.
- **Competitive pressure.** 11x raised more capital and reportedly has higher ARR; Artisan needs to differentiate on product quality.
- **Employee vs. tool framing.** The "AI employee" positioning may face regulatory scrutiny as labor laws evolve.
