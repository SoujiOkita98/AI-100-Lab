# Simile

## Overview

Simile is an AI startup spun out of Stanford University that builds high-fidelity digital twins — AI agent populations trained to simulate how real people think, decide, and behave. The company's core technology originated from the widely cited 2023 "Generative Agents: Interactive Simulacra of Human Behavior" paper (commonly known as the "Smallville" paper), which demonstrated that LLM-based agents could exhibit believable humanlike behavior in a simulated town environment.

Founded by Joon Sung Park (CEO), Michael Bernstein (CPO), Percy Liang (Chief Scientist), and Elaina "Lainie" Yallen (CCO), the company raised a $100M Series A led by Index Ventures in February 2026 after approximately seven months in stealth. No prior seed round has been publicly reported.

## Technology

Simile's platform generates AI agent populations by training on three categories of data:

- **Deep interview transcripts** — extended conversations with real people about their lives, preferences, and decision-making processes.
- **Historical transaction and behavioral data** — sourced from enterprise customers (e.g., CVS Health contributed ~3 million consented responses from 400,000+ people).
- **Behavioral science literature** — academic research on human cognition and choice behavior.

These agents can then be placed in simulated scenarios to predict how populations of real people would react to product launches, policy changes, pricing decisions, corporate communications, and more. The company claims approximately 85% accuracy in predicting human behavior, though this figure has not been independently validated at scale.

## Business Model

Simile operates as an enterprise SaaS platform. Customers pay annual contracts ranging from ~$150,000 to several million dollars to access Simile's AI agent simulations. The company positions itself as a faster, cheaper, and more accurate alternative to traditional market research methods — focus groups, surveys, and A/B testing.

## Key Use Cases

- **Retail/CPG:** CVS Health uses Simile to test product placement, optimize inventory decisions, and prepare for earnings calls.
- **Financial services:** Wealthfront and Banco Itau are listed as customers.
- **Telecommunications:** Telstra uses the platform for consumer modeling.
- **Private equity:** Garnett Station Partners applies simulations to investment due diligence.
- **Litigation modeling:** Companies use Simile to model litigation outcomes before proceeding.

## Founding Team

The founding team is unusual in that it includes two Stanford CS professors, a PhD student, and a business-side co-founder — all from the same university ecosystem:

- **Joon Sung Park (CEO):** Lead author of the Generative Agents paper. Stanford CS PhD candidate, Microsoft Research Fellow, Siebel Scholar. Previously studied at Swarthmore (BA) and UIUC (MS).
- **Michael Bernstein (CPO):** Stanford Associate Professor of CS. Co-author on the ImageNet project. HCI and crowdsourcing expert.
- **Percy Liang (Chief Scientist):** Stanford Professor of CS. Director of CRFM. Coined the term "foundation model." Co-founded Together.ai.
- **Elaina "Lainie" Yallen (CCO):** Stanford-affiliated. Specific prior career background not well documented publicly.

## Investors

The $100M Series A was led by **Index Ventures** (partner Shardul Shah), with participation from **Bain Capital Ventures**, **A***, and **Hanabi Capital**. Notable angel investors include **Fei-Fei Li** (Stanford professor, ImageNet creator, World Labs founder), **Andrej Karpathy** (ex-Tesla AI, ex-OpenAI), **Adam D'Angelo** (Quora CEO, OpenAI board member), **Guillermo Rauch** (Vercel CEO), and **Scott Belsky** (Adobe CPO).
