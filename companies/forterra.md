# Forterra

## Overview

Forterra develops autonomous systems for defense and military applications, enabling ground vehicles to operate independently, in coordinated teams, or in full swarm formations. Founded in 2002, the company has been building autonomous vehicle technology for the U.S. Department of Defense for over two decades, making it one of the most established players in defense autonomy.

The company recently opened an office in Arlington, Virginia to be closer to Pentagon decision-makers, signaling aggressive growth in government contracts.

## Funding Story

Forterra's $238M Series C in early 2026, led by Moore Strategic Ventures, pushed the company past unicorn status at a $1B+ valuation. The round attracted a diverse investor base spanning defense-focused funds (Enlightenment Capital, NightDragon), strategic players (Hanwha, Salesforce Ventures), and generalist investors (Franklin Templeton, Balyasny).

## What Makes Them Interesting

1. **Proven defense contracts.** Over 20 years of autonomous systems development for the U.S. military gives Forterra deep institutional relationships and contract history.
2. **Swarm autonomy.** The ability to coordinate multiple autonomous vehicles in formation is a key capability the DoD is actively pursuing.
3. **Defense spending tailwinds.** 2026 defense budgets are prioritizing AI-enabled systems, autonomous platforms, and collaborative combat vehicles -- Forterra's exact capabilities.
4. **Interoperability focus.** The company emphasizes working across vehicle types and existing military platforms, reducing adoption friction.
