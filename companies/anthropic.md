# Anthropic

## Overview

Anthropic is an AI safety company and one of the three leading frontier model labs globally (alongside OpenAI and Google DeepMind). Founded in 2021 by seven former OpenAI researchers -- siblings Dario and Daniela Amodei, Tom Brown, Jack Clark, Jared Kaplan, Sam McCandlish, and Chris Olah -- Anthropic builds the Claude family of large language models. The company's stated mission is the responsible development and maintenance of advanced AI for the long-term benefit of humanity.

Anthropic is headquartered in San Francisco and, as of March 2026, is valued at $380 billion, making it one of the most valuable private companies in the world. It has raised approximately $67.3 billion in total funding across 17 rounds. The company's annual revenue run rate reached $19 billion in March 2026, with 85% derived from enterprise and API customers -- a fundamentally different mix than OpenAI's consumer-heavy model.

## Funding story

Anthropic's funding trajectory is one of the most aggressive capital accumulation stories in venture history.

**2021 -- Seed/Series A ($124M at $550M valuation).** The company launched with backing from Jaan Tallinn (Skype co-founder) and Dustin Moskovitz (Facebook co-founder), both connected to the effective altruism community.

**2022 -- Series B ($580M at ~$4B valuation).** Led by FTX and Sam Bankman-Fried. This would later become awkward after FTX's collapse in November 2022, though Anthropic's operations were not materially affected.

**2023 -- A pivotal year.** Google invested approximately $2 billion across multiple tranches starting in early 2023. Spark Capital led the $450M Series C. Most importantly, Amazon committed $4 billion in a landmark strategic deal (initial $1.25B in September 2023, remainder in March 2024), with Anthropic naming AWS its primary cloud provider and making Claude available through Amazon Bedrock.

**2024 -- Acceleration.** Menlo Ventures led a $750M Series D at an $18.4B valuation (structured as an SPV). Amazon doubled its total commitment to $8 billion with another $4B round in November, valuing Anthropic at ~$41B.

**2025 -- Into the stratosphere.** The $3.5B Series E led by Lightspeed ($1B from Lightspeed alone) at $61.5B valuation was followed by a massive $13B Series F at $183B, led by GIC (Singapore sovereign wealth fund) and Coatue.

**2026 -- Record-breaking.** In February 2026, Anthropic closed a $30B Series G at $380B valuation -- the second-largest venture funding deal of all time, behind only OpenAI's $40B raise. Led by GIC, Coatue, D.E. Shaw, Founders Fund, ICONIQ, and MGX. Microsoft and Nvidia also participated, notable given their existing relationships with OpenAI and Google respectively.

The investor base is remarkable for its breadth: both major cloud hyperscalers (Amazon, Google, Microsoft), sovereign wealth funds (GIC, MGX/Abu Dhabi), and elite venture funds (Lightspeed, Sequoia, Founders Fund, Menlo).

## Team

**Founding team profile:** The seven co-founders are unified by a shared OpenAI background and a conviction that AI development requires a safety-first approach. The team is unusually heavy on theoretical physicists (Kaplan, McCandlish, Dario Amodei) -- a distinctive feature that may explain the company's emphasis on scaling laws and empirical rigor.

- **Dario Amodei (CEO):** The intellectual leader. Princeton PhD in biophysics. Co-invented RLHF. Led GPT-2 and GPT-3 development at OpenAI. Italian-American and Jewish-American heritage.
- **Daniela Amodei (President):** The operational leader. Non-technical background (English literature, politics, Stripe). Managed OpenAI during critical growth phase. Handles business, policy, and culture.
- **Jared Kaplan (Chief Science Officer):** Johns Hopkins physics professor. Co-authored the foundational scaling laws paper. His academic rigor underpins Anthropic's research strategy.
- **Sam McCandlish (Chief Architect):** Stanford theoretical physics PhD. Instrumental in GPT-3 at OpenAI. Oversees Claude's architecture.
- **Chris Olah (Research Director):** Canadian. Self-taught (dropped out, Thiel Fellow). Pioneer of mechanistic interpretability. His work on understanding neural networks is arguably Anthropic's core intellectual differentiator.
- **Tom Brown:** MIT. Led GPT-3 engineering. Previously at Google Brain.
- **Jack Clark (Head of Policy):** Former tech journalist (Bloomberg). Built OpenAI's policy function. Publishes the influential "Import AI" newsletter.

**Key hires:** Krishna Rao (first CFO, 2024), Mike Krieger (Instagram co-founder, Head of Product 2024-2025), Jan Leike (left OpenAI's superalignment team, now leads safety), Paul Smith (first CCO), Chris Ciauri (MD International, ex-Google Cloud).

## What makes them interesting

1. **Enterprise-first model works.** 85% of revenue from business customers vs. OpenAI's 85% from consumers. 40% of enterprise LLM spending now goes to Claude, surpassing OpenAI (27%) and Google (21%). This is arguably a more defensible position.

2. **Growth rate is extraordinary.** From ~$1B ARR in late 2024 to $19B annualized in March 2026. Claude Code alone is at $2.5B annualized and doubling rapidly.

3. **Safety as a moat.** Constitutional AI, mechanistic interpretability research, and the Responsible Scaling Policy are genuine technical innovations, not just marketing. This resonates with enterprise buyers (European Parliament, Deloitte) who need governance stories.

4. **Scaling laws insight.** The founding team literally wrote the paper on neural scaling laws. This gives them an unusually principled approach to compute allocation and model development.

5. **Investor diversification as strategy.** By taking money from Amazon, Google, Microsoft, and sovereign wealth funds, Anthropic has secured compute access across multiple clouds while maintaining independence. No single investor controls the board.

6. **Claude Code as a second product.** The $2.5B run rate on Claude Code (a developer-facing agentic coding tool) shows the company can build products beyond the core API/chatbot, opening a large new market.

## Notes

- **FTX exposure:** The Series B was led by FTX. After FTX's collapse, the bankruptcy estate sold its Anthropic stake. This was a reputational headache but not a financial one.
- **Valuation concern:** At $380B and ~$19B ARR, the company trades at roughly 20x revenue -- high but not absurd by AI frontier lab standards. The $70B 2028 revenue target, if achieved, would make the valuation look reasonable.
- **IPO watch:** Multiple reports suggest Anthropic is preparing for an IPO, potentially in 2026 or 2027. Forge Global and secondary market activity point to strong pre-IPO interest.
- **Compute dependency:** Despite investor diversification, Anthropic is heavily dependent on AWS infrastructure per the Amazon deal terms. This is both an advantage (guaranteed capacity) and a constraint.
- **Competitive position:** Anthropic sits in a strong but pressured position between OpenAI (larger, consumer-dominant) and Google DeepMind (deeper research bench, integrated distribution). Its enterprise moat and safety reputation are real differentiators, but the capital requirements to stay at the frontier are immense -- hence the aggressive fundraising.
- **Confidence note:** Funding figures for Series A-C have some variance across sources. The numbers above represent the most commonly cited figures. Series E onward are well-documented by primary sources (Anthropic press releases, CNBC, Bloomberg). Total raised figure of $67.3B comes from Tracxn and may include smaller undisclosed rounds.
