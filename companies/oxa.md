# Oxa

## Overview

Oxa (formerly Oxbotica) is a UK-based autonomous driving software company founded in 2014, spun out of Oxford University by robotics professors Paul Newman and Ingmar Posner. Unlike the high-profile consumer autonomous vehicle companies (Waymo, Cruise, Tesla FSD), Oxa focuses exclusively on industrial vehicle autonomy -- ports, airports, manufacturing sites, and logistics hubs.

In March 2026, the company raised $103 million in the first close of its Series D, anchored by a $50 million commitment from the UK's National Wealth Fund, with Nvidia's NVentures, IP Group, Hostplus, and bp Ventures also participating. Total funding stands at $277 million.

## Funding story

Oxa has raised $277 million across multiple rounds, with the most recent being a $103M first close of Series D in March 2026. A second and final close is expected in H1 2026.

The $50 million commitment from the UK's National Wealth Fund is strategically significant -- it positions Oxa as a national champion for UK autonomous vehicle technology and provides a government-backed signal to other investors and customers.

## Team

- **Paul Newman (CTO & Co-Founder):** Oxford professor of Information Engineering. One of the UK's most prominent robotics researchers. Provides the deep technical foundation.
- **Ingmar Posner (CSO & Co-Founder):** Oxford associate professor. ML for robotics expert. Complements Newman's systems focus with machine learning expertise.

## What makes them interesting

1. **Industrial AV is the smarter market.** Consumer autonomous driving (Waymo, Tesla) gets the headlines but faces enormous regulatory, safety, and public perception challenges. Industrial autonomy in controlled environments (ports, factories, airports) has lower regulatory barriers, more predictable environments, and clearer ROI for customers.

2. **Real customers, real deployments.** DHL, Vantec, and bp are not pilot customers -- they are major industrial operators. This is a level of commercial traction that many AV companies at similar funding levels cannot claim.

3. **UK National Wealth Fund backing** provides a layer of strategic protection and credibility that pure venture-backed competitors lack. It also de-risks the company against the US-China dynamic in autonomous vehicles.

4. **Oxford pedigree.** The company benefits from one of the world's strongest robotics research ecosystems and a continuous pipeline of talent.

5. **Geographic differentiation.** While US and Chinese AV companies focus on their home markets, Oxa is targeting UK, Europe, and the Middle East -- large markets where Western autonomous vehicle technology is preferred but US companies have limited presence.

## Notes

- **Valuation not disclosed** for the Series D, which makes it harder to assess capital efficiency.
- **Scale question:** $277M total raised is modest compared to Waymo ($16B), Aurora, or Chinese AV companies. Oxa's industrial focus is capital-efficient relative to consumer AV, but scaling across multiple geographies and verticals will require significantly more capital.
- **Founded in 2014** -- this is a 12-year-old company, not a hot new startup. The long gestation period is common in AV but raises questions about pace of commercialization.
- **Nvidia participation** (NVentures) is strategically important for hardware partnership and market validation.
