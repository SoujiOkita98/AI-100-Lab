# Rune Technologies

## One-liner

AI-enabled predictive logistics software for the U.S. military, bringing modern software to replace legacy spreadsheet-based supply chain management.

## Founders & Leadership

### David Tuttle -- Co-Founder & CEO

- Former U.S. Army officer with firsthand experience of military logistics pain points.
- Anduril Industries alumnus -- part of the defense tech vanguard.

### Peter Goldsborough -- Co-Founder & CTO

- Former Meta engineer with deep AI/ML expertise.
- Anduril Industries alumnus who saw the opportunity to modernize military supply chains.

## Founding Story

Founded in 2024 by two Anduril Industries alumni, Rune Technologies was born from the founders' direct observation that U.S. military logistics -- responsible for maintaining billions of dollars of equipment -- still runs largely on Excel spreadsheets and legacy systems. David Tuttle's military background combined with Peter Goldsborough's AI engineering skills created the ideal founding team to tackle this problem.

## Product & Business Model

### What Rune Technologies Does

1. **TyrOS** -- Flagship operating system for military logistics that transforms how the military tracks, predicts, and manages all aspects of supply chain and maintenance.
2. **Predictive maintenance** -- Uses AI to forecast equipment failures and optimize parts supply chains.
3. **Real-time visibility** -- Provides commanders with unified visibility into logistics status across units and equipment.

### Pricing / Monetization

Government contracts with the U.S. Department of Defense. Currently deployed under both U.S. Army and U.S. Marine Corps programs.

## What Makes Rune Technologies Interesting

1. **Anduril alumni pedigree.** Founders learned defense tech from one of the most successful modern defense startups.
2. **Clear pain point.** Military logistics on spreadsheets is an obvious modernization opportunity.
3. **Already deployed.** Active deployments with U.S. Army and Marine Corps provide revenue and validation.

## Risks & Open Questions

- Government procurement cycles are notoriously slow and unpredictable.

- Competition from Palantir and other defense platforms expanding into logistics.
