# TeraDAR

## Overview

TeraDAR is developing the world's first solid-state terahertz vision sensors for autonomous systems. The company's proprietary silicon-based chipsets operate in the terahertz band (between microwave and infrared), delivering 4D imaging with detailed spatial and temporal resolution in all weather and lighting conditions -- something neither lidar nor traditional radar can achieve.

Incubated at MIT's The Engine, TeraDAR spent years in stealth developing its sensing technology before emerging in late 2025 with $150M in funding. In January 2026, the company revealed its first terahertz-band vision sensor designed for automotive applications.

## Founding Story

TeraDAR was born from conversations between CEO Matt Carey and Gregory Charvat (CTO of spatial intelligence company Humatics) about the possibilities of terahertz imaging. They launched the company in 2021 with MIT's The Engine leading the seed round, bringing in Nick Saiz for his terahertz chip design expertise.

## What Makes Them Interesting

1. **New sensing modality.** Terahertz sits between radar and lidar on the electromagnetic spectrum, potentially offering the best of both worlds: high resolution and all-weather operation.
2. **Lockheed Martin backing.** Lockheed Martin Ventures as an investor signals defense and aerospace applications beyond automotive.
3. **MIT pedigree.** Incubated at The Engine, MIT's hard-tech venture fund, which selects for breakthrough science.
4. **Automotive + defense dual-use.** The sensor technology applies to both autonomous vehicles and military ISR, doubling the addressable market.
