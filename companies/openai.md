# OpenAI

## Overview

OpenAI is the company most responsible for igniting the generative AI era. Founded in December 2015 as a nonprofit AI research lab, it has since transformed into the world's most valuable private company (post-money ~$840B as of February 2026). Its products -- GPT-series large language models and the ChatGPT consumer application -- redefined public understanding of artificial intelligence when ChatGPT launched in November 2022 and reached 100 million users within two months.

The company completed a controversial restructuring in October 2025, splitting into the nonprofit OpenAI Foundation (retaining a 26% stake) and the for-profit OpenAI Group, a public benefit corporation. Microsoft holds 27% of the for-profit entity.

OpenAI now serves 910 million weekly active users, over 9 million paying business users, and more than 1 million organizations. Its product line spans ChatGPT (consumer), an enterprise suite, a developer API platform, and the Sora video generation tool.

## Funding story

OpenAI's capital trajectory is unlike anything in technology history. It went from a $100M nonprofit research lab to an $840B company in a decade.

**2015 -- The nonprofit origins.** A group including Sam Altman, Elon Musk, Ilya Sutskever, Greg Brockman, and others pledged $1B to found OpenAI as a nonprofit. Musk contributed roughly $38M (about 60% of initial deployed capital). The mission: ensure artificial general intelligence benefits all of humanity.

**2019 -- The Microsoft era begins.** OpenAI created a "capped-profit" subsidiary (OpenAI LP) and took $1B from Microsoft, roughly half in Azure credits. This marked the philosophical shift from pure nonprofit to commercial entity.

**2021-2023 -- Microsoft doubles and triples down.** An estimated $2B in interim funding, followed by a landmark ~$10B investment in January 2023, shortly after ChatGPT went viral. This valued OpenAI at roughly $29B and gave Microsoft exclusive cloud-provider status and a significant profit share.

**October 2024 -- First traditional VC round.** Thrive Capital led a $6.6B round at $157B valuation. SoftBank, Nvidia, Khosla, Fidelity, and others participated. The round carried a condition: OpenAI must convert to a for-profit within two years, or the equity converts to debt.

**March 2025 -- SoftBank mega-round.** $40B raised at $300B valuation, the largest private fundraise on record at the time. SoftBank led.

**October 2025 -- Secondary at $500B.** Employees and early investors sold $6.6B in shares at a $500B valuation, making OpenAI the world's most valuable startup, surpassing SpaceX. No new capital entered the company.

**February 2026 -- The $110B round.** Amazon ($50B), Nvidia ($30B), and SoftBank ($30B) invested in what became the largest private financing in history. Pre-money valuation: $730B. The round included an expanded $100B AWS partnership over eight years, with AWS becoming the exclusive third-party cloud distribution provider for OpenAI's enterprise platform. The round remains open to additional investors.

**Total raised: ~$168B** across all rounds. Despite this, OpenAI is projected to lose approximately $14B in 2026 due to massive compute infrastructure costs.

## Team

**Founders.** OpenAI had 11 co-founders, reflecting an unusual breadth of talent and ambition:

- **Sam Altman** (CEO) -- Jewish American from St. Louis. Stanford dropout, built Loopt, ran Y Combinator. The public face of OpenAI and arguably the most influential figure in the AI industry. Briefly fired by the board in November 2023, reinstated within days after mass employee protest.
- **Greg Brockman** (President) -- American from small-town North Dakota. MIT dropout, former CTO of Stripe. The operational backbone of OpenAI's early engineering.
- **Ilya Sutskever** (former Chief Scientist) -- Born in Russia, raised in Israel, educated in Canada under Geoffrey Hinton. Co-authored the AlexNet paper that launched the deep learning revolution. Departed OpenAI in mid-2024 to found Safe Superintelligence Inc. (SSI). One of the board members who voted to fire Altman in 2023.
- **Elon Musk** (former co-chair) -- South African-American. Provided critical early funding and credibility. Left in 2018, later became an adversary, suing OpenAI over alleged mission abandonment and launching rival lab xAI (creator of Grok).
- Other co-founders include **Wojciech Zaremba** (Polish, NYU PhD), **John Schulman** (American, RL pioneer -- departed to Anthropic in 2024), **Andrej Karpathy** (Slovak-Canadian, Stanford PhD, ex-Tesla Autopilot lead -- departed 2024), and several others.

**Current leadership.** The executive team has been heavily refreshed:
- **Fidji Simo** -- CEO of Applications (ex-Instacart CEO, ex-Meta)
- **Mark Chen** -- Chief Research Officer
- **Jakub Pachocki** -- Chief Scientist (succeeded Sutskever)
- **Brad Lightcap** -- COO
- **Denise Dresser** -- CRO (ex-Slack CEO)
- **Kevin Weil** -- VP of AI for Science (formerly CPO)

**China connection.** OpenAI has no Chinese-origin founders or prominent mainland Chinese executives. The company restricted API access in China starting mid-2024. This is a notable contrast to some competitors (e.g., Anthropic's Dario Amodei has Italian-American roots; several other AI startups have Chinese-origin founders).

## What makes them interesting

1. **Market-defining products.** ChatGPT created the consumer AI category. GPT-4 set the frontier benchmark. The API platform became the default infrastructure for thousands of AI startups.

2. **Revenue velocity.** From $0 to $20B ARR in roughly three years (2022-2025). First $1B revenue month in July 2025. This is the fastest revenue ramp in software history, surpassing even the early trajectories of Google and Facebook.

3. **Capital scale without precedent.** $168B total raised, including a single $110B round. OpenAI operates at a financial scale previously reserved for sovereign wealth funds and nation-state infrastructure projects.

4. **Strategic partnerships as moats.** Microsoft (cloud, distribution via Copilot/Azure), Amazon/AWS (government, enterprise distribution), Apple (device-level integration in iPhones/Macs), Disney (entertainment content). These partnerships embed OpenAI into platforms reaching billions of users.

5. **Government positioning.** Pentagon contract ($200M), classified AWS deal, and positioning as the U.S. government's preferred AI provider following a ban on competitor Anthropic from government contracts (March 2026).

6. **The nonprofit-to-megacorp transformation.** The organizational arc -- from idealistic nonprofit to $840B for-profit juggernaut -- is one of the most dramatic corporate evolutions in technology history. The October 2025 restructuring into a PBC with the nonprofit retaining 26% attempts to preserve some original mission alignment.

7. **Talent exodus is a risk signal.** Multiple co-founders have departed: Sutskever (to SSI), Schulman (to Anthropic), Karpathy (independent). The November 2023 board crisis revealed deep internal tensions about safety vs. commercialization.

## Notes

- **Valuation confidence:** High for post-2024 rounds (well-documented). Microsoft-era valuations (2019-2023) are less precisely sourced because the deals had unusual structures (Azure credits, profit-sharing, not traditional equity rounds). The early round amounts labeled "Series A/B/C" on some tracking sites (Tracxn) appear to be retroactive classifications and may not reflect formal round designations.
- **Profitability:** Despite $20B+ ARR, OpenAI projects $14B in losses for 2026. The path to profitability depends on compute cost curves declining faster than revenue growth.
- **IPO speculation:** No confirmed timeline, but the PBC restructuring and massive late-stage rounds suggest an eventual public listing. The $280B revenue projection for 2030 (per Fortune, Feb 2026) implies a growth rate that would need to be sustained at extraordinary levels.
- **Competitive landscape:** Key competitors include Anthropic (Claude), Google DeepMind (Gemini), Meta AI (Llama), xAI (Grok), and Mistral. The open-source model ecosystem (Llama, DeepSeek) poses a commoditization risk to API revenue.
