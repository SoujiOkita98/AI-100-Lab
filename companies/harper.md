# Harper

## Overview

Harper is an AI-native commercial insurance brokerage that uses artificial intelligence to automate the operational workflows traditionally handled by human brokers -- including submission routing, underwriter follow-ups, document collection, and pipeline management. The company serves small and midsize businesses (SMBs) across workers' compensation, general liability, and professional liability, matching buyers to more than 160 insurance carriers.

Harper was part of Y Combinator's W25 batch and raised $47M in combined seed and Series A funding less than a year after founding. The company has grown to over 5,000 cumulative customers and $6M+ in annualized premiums written across 35 states.

## Founders and Team

### Dakotah Rice (CEO)
- Worked in Investment Banking on Goldman Sachs' Technology, Media, and Telecommunications (TMT) team.
- Co-founded Poolit with Tushar Nair, an investment company that scaled to $100M AUM before closing in 2023.
- Brings deep financial services and business development expertise.

## Business Model

### How It Works

1. **AI-powered brokerage:** Harper operates as a licensed insurance brokerage where AI handles the majority of operational tasks -- routing submissions to appropriate carriers, following up with underwriters, collecting and processing documents, and managing the sales pipeline.
2. **Carrier network:** The platform connects SMB customers to 160+ insurance carriers, optimizing for coverage quality and price.
3. **Revenue model:** As a licensed brokerage, Harper earns commissions on insurance policies sold, similar to traditional brokers but with dramatically lower operational costs due to AI automation.
4. **Target market:** SMBs in "middle America" -- daycares, manufacturers, car dealerships, local bars and restaurants -- businesses that are typically underserved by large insurance brokerages.

### Traction (as of early 2026)
- 5,000+ cumulative customers.
- $6M+ in annualized premiums written.
- Operating across 35 states.
- Handling 1,000+ customers per month using AI automation.

## What Makes Harper Interesting

1. **AI applied to a massive, inefficient market:** Commercial insurance brokerage is a $100B+ market characterized by manual processes, paper documents, and phone calls. AI automation can dramatically improve margins and customer experience.

2. **Experienced financial services founders:** Both founders spent significant time at Goldman Sachs and previously co-founded a fintech company together, giving them deep domain expertise and a strong working relationship.

3. **Strong early traction:** Growing to 5,000 customers and $6M in annualized premiums within months of launch, while raising $47M, signals product-market fit.

4. **Scalability thesis:** If AI can handle 90%+ of brokerage operations, Harper's marginal cost per customer approaches zero -- enabling it to profitably serve SMBs that traditional brokers consider too small.

5. **Risks and open questions:**
   - **Regulatory complexity:** Insurance is heavily regulated state by state; scaling across all 50 states requires navigating different regulatory frameworks.
   - **Trust and accuracy:** Insurance is a high-stakes purchase; errors in coverage recommendations could lead to significant liability.
   - **Incumbent response:** Large brokerages (Marsh, Aon, Willis Towers Watson) and InsurTech competitors could adopt similar AI capabilities.
