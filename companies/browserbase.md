# Browserbase

## Overview

Browserbase is a San Francisco-based cloud browser infrastructure company founded in 2024 by solo founder Paul Klein IV. The platform provides headless browser sessions as a service, enabling AI agents and developers to interact with the web at scale. Browserbase has served over 50 million browser sessions and is used by 1,000+ companies and 20,000+ developers.

The company has raised $67.5M in total funding across three rounds in under 16 months, reaching a $300M valuation at its April 2025 Series B led by Notable Capital. Notable angel investors include the CEOs and founders of Twilio, Vercel, Stripe, and Okta.

## Funding story

**June 2024 -- Seed ($6.5M).** Co-led by Kleiner Perkins and Basecase Capital, with AI Grant. Paul Klein famously applied to 500+ investors before closing this round.

**October 2024 -- Series A ($21M).** Co-led by CRV and Kleiner Perkins. Okta Ventures participated. Just four months after the seed, reflecting rapid traction.

**April 2025 -- Series B ($40M at $300M valuation).** Led by Notable Capital, with CRV and Kleiner Perkins continuing. Nearly 4x the Series A valuation. Launched Director, a no-code web automation tool.

## What makes them interesting

1. **Infrastructure layer for the agentic web.** As AI agents proliferate, they need to interact with the web -- browse pages, fill forms, click buttons, extract data. Browserbase provides this fundamental capability as managed infrastructure.

2. **Solo founder resilience.** Paul Klein built Browserbase solo after 500+ investor rejections, growing to nearly 50 employees and $300M valuation in 16 months. This is one of the more compelling solo founder stories in current tech.

3. **Network effect through angel investors.** Having the CEOs of Twilio, Vercel, Stripe, and Okta as angel investors creates powerful distribution channels and technical validation in the developer infrastructure space.
