# Tome

## Company Overview

Tome was an AI-native storytelling and presentation platform founded in 2020 by former Meta executives Keith Peiris and Henri Liriani. The company became one of the fastest-growing productivity tools in history, reaching 1 million users within weeks of its public launch in late 2022 and scaling to over 25 million users by mid-2025. Tome allowed users to generate entire presentations -- text, images, and layouts -- from a single prompt, positioning itself as a generative-AI alternative to PowerPoint and Google Slides.

However, Tome's story is ultimately one of explosive consumer growth that failed to convert into a sustainable business. With fewer than 2% of users converting to paid plans and only ~$3.5M ARR from 20+ million users, the company underwent a series of pivots: first toward enterprise sales teams in April 2024, then a full shutdown of presentation features in March 2025, and finally a complete rebrand as **Lightfield**, an AI-native CRM. The "Tome" brand and its legal-document AI technology were separately acquired by **AngelList** in April 2025.

## Growth Timeline

- **2020:** Founded; Keith Peiris is EIR at Greylock, begins building Tome.
- **Jan 2021:** Seed round closes ($6.3M).
- **Sep 2021:** Series A closes (~$32M at $175M valuation).
- **Mar 2022:** Public launch with $32M in total funding announced.
- **Dec 2022:** Integrated generative AI (OpenAI-powered) for full presentation generation from prompts. Growth explodes.
- **Feb 2023:** Reaches 1M users, fastest productivity tool to hit that milestone. Series B closes ($43M at $300M).
- **Jul 2023:** Surpasses 10M users. Strong Gen Z adoption -- younger users who never adopted PowerPoint embrace Tome as their default.
- **Early 2024:** Reaches ~20M users, but conversion to paid remains under 2%. ARR estimated at ~$3.5M.
- **Apr 2024:** Major pivot announced. Lays off ~20% of 59-person team. Shifts focus entirely to enterprise sales and marketing teams (salespeople were power users, creating "eight Tomes a day").
- **Mar 2025:** Discontinues all presentation features. Pivots fully to sales automation and account research.
- **Apr 2025:** AngelList acquires the "Tome" brand and its legal-document summarization AI technology.
- **Nov 2025:** Keith Peiris and Henri Liriani publicly launch **Lightfield**, an AI-native CRM, built by the core Tome engineering team after a year of stealth development. 100+ early customers reported.

## Business Model

### Tome Era (2020-2025)
- **Freemium SaaS:** Free tier with limited AI credits; Pro plan at ~$16-20/user/month; Enterprise plan with custom pricing.
- **Challenge:** Massive free user base (25M+) but extremely low conversion (<2%). Revenue reached only ~$3.5M ARR despite enormous adoption -- a cautionary tale about generative-AI products struggling to monetize consumer usage.
- **Revenue reported at ~$18.8M by one source (Latka) for 2024**, though this conflicts with earlier $3.5M ARR figures. The discrepancy may reflect different measurement periods or inclusion of enterprise contracts. [Treat with caution.]

### Lightfield Era (2025-present)
- AI-native CRM targeting sales teams. Replaces manual data entry with automatic capture and organization of customer interactions.
- Pricing details not yet publicly available as of March 2026.

## Customers & Use Cases

### Tome Era
- Broad consumer adoption, especially among Gen Z users, students, and freelancers for presentations, pitch decks, and visual storytelling.
- Enterprise sales teams emerged as the most engaged paid users -- salespeople used Tome to rapidly generate tailored pitch decks.
- Used by marketers, consultants, and startup founders for investor decks and client proposals.

### Lightfield Era
- 100+ early enterprise customers as of November 2025 launch, with over half spending more than one hour per day in the product.
- Directly competing with Salesforce and HubSpot in the CRM market.

## What Makes Tome Interesting (Stanford GSB Perspective)

1. **The virality-to-monetization gap:** Tome is a textbook case study in the challenge of converting explosive free-user growth into revenue. 25M users and <2% conversion rate raises fundamental questions about the value capture problem in generative-AI consumer tools. The marginal cost of AI inference (OpenAI API calls) made free users actively costly.

2. **Fastest pivot in unicorn-adjacent history:** Few startups have gone from 25M users to a complete product shutdown and rebrand in under two years. The willingness to abandon a product with massive adoption because the unit economics did not work demonstrates unusual founder discipline -- or desperation, depending on perspective.

3. **The "AI wrapper" problem:** Tome relied heavily on OpenAI's models for its core value proposition. As OpenAI and competitors improved their own presentation capabilities (ChatGPT creating slides, Gamma.app), Tome's differentiation eroded. This illustrates the strategic vulnerability of application-layer AI companies that do not control their own models.

4. **Gen Z adoption signal:** Tome's organic virality among Gen Z users -- who bypassed PowerPoint entirely -- suggests a generational shift in how knowledge workers will create and consume information. Even though Tome itself pivoted away, this user behavior signal remains significant.

5. **Founder background as both asset and constraint:** Deep Meta product expertise enabled Tome to build a polished, viral consumer product. But the same consumer-growth playbook (maximize users, monetize later) proved ill-suited to an AI product with high per-query costs. The pivot to Lightfield/CRM leverages the founders' enterprise sales insights gained from observing Tome's most engaged users.

6. **Brand asset disposition:** The AngelList acquisition of the "Tome" brand for legal-AI use is an unusual example of a startup's brand being more valuable as a transferable asset than as the company's own identity.

## Competitive Landscape

- **Gamma.app:** Emerged as Tome's most direct competitor in AI presentations and has continued to grow after Tome's exit from the space.
- **Beautiful.ai, Canva, Microsoft Copilot (PowerPoint):** Broader presentation tools with AI features.
- **In CRM (Lightfield):** Salesforce, HubSpot, Attio, Clay, and other AI-native CRM startups.
