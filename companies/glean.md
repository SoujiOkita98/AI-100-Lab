# Glean -- AI-Powered Enterprise Search & Work AI Platform

## Company Overview

Glean is an enterprise AI platform that unifies search, assistants, and AI agents across an organization's internal knowledge and SaaS tools. Originally built as an enterprise search product -- connecting to 100+ business applications (Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Slack, Salesforce, Jira, ServiceNow, etc.) -- the company has expanded into a full "Work AI" platform that includes a conversational AI assistant and an agent framework for automating enterprise workflows.

Glean's core thesis is that the most valuable AI layer in the enterprise sits between foundation models and a company's proprietary data. Rather than committing customers to a single LLM, Glean acts as an abstraction layer, allowing enterprises to swap or combine models while Glean handles data connectors, permissions, and context retrieval.

As of December 2025, Glean reported surpassing **$200M in ARR**, having doubled from $100M in roughly nine months -- making it one of the fastest-scaling enterprise SaaS companies in history.

## Founders & Team

All four co-founders are alumni of major Silicon Valley engineering organizations, with deep roots in search and infrastructure.

### Arvind Jain -- Co-founder & CEO
- **Education:** BTech in Computer Science, IIT Delhi; MS in Computer Science, University of Washington.
- **Prior experience:** 10+ years at **Google** as a Distinguished Engineer, leading teams across Google Search, Maps, and YouTube. Earlier roles at Microsoft and Akamai. Co-founded **Rubrik** (now publicly traded, NYSE: RBRK), a data security/cloud data management company, where he led R&D.
- Jain's combination of deep search expertise at Google and startup-building experience at Rubrik is central to Glean's DNA.

### Piyush Prahladka -- Co-founder (former Head of Search & AI)
- **Education:** BTech in Computer Science, IIT Bombay (2001-2005).
- **Prior experience:** 12 years at **Google** (2005-2017) on Maps and Search Quality Ranking teams; then **Uber** (2017-2019) working on Transit and Shared Bikes.
- Built Glean's core search product from 2019-2023. **Departed Glean in 2023** to found a new startup (stealth mode as of early 2026).

### T.R. Vishwanath -- Co-founder & Technical Infrastructure Lead
- **Prior experience:** Nearly a decade at **Facebook/Meta**, contributing to developer platform, News Feed, and Ads engineering teams.

### Tony Gentilcore -- Co-founder & Product Engineering Lead
- **Prior experience:** 10+ years at **Google**, where he helped modernize Google Search's front-end from the "ten blue links" era into the modern JavaScript-based interface. 20+ years of product UI/UX engineering.

### Team Origin Pattern
The founding team represents a classic "Google Search mafia" origin story -- three of four founders spent significant time at Google, with deep expertise in search relevance, ranking, and infrastructure. This pedigree gave Glean immediate credibility in the enterprise search space and a technical head start on building high-quality retrieval systems.

## Business Model

- **Enterprise SaaS subscription** -- seat-based pricing (details not publicly disclosed; estimated mid-five to six figures annually for mid-market, and seven figures for large enterprises).
- **Product tiers:** Glean offers an integrated platform with three core pillars:
  1. **Glean Search** -- Unified search across 100+ enterprise connectors with permission-aware retrieval.
  2. **Glean Assistant** -- Conversational AI assistant grounded in company-specific data, powered by a mix of proprietary and open-source LLMs.
  3. **Glean Agents** -- A platform for building, deploying, and governing AI agents that automate enterprise workflows. Powers 100M+ agent actions annually as of mid-2025.
- **Key differentiator:** Model-agnostic architecture. Glean is not locked to a single LLM provider; it orchestrates across models while providing the enterprise data/permissions layer.
- **No costly professional services required** -- positions itself as a turnkey, self-serve platform vs. consulting-heavy competitors.

## What Makes Glean Interesting (Research Notes)

1. **Fastest ARR growth in enterprise SaaS:** $0 to $100M in ~3 years, then $100M to $200M in 9 months. This pace rivals or exceeds peers like Wiz and Deel.

2. **Positioned at the enterprise AI infrastructure layer:** Glean is not building a foundation model -- it is building the connective tissue between LLMs and enterprise data. This "picks-and-shovels" positioning may prove more durable than companies tied to a single model.

3. **Model-agnostic moat:** By abstracting away the LLM layer, Glean avoids the commodity risk facing single-model wrapper companies. Its moat is in the data connectors, permission graphs, and organizational knowledge index -- assets that deepen with usage.

4. **Extraordinary founder-market fit:** Arvind Jain's background (Distinguished Engineer at Google Search + co-founder of Rubrik at $5B+ valuation) represents an unusually strong combination of technical depth in search/retrieval and proven startup scaling ability. The full founding team's Google/Meta pedigree in search and infrastructure is arguably the strongest technical team in the enterprise AI search space.

5. **Valuation trajectory signals strong demand:** $1B (May 2022) -> $2.2B (Feb 2024) -> $4.6B (Sep 2024) -> $7.2B (Jun 2025). The consistent ability to raise at increasing valuations from top-tier investors (Sequoia, Kleiner Perkins, Lightspeed, Altimeter, DST, Wellington) with short intervals between rounds suggests genuine pull, not hype-driven pricing.

6. **Platform expansion from search to agents:** The evolution from pure enterprise search to a full Work AI platform (search + assistant + agents) significantly expands TAM. The Glean Agents platform, already powering 100M+ actions annually, positions the company to capture workflow automation spend beyond search.

7. **Acquisition activity:** The March 2026 acquisition of Aryn suggests Glean is beginning to use M&A to accelerate capabilities -- a sign of strategic maturity.

8. **IPO candidacy:** With $200M+ ARR, rapid growth, and a $7.2B valuation, Glean is a strong IPO candidate for 2026-2027. No official timeline has been announced.

## Competitive Landscape

- **Microsoft Copilot** -- Deeply integrated with M365 but limited to the Microsoft ecosystem.
- **Google Vertex AI Search** -- Strong on Google Workspace but less multi-platform.
- **Coveo, Elastic, Lucidworks** -- Legacy enterprise search players being disrupted.
- **Moveworks** -- AI-powered IT service desk; overlaps in employee assistant space.
- **Guru, Notion AI, Atlassian Rovo** -- Point solutions in knowledge management.

Glean's advantage is breadth of connectors + model-agnostic architecture + unified platform spanning search, assistant, and agents.
