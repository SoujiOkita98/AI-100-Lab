# WEKA

## Overview

WEKA builds a high-performance, software-defined data platform purpose-built for AI, machine learning, and GPU-accelerated workloads. At its core is **WekaFS**, a fully distributed parallel file system that leverages NVMe flash to deliver extreme throughput (hundreds to thousands of GB/s), sub-200-microsecond latencies, and millions of IOPS from a single cluster. The platform runs on-premises, in public clouds (AWS, Azure, GCP), and in hybrid configurations, addressing the critical data pipeline bottleneck that constrains GPU utilization in AI training and inference.

WEKA claims to serve 12 of the Fortune 50 and hundreds of enterprise and research organizations worldwide. The company crossed $100 million in annual recurring revenue (ARR) in 2024-2025 and is positioning itself as essential infrastructure for the "AI factory" era.

## Technology and Product

### WekaFS / NeuralMesh Architecture

WEKA's core product is **WekaFS**, a POSIX-compliant parallel file system redesigned for the NVMe era. In 2023+, WEKA rebranded its architecture as **NeuralMesh**, containerizing the file system into microservices for cloud-native deployment.

Key technical characteristics:

- **4KB block alignment**: Files are broken into 4KB blocks aligned with NVMe's native block size, eliminating translation overhead
- **Fully distributed metadata**: Metadata is striped across all cluster servers -- no single metadata bottleneck
- **Massive scale**: Supports up to 6.4 trillion files/directories in a single filesystem
- **Performance**: Hundreds to thousands of GB/s throughput; ~150 microsecond average latency; millions of IOPS per cluster
- **Any-to-any data protection**: Data chunks are distributed across failure domains with rapid recovery

### Augmented Memory Grid (2025)

Announced at NVIDIA GTC 2025, this capability integrates WEKA with NVIDIA accelerated computing and networking to accelerate AI inference by maximizing tokens processed per second. Together AI is an early adopter, using it for prompt caching across multiple nodes.

### WEKApod

A turnkey data platform appliance certified for NVIDIA DGX SuperPOD with DGX H100 systems, capable of 18.3 million IOPS in its starting configuration.

Sources: [WEKA Architecture Whitepaper](https://www.weka.io/resources/white-paper/wekaio-architectural-whitepaper/), [WEKA at GTC 2025](https://www.weka.io/company/weka-newsroom/press-releases/weka-unveils-nvidia-integrations-and-certifications-at-gtc-2025/), [TechTarget - NeuralMesh](https://www.techtarget.com/searchstorage/news/366626248/Weka-connects-storage-AI-with-new-NeuralMesh-architecture)

## Business Model

WEKA is a **software-defined storage** company selling enterprise software subscriptions:

- **Software licensing**: WekaFS is sold as software that runs on commodity or certified hardware (Dell, HPE, Supermicro, Lenovo, etc.) or in the cloud
- **Cloud marketplace**: Available on AWS, Azure, and GCP marketplaces
- **Appliance partnerships**: WEKApod sold through hardware partners as integrated solutions
- **Support and services**: Enterprise support tiers

The software-only model differentiates WEKA from vertically integrated competitors (DDN) and gives customers flexibility in hardware selection. Revenue model is subscription-based (ARR).

**Revenue milestones:**
- ~$63M ARR in 2023 (per Latka)
- Surpassed $100M ARR in 2024-2025
- Exact 2026 figures not publicly disclosed

## Customers and Use Cases

WEKA targets workloads where data I/O is the bottleneck for GPU utilization:

- **AI/ML model training**: Keeping GPUs fed with training data at scale
- **AI inference**: Low-latency data access for real-time model serving
- **Life sciences / genomics**: Sequencing pipelines, drug discovery
- **Financial services**: Quantitative modeling, risk analytics
- **Visual effects / media**: Rendering, post-production
- **Research / HPC**: National labs, academic supercomputing

Named/referenced customers and partners:
- **Together AI** (inference platform, using Augmented Memory Grid)
- **11-12 of the Fortune 50** (unnamed)
- Deep NVIDIA partnership (investor + technology integration + DGX certification)
- Hardware partnerships with Dell, HPE, Supermicro, Lenovo

## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Positioning | Key Difference from WEKA |
|---|---|---|
| **VAST Data** | Unified data platform with integrated database layer | VAST bundles analytics/SQL; WEKA is pure high-performance file system |
| **DDN** | Turnkey HPC/AI storage appliances | DDN sells integrated hardware+software; WEKA is software-only |
| **NetApp** | Enterprise hybrid cloud storage | Broader portfolio but lower extreme-performance tier |
| **Pure Storage** | Flash-first enterprise storage | General-purpose flash; less HPC/AI-specialized |
| **IBM Storage Scale (GPFS)** | Legacy parallel file system | Ironic competitor given founders' XIV/IBM roots; older architecture |

VAST Data is the closest competitor and appears to be growing faster (reportedly ~$200M ARR in early 2025, with a $1.17B CoreWeave deal). WEKA differentiates on raw performance, software-only flexibility, and deep NVIDIA integration.

## What Makes WEKA Interesting

1. **XIV reunion with a thesis**: All three founders built and sold a storage company to IBM, watched it scale to $500M in revenue inside IBM, and then set out to build what storage should look like for the flash/AI era. This is a team with both startup and scale experience in exactly this domain.

2. **Stanford + IDF pedigree**: Co-founder Omri Palmon holds a Stanford CS Ph.D.; the founding team has deep roots in Israeli defense engineering and Tel Aviv University. The technical credibility is high.

3. **NVIDIA as investor and partner**: NVIDIA has invested in WEKA across multiple rounds and deeply integrated WEKA into its reference architectures (DGX SuperPOD, GB200 NVL72). As NVIDIA's ecosystem becomes the de facto AI infrastructure standard, WEKA is positioned as a preferred storage layer.

4. **Software-only in a hardware world**: Unlike DDN or legacy vendors, WEKA sells software that runs on any commodity NVMe hardware or in the cloud. This gives customers flexibility and avoids hardware lock-in -- a meaningful advantage as AI infrastructure evolves rapidly.

5. **The GPU utilization problem**: The fundamental insight is that in AI workloads, GPUs -- the most expensive resource -- are often idle waiting for data. WEKA's entire architecture is optimized to keep GPUs saturated. As GPU costs increase (H100s, B200s), the ROI of better data delivery compounds.

6. **Restructuring risk**: The 2025 layoffs, go-to-market reorganization, and CFO turnover suggest operational turbulence. Whether this reflects healthy adaptation to a fast-moving market or internal dysfunction is worth monitoring.

7. **IPO trajectory**: At $1.6B valuation and $100M+ ARR, WEKA is in the zone for a potential IPO in 2026-2027, though no filing has been announced as of March 2026. The competitive pressure from VAST Data (which landed the massive CoreWeave deal) may accelerate or complicate this timeline.
