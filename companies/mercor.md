# Mercor

## Overview

Mercor is an AI infrastructure company that connects frontier AI labs with domain experts for model post-training. Originally launched as an AI-powered recruiting platform, the company pivoted in 2024-2025 to become a critical supplier of human expertise for reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF), supervised fine-tuning (SFT), model evaluation, and related AI training workflows. The pivot proved transformative: Mercor went from ~$75M ARR in early 2025 to roughly $450-500M ARR by October 2025, making it one of the fastest-growing startups in history.

All three co-founders were 21 years old at founding and 22 at the time of the Series C, making them the youngest self-made billionaires in history as of late 2025.

## Founders and Team

### Brendan Foody (CEO)
- Attended Georgetown University; dropped out sophomore year (spring 2023) to build Mercor.
- From the San Francisco Bay Area. Attended Bellarmine College Preparatory in San Jose, where he was on the speech and debate team alongside the other co-founders.
- Thiel Fellow.

### Adarsh Hiremath (CTO)
- Attended Harvard University; dropped out sophomore year to join Mercor.
- Also from the Bay Area and attended Bellarmine College Prep.
- Thiel Fellow.

### Surya Midha (Board Chairman, formerly COO)
- Attended Georgetown University with Foody; dropped out to co-found Mercor.
- National champion policy debater at Bellarmine -- won the Tournament of Champions (TOC), NDCA, and NSDA national tournaments. Named best speaker at both TOC and NDCA.
- Guided Mercor through its early operational growth as COO before transitioning to Board Chairman around the time of the Series C.
- Thiel Fellow.
- Of Indian descent; raised in San Jose, CA.

All three founders were named to the Forbes 30 Under 30 list for 2025.

## Business Model

### How It Works

1. **Expert sourcing and vetting:** Mercor recruits and vets domain experts -- scientists, doctors, lawyers, investment bankers, management consultants, software engineers -- using AI-powered screening and interviews.
2. **Matching to AI labs:** These experts are matched to projects at frontier AI companies (OpenAI, Anthropic, Meta, Google DeepMind, and others) that need high-quality human feedback for model training.
3. **Work types:** Experts perform RLHF, supervised fine-tuning (generating gold-standard prompt-response pairs), model evaluation, rubric development, and reinforcement learning environment construction.
4. **Revenue model:** Mercor charges an hourly finder's fee / matching rate on top of contractor pay. Contractors are reportedly paid an average of $85+/hour, with specialized experts commanding up to $200/hour.

### Scale (as of late 2025)

- 30,000+ active contractors.
- Paying out over $1.5 million per day to contractors.
- ~$450-500M ARR at the time of the Series C (October 2025). Some sources cite a run rate approaching $840M by late 2025, though exact figures vary. [Uncertainty: revenue figures from different sources are inconsistent; the precise ARR depends on measurement date and methodology.]

### Original Business (AI Recruiting)

Mercor initially launched as an AI-driven hiring platform that automated resume screening, candidate matching, and AI-powered interviews. This capability still exists and was the foundation for the pivot -- the same matching and vetting technology that screened job candidates now screens domain experts for AI training work.

## Customers

Confirmed customers include:
- **OpenAI** -- RLHF and post-training data
- **Anthropic** -- AI training expertise
- **Meta** -- AI model training

Reported/likely customers:
- **Google DeepMind** -- multiple sources reference this relationship, particularly after DeepMind reportedly reduced reliance on Scale AI following Meta's $14B investment in Scale and hiring of its CEO.

Mercor's growth was accelerated by the fallout from Meta's investment in Scale AI in 2025, which led competing AI labs (OpenAI, Google DeepMind) to seek alternative data-labeling and expert-feedback providers.

## What Makes Mercor Interesting

1. **Speed of growth:** From $0 to ~$500M ARR in roughly two years is extraordinary, even by Silicon Valley standards. The $10B valuation in October 2025 was a 5x increase from the $2B valuation just eight months earlier.

2. **Strategic positioning in AI supply chain:** Mercor sits at a critical chokepoint -- frontier AI labs need high-quality human expertise to improve their models, and Mercor has built the largest vetted network of domain experts for this purpose. As models get more capable, the bar for useful human feedback rises, making specialized experts (PhDs, doctors, lawyers) more valuable than generic crowd workers.

3. **Founder age and trajectory:** Three 22-year-old college dropouts becoming the world's youngest self-made billionaires is a compelling narrative. Their background in competitive debate (rigorous argumentation and analytical thinking) is an unusual but fitting origin story for a company that helps AI reason better.

4. **Pivot execution:** The shift from AI recruiting to AI training infrastructure was well-timed and leveraged existing capabilities. The same AI that screened job candidates could screen domain experts, giving Mercor a head start over competitors.

5. **Market timing and tailwinds:** Mercor benefited from the Meta-Scale AI disruption, which sent frontier labs looking for alternatives. This was partly luck, partly preparedness.

6. **Risks and open questions:**
   - **Concentration risk:** Heavy dependence on a small number of frontier AI lab customers. If one major customer churns, revenue impact would be significant.
   - **Durability of demand:** If AI models improve enough to reduce the need for human feedback, Mercor's core business could shrink. The company's bet is that the demand for expert human feedback will grow as models tackle harder domains.
   - **Competition:** Scale AI (backed by Meta's $14B), Surge AI, Appen, Invisible Technologies, and others compete in adjacent spaces.
   - **Margin structure:** Paying contractors $85-200/hour means the business has real COGS. The take rate / margin on each hour of expert work is not publicly disclosed. [Uncertainty: gross margins are not confirmed publicly.]
