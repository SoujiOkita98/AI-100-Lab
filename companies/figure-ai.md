# Figure AI

## One-liner

Figure AI is building general-purpose humanoid robots for commercial labor markets, starting with manufacturing and logistics, with an eventual path to consumer/home deployment.

## Company Overview

Figure AI was founded in May 2022 by serial entrepreneur Brett Adcock, who personally seeded the company with approximately $100 million. The company is headquartered in Sunnyvale, California, and had roughly 479 employees as of early 2026. Figure's stated mission is to expand what is possible for humanity by deploying autonomous humanoid workers into environments that are dangerous, undesirable, or face chronic labor shortages.

The company has iterated rapidly through three generations of hardware:

- **Figure 01** -- Unveiled in early 2023; proof-of-concept humanoid.
- **Figure 02** -- Deployed commercially at BMW's Spartanburg, SC plant starting in 2024.
- **Figure 03** -- Unveiled October 2025; redesigned from the ground up for mass manufacturing and home environments. Target: "in select homes" by late 2026 (likely limited pilot).

## Founding Team & Talent

Brett Adcock recruited aggressively from the top of the robotics world. Early hires came from Boston Dynamics, Tesla, Google DeepMind, Waymo, Apple, Google X, and IHMC. Former CTO Jerry Pratt (IHMC, 20+ years of bipedal robotics) was a marquee early hire but departed mid-2024 to co-found Persona AI. The current CTO/leadership below Adcock is not as prominently profiled in public sources.

**Founder origin (for global competitive landscape):** Brett Adcock is American (white, raised in rural central Illinois). Figure AI is a US-domestic startup, not an immigrant-founder story -- notable given that many leading AI/robotics companies have immigrant founders.

## Technology

### Helix AI System

In February 2025, Figure publicly ended its collaboration with OpenAI and unveiled **Helix**, a proprietary Vision-Language-Action (VLA) model that unifies perception, language understanding, and learned motor control. This was a significant strategic shift toward full vertical integration of AI.

- **Helix 02** (January 2026): Extended capabilities to full-body control including walking and balance. Figure deleted the final 109,504 lines of hand-engineered C++ code from its robots -- a "Software 2.0" milestone where learned models fully replaced traditional control code.
- Figure demonstrated a humanoid autonomously unloading a dishwasher, walking across a kitchen, placing items in cabinets, reloading the dishwasher, and starting it -- claimed as "the longest horizon, most complex task completed autonomously by a humanoid robot to date."

### BotQ Manufacturing Facility

Figure opened **BotQ**, a dedicated humanoid-robot factory with a first-generation line capable of ~12,000 units/year, being outfitted for ~50,000 units/year. The facility integrates Figure humanoid robots into its own assembly line (robots building robots). The company targets 100,000 robots shipped over four years (by ~2029).

Source: [https://www.figure.ai/news/botq](https://www.figure.ai/news/botq)

### Hark AI Lab

In late 2025, Adcock launched **Hark**, an independent AI research lab self-funded with $100 million of personal capital, focused on "human-centric AI" -- proactive reasoning, recursive self-improvement. Adcock remains CEO of both Figure and Hark. The strategic logic: Figure generates real-world embodied data; Hark builds frontier models that may be applicable beyond Figure's own robots.

Source: [https://mikekalil.com/blog/figure-ai-ceo-hark-lab/](https://mikekalil.com/blog/figure-ai-ceo-hark-lab/)

## Business Model

Figure's go-to-market follows a **commercial-first, consumer-later** strategy:

1. **Phase 1 (current):** Deploy humanoids into industrial/manufacturing environments via partnerships with large enterprises. Revenue model is likely robot-as-a-service or unit sales to enterprise customers (specific pricing not publicly disclosed).
2. **Phase 2 (planned ~2026-2027):** Expand into logistics, warehousing, and broader commercial labor.
3. **Phase 3 (longer-term):** Consumer/household market. Figure 03 is designed with home environments in mind.

## What Makes Figure Notable

1. **Speed of execution.** From founding (May 2022) to first commercial deployment (BMW, 2024) in roughly two years -- among the fastest timelines in humanoid robotics history.
2. **Vertical integration.** Owns the full stack: hardware design, proprietary AI (Helix VLA model), manufacturing (BotQ), and now a separate frontier-AI lab (Hark).
3. **Valuation trajectory.** $0 to $39B in ~3.5 years, making it one of the fastest-appreciating private companies in deep tech. The 15x jump from Series B ($2.6B) to Series C ($39B) in 18 months is extraordinary.
4. **Software 2.0 milestone.** Deletion of all hand-coded C++ control code in favor of learned neural-network policies (Helix 02, Jan 2026) is a significant technical landmark.
5. **Founder profile.** Adcock is a rare repeat-exit founder (Vettery, Archer Aviation) who self-funded a hardware company at $100M scale and assembled an elite team by force of capital and vision.

## Key Risks & Open Questions

- **Valuation vs. revenue.** $39B valuation with limited disclosed commercial revenue. The BMW deployment is impressive but small-scale relative to the valuation.
- **CTO departure.** Jerry Pratt's exit (mid-2024) removed a marquee technical leader. The current technical leadership bench is less publicly visible.
- **Competition.** Tesla (Optimus), Boston Dynamics (Atlas), Agility Robotics (Digit), 1X Technologies, Apptronik, Unitree, and others are pursuing similar markets with significant resources.
- **Founder attention.** Adcock now runs Figure, Hark, and previously Cover -- splitting CEO attention across multiple ventures is a known risk factor.
- **Path to profitability.** Hardware robotics companies historically face brutal unit economics. BotQ's scale targets (50K-100K units) require enormous demand that does not yet exist.

## Competitive Landscape Position

Among the "Big 5" humanoid robotics startups (Figure, 1X, Apptronik, Agility, Unitree), Figure has the highest valuation and most capital raised. Its primary megacap competitor is Tesla Optimus, which has manufacturing scale advantages but less robotics-specific heritage. Figure's OpenAI breakup and pivot to proprietary Helix AI signals confidence in building a defensible, vertically-integrated moat.
