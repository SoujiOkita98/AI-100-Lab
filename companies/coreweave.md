# CoreWeave (CRWV)

## Overview

CoreWeave is a specialized GPU cloud infrastructure provider purpose-built for AI workloads, high-performance computing (HPC), and visual effects (VFX). Unlike general-purpose hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, GCP), CoreWeave offers bare-metal and Kubernetes-orchestrated GPU compute optimized for large-scale AI model training and inference. The company has become the fastest-growing cloud provider in history, reaching $5.1 billion in revenue in 2025 -- up from $229 million in 2023.

## Founding Story and Team Origins

CoreWeave was originally founded as **Atlantic Crypto** in September 2017 by three former commodities traders from the hedge fund world:

- **Michael Intrator (CEO)**: Co-founder and CEO of Hudson Ridge Asset Management, a natural gas hedge fund. Prior to that, he spent 16 years (1998-2014) at Natsource Asset Management, rising to Principal Portfolio Manager. He also managed one of the world's largest private capital pools focused on climate change. Columbia Business School alum.
- **Brian Venturo (CTO)**: Worked alongside Intrator at Hudson Ridge. Brings systems engineering and quantitative expertise.
- **Brannin McBee (Chief Strategy Officer)**: Also from the Hudson Ridge team; focused on strategy and business development.

The origin story is distinctive: Intrator and Venturo purchased their first GPU in 2016 to experiment with Ethereum mining. They discovered GPUs could pay for themselves in days during the crypto bull market. When their hedge fund began underperforming the S&P 500, they closed it and pivoted fully into GPU-based crypto mining as Atlantic Crypto.

In 2019, the company rebranded to **CoreWeave** and pivoted from crypto mining to providing GPU cloud infrastructure -- a prescient move that positioned them perfectly for the AI boom that followed. The founders' background in energy trading, risk management, and quantitative finance gave them an unusual edge in building capital-intensive infrastructure at scale.

**Note**: Some sources also mention Peter Salanki as an early co-founder, though he is less prominently featured in current leadership.

The heavy use of debt financing is a key risk factor -- CoreWeave's capital structure is more leveraged than typical tech companies, resembling an infrastructure/energy play.

## Business Model

CoreWeave operates a **GPU-as-a-Service (GaaS)** model:

- **Usage-based rental**: Customers pay for GPU compute on a consumption or reserved-capacity basis. Revenue is primarily driven by renting GPU clusters (NVIDIA H100, H200, and Blackwell GPUs) for AI training and inference.
- **Take-or-pay contracts**: Many large customers commit to multi-year take-or-pay agreements, providing revenue visibility. Remaining performance obligations stood at **$60.7B** as of December 31, 2025 (up from $15.1B a year prior).
- **Sales-led GTM**: CoreWeave targets high-value enterprise contracts rather than self-serve developer adoption.
- **Managed services**: Kubernetes orchestration, Slurm for HPC job scheduling, networking, and storage services layered on top of raw compute.

## Key Customers

Customer concentration is a notable risk:

- **Microsoft**: ~67% of 2025 revenue. $10B of $17B in booked contracts came from Microsoft for multi-year GPU compute supply.
- **OpenAI**: Significant long-term commitment (exact figures less public).
- **Meta**: Announced a $14.2B deal in late 2025 / early 2026 for computing capacity.
- Other customers include AI labs, VFX studios, and enterprises running HPC workloads.

**Revenue backlog**: $66.8B as of December 31, 2025.

## What Makes CoreWeave Interesting

1. **Fastest-growing cloud provider in history**: Revenue trajectory from $229M (2023) to $5.1B (2025) to a guided $12-13B (2026) is unprecedented in cloud infrastructure.

2. **Unusual founder DNA**: Former natural gas traders and crypto miners who pivoted into AI infrastructure. Their energy-trading background gives them an edge in capital-intensive, commodity-like infrastructure buildout and risk management.

3. **The "anti-hyperscaler" hyperscaler**: CoreWeave competes with AWS/Azure/GCP not by being general-purpose, but by being 20-30% more efficient for AI training workloads due to purpose-built architecture using NVIDIA InfiniBand fabric and bare-metal GPU access.

4. **Massive contracted backlog**: $60.7B in remaining performance obligations provides exceptional revenue visibility, but also creates execution risk -- they must build and deliver enormous amounts of infrastructure.

5. **Capital structure as strategy and risk**: CoreWeave has raised $25B+ in debt, making it look more like an energy infrastructure company than a typical tech firm. This leverage is a double-edged sword -- it enables rapid scaling but creates significant financial risk if AI demand softens.

6. **Customer concentration dilemma**: Microsoft alone accounts for ~67% of revenue. This is simultaneously a validation of CoreWeave's value proposition and a significant concentration risk.

7. **Crypto-to-AI pivot narrative**: The company's successful pivot from Ethereum mining to AI cloud infrastructure is a compelling case study in recognizing technological inflection points and repurposing existing assets.

## Key Risks

- **Debt load**: Heavy reliance on debt financing in a capital-intensive business. If AI demand growth slows, the debt burden could become problematic.
- **Customer concentration**: Over-reliance on Microsoft (67% of 2025 revenue), though the Meta deal is diversifying the base.
- **Execution risk**: Must rapidly build $30-35B in infrastructure in 2026 alone.
- **Competition**: Hyperscalers are investing heavily in their own GPU infrastructure; Lambda Labs, Crusoe Energy, and Nebius compete in the specialized GPU cloud segment.
- **GPU supply chain**: Dependency on NVIDIA for next-generation GPUs.

## Competitors

- **Hyperscalers**: AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud (general-purpose but massive scale)
- **Specialized GPU clouds**: Lambda Labs, Crusoe Energy, Nebius, Together AI
- **Infrastructure peers**: Equinix, Digital Realty (colocation/data center)
