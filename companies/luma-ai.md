# Luma AI

## Company Overview

Luma AI is a Palo Alto-based AI company building multimodal generative models for video, 3D, and creative content. Originally focused on neural radiance fields (NeRF) for photorealistic 3D capture, the company pivoted into AI video generation with its flagship product **Dream Machine** (launched mid-2024), which rapidly became one of the leading platforms alongside Runway and OpenAI's Sora. In March 2026, Luma expanded further into **agentic creative AI**, launching Luma Agents powered by its new "Unified Intelligence" (Uni-1) model family.

The company's current tagline is "AI Agents for Creative Work."

## Founders

Luma AI was co-founded in 2021 by three former Apple engineers with deep expertise in 3D computer vision, neural rendering, and augmented reality.

| Founder | Role | Background |
|---|---|---|
| **Amit Jain** | CEO & Co-Founder | Indian-origin. BS in Mathematics and Computer Science from Missouri Valley College. Worked at Apple as a Systems and Machine Learning Engineer, leading work on the Passthrough feature for Apple Vision Pro and integration of the first LiDAR sensors for iPhone. Previously a Software Engineer at Circle Medical (2015-2017). Brings deep expertise in 3D computer vision, cameras, ML, and systems engineering. |
| **Alex Yu** | CTO & Co-Founder | UC Berkeley graduate. Conducted pioneering research on NeRF neural rendering under Professor Angjoo Kanazawa. Turned down PhD offers from Stanford and MIT to co-found Luma. Previously a Research Intern at Adobe and SWE Intern at Google. Authored key papers on real-time neural rendering of 3D scenes and single-image 3D generation. |
| **Alberto Taiuti** | Co-Founder | Attended Abertay University (Scotland). Previously a Senior Autonomy Software Engineer at Skydio and worked at Apple on AR/VR. Also worked at Codeplay Software. Background in augmented reality and iOS development. |

**Notable:** All three founders came from Apple or had significant Apple experience, giving them a shared sensibility around hardware-software integration and spatial computing. The team's NeRF/3D research roots are what originally distinguished Luma from purely 2D-focused video generation competitors.

## Product & Technology

### Dream Machine

Luma's flagship consumer and developer product for AI video generation.

| Feature | Details (as of early 2026) |
|---|---|
| Input modalities | Text, images, video, 3D assets |
| Output | High-fidelity video generation with physics-compliant rendering |
| Resolution | Up to 4K at 60fps |
| Key capabilities | Character consistency across clips, keyframe control, camera motion SDK, gravity/collision awareness |
| Access | Web dashboard, SDK/API |

### Ray 3.14

Luma's latest video generation model, used as the backbone for creative generation tasks and integrated into the Luma Agents platform.

### Unified Intelligence (Uni-1)

Announced March 2026, Uni-1 is a single multimodal reasoning system trained on audio, video, image, language, and spatial reasoning. Rather than chaining separate models per modality, Uni-1 handles understanding and generation across formats within one architecture.

### Luma Agents (March 2026)

Agentic creative platform built on Uni-1. Agents can plan and generate across text, image, video, and audio, and coordinate with external models (Google Veo 3, ByteDance Seedream, ElevenLabs voice models). Early customers include Publicis Groupe, Serviceplan, Adidas, Mazda, and Humain.

### Historical: Genie / NeRF Tools

Luma's original product line focused on 3D capture and generation using neural radiance fields. Genie 1.0 (released alongside the Series B) generated 3D objects from text/images. This 3D heritage continues to inform the company's spatial reasoning capabilities.

## Strategic Partnerships

### Project Halo (with Humain)

As part of the $900M Series C, Luma and Saudi AI firm Humain are partnering to build a **2-gigawatt AI supercluster** in Saudi Arabia. Deployment begins Q1 2026, with completion targeted for 2028-2029. This is a significant compute infrastructure play that could give Luma a training advantage.

### Cannes Lions "Dream Brief"

Luma offered $1M for a Cannes Lions Gold-winning campaign using Dream Machine, positioning itself aggressively in the advertising/creative agency market.

### Hiring Expansion

Luma plans to add 200 jobs in the UK by early 2027, with additional hires anticipated in the EU and Saudi Arabia by 2028.

## What Makes Luma AI Interesting

1. **3D-native DNA in a 2D video world.** Unlike competitors who started from text-to-video, Luma's founders came from NeRF research and 3D computer vision at Apple. This spatial understanding is a genuine technical differentiator -- their physics-compliant rendering and spatial reasoning trace back to this origin. The Uni-1 model's spatial reasoning training is a direct descendant.

2. **Speed of evolution from niche to platform.** In under five years, Luma went from a NeRF-based 3D scanning tool to a $4B multimodal AI agent platform. The pivot from 3D capture to video generation to agentic creative AI shows unusual strategic agility for a deep-tech startup.

3. **The Humain mega-round and compute play.** The $900M Series C is one of the largest rounds in AI video generation. The attached 2GW supercluster partnership with Humain gives Luma access to compute infrastructure that most startups cannot match, potentially enabling training of larger models than competitors of similar size.

4. **Unified Intelligence architecture.** While most creative AI companies chain together separate models (one for text, one for image, one for video, one for audio), Luma is training a single multimodal system. If this approach works at scale, it could produce more coherent cross-modal outputs and enable genuinely agentic creative workflows.

5. **Immigrant founder story with Apple pedigree.** Amit Jain (Indian-origin), Alex Yu (UC Berkeley NeRF researcher who turned down Stanford/MIT PhD offers), and Alberto Taiuti (Scottish-educated, ex-Skydio/Apple) represent a diverse, technically elite founding team. All three left promising careers (two at Apple, one declining top PhD programs) to start the company.

6. **Agency and brand traction.** Landing Publicis Groupe, Adidas, and Mazda as early Luma Agents customers signals enterprise willingness to adopt AI-native creative workflows, not just individual creator tools.

## Key Risks & Open Questions

- **Revenue-to-valuation gap:** At ~$20M revenue on a $4B valuation, Luma needs to grow revenue dramatically to justify the multiple. The advertising/enterprise pivot via Luma Agents is the likely path, but it is unproven at scale.
- **Saudi Arabia dependence:** The $900M Series C is heavily tied to Humain and Project Halo. Geopolitical risk and execution risk on a 2GW supercluster are non-trivial.
- **Commoditization pressure:** Video generation quality is converging rapidly. OpenAI, Google, and Meta all have massive distribution and compute advantages.
- **Agentic pivot timing:** Luma Agents launched March 2026 -- very early. Whether creative agencies will adopt AI agents for end-to-end production (vs. point tools) is an open question.
- **Founder education uncertainty:** Some sources list conflicting education details for Amit Jain (Missouri Valley College vs. IIT Delhi executive program). The exact educational background could not be fully verified from public sources.
