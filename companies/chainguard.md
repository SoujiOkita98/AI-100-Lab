# Chainguard

## Overview

Chainguard is a software supply chain security company founded in 2021 by a team of former Google engineers who built key open-source security projects including Sigstore, Distroless, and Kubernetes security tooling. The company provides hardened, minimal container images with zero known CVEs, enabling enterprises to build, deploy, and run open-source software with minimized vulnerability risk.

Chainguard has raised $612M in total funding across five rounds, reaching a $3.5B valuation as of April 2025. The company's ARR grew 7x to $40M in FY2025.

## Funding story

**December 2021 -- Seed ($5M).** Amplify Partners led the initial seed round to develop open-source supply chain security tooling.

**June 2022 -- Series A ($50M).** Sequoia Capital led. Introduced the Chainguard Images product, establishing the company's core commercial offering of secure container base images.

**November 2023 -- Series B ($61M).** Spark Capital led, with Sequoia and other existing investors participating. Enterprise demand for open-source software security was accelerating post-Log4Shell.

**July 2024 -- Series C ($140M at $1.12B valuation).** Co-led by Redpoint Ventures, Lightspeed Venture Partners, and IVP. The company expanded into securing AI workloads and reached unicorn status.

**April 2025 -- Series D ($356M at $3.5B valuation).** Co-led by Kleiner Perkins and IVP, with Salesforce Ventures and Datadog Ventures joining. Valuation tripled from $1.1B in under a year. Total raised reached $612M.

## What makes them interesting

1. **Critical infrastructure for AI.** As AI workloads proliferate, the supply chain security of container images and open-source dependencies becomes a critical attack surface. Chainguard is well-positioned as the default security layer.

2. **Revenue velocity.** 7x ARR growth to $40M in a single fiscal year, with a credible path to $100M+ ARR, demonstrates strong product-market fit in enterprise security.

3. **Open-source credibility.** The founding team created Sigstore (now an industry standard for software signing) and Distroless images at Google, giving them unique authority in the space.
