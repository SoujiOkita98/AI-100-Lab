# EvenUp -- Company Profile

## Overview

EvenUp is an AI-powered platform for personal injury law that builds what the company calls a "Claims Intelligence Platform." Powered by its proprietary Piai model -- trained on hundreds of thousands of injury cases and millions of medical records -- EvenUp automates the creation of demand letters, case analysis, medical record review, and litigation strategy for personal injury attorneys. The company has raised four financing rounds in just two years, each preempted by investors, reaching a $2B+ valuation by October 2025.

## Founders & Key People

### Rami Karabibar -- CEO & Co-Founder

- **Education:** Bachelor of Commerce and Bachelor of Engineering from McGill University; MBA from Harvard Business School (2014).
- **Prior career:** Tech growth investor at Warburg Pincus (2016-2018); Corporate Development lead at Waymo (2018-2020).
- **Role at EvenUp:** CEO, responsible for overall strategy and business operations.

### Saam Mashhad -- CPO & Co-Founder

- **Background:** Former practicing litigation attorney. As a practicing lawyer, Mashhad experienced firsthand how much value is lost in personal injury cases without the right tools.
- **Role at EvenUp:** Chief Product Officer, leading product and legal operations.

### Raymond Mieszaniec -- COO & Co-Founder

- **Background:** Mieszaniec's father was permanently disabled after being hit by a car during a police chase. Because their lawyer did not know the appropriate compensation, the settlement was insufficient to support their family. This personal experience became the founding motivation for EvenUp.
- **Role at EvenUp:** COO, leading operations.

### Origin Story

EvenUp was born from Raymond Mieszaniec's personal experience with inadequate legal representation after his father's disabling accident. The three founders -- combining Mieszaniec's personal motivation, Mashhad's legal expertise, and Karabibar's data and AI background -- set out to close the "justice gap" in personal injury law by building AI that could level the playing field between injury victims and insurance companies.

## Business Model

- **SaaS platform for personal injury attorneys.** EvenUp charges law firms for access to its Claims Intelligence Platform on a subscription basis.
- **AI-generated demand letters.** The core product automates the drafting of demand letters, which are critical documents in personal injury settlement negotiations.
- **Full case lifecycle.** The platform now covers medical record review, case valuation, demand drafting, litigation strategy, and settlement optimization.
- **Expansion:** EvenUp is broadening beyond personal injury into adjacent legal verticals.

## Product

- **Piai Model:** EvenUp's proprietary AI model, trained on hundreds of thousands of injury cases and millions of medical records. Unlike general-purpose LLMs, Piai acts proactively -- drafting, reviewing, and strategizing at scale across the entire case lifecycle.
- **Demand Letters:** AI-generated demand packages that synthesize medical records, case facts, and relevant precedents.
- **Medical Record Analysis:** Automated extraction and summarization of medical records.
- **Case Valuation:** AI-driven assessment of case value based on historical data.
- **Litigation Strategy:** Strategic recommendations powered by pattern analysis across thousands of similar cases.

## What Makes EvenUp Interesting

1. **Mission-driven founding.** Built from a deeply personal experience with inadequate legal representation, giving the company authentic purpose and domain conviction.

2. **Proprietary legal AI model.** The Piai model trained on hundreds of thousands of actual injury cases creates a data moat that is difficult to replicate.

3. **Rapid investor conviction.** Four funding rounds in two years, each preempted by investors, signals exceptional market demand and business metrics.

4. **Strategic investor: RELX.** Having the parent company of LexisNexis as an investor provides distribution and data advantages.

5. **Large addressable market.** Personal injury law is a $50B+ annual market in the U.S. alone, with highly fragmented, technology-underserved practitioners.

## Risks & Open Questions

- **Narrow vertical.** Heavy concentration in personal injury law creates market size constraints unless expansion into adjacent legal verticals succeeds.
- **Ethical considerations.** AI-generated legal documents raise questions about liability, accuracy, and the unauthorized practice of law.
- **Competitive threat from Harvey AI.** As Harvey expands beyond BigLaw, overlap in the legal AI space could intensify.
