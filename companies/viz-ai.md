# Viz.ai

## Overview

Viz.ai is an AI-powered care coordination platform that uses deep learning to analyze medical imaging in real time, detect time-sensitive conditions (originally large vessel occlusion strokes, now spanning cardiology, vascular, trauma, and more), and instantly alert the right specialist -- cutting critical minutes from diagnosis-to-treatment workflows. The company received the first-ever FDA clearance (De Novo) for an AI-powered stroke triage system in 2018, a landmark regulatory moment for clinical AI.

As of January 2026, Viz.ai is adopted in nearly 2,000 hospitals across the United States, including the majority of the 50 largest health systems, supporting care for more than 230 million lives. The platform boasts a 90% click-through rate on clinical alerts, indicating deep integration into daily clinical workflows. The company achieved profitability in its healthcare business in 2025.

## Founders & Team

### Chris Mansi, MD -- Co-Founder & CEO

- **Education:** Trained as a neurosurgeon at Cambridge and the National Hospital for Neurology and Neurosurgery at Queen Square (London). Subsequently came to Stanford, where he took the graduate-level Biodesign Innovation course at the Stanford Byers Center for Biodesign.
- **Origin story:** Mansi was inspired to found Viz.ai after a patient underwent a successful brain surgery but died because the surgery came too late. Frustrated by unnecessary delays in the care pathway -- not failures of skill, but failures of coordination -- he committed to using AI to eliminate those delays.
- **Recognition:** Named to the Healthcare Technology Report's Top 25 Digital Health Executives (2025). Honored by Goldman Sachs for Entrepreneurship (2023).

### David Golan, PhD -- Co-Founder & CTO (later Co-Founder & Board Member)

- **Education:** PhD in Statistics and Machine Learning from Tel Aviv University; Fulbright postdoctoral scholar at Stanford University (2014-2016), focused on deep learning for medical imaging and genetic data analysis.
- **Background:** Israeli-born. Prior to academia, founded the machine learning team at b-hive Networks, an Israeli startup acquired by VMware in 2008. Co-authored 20+ scientific papers, including three in the journal *Science*.
- **Role evolution:** Golan served as CTO from founding through the company's early growth. As of recent public records, he is listed as Co-Founder and Board Member (no longer day-to-day CTO). *(Exact transition date uncertain.)*

### How They Met

Mansi (the neurosurgeon) and Golan (the ML researcher) met at Stanford, where Mansi was studying biodesign innovation and Golan was a postdoc applying deep learning to medical imaging. They co-founded Viz.ai in October 2016. The complementary pairing -- clinical domain expertise plus deep ML capability -- is a textbook example of the Stanford biodesign model producing successful health-tech startups.

### Other Key Leaders

- The broader team includes clinical, engineering, and commercial professionals. Estimated headcount was ~325 as of 2024 (per Latka).

## Technology & Products

### Viz.ai One Platform

The core product is the **Viz.ai One** care coordination platform, which integrates AI-powered detection algorithms with a mobile communication layer. The workflow is:

1. **Detection:** AI algorithms automatically analyze CT scans, CTs with perfusion, echocardiograms, ECGs, and other imaging modalities as they are acquired.
2. **Alert:** When a suspected condition is detected, the platform sends an instant mobile alert (with images) directly to the relevant on-call specialist -- bypassing traditional paging and call chains.
3. **Coordination:** Specialists can review images, communicate with the care team, and initiate treatment decisions from their mobile device, all within the Viz.ai app.

## Business Model

### Healthcare (Core)

- **SaaS subscription model:** Hospitals and health systems pay a subscription fee for access to the Viz.ai One platform and its suite of AI algorithms. Pricing details are not publicly disclosed but the model involves per-facility or per-system licensing.
- **Deep integration:** The platform integrates directly with hospital PACS (picture archiving and communication systems) and IT infrastructure, creating high switching costs once deployed.
- **Revenue:** Estimated ~$23M (2022), ~$40M (2023), ~$48.8M (2024). The healthcare business achieved profitability in 2025.

### Life Sciences (Growing)

- **Pharma partnerships:** Viz.ai's network of ~2,000 hospitals creates a uniquely embedded clinical network for pharmaceutical and medical device companies. Partners pay Viz.ai to:
  - Identify eligible patients for clinical trials in real time
  - Support therapy initiation and adoption for new treatments
  - Engage healthcare professionals within clinical workflows
- **Scale:** Doubled the life sciences business in the 18 months through end of 2025, growing from 7 to 13 partnerships.
- This is an increasingly important second revenue stream and leverages the existing hospital footprint as a platform.

## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Focus | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| **RapidAI** | Stroke and neurovascular AI | Primary competitor. Claims superiority in LVO detection sensitivity (98% vs. 74% in one head-to-head study). Global footprint across 100+ countries. |
| **Aidoc** | AI-powered radiology triage | Broader radiology focus; less stroke-specific. |
| **Brainomix** | Stroke imaging AI | European-headquartered; strong in EU markets. |
| **Avicenna.AI** | Neuro and vascular AI | Emerging competitor with FDA-cleared products. |

Viz.ai differentiates primarily on **care coordination**, not just detection. While competitors focus on the imaging algorithm itself, Viz.ai wraps detection in a full workflow platform -- alert routing, mobile communication, specialist engagement -- which creates stickier adoption and broader utility beyond a single disease state.

RapidAI's published studies claiming higher detection sensitivity (particularly in medium vessel occlusions) are a competitive concern, though Viz.ai's installed base and workflow integration may be harder to displace than pure algorithm performance would suggest.

## What Makes Viz.ai Interesting

1. **Regulatory pioneer:** Received the first-ever FDA De Novo authorization for AI-powered clinical triage (stroke, 2018). This was a watershed moment for healthcare AI regulation and gave Viz.ai first-mover advantage and credibility.

2. **Platform, not point solution:** Unlike many medical AI companies that sell a single algorithm, Viz.ai built a platform (Viz.ai One) that now hosts 50+ algorithms across neurology, cardiology, vascular, and trauma. This creates a land-and-expand dynamic within health systems.

3. **Network effects via life sciences:** The hospital footprint becomes a two-sided platform -- more hospitals attract more pharma partners, and pharma revenue subsidizes further hospital adoption. This is a strategically elegant business model that most clinical AI companies lack.

4. **Profitability milestone:** Achieving profitability in the healthcare business by 2025 (without a new funding round since 2023) is notable in a sector where most AI health-tech companies burn cash aggressively.

5. **Stanford Biodesign origin story:** The company is a direct product of the Stanford Biodesign process -- a neurosurgeon (Mansi) meeting an ML researcher (Golan) in a structured innovation program. This is the canonical pathway the program was designed to enable.

6. **Time-sensitive medicine:** Viz.ai operates in disease areas where minutes literally save lives (stroke, aortic dissection, pulmonary embolism). The value proposition is visceral and measurable -- peer-reviewed studies show Viz.ai reduces door-to-treatment times for stroke patients.

7. **IPO candidate:** With ~$50M+ revenue run rate, profitability in the core business, a $1.2B valuation, and strong health system adoption, Viz.ai is a plausible near-term IPO candidate in health-tech AI. *(No IPO filing has been announced as of March 2026.)*

## Key Risks & Open Questions

- **RapidAI competition:** Head-to-head studies favoring RapidAI on detection accuracy could erode Viz.ai's position, particularly in new hospital wins.
- **Valuation staleness:** The $1.2B valuation is from April 2022 (pre-tech correction). The true current valuation is unknown. A down round or flat round could affect employee retention and perception.
- **International expansion:** Viz.ai appears heavily U.S.-focused (~2,000 domestic hospitals). Global expansion is a significant untapped opportunity but also requires navigating diverse regulatory regimes (CE marking, etc.).
- **Life sciences dependency:** As pharma partnerships grow as a revenue share, the company could face reputational tension between its role as a trusted clinical tool and its commercial relationships with drug/device companies.
