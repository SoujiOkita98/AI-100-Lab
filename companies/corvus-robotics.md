# Corvus Robotics

## Overview

Corvus Robotics builds fully autonomous drone systems for warehouse inventory management. Founded in 2017 by four MIT alumni and graduates of Y Combinator (S18), the company has developed the Corvus One -- described as the world's first fully autonomous warehouse inventory management drone.

Unlike warehouse drones that require human pilots or pre-programmed flight paths, the Corvus One navigates autonomously through warehouse aisles, scanning barcodes and recording product locations without any human intervention.

## Products

### Corvus One
- **Sensors**: Equipped with 14 cameras and an AI navigation system
- **Capabilities**: Autonomous navigation through warehouse aisles; barcode scanning; product location recording; cycle counting; putaway audits
- **Autonomy**: Fully autonomous -- no human pilot or predefined flight paths required
- **Deployment model**: Drones-as-a-Service (DaaS) -- Corvus deploys and manages the drone fleet

## Business Model

Corvus Robotics operates a Drones-as-a-Service (DaaS) model:
1. **Service subscription**: Customers pay for ongoing inventory scanning services rather than purchasing drones outright
2. **Managed fleet**: Corvus deploys, maintains, and operates the drone fleet at customer sites
3. **Data analytics**: Inventory data collected by drones feeds into analytics for supply chain optimization

This model reduces customer adoption friction (no capital expenditure required) and creates predictable recurring revenue.

## What Makes Corvus Robotics Interesting

1. **Fully autonomous operation**: While competitors like Gather AI use off-the-shelf drones that require some human oversight, Corvus One is designed for fully autonomous operation with 14 cameras and AI navigation -- no human pilot needed.

2. **MIT + YC pedigree**: The founding team combines MIT engineering talent with Y Combinator's startup methodology and network.

3. **DaaS model**: The service model rather than hardware sales aligns incentives with customers (pay for outcomes, not equipment) and creates recurring revenue.

4. **Warehouse inventory is a massive pain point**: Manual cycle counting is labor-intensive, error-prone, and disruptive to warehouse operations. Autonomous drones can scan continuously without interrupting workflows.

5. **Supply chain intelligence**: Beyond basic barcode scanning, the data collected by autonomous drones enables predictive analytics for stockouts, misplacements, and supply chain optimization.
