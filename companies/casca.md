# Casca

## Overview

Casca (legal name: Cascading AI) is a San Francisco-based fintech company founded in 2023 by Lukas Haffer (Stanford GSB, ex-Avaloq) and Isaiah Williams (Stanford CS, ex-EliseAI). A Y Combinator S23 graduate, Casca builds an AI-native loan origination platform designed to replace legacy lending systems at community banks and regional financial institutions.

The company has raised $33M in total funding, with a $29M Series A led by Canapi Ventures in August 2025. Notably, major bank customers including Live Oak Bank, Huntington National Bank, and Bankwell invested in the round -- a strong signal of product-market fit.

## Funding story

**August 2023 -- Seed (~$4M).** Y Combinator S23 batch. Initial capital to build the AI loan origination platform.

**August 2025 -- Series A ($29M).** Led by Canapi Ventures (fintech-focused VC). Strategic investments from bank customers Live Oak, Huntington, and Bankwell, plus Y Combinator and Peterson Ventures. Customer-investors are a rare and powerful validation signal.

## What makes them interesting

1. **Customers investing in the company.** Live Oak Bank, Huntington National Bank, and Bankwell are both customers and investors. Banks are notoriously conservative -- their willingness to invest signals genuine product value and deep integration.

2. **Massive legacy replacement opportunity.** Loan origination systems at community banks are often decades old. The gap between legacy systems and modern AI capabilities is enormous, creating a greenfield replacement opportunity.

3. **Founder-market fit from Stanford.** Haffer's deep banking infrastructure experience from Avaloq combined with Williams' ML engineering from EliseAI gives Casca credibility with both the banking and technical communities.
