# HappyRobot

> AI agents automating logistics operations for enterprises like DHL, Ryder, and Schneider -- built by Spanish-born founders out of YC S23.

## Company Overview

HappyRobot builds AI-powered agents for supply chain and logistics operations. A Y Combinator Summer 2023 graduate, the company has grown rapidly to serve more than 70 enterprise customers including DHL, Ryder, Schneider, and Werner. Revenue exceeds $10 million, and the platform automates voice, email, and messaging workflows across freight brokerage, warehousing, and carrier operations.

## Founders

- **Pablo Palafox** -- Co-Founder and CEO. Spanish-born entrepreneur.
- **Diego Palafox** -- Co-Founder. Also Spanish-born.

The brothers are notable as immigrant founders building AI for one of the most traditionally analog industries -- freight and logistics.

## Business Model

Enterprise SaaS with usage-based pricing for AI agent interactions. HappyRobot's AI agents handle calls, emails, and operational tasks for logistics companies, replacing manual dispatcher and carrier coordination workflows.

## What Makes HappyRobot Interesting

1. **Vertical AI in a massive industry:** Freight and logistics is a $4 trillion+ global industry that remains deeply analog. HappyRobot targets the core operational workflows (carrier communication, load booking, tracking) with AI agents.
2. **a16z portfolio company:** Selected for a16z's infrastructure stack alongside companies like Sierra and Glean, positioned as a leading example of "vertical agentic AI."
3. **Enterprise traction:** 70+ customers including blue-chip logistics brands like DHL and Ryder, with $10M+ revenue.
4. **YC to a16z pipeline:** Classic YC S23 to a16z Series A trajectory, signaling strong early product-market fit.
5. **Immigrant founder story:** Spanish-born founders building for a traditionally American industry (freight brokerage), illustrating the cross-cultural innovation pattern in AI startups.
