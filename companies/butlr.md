# Butlr

## Overview

Butlr is a Boston-based smart building technology company spun out of the MIT Media Lab in 2019 by Forbes 30 Under 30 entrepreneurs Honghao Deng and Jiani Zeng. The company builds privacy-first AI people sensing systems that use body heat detection (thermal sensors) instead of cameras, enabling anonymous occupancy analytics for buildings without collecting personally identifiable information.

Butlr has raised $68M in total funding across three rounds, with a $38M Series B led by Foundry in 2024. The company has achieved triple-digit growth and expanded globally.

## Funding story

**2019 -- Seed (~$10M).** Initial capital post-MIT Media Lab spinout to develop and commercialize privacy-preserving sensing technology.

**July 2022 -- Series A ($20M).** Tiger Global invested alongside Carrier Global Corporation (strategic), Ray Stata, Primetime Partners, E14, Unionlabs, Hyperplane, and Tectonic Ventures.

**January 2024 -- Series B ($38M).** Led by Foundry, with Pacific Alliance Ventures, Draper Nexus, and Ray Stata. Brought total to $68M.

## What makes them interesting

1. **Privacy-first approach.** In an era of growing surveillance concerns, Butlr's anonymous body heat sensing offers occupancy intelligence without the privacy liabilities of cameras or tracking systems. This is a genuine differentiator.

2. **MIT Media Lab pedigree.** The founders bring deep academic research in human-computer interaction and sensing technology, translated into a commercially viable product.

3. **Strategic investors.** Carrier Global (HVAC giant) as a strategic investor validates the building efficiency use case and provides distribution channels.
