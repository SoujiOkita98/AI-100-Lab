# SenseTime

## Overview

SenseTime is one of China's original "Four AI Dragons" (alongside Megvii, CloudWalk, and Yitu) that defined the first wave of Chinese AI companies in the mid-2010s. Originally built on computer vision and facial recognition, SenseTime has pivoted aggressively toward generative AI and AI infrastructure, with generative AI now accounting for 77% of revenue.

The company is publicly traded on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange (ticker 0020) and is notable for its massive AI computing infrastructure (SenseCore platform at 25,000 PetaFLOPS), its pivot from surveillance/CV to generative AI, and its complex relationship with U.S. sanctions. SenseTime is on both the U.S. Commerce Department's Entity List and the Treasury Department's SDN (sanctions) list.

## Founders

### Tang Xiao'ou (1968-2023)
Tang Xiao'ou was a professor at the Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK) who co-founded SenseTime in 2014. He was a pioneer in computer vision and deep learning in China, with research that influenced an entire generation of Chinese AI researchers. He held a PhD from MIT. Tang passed away on December 15, 2023, at age 55, a loss that sent shockwaves through the Chinese AI community.

Many of China's top AI founders are Tang's former students or colleagues, including the founders of MiniMax and other major AI companies. SenseTime alumni have become a major source of AI startup founders in China -- similar to the "PayPal mafia" in Silicon Valley. [KR-Asia](https://kr-asia.com/after-dji-sensetime-alumni-emerge-as-a-new-draw-for-investors)

### Xu Li (CEO)
Xu Li co-founded SenseTime with Tang and serves as Executive Chairman, CEO, and Executive Director. He is a computer scientist who has led the company's strategic pivot from computer vision to generative AI. Under Xu's leadership post-Tang's passing, SenseTime reorganized into three business segments: Generative AI, Computer Vision, and "X Businesses" (autonomous driving, robotics, world models).

## Financial Performance (2025)

- **H1 2025 revenue**: 2.36B RMB (~$330M), up 35.6% YoY
- **Generative AI revenue**: 1.815B RMB (~$253M), up 73% YoY, representing 77% of total
- **Adjusted net loss**: 1.16B RMB, halved from 2.33B RMB a year earlier (50% improvement)
- **SenseCore computing platform**: 25,000 PetaFLOPS, upgraded to SenseCore 2.0

## What Makes SenseTime Interesting (GSB Research Perspective)

1. **First-generation to second-generation pivot.** SenseTime's transition from computer vision/surveillance to generative AI is one of the most significant strategic pivots in Chinese AI. It demonstrates how the GenAI wave disrupted even established AI companies.

2. **"AI Dragon" to "AI infrastructure provider."** Rather than competing head-to-head with the "Six Tigers" on foundation models, SenseTime is positioning itself as an AI infrastructure and computing platform provider -- selling picks and shovels rather than mining gold.

3. **Founder network effects.** Tang Xiao'ou's academic lineage means SenseTime alumni are now founders of multiple major Chinese AI companies, creating a network effect analogous to Stanford/Google's role in Silicon Valley.

4. **Sanctions resilience.** Despite being on multiple U.S. sanctions lists, SenseTime continues to grow, demonstrating the limits of sanctions in stopping a well-resourced Chinese AI company with a primarily domestic customer base.

5. **State ownership component.** SenseTime is partly state-owned, blurring the line between private AI startup and state-backed enterprise -- a governance structure common in China but unfamiliar to Western investors.

## Key Risks

- U.S. sanctions constrain international expansion and technology access
- Competition from both GenAI startups (DeepSeek, Zhipu) and hyperscalers (Alibaba, Baidu)
- Still unprofitable despite being founded in 2014
- Post-founder leadership transition after Tang Xiao'ou's death
