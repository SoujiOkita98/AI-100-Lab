# Reflection AI

> Former DeepMind researchers building autonomous coding agents and open-weight frontier models, positioning as America's open-source alternative to DeepSeek.

## Company Overview

Reflection AI was launched in March 2024 by Misha Laskin and Ioannis Antonoglou, both veterans of Google DeepMind. The company emerged from stealth in March 2025 with an ambitious dual mission: building autonomous coding agents and developing open-weight frontier AI models to rival closed labs like OpenAI and Anthropic while serving as a Western counterpart to China's DeepSeek.

The company has grown at extraordinary speed — reaching an $8B valuation within 18 months of founding and reportedly targeting a $20B valuation in its next round as of early 2026.

## Founders and Team

### Misha Laskin — CEO

- **Background:** Led reward modeling for Google DeepMind's Gemini project; architect of Google's agent systems
- **Education:** PhD in AI/reinforcement learning (institution unconfirmed in public sources)

## Product: Asimov

**Asimov** is Reflection AI's flagship product, launched in July 2025. It is described as a code comprehension agent that helps engineering teams navigate complex codebases and surface relevant knowledge.

**Architecture:** Asimov uses a novel multi-agent system where smaller models retrieve information from disparate data sources (documentation, code repositories, internal wikis), while a larger "reasoning agent" synthesizes this input into coherent answers and actionable suggestions. Its capabilities include:

- Interpreting documentation
- Refactoring codebases
- Suggesting architectural modifications
- Tracking every step of software development

## Business Model

Reflection AI operates on two tracks:

1. **Enterprise product (Asimov):** API-based access to autonomous coding and code comprehension agents for engineering teams
2. **Open-weight frontier models:** The company is building open-source foundation models as a strategic counterweight to closed-source labs, which may serve as a platform for third-party applications

## What Makes Reflection AI Interesting

1. **DeepMind pedigree:** Co-founded by the architect of Gemini's reward modeling and a founding DeepMind engineer who co-created AlphaGo — deep reinforcement learning expertise that may yield differentiated agent capabilities.

2. **Fastest valuation growth in AI:** From $545M to $8B in 7 months, and reportedly targeting $20B — one of the steepest valuation trajectories in the current AI wave.

3. **Open-source + geopolitical positioning:** Explicitly marketing itself as America's answer to DeepSeek, combining open-weight models with a national competitiveness narrative.

4. **Novel multi-agent architecture:** Asimov's design — small retrieval models feeding a large reasoning agent — differs from monolithic approaches used by competitors.

5. **Politically connected investor base:** 1789 Capital (Trump Jr.), Eric Schmidt, and Citi suggest the company is cultivating relationships across the political and financial establishment.

## Uncertainties and Open Questions

- **Revenue and traction:** No public revenue figures. Asimov launched only in July 2025.
- **Headquarters location:** Not explicitly confirmed as San Francisco in public sources.
- **Open-source delivery:** Despite positioning as an open frontier lab, the company has been criticized for not yet shipping open-weight models as of early 2026 (per TuringPost reporting).
- **Competitive positioning:** The autonomous coding agent space is crowded (Cognition/Devin, Anysphere/Cursor, Augment, GitHub Copilot).
