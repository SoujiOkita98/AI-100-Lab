# Anysphere (Cursor)

## Overview

Anysphere is the company behind **Cursor**, an AI-native code editor built as a fork of VS Code. Cursor integrates large language models directly into the development workflow, offering intelligent code completion, multi-file editing, natural-language code generation, and an agentic coding assistant. It has become the fastest SaaS product in history to scale from $1M to $500M ARR and is widely regarded as the defining company of the "vibe coding" movement.

## Founders

All four co-founders were MIT Computer Science graduates (Class of 2022). Several were Math Olympiad champions and Neo Scholars. They conducted research at MIT CSAIL and participated in OpenAI's accelerator program before founding Anysphere.

## Business Model

### Pricing Tiers (as of early 2026)

- **Free (Hobby):** Limited AI completions and chat queries per month.
- **Pro ($20/month):** Core tier for individual developers. Includes generous AI completion and chat usage.
- **Ultra ($200/month, [unconfirmed exact name/price]):** Power-user tier with significantly more compute. *Uncertainty note: naming and exact pricing may have shifted; multiple sources cite this tier.*
- **Enterprise:** Per-seat licensing with SSO, admin controls, and compliance features. Custom pricing.

### Revenue Mix

- Enterprise contracts accounted for an estimated 45-60% of revenue by late 2025 / early 2026, up from a predominantly individual-subscriber base earlier.
- Over 50,000 businesses use Cursor, including 64% of the Fortune 500 (as reported in early 2026).

### Cost Structure

Multiple reports indicate Cursor spends nearly 100% of revenue on AI inference costs (calls to OpenAI, Anthropic, and its own models). The company is **not yet profitable**. To address margins, Cursor launched **Composer** in October 2025 -- a proprietary coding model that reportedly runs 4x faster than competing frontier models on multi-file tasks, which should reduce per-query costs over time.

## Key Products and Features

- **Cursor Editor:** AI-native IDE (VS Code fork) with inline code generation, intelligent autocomplete, and codebase-aware chat.
- **Composer (Oct 2025):** Proprietary AI coding model optimized for multi-file, multi-step tasks. Runs 4x faster than frontier models on coding benchmarks.
- **Cursor Automations (Mar 2026):** AI agents that autonomously handle tasks like incident response, code generation, and workflow automation.

## What Makes Them Interesting

1. **Fastest-growing SaaS company ever.** No company has scaled from $1M to $2B ARR faster. The revenue trajectory is without historical precedent.

2. **Product-led growth at enterprise scale.** Cursor spread bottom-up through individual developers, then converted into enterprise seat deals -- a classic PLG motion executed at remarkable speed.

3. **Four MIT undergrads vs. Big Tech.** A team of four recent graduates built a product that competes with (and is displacing) GitHub Copilot, a product backed by Microsoft's resources and OpenAI's models.

4. **The "vibe coding" catalyst.** Cursor popularized the idea that developers can describe intent in natural language and have AI generate, refactor, and debug code across entire projects. This is shifting the developer role from writing code to directing AI agents.

5. **Moving toward vertical integration.** The launch of Composer (proprietary model) and Automations (agentic workflows) signals a shift from being an LLM wrapper to building its own AI stack, which could improve margins and defensibility.

6. **Profitability remains an open question.** Despite $2B ARR, near-100% of revenue goes to AI inference costs. Whether Cursor can achieve sustainable unit economics -- through proprietary models, pricing changes, or cost reductions -- is the key risk.

7. **Co-founder departure.** CTO Arvid Lunnemark left in Oct 2025 to start an AI safety lab. This is notable but has not visibly slowed execution.

8. **Acquisition interest from OpenAI.** OpenAI explored acquiring Anysphere in 2024-2025 but ultimately acquired competitor Windsurf instead. Anysphere declined multiple acquisition offers to remain independent.

## Competitive Landscape

- **GitHub Copilot** (Microsoft/OpenAI) -- incumbent; larger user base but lower engagement growth.
- **Windsurf** (acquired by OpenAI, 2025) -- closest product competitor before acquisition.
- **Augment Code**, **Sourcegraph Cody**, **Tabnine** -- other AI coding assistants.
- **Claude Code** (Anthropic), **Gemini Code Assist** (Google) -- foundation model providers offering direct coding tools.
