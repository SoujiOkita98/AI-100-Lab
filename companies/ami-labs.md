# AMI Labs (Advanced Machine Intelligence)

## Overview

AMI Labs is an AI research lab founded by Turing Award winner Yann LeCun after leaving Meta, headquartered in Paris with planned offices in New York, Montreal, and Singapore. The company raised $1.03 billion in a seed round announced in March 2026 -- the largest seed ever for a European startup -- at a $3.5 billion pre-money valuation.

AMI Labs is building "world models" -- AI systems designed to learn from and interact with the physical world, not just language. The approach is based on JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture), proposed by LeCun in a 2022 position paper, which represents a fundamentally different bet from the transformer architecture that dominates current AI.

## Funding story

**2026 -- $1.03B Seed at $3.5B valuation.** Co-led by Cathay Innovation, Greycroft, Hiro Capital, HV Capital, and Bezos Expeditions. The investor list reads like a who's who of tech: Nvidia, Samsung, Temasek, Tim Berners-Lee, Jim Breyer, Mark Cuban, Mark Leslie, Xavier Niel, and Eric Schmidt all participated. This is one of the three largest seed rounds in venture history, behind only Thinking Machines Lab ($2B) and comparable to the scale of frontier model lab launches.

## Team

- **Yann LeCun (Chairman):** The intellectual north star. Turing Award winner. Invented convolutional neural networks. Spent over a decade as Meta's Chief AI Scientist. Remains a professor at NYU. His public skepticism of autoregressive LLMs and advocacy for world models is AMI's entire thesis.
- **Alex LeBrun (CEO):** The operator. Serial entrepreneur. Founded Nabla (digital health AI). Former Meta engineer. Nabla is AMI's first announced partner.

## What makes them interesting

1. **A contrarian bet by the field's most credentialed skeptic.** LeCun has been the most prominent critic of LLM-only approaches, arguing that autoregressive token prediction cannot lead to real understanding. AMI is the vehicle for proving this thesis. If JEPA-based world models work, it could reshape the entire AI landscape.

2. **European AI flagship.** AMI is by far the most valuable and well-funded AI startup in Europe. Paris HQ signals a potential shift in where frontier AI research happens, especially with European regulatory advantages.

3. **The investor signal.** Bezos, Nvidia, Schmidt, Cuban, and sovereign wealth (Temasek) all backed this at the seed stage. This is an extraordinary concentration of smart money betting on a non-consensus technical approach.

4. **World models vs. LLMs.** If JEPA succeeds, it opens up robotics, autonomous systems, and physical AI in ways that language models fundamentally cannot. The TAM for "AI that understands physics" could dwarf the TAM for chatbots.

5. **First-mover in a new paradigm.** While others are spending billions scaling transformers, AMI is the best-funded attempt to build something architecturally different. The risk is high, but so is the potential reward.

## Notes

- **Research risk:** JEPA is still unproven at scale. LeCun's position paper is compelling but the gap between theory and working products is enormous. This is a genuine moonshot.
- **LeCun remains at NYU** as a professor, so AMI is not his sole focus. The CEO Alex LeBrun runs day-to-day operations.
- **Nabla partnership** suggests healthcare may be an early application domain.
- **Competition:** Meta itself continues to invest heavily in world models research, and Google DeepMind's Genie and Veo projects target similar territory. AMI's advantage is focus -- it is building nothing else.
