# Baseten

## Overview

Baseten is an AI model inference infrastructure company that enables teams to deploy, serve, and scale machine learning models in production. The platform converts ML models into production-ready APIs and serves open-source, custom, and fine-tuned AI models on infrastructure purpose-built for high-performance inference. In January 2026, Baseten expanded beyond inference into model training, positioning itself as a full-lifecycle AI infrastructure platform. The company has experienced explosive growth, reporting 100x inference volume growth and 10x revenue growth year-over-year in 2025.

**Origin story:** The founders recognized a shared frustration -- deploying even simple ML models to production routinely took six months or more due to the need for full-stack engineering skills. They set out to reduce that to hours. The company name references base-ten blocks used to teach arithmetic: just as those blocks simplify number systems, Baseten aims to simplify ML infrastructure.

## Business Model

Baseten generates revenue through several channels:

1. **Managed Inference Platform (primary revenue driver):** Pay-as-you-go pricing for deploying and serving AI models via Baseten's cloud infrastructure. Customers deploy custom, fine-tuned, or open-source models and pay based on compute consumption. The vast majority of inference revenue comes from custom models that customers train elsewhere.

2. **Self-Hosted / BYOC (Bring Your Own Cloud):** Customers can deploy Baseten's software on their own infrastructure across 10+ IaaS providers. This appeals to enterprises with strict data residency or security requirements.

3. **Model Training (launched Jan 2026):** A new product line that lets teams run training jobs on scalable compute, tightly integrated with the inference stack for seamless train-to-deploy workflows.

4. **Model APIs:** Pre-optimized endpoints for popular open-source models (e.g., Llama, Whisper, Stable Diffusion) with Baseten-managed optimization.

**Revenue note:** One source ([Latka](https://getlatka.com/companies/baseten.co)) reports $15.8M in 2025 revenue with 10x YoY growth. This figure may reflect recognized revenue rather than ARR/run-rate, and may undercount usage-based revenue at scale. Given the 100x inference volume growth and $5B valuation, actual revenue or contracted run-rate may be significantly higher. Treat specific revenue figures with caution.

## Key Technology

### Truss (Open Source)
Baseten maintains **Truss**, an open-source Python framework for packaging and serving ML models. Truss lets data scientists containerize models without learning Docker, supports any framework (PyTorch, TensorFlow, vLLM, SGLang, TensorRT-LLM), and deploys to Baseten, AWS, or GCP. Available at [github.com/basetenlabs/truss](https://github.com/basetenlabs/truss).

### Baseten Inference Stack
The proprietary infrastructure layer that combines optimized inference runtimes with production features: autoscaling, multi-cloud reliability, fast cold starts, and GPU-level optimization. Baseten claims 225% better cost-performance for high-throughput inference using Google Cloud A4 VMs with NVIDIA Blackwell GPUs.

## What Makes Baseten Interesting

### 1. Inference-First Positioning in the Right Market at the Right Time
As AI moves from training to deployment, inference spend is growing faster than training spend. Baseten bet early on inference infrastructure and now captures demand from companies deploying models at scale. The 100x inference volume growth in 2025 validates this positioning.

### 2. Custom Model Focus Creates Stickiness
Unlike platforms that primarily serve popular open-source models (where switching costs are low), the majority of Baseten's revenue comes from custom models. This creates deeper integration with customer workflows and higher switching costs.

### 3. Velocity of Fundraising Signals Strong Demand
Three funding rounds in 11 months (Feb 2025 - Jan 2026) taking the company from $825M to $5B valuation is unusual even by AI infrastructure standards. NVIDIA's $150M investment provides both capital and strategic alignment.

### 4. Expanding From Inference to Training
The January 2026 launch of a training product positions Baseten as a full-lifecycle AI infrastructure platform. Notably, the training product supports non-LLM workloads (audio models like Orpheus and Whisper, multi-node jobs), differentiating it from point-and-click training solutions.

### 5. Operator-Founders With Shared History
The founding team has over a decade of working together (Gumroad, Shape, and now Baseten). This continuity is unusual and reduces co-founder risk. Their backgrounds combine ML engineering and infrastructure experience rather than pure research -- reflecting Baseten's focus on practical deployment over research novelty.

### 6. Exceptional Seed-Stage Angel Investors
The seed round included Greg Brockman (OpenAI co-founder), Dylan Field (Figma CEO), and Mustafa Suleyman (DeepMind co-founder). This level of angel interest in a 2019 ML infrastructure startup, before the LLM boom, suggests the founders had unusual credibility in the ML community.

## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Differentiation |
|------------|----------------|
| Together AI | Deeper research bench (FlashAttention); more focused on open-source model ecosystem |
| Replicate | More developer/hobbyist oriented; simpler UX but less enterprise-grade |
| Modal | General-purpose serverless compute; not AI-inference-specific |
| Fireworks AI | Direct competitor in optimized inference; strong on speed benchmarks |
| Anyscale / Ray Serve | Open-source Ray ecosystem; broader distributed computing scope |
| Hyperscalers (AWS SageMaker, GCP Vertex, Azure ML) | Broader ecosystem but less specialized; Baseten partners with rather than competes against them |

Baseten's edge vs. pure inference competitors is the custom-model focus and train-to-deploy pipeline; vs. hyperscalers it is specialization, speed, and developer experience.

## Key Risks

- **Revenue opacity:** Publicly reported revenue figures ($15.8M) seem low relative to the $5B valuation, suggesting either rapid recent growth not captured in older data, or a valuation heavily based on forward expectations. If growth slows, the valuation premium could compress.
- **Margin pressure:** AI inference is trending toward commoditization as more players (Groq, Cerebras, hyperscalers) enter the market and prices decline.
- **GPU supply dependency:** Heavy reliance on NVIDIA GPUs; supply constraints or pricing shifts could impact operations and margins.
- **Competition from model providers:** Companies like OpenAI, Anthropic, and Mistral increasingly offer optimized inference for their own models, potentially reducing demand for third-party inference platforms.
