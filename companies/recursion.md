# Recursion Pharmaceuticals

## Overview

Recursion Pharmaceuticals is a clinical-stage TechBio company that uses large-scale automation, proprietary biological and chemical datasets, and AI/ML models to industrialize the drug discovery process. The company's core thesis is that biology can be systematically decoded through massive experimental data generation combined with machine learning, compressing timelines from target identification to IND-enabling studies from the industry-standard ~42 months down to under 18 months.

In November 2024, Recursion completed its acquisition of Exscientia (formerly a UK-based AI drug discovery company) in an all-stock deal valued at ~$688 million, creating what management describes as a vertically integrated, end-to-end AI drug discovery platform.

## Founders and Team Origins

Recursion was founded by three individuals who met through the University of Utah ecosystem:

**Chris Gibson, Ph.D.** (Co-Founder, Board Chair)
- B.S. in Bioengineering and Management from Rice University
- Ph.D. from University of Utah (bioengineering); left medical school to build Recursion
- Completed an entrepreneurship course at Stanford GSB
- Served as CEO from founding through ~2025; transitioned to Board Chair role

**Blake Borgeson, Ph.D.** (Co-Founder)
- B.S. in Electrical Engineering from Rice University (where he met Gibson)
- Previously co-founded BuildASign.com (e-commerce, grew to 350+ employees, $80M+ revenue)
- Ph.D. in Bioinformatics from UT Austin (Marcotte Lab), focused on using ML to map protein complexes

**Dean Li, M.D., Ph.D.** (Co-Founder)
- Physician-scientist; spent 20+ years in translational medical research at the University of Utah
- The foundational technology for Recursion emerged from his lab
- Departed to join Merck Research Laboratories (now President of Merck Research Labs)
- Also co-founded Hydra Biosciences and Navigen Pharmaceuticals

The founding story: Gibson developed Recursion's approach during his graduate work in Dean Li's lab. The trio -- a doctor, a computer scientist, and a bioengineer -- spun the company out of the University of Utah in 2013.

## Business Model

Recursion operates a hybrid model:

1. **Internal pipeline development:** Advancing proprietary drug candidates through clinical trials, with the goal of eventual commercialization or licensing.

2. **Platform partnerships:** Large pharma partners (Roche/Genentech, Bayer) pay upfront fees, milestones, and royalties to use Recursion's platform for target identification and drug discovery in specific therapeutic areas.
   - **Roche/Genentech (Dec 2021):** Multi-year collaboration in neuroscience and oncology. $150M upfront; up to 40 programs; each program potentially worth $300M+ in milestones plus royalties.
   - **Bayer (updated Nov 2023):** Precision oncology programs leveraging Recursion's platform.
   - **NVIDIA (July 2023):** $50M strategic investment plus collaboration on AI foundation models for biology and chemistry.

3. **Technology platform (Recursion OS):** Spans target identification through clinical trial enrollment. Key assets include:
   - Automated wet labs processing up to 2.2 million samples/week
   - One of the largest proprietary biological and chemical datasets in the world
   - BioHive-2: the largest supercomputer wholly owned by a pharma/biotech company (built with NVIDIA, announced May 2024)
   - "Maps of Biology and Chemistry" -- proprietary embeddings/models for predicting drug-target interactions

4. **Post-Exscientia integration:** Added precision chemistry design and automated small molecule synthesis capabilities, creating end-to-end coverage from target ID through molecule optimization.

## What Makes Recursion Interesting

**1. Largest proprietary biological dataset in pharma.** Recursion has spent over a decade generating experimental data at massive scale -- not scraping public databases, but running its own automated wet-lab experiments. This is a genuine moat that is expensive and time-consuming to replicate.

**2. Closed-loop system.** Unlike many AI drug discovery companies that are purely computational, Recursion owns both the data generation (automated labs) and the AI/ML layer. Experiments inform models; models suggest new experiments. This flywheel is core to their thesis.

**3. First clinical validation of the AI-native approach.** REC-4881's positive Phase 1b/2 results in FAP represent one of the first demonstrations that an AI-discovered drug candidate can show meaningful clinical activity. This is significant for the entire TechBio sector, not just Recursion.

**4. Exscientia merger creates end-to-end coverage.** Recursion was strong in biology exploration and target identification; Exscientia was strong in precision chemistry and molecule design. Combined, they cover the full drug discovery value chain with AI.

**5. NVIDIA backing and compute infrastructure.** The BioHive-2 supercomputer and NVIDIA's strategic investment signal that Recursion is treated as a serious AI infrastructure play, not just a biotech. NVIDIA's $50M PIPE and ongoing collaboration are notable endorsements.

**6. Roche/Genentech deal structure.** The 40-program framework with Roche, with $300M+ per-program milestone potential and tiered royalties, represents a massive embedded option value if the platform delivers.

**7. Cash position and runway.** With ~$754M in cash and runway into early 2028, Recursion has time to prove out its thesis -- a critical advantage in a capital-constrained biotech environment.

## Key Risks and Uncertainties

- Stock price has declined ~80% from IPO price, reflecting market skepticism about timeline to profitability
- Revenue remains minimal relative to operating expenses; the company is pre-profit
- Pipeline is still early-stage; no approved products
- The 2025 layoffs (~20%) and pipeline cuts suggest the company needed to rationalize spending
- Integration risk from the Exscientia merger (different cultures, UK + US operations)
- Whether AI-discovered drugs will have meaningfully better clinical success rates than traditional approaches remains unproven at scale
- Competition from other AI drug discovery players (Insilico Medicine, Isomorphic Labs/DeepMind, Absci, etc.)
