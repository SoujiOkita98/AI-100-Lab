# Galbot (Galaxy General Robotics)

## Overview

Galbot (Galaxy General Robotics) is a Beijing-based humanoid robotics company founded in May 2023 by Peking University Professor He Wang. The company develops mobile manipulation robots powered by embodied AI, with a focus on retail, logistics, and service applications. Galbot's robots are reportedly operational in over 30 cities across China.

In under three years, Galbot has raised over $363 million in funding at a $3 billion valuation, making it one of the fastest-funded robotics startups globally. Its investor roster includes CATL (the world's largest EV battery maker), Bosch's investment arm, Meituan, IDG Capital, GGV Capital, and multiple Chinese government-backed funds. [Caproasia](https://www.caproasia.com/2026/03/03/china-robot-startup-galbot-galaxy-general-robotics-raised-363-million-cny-2-5-billion-in-new-funding-round-raised-300-million-at-3-billion-valuation-in-2025-december-founded-in-2023-by-peking/)

## Founder: He Wang

He Wang is a professor at Peking University's School of Intelligence Science and Technology. His research focuses on robot perception, manipulation, and embodied AI. He founded Galbot while maintaining his academic position, following a pattern common in China's AI startup ecosystem (similar to Zhipu AI's Tsinghua professors).

## Technology

- **GraspVLA Model**: Launched in January 2025, described as the world's first end-to-end embodied AI model trained on billions of simulated physical interactions. The model enables robots to grasp and manipulate objects in unstructured environments.
- **Simulation-first approach**: Galbot uses simulated synthetic action datasets for pre-training, with real-world data for fine-tuning -- reducing the need for expensive real-world data collection.

## What Makes Galbot Interesting (GSB Research Perspective)

1. **Academic-to-startup pipeline.** He Wang's transition from Peking University professor to robotics startup CEO mirrors the Zhipu AI (Tsinghua) pattern. China's top universities are increasingly serving as direct incubators for frontier AI companies.

2. **CATL as a strategic investor.** CATL -- the world's largest EV battery manufacturer -- investing in humanoid robots signals the convergence of electric vehicles, battery technology, and robotics in China's industrial strategy.

3. **Record angel round.** A ~$96M angel round is extraordinary for a robotics company, reflecting the intense capital allocation toward humanoid robots in China.

4. **Simulation-to-real transfer.** Galbot's approach of training on billions of simulated interactions addresses one of the fundamental bottlenecks in robotics: the cost of collecting real-world training data.

## Key Risks

- Extremely competitive Chinese robotics market
- Commercialization challenges (moving from demos to reliable deployed systems)
- No publicly disclosed revenue figures
- Dependence on continued government and strategic investor support
