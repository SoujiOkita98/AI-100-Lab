# Sakana AI

## Overview

Sakana AI is a Tokyo-based AI research lab that develops nature-inspired artificial intelligence. The name "Sakana" means "fish" in Japanese, reflecting the company's philosophy of drawing on collective intelligence found in nature -- swarms, evolution, and emergent behavior -- rather than brute-force scaling. Founded in 2023 by two former Google Brain researchers and a former Japanese diplomat, Sakana has quickly become Japan's most valuable AI startup and its highest-profile unicorn.

## Founders and Leadership

**David Ha (CEO)** brings a rare combination of deep AI research credentials and Wall Street experience. After years in fixed-income trading at Goldman Sachs Japan, he pivoted to a PhD at UBC focused on computational neuroscience and machine learning. At Google Brain in Tokyo, he led work on neural network architectures and evolutionary algorithms (notably EvoJAX, a hardware-accelerated neuroevolution toolkit). He then served as Head of Research at Stability AI before co-founding Sakana.

**Llion Jones (CTO)** is one of the eight co-authors of "Attention Is All You Need" (Vaswani et al., 2017), the paper that introduced the Transformer architecture underpinning virtually all modern large language models. After nearly 12 years at Google, Jones left to co-found Sakana AI. He is the last of the original Transformer paper authors to leave Google, and his departure underscored the significance of the venture.

**Ren Ito (COO)** provides operational and institutional heft. A 15-year career in the Japanese diplomatic corps -- including stints as a speechwriter for PM Abe and as Japan's representative on the World Bank board -- gave him deep government networks. He subsequently became an early employee at Mercari (Japan's first unicorn), where he led European operations as CEO of Mercari Europe through its $3.7B IPO.

## Core Technology and Research

Sakana AI's research agenda is built around the thesis that evolutionary and nature-inspired methods can produce capable AI systems more efficiently than pure scaling. Key contributions:

- **Evolutionary Model Merge**: An automated method using evolutionary algorithms to discover optimal ways to merge existing open-source models, creating new foundation models *without gradient-based training*. Published in *Nature Machine Intelligence* (2024). Widely adopted via mergekit and Optuna Hub.

- **The AI Scientist**: A multi-agent system that autonomously conducts scientific research -- from hypothesis generation through experimentation to paper writing.

- **Darwin Godel Machine (DGM)**: A self-improving system where models and code iteratively enhance themselves.

- **ShinkaEvolve**: An open-source system for evolving LLM-generated programs.

- **AB-MCTS (Alpha-Beta Monte Carlo Tree Search)**: An algorithm for collaborative AI reasoning that leverages collective intelligence at inference time.

- **Continuous Thought Machine (CTM)**: A novel architecture inspired by temporal processing in the human brain.

- **M2N2 (Model Merging of Natural Niches)**: An advanced model merging technique using competition-and-attraction dynamics from ecology to maintain diverse, high-performing model populations.

## Business Model

Sakana operates a **dual model**: foundational research plus enterprise AI deployment.

- **Enterprise AI**: Partnerships with major Japanese firms (MUFG, Daiwa Securities Group, and others) to build domain-specific AI for financial operations, tailored to Japan's unique regulatory environment, professional culture, and tacit business knowledge. Plans to expand into industrial, manufacturing, defense, and government sectors in 2026.

- **Research-to-product pipeline**: Techniques like evolutionary model merge enable the creation of capable, smaller models that can run on edge devices -- a significant advantage for energy efficiency and deployment in resource-constrained settings.

- **Revenue**: Reported at ~$30M in 2025, per Latka (note: this figure should be treated with some uncertainty as the source is third-party estimated data).

## What Makes Sakana AI Interesting

1. **Contrarian technical thesis**: While most frontier labs pursue ever-larger models, Sakana bets on evolutionary algorithms and model merging to achieve capability without brute-force scaling. If this thesis holds, it could dramatically lower the cost of producing capable AI systems.

2. **Founder pedigree**: A Transformer paper co-author (Jones) paired with a neuroevolution expert and former Goldman Sachs trader (Ha), plus a diplomat-turned-startup-operator (Ito). This is an unusually complementary founding team.

3. **Japan as strategic moat**: Deep relationships with Japan's corporate establishment (every major bank, NVIDIA, NEC, KDDI, Fujitsu) give Sakana unmatched enterprise distribution in the world's fourth-largest economy. Japan's AI market is underserved by Western labs due to language and cultural barriers.

4. **Sovereign AI angle**: Japan's government has been aggressively promoting domestic AI capability. Sakana is the flagship beneficiary. The In-Q-Tel investment and defense-sector expansion signal that sovereign/national-security AI is a real revenue opportunity.

5. **Speed of growth**: From founding to $2.65B valuation in roughly two years, with a team of ~138 people, is exceptional capital efficiency by frontier AI standards.

6. **Published in Nature Machine Intelligence**: The evolutionary model merge work being accepted into a top-tier journal lends scientific credibility beyond the typical startup research blog post.

## Risks and Open Questions

- Whether nature-inspired / evolutionary approaches can keep pace with the raw scaling results from labs like OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google DeepMind remains unproven at frontier capability levels.
- Heavy reliance on the Japanese enterprise market could limit global ambitions if expansion outside Japan is slow.
- The $2.65B valuation is rich relative to ~$30M estimated revenue; execution on enterprise contracts will need to accelerate.
- Competition from larger Japanese efforts (e.g., Preferred Networks, NTT, SoftBank-backed initiatives) is increasing.
