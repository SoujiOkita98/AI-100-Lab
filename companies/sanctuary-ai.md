# Sanctuary AI

## Overview

Sanctuary AI (legally Sanctuary Cognitive Systems Corporation) is a Vancouver-based robotics company building **Phoenix**, a general-purpose humanoid robot, controlled by **Carbon**, a proprietary AI cognitive architecture. The company's stated mission is to create the world's first human-like intelligence in general-purpose robots, aiming to address labor shortages by deploying robots that can perform a wide range of physical tasks previously requiring human workers.

## Founding Story and Team Origins

Sanctuary AI was founded in **2018** by **Geordie Rose**, **Suzanne Gildert**, **Ajay Agrawal**, and **Olivia Norton** in Vancouver, Canada.

**Geordie Rose** is a serial deep-tech founder with a distinctive track record:
- **D-Wave Systems** (founded ~1999): Built the world's first commercial quantum computer. Rose served as CTO and later CEO. He was named Canadian Innovator of the Year (2011), listed on Foreign Policy's 100 Leading Global Thinkers (2013), and won the Canadian Technology Leader award (2014).
- **Kindred Systems** (founded ~2014): Created the first robotics company to deploy reinforcement learning in production warehouse environments (notably for Gap Inc.). This experience directly informed Sanctuary's approach to embodied AI.
- **Academic background**: PhD in theoretical physics from the University of British Columbia (quantum effects in materials); BEng in Engineering Physics from McMaster University (graduated first in class).

Rose's pattern is notable: quantum computing to robotic RL to humanoid general-purpose AI — each venture tackling a harder frontier problem.

**Suzanne Gildert** served as CTO and was the technical architect of Sanctuary's AI approach. She departed in **April 2024** to pursue a stealth AI project.

**Ajay Agrawal** is a professor at the University of Toronto's Rotman School of Management and a leading researcher on the economics of AI (co-author of *Prediction Machines*). He brings an academic and economic lens to the venture.

### Leadership Upheaval (2024)

In **November 2024**, Sanctuary's board pushed out Geordie Rose as CEO. **James Wells**, then Chief Commercial Officer (who had been with the company for five years), was appointed **interim CEO**. The board engaged a global search firm to find a permanent replacement. As of the research date, it is **unclear whether a permanent CEO has been named** — this is a key open question.

The loss of both co-founders (Gildert in Apr 2024, Rose in Nov 2024) within the same year represents significant leadership risk for a pre-revenue deep-tech company.

## Technology

### Phoenix (Humanoid Robot)

Phoenix is Sanctuary's general-purpose humanoid robot, now in its **8th generation** (Gen 8, released January 2025). Key specs and features:

- **Form factor**: Human-scale bipedal humanoid designed for human work environments
- **Hands/Manipulation**: Advanced dexterous hands with tactile sensors; as of Feb 2025, each finger pad contains a 7-cell touch sensor array using micro-barometers, detecting forces as low as 5 millinewtons
- **Cameras and sensing**: Gen 8 introduced improved cameras and telemetry for better data capture
- **Task learning speed**: New tasks can be automated in under 24 hours (down from weeks in earlier generations) — a claimed major inflection point
- **Manufacturing**: Sanctuary has stated that high-volume manufacturing should be outsourced, not kept in-house, differentiating from vertically-integrated competitors

### Carbon (AI Control System)

Carbon is the proprietary cognitive architecture powering Phoenix. It is **not** a single neural network but a **hybrid system** combining:

- Symbolic and logical reasoning
- Large language models (LLMs)
- Deep learning and reinforcement learning
- Explainability: Carbon can explain why it chose a given action plan

Sanctuary is developing what they call **Large Behavior Models (LBMs)** — extending the LLM paradigm to physical-world behaviors. LBMs are designed to ground AI in real-world experience, enabling robots to learn from and reason about physical tasks.

## Business Model

- **Enterprise / B2B sales model**: Phoenix is available through enterprise partnership agreements, not direct consumer sales
- **Pricing**: Expected in the **six-figure range** per unit (exact pricing undisclosed)
- **"Labor-as-a-Service" framing**: Sanctuary has described their offering as providing labor capacity rather than selling hardware
- **Target verticals**: Automotive manufacturing, logistics, warehousing, and other sectors with dull, dirty, or dangerous tasks
- **Current stage**: Pilot deployments; not yet at commercial scale

## Funding and Financial Position

**Total raised**: Approximately **US$140M** through mid-2024 across multiple rounds.

**Key rounds**:
- **Series A (March 2022)**: US$58.5M (C$75.5M), oversubscribed. Led by Magna International with participation from Bell/BCE, Verizon Ventures, Workday Ventures, and others.
- **Follow-on (July 2024)**: Additional funding from BDC Capital and InBC Investment Corp.
- **Convertible Note (Jan 2025)**: US$10M target (50% subscribed at time of announcement) to finance FY2025 operations.

**Active fundraise (as of early-mid 2025)**:
- Targeting **US$175M** in total new capital
- Selling a ~US$125M majority stake in **Apptronik** (originally acquired for ~US$10M in 2022; valued at ~12x return based on Apptronik's US$1.4B valuation)
- Raising **US$50M** in a Series B-2 round with **NEA** and **Kleiner Perkins** as co-leads (minimum target: US$30M)
- **Status: unconfirmed whether this round has closed** as of March 2026

**Financial risk**: With ~$140M raised against competitors like Figure AI (~$750M+), Tesla (unlimited corporate treasury), and others with billions in backing, Sanctuary faces a significant capital disadvantage. The convertible note offering and urgency around the $175M raise suggest cash runway pressure.

## What Makes Sanctuary AI Interesting

1. **Geordie Rose's track record**: A founder who built D-Wave (quantum computing pioneer) and Kindred (first production RL robotics) brings rare credibility to the hard problem of general-purpose humanoid AI. However, his departure complicates this narrative.

2. **Carbon's hybrid cognitive architecture**: Unlike competitors relying primarily on end-to-end deep learning, Carbon blends symbolic reasoning with neural approaches and includes explainability — potentially important for enterprise trust and safety.

3. **Large Behavior Models (LBMs)**: The conceptual leap from LLMs to LBMs is compelling. If Sanctuary can build foundation models that generalize across physical tasks the way LLMs generalize across language, the moat would be enormous.

4. **Apptronik investment as asymmetric bet**: Turning a $10M investment into a ~$125M stake in a competitor demonstrates sharp strategic thinking (and/or luck). The liquidity event, if realized, could fund Sanctuary's next phase.

5. **Canadian deep-tech ecosystem**: One of Canada's most ambitious robotics ventures, with government-backed investors (BDC, InBC, EDC) providing patient capital uncommon in US VC.

6. **Task learning velocity**: The claimed reduction from weeks to <24 hours for new task automation, if validated at scale, represents a meaningful capability threshold for commercial viability.

## Key Risks

- **Leadership vacuum**: Both original technical co-founders departed in 2024. Interim CEO status creates uncertainty.
- **Capital gap**: Dramatically underfunded vs. peers (Figure AI, Tesla, Agility). The $175M raise is critical.
- **Pre-revenue**: Still in pilot stage with no confirmed large-scale commercial deployments.
- **Execution risk**: Outsourced manufacturing strategy is unproven for humanoid robots at scale.
- **Competitive pressure**: The humanoid robotics space has attracted massive capital inflows in 2024-2026, raising the bar for differentiation.
