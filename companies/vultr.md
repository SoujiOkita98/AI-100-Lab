# Vultr

## Overview

Vultr is a cloud infrastructure provider that has pivoted aggressively toward AI workloads. Founded in 2014 as a general-purpose cloud platform, Vultr now offers GPU cloud compute, bare-metal servers, and managed Kubernetes optimized for AI training and inference. The company operates 32+ data center locations globally and serves over 1.5 million customers. Vultr was bootstrapped for a decade before raising its first outside funding in 2024.

## Founding Story and Team Origins

Vultr was founded in 2014 by David Aninowsky, an entrepreneur with a background in hosting and cloud infrastructure. The company was bootstrapped and profitable for its first decade, a rarity in the capital-intensive cloud computing space. J.J. Kardwell serves as CEO.

Vultr was recognized as an "Emerging Visionary" in Gartner's 2025 Innovation Guide for Generative AI Specialized Cloud Infrastructure.

## Business Model

- **GPU cloud compute:** Offers NVIDIA GPU instances (A100, H100, and newer) for AI/ML workloads
- **Bare-metal and VPS:** Traditional cloud compute offerings across 32+ global data centers
- **Kubernetes:** Managed Kubernetes for container orchestration
- **AI-specific services:** Inference endpoints, model hosting, and GPU-optimized networking
- **Self-service model:** Developer-friendly, lower-cost alternative to hyperscalers (AWS, GCP, Azure)

Vultr differentiates on price, simplicity, and global reach, positioning itself as a more accessible GPU cloud for startups and mid-market enterprises.
