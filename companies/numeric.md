# Numeric -- Company Profile

## Overview

Numeric is an AI-powered accounting platform that unifies close management, financial reporting, and cash management for enterprise finance teams. The company serves high-profile customers including Wealthfront, Brex, OpenAI, and Plaid, and is expanding from its core close management product into a broader "compound startup" model delivering a unified data platform for enterprise finance. Founded in 2020, Numeric has raised $89 million in total funding and is backed by IVP, Menlo Ventures, Founders Fund, and others.

## Founders & Key People

### Parker Gilbert -- Co-Founder & CEO

- **Prior career:** VP of Finance and Operations at Hearth, where he built the finance organization from the ground up. The experience of being buried under manual work during the month-end close directly inspired Numeric.
- **Role at Numeric:** CEO, strategy, and go-to-market.

### Andrew Bihl -- Co-Founder & CTO

- **Prior career:** Worked at Segment and BlackRock, where he developed expertise in stitching together complex, messy data sources into consumable products.
- **Role at Numeric:** CTO, engineering and data infrastructure.

### Anthony Alvernaz -- Co-Founder & CPO

- **Prior career:** Also worked at Segment and BlackRock alongside Bihl, gaining deep experience in data integration and product development.
- **Role at Numeric:** CPO, product strategy and design.

### Origin Story

CEO Parker Gilbert founded Numeric after leading finance and accounting at Hearth, where he experienced firsthand the inefficiency and manual burden of the month-end financial close process. Recognizing the potential for AI to transform accounting, Gilbert partnered with Andrew Bihl and Anthony Alvernaz -- who had built data integration products at Segment and BlackRock -- to create the platform Gilbert had longed for. The complementary skill sets of a finance operator (Gilbert) and two data infrastructure engineers (Bihl and Alvernaz) proved to be an effective founding combination.

## Product & Technology

### Core Platform

Numeric provides a unified AI platform for enterprise accounting teams:

- **Close Management:** Automates the month-end and quarter-end financial close process, including task management, flux analysis, and reconciliation workflows.
- **Financial Reporting:** AI-assisted generation and review of financial reports.
- **Cash Management (launched 2025):** Automates cash reconciliation by matching bank transactions with general ledger entries.
- **Planned Products (2026-2027):** Additional modules designed to automate other critical accounting workflows, following a compound startup model.

### Technical Approach

Numeric builds on a unified data layer that connects to a company's ERP, banking, and other financial systems. The AI layer sits atop this data platform to automate workflows that traditionally require extensive manual effort. The approach of building a data graph connecting all financial systems -- rather than point automation -- enables the compound startup strategy of launching multiple products from a shared data foundation.

## Business Model

- **Target customers:** Enterprise finance and accounting teams at high-growth technology companies and mid-to-large enterprises.
- **Go-to-market:** Enterprise SaaS sales with product-led expansion across accounting workflows.
- **Revenue model:** SaaS subscription (specific pricing not disclosed).
- **Compound startup strategy:** Building multiple products (close management, reporting, cash management, and more planned) on a unified data platform to increase customer lifetime value and create switching costs.

## Competitive Landscape

- **BlackLine** -- Incumbent market leader in financial close management (public company, ~$1.5B+ market cap).
- **Basis** -- AI agents for accounting firms ($138M raised, $1.15B valuation).
- **FloQast** -- Close management platform for mid-market companies.
- **Accrual** -- AI for accounting preparation and review ($75M raised).

Numeric differentiates through its compound startup approach (building multiple products on a shared data platform), its AI-native architecture (vs. incumbents adding AI to legacy products), and its strong traction with high-profile tech companies.

## What Makes Numeric Interesting

1. **Compound startup model.** Rather than building a single product, Numeric is building a data platform that enables multiple accounting products -- a strategy pioneered by companies like Rippling and Deel that has proven highly effective for expanding within existing customers.

2. **BlackLine disruption opportunity.** The former CEO of BlackLine (Marc Huffman) investing in Numeric is a powerful signal that the incumbent's architecture is ripe for AI-native disruption.

3. **Marquee customer base.** Serving OpenAI, Brex, Wealthfront, and Plaid provides strong social proof and positions Numeric as the accounting platform of choice for the next generation of technology companies.

4. **Founder-market fit.** Gilbert's direct experience with the pain of month-end close as a VP of Finance, combined with Bihl and Alvernaz's data infrastructure expertise from Segment/BlackRock, is an ideal founding team composition.

5. **Rapid funding trajectory.** Three rounds totaling $89M in 18 months, with tier-one investors (IVP, Menlo, Founders Fund), reflects strong growth metrics.

## Key Risks & Open Questions

- **Valuation undisclosed.** Cannot assess capital efficiency or investor expectations.
- **Competition with well-funded Basis.** Basis has more funding ($138M vs. $89M) and a higher valuation ($1.15B), though the two companies target somewhat different buyers (accounting firms vs. enterprise finance teams).
- **BlackLine's competitive response.** As the incumbent, BlackLine has significant resources, market share, and customer relationships.
- **Revenue and retention unknown.** No public ARR or churn figures despite impressive customer logos.
- **Compound startup execution risk.** Building multiple products simultaneously requires careful prioritization and strong engineering execution.
