# Databricks

## Overview

Databricks is one of the most valuable private technology companies in the world, operating a unified Data Intelligence Platform built on the "lakehouse" architecture -- a paradigm that combines the best of data lakes and data warehouses. Founded in 2013 by the creators of Apache Spark at UC Berkeley's AMPLab, the company has grown from an open-source project commercialization effort into a dominant enterprise data and AI platform with over 20,000 customers, including more than 60% of the Fortune 500.

As of February 2026, Databricks has a $5.4 billion annualized revenue run rate (growing >65% YoY), a $134 billion valuation, and is widely expected to pursue an IPO in 2026.

Sources: [Databricks press release, Feb 2026](https://www.databricks.com/company/newsroom/press-releases/databricks-grows-65-yoy-surpasses-5-4-billion-revenue-run-rate); [CNBC, Feb 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/09/databricks-completes-5-billion-funding-round-with-2-billion-in-debt.html)

## Founding Story & Team

Databricks emerged from UC Berkeley's AMPLab, where a group of researchers created Apache Spark as a faster alternative to Hadoop MapReduce. Seven co-founders spun out the project into a company in 2013:

- **Ali Ghodsi (CEO)**: Born in Iran during the 1979 revolution; his family fled to Sweden when he was five. He earned his PhD in Distributed Computing from KTH Royal Institute of Technology in Stockholm and later became an adjunct professor at UC Berkeley. He became CEO in 2016.
- **Ion Stoica (Executive Chairman)**: Born and raised in Romania. He earned his MS from Polytechnic University of Bucharest before moving to the US in 1994 for graduate studies. He is a professor of computer science at UC Berkeley and co-directed the AMPLab.
- **Matei Zaharia (CTO)**: Romanian-Canadian. He attended the University of Waterloo as an undergraduate and created Apache Spark while pursuing his PhD at UC Berkeley in 2009. He later joined the faculty at MIT and then Stanford.
- **Patrick Wendell, Andy Konwinski, Reynold Xin**: UC Berkeley PhD students/researchers at AMPLab who were early contributors to the Spark codebase.
- **Arsalan Tavakoli-Shiraji**: Came from McKinsey & Company and contributed to the Spark codebase.

The founding team is notably international: Iranian-Swedish (Ghodsi), Romanian (Stoica), Romanian-Canadian (Zaharia), and likely Iranian-American (Tavakoli-Shiraji, based on name -- unconfirmed). This reflects the broader pattern of UC Berkeley's computer science ecosystem attracting global talent.

## Business Model

Databricks operates a **consumption-based SaaS model**. Customers pay based on the compute resources they consume on the platform (measured in Databricks Units, or DBUs), rather than fixed seat-based licensing. The platform runs on all three major clouds (AWS, Azure, GCP), with a particularly deep partnership with Microsoft Azure.

### Core Products

- **Lakehouse Platform**: Unified architecture combining data lake and data warehouse capabilities
- **Databricks SQL**: Serverless SQL analytics (>$1B revenue run-rate from data warehousing alone)
- **Unity Catalog**: Unified governance layer for data and AI assets
- **MLflow**: Open-source ML lifecycle management
- **Lakebase**: Serverless Postgres database optimized for AI agents (launched 2025; reached 2x the revenue trajectory of Databricks SQL at the same stage)
- **Genie**: Conversational AI assistant for natural-language data queries
- **Mosaic AI**: LLM training and fine-tuning capabilities (from MosaicML acquisition)
- **Lakeflow**: Data engineering / ETL orchestration

## What Makes Databricks Notable

1. **Open-source foundation with commercial dominance**: Databricks commercialized Apache Spark, Delta Lake, and MLflow -- all of which became industry-standard open-source projects. This "open core" strategy built enormous community adoption before monetization.

2. **Lakehouse paradigm shift**: Databricks coined and popularized the "lakehouse" architecture, challenging the entrenched data warehouse incumbents (Snowflake, legacy on-prem systems) by arguing that a single platform could serve both analytics and AI workloads.

3. **Fastest-growing enterprise software company at scale**: At $5.4B run-rate growing 65% YoY, Databricks is one of the fastest-growing enterprise software companies in history at this revenue scale. For comparison, Snowflake -- its closest public competitor -- grew ~25% YoY at a similar revenue level.

4. **AI-native evolution**: The MosaicML acquisition and subsequent Mosaic AI product line positioned Databricks as not just a data platform but an end-to-end AI platform. AI products already represent $1.4B in run-rate revenue.

5. **Extraordinary capital raises**: The $10B Series J (Dec 2024) was one of the largest private funding rounds ever for a software company. Total capital raised exceeds $20B.

6. **Academic pedigree**: All seven co-founders came from UC Berkeley's AMPLab, and the founding team includes current/former professors at UC Berkeley, Stanford, and MIT. This academic DNA drives a research-first culture and deep ties to the open-source community.

## Competitive Landscape

- **Snowflake**: Primary competitor in cloud data warehousing; Databricks has been gaining share
- **Google BigQuery / Amazon Redshift / Azure Synapse**: Cloud-native data warehouse offerings from hyperscalers
- **Palantir**: Overlaps in enterprise data/AI platform positioning
- **Confluent**: Competes in real-time data streaming
- **dbt Labs**: Complementary and competitive in data transformation

## Outlook

Databricks is widely considered the most likely major tech IPO of 2026. CEO Ali Ghodsi has publicly stated he would not rule out going public this year. The company has secured over $7 billion in debt capacity, a typical pre-IPO treasury move. At $134B valuation and $5.4B revenue run-rate, it would enter the public markets as one of the largest enterprise software companies by market cap.
