# DeepSeek

## Overview

DeepSeek is a Chinese AI research lab that builds open-weight large language models. It became globally famous in January 2025 when its R1 reasoning model matched or exceeded the performance of leading Western frontier models -- at a reported training cost orders of magnitude lower. The episode, dubbed the "DeepSeek moment," triggered the largest single-day market-cap loss in U.S. stock-market history and forced a reassessment of the economics of AI infrastructure worldwide.

The company is unusual in almost every respect: it is funded not by venture capital but by a quantitative hedge fund; its team is small and drawn largely from fresh Chinese university graduates; and it releases its models as open weights, with no clear near-term monetization imperative.

## Founder: Liang Wenfeng

Liang Wenfeng (born ~1985) studied electronics and communication engineering at Zhejiang University, completing a master's in information and communication engineering in 2010. He co-founded **High-Flyer Capital Management** in 2015, a Hangzhou-based quantitative hedge fund that manages roughly **$8 billion in assets** and relies heavily on AI-driven trading strategies. [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2025/01/27/deepseek-founder-liang-wenfeng-hedge-fund-manager-high-flyer-quant-trading/); [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liang_Wenfeng)

In 2021, Liang began acquiring Nvidia GPUs at scale for a side AI research project. High-Flyer reportedly accumulated **over 10,000 Nvidia A100 GPUs** before U.S. export controls restricted chip sales to China in October 2022. This stockpile gave DeepSeek a critical hardware head start. [ChinaTalk](https://www.chinatalk.media/p/deepseek-from-hedge-fund-to-frontier); [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/chinese-quant-whiz-built-deepseek-105558643.html)

In May 2023, Liang announced that High-Flyer would pursue artificial general intelligence and formally launched DeepSeek. His business partners reportedly thought such an ambition was only feasible for Chinese tech giants like ByteDance or Alibaba. As of 2026, Liang ranks #5 on AI Magazine's Top 100 AI Leaders list. [AI Magazine](https://aimagazine.com/news/liang-wenfeng-top-100-ai-leaders-2026)

## Team and Culture

DeepSeek operates with a remarkably lean team of approximately **150-200 people** -- a fraction of the thousands employed at OpenAI, Google DeepMind, or Anthropic. The Beijing office alone houses over 100 engineers, with an infrastructure group based in Hangzhou. [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/deepseek-clearly-not-interested-in-scaling-up-160-person-team-focused-on-developing-new-models)

Key characteristics of the team:

- **Domestically trained.** The researchers behind models like V2 do not include anyone returning from overseas positions -- they were all educated and trained at top Chinese institutions (Zhejiang University, Peking University, Tsinghua University, etc.). [CollegeTowns](https://www.collegetowns.org/p/where-did-the-deepseek-team-study); [Hoover Institution](https://www.hoover.org/sites/default/files/research/docs/Zegart_DeepSeekAI_Talent_FINAL_4-21.pdf)
- **Young and unconventional.** Liang described his team as "fresh graduates from top universities, doctoral interns in their fourth or fifth year, and early-career professionals just a few years out of school." Many recruits lack prior model-training experience; one team member was a physicist who self-taught AI. [KR-Asia](https://kr-asia.com/fresh-faces-bold-results-deepseeks-rise-in-ai)
- **Flat hierarchy.** The lab emphasizes collaboration in resource allocation and research-topic selection, with minimal corporate bureaucracy. [AmCham China](https://www.amchamchina.org/the-story-behind-the-chinese-ai-app-deepseek-the-triumph-of-curiosity/)

## Funding and Ownership

DeepSeek's funding model is one of its most distinctive features:

- **84% owned by Liang Wenfeng personally**, with the remainder held by individuals affiliated with High-Flyer. [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/03/10/deepseek-isnt-taking-vc-money-yet-here-are-3-reasons-why/)
- **Primary funding comes from High-Flyer's trading profits**, not venture capital. This eliminates the commercial pressure, rigid KPIs, and exit timelines that shape most AI startups.
- A **$520M Series C** was reported, valuing the company at approximately $3.4B with total funding over $1.1B. *(Note: these figures are from Crunchbase and press reports; the degree to which this represents external capital vs. internal High-Flyer transfers is unclear.)*
- Despite intense VC interest post-January 2025, Liang has not announced any major external fundraise. [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/03/10/deepseek-isnt-taking-vc-money-yet-here-are-3-reasons-why/)

**Estimated annual revenue run rate** reached ~$220M by mid-2025, primarily from API and enterprise services. [Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/deepseek)

## Business Model

DeepSeek operates a hybrid open-source/API-service model:

1. **Open-weight model releases.** All major models (V3, R1, V3.2) are released with open weights, allowing anyone to download, run, and fine-tune them. This differs sharply from the closed approach of OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google. [World Economic Forum](https://www.weforum.org/stories/2025/02/open-source-ai-innovation-deepseek/)
2. **API access at aggressive pricing.** As of March 2026, DeepSeek V3.2 API costs $0.28/$0.42 per million input/output tokens; R1 costs $0.50/$2.18. This is **up to 95% cheaper** than comparable offerings from OpenAI or Anthropic. Cache hits reduce costs by a further 90%. [DeepSeek API Docs](https://api-docs.deepseek.com/quick_start/pricing)
3. **Cross-subsidization from High-Flyer.** The hedge fund's quantitative trading profits provide a financial backstop, freeing DeepSeek from the need to maximize short-term revenue. [Interconnect/Substack](https://interconnect.substack.com/p/no-business-model-deepseeks-enduring)

The sustainability question: as one analyst noted, "it is hard to build a very successful commercial business on open source" when competitors can take your model and undercut you on price. DeepSeek's advantage is precisely that it does not need to build a standalone commercial business -- yet. [Lago Blog](https://getlago.com/blog/deepseek-open-source)

## Technical Innovations

DeepSeek's models introduced several architectural and training innovations that contributed to their efficiency:

- **Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) architecture.** DeepSeek-V3 has 671 billion total parameters but activates only ~37 billion per inference pass. This makes it dramatically cheaper to run than dense models of comparable capability. For comparison: V3 requires 250 GFLOPs/token vs. Llama 3.1 405B at 2,448 GFLOPs/token. [IntuitionLabs](https://intuitionlabs.ai/articles/deepseek-inference-cost-explained)
- **Multi-Head Latent Attention (MLA).** Reduces Key-Value cache usage by 93.3%, slashing memory requirements during inference. [BentoML](https://www.bentoml.com/blog/the-complete-guide-to-deepseek-models-from-v3-to-r1-and-beyond)
- **Reinforcement learning for reasoning (R1).** DeepSeek-R1 uses reinforcement learning to develop chain-of-thought reasoning capabilities, achieving performance comparable to OpenAI's o1 at a fraction of the cost.

### The Training Cost Controversy

DeepSeek reported that V3 was trained for approximately **$5.6 million** over 55 days on 2,048 Nvidia H800 GPUs, with R1's RL phase adding ~$294K. These figures generated enormous attention.

However, the $5.6M figure is **misleading as a total cost**. SemiAnalysis and other analysts pointed out it excludes:
- ~$51M+ for hardware acquisition
- Total hardware expenditure "well higher than $500 million" over the company's history
- Infrastructure, power, research team compensation, and failed experiments
- Prior training runs and data preparation

The $5.6M represents only the marginal GPU-hour cost of the final successful training run. [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2025/09/19/deepseek_cost_train/); [The Recursive](https://therecursive.com/martin-vechev-of-insait-deepseek-6m-cost-of-training-is-misleading/)

## The "DeepSeek Moment" (January 2025)

### What Happened

- **Late December 2024:** DeepSeek released V3, an open-weight model that matched frontier performance at dramatically lower reported training cost.
- **January 20, 2025:** DeepSeek released R1, a reasoning model rivaling OpenAI's o1 -- but open-weight and reportedly trained for under $6M.
- **January 27, 2025:** Markets reacted violently. **Nvidia lost ~$589 billion in market cap in a single day** -- the largest single-day loss in U.S. stock market history. Broadcom fell 17%, Oracle 14%, ASML 6%. The Nasdaq experienced a broad sell-off. [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/01/27/nx-s1-5276097/wall-street-stock-markets-tumble-deepseek-ai-tech-stock); [Analytics Vidhya](https://www.analyticsvidhya.com/blog/2025/01/how-deepseek-sparked-the-biggest-stock-market-crash-in-history/)

### Why It Mattered

The "DeepSeek moment" challenged three core assumptions underpinning the AI investment thesis:

1. **Frontier models require billions of dollars to train.** DeepSeek's headline cost figures (even if understated) demonstrated that architectural innovation could substitute for brute-force compute.
2. **U.S. export controls would prevent China from competing at the frontier.** DeepSeek achieved frontier performance using H800 chips (a lower-spec export-compliant variant), proving that constraints can drive innovation.
3. **AI infrastructure demand would grow monotonically.** If comparable models could be trained and run with far less compute, the market for GPUs, data centers, and power might not grow as fast as priced in.

Markets recovered within days (Nvidia clawed back $260B on January 28), but the episode permanently shifted the conversation about AI cost curves and China's AI competitiveness. [Booz Allen](https://www.boozallen.com/insights/ai-research/deepseek-impact-on-ai-market.html); [Darden/UVA](https://news.darden.virginia.edu/2025/01/29/qa-what-is-deepseek-the-bargain-ai-roiling-the-stock-market/)

## What Makes DeepSeek Interesting (GSB Research Perspective)

1. **Heterodox funding structure.** A quant fund bankrolling an open-source AI lab with no clear monetization path is a novel corporate form. It eliminates VC-driven short-termism but raises questions about governance, sustainability, and what happens if High-Flyer's trading performance declines.

2. **Constraints as innovation drivers.** U.S. chip export controls forced DeepSeek to maximize efficiency rather than simply scaling compute. The resulting architectural innovations (MoE, MLA, efficient RL) have influenced the entire field, including Western labs.

3. **Open-source as strategic weapon.** By releasing open weights, DeepSeek commoditizes the model layer -- undermining the business models of closed-model competitors (OpenAI, Anthropic) while building ecosystem influence without needing revenue.

4. **Talent model.** A ~200-person team of mostly young, domestically-trained Chinese engineers competing with organizations 10-50x their size challenges assumptions about the relationship between headcount, pedigree, and research output.

5. **Geopolitical implications.** DeepSeek's success complicates the U.S. strategy of using export controls to maintain an AI lead. It demonstrated that chip restrictions may slow but cannot stop Chinese AI progress, and may even accelerate architectural innovation.

6. **Market-repricing power.** No other AI startup has single-handedly triggered a ~$1 trillion repricing of the global technology sector. The "DeepSeek moment" revealed how fragile consensus assumptions about AI economics were.

## Key Uncertainties

- **True cost basis.** The gap between the headline $5.6M training cost and the estimated $500M+ total hardware spend remains poorly understood.
- **Hardware access.** DeepSeek's future depends partly on continued access to advanced chips. Tightening U.S. export controls could constrain future model development.
- **Commercial sustainability.** If High-Flyer's fund performance deteriorates, DeepSeek's funding model is at risk. The company has no demonstrated path to self-sustaining revenue.
- **Censorship and alignment.** As a Chinese company, DeepSeek's models comply with PRC content regulations, which limits their utility for certain applications and raises trust concerns for some international users.
- **R2 delays.** The successor reasoning model has been delayed multiple times, suggesting possible technical or resource bottlenecks.
