# Axiom Math

## Overview

Axiom Math (officially Axiom Quant Inc.) is building AI systems that generate formally verified outputs using Lean, a specialized programming language designed for mathematical proofs. Every step of an AI model's reasoning process is machine-checkable and logically guaranteed, making Axiom's approach fundamentally different from probabilistic AI outputs.

The startup achieved a landmark result in December 2025 when its deterministic AI scored perfectly on the Putnam Competition, one of the most prestigious undergraduate mathematics contests.

## Funding Story

Founded in 2024 by Stanford PhD dropout Carina Hong, Axiom raised a $50M seed at $300M valuation in mid-2025, extended to $64M by October 2025. The $200M Series A in March 2026 at $1.6B valuation, led by Menlo Ventures, positions Axiom as the leading venture-backed company in the formal verification space.

## What Makes Them Interesting

1. **Provably correct AI code.** While the rest of the industry generates probabilistic outputs, Axiom trains AI to produce formally verified results -- each step is mathematically proven correct.
2. **Putnam perfect score.** A concrete, auditable demonstration of mathematical reasoning capabilities that no other AI system has matched.
3. **Founder pedigree.** Carina Hong (Morgan Prize, Rhodes Scholar) combined with Shubho Sengupta (Meta AI supercluster) and Ken Ono (world-class number theorist) is an unusually deep technical bench.
4. **Massive market if AI-generated code grows.** As coding agents write more production code, the demand for verification that code actually does what it claims will be enormous.
