# Catena Labs

## Overview

Catena Labs is a Boston-based fintech company founded in 2021 by Sean Neville and Matt Venables, both co-founders of Circle (the company behind USDC, the world's second-largest stablecoin). Catena is building the first fully regulated AI-native financial institution designed to serve autonomous AI agents -- enabling machines to open accounts, make payments, and conduct financial transactions.

The company emerged from stealth in May 2025 with $18M in seed funding led by a16z crypto, with strategic participation from Circle Ventures, Coinbase Ventures, and notable angels including Tom Brady and Balaji Srinivasan.

## Funding story

**May 2025 -- Seed ($18M).** Led by a16z crypto. Participation from Breyer Capital, Circle Ventures, Coinbase Ventures, CoinFund, Pillar VC, and Stanford Engineering VF. Angel investors include Tom Brady, Bradley Horowitz, Kevin Lin (Twitch co-founder), Sam Palmisano (former IBM CEO), and Balaji Srinivasan. The company had been operating in stealth since 2021.

## What makes them interesting

1. **Circle founders building for AI agents.** Neville and Venables helped create USDC ($30B+ market cap). They understand regulated financial infrastructure at a level few founders can match, and they're applying that expertise to the emerging AI agent economy.

2. **First-mover on agent finance.** As AI agents become more autonomous, they will need financial identities, payment capabilities, and regulatory compliance. Catena is positioning to be the financial infrastructure layer for this new economy.

3. **Open-source ecosystem play.** The Agent Commerce Kit (ACK) builds developer adoption and standards, similar to how Circle's open USDC standard built ecosystem lock-in.

4. **The risk.** The AI agent economy is still largely theoretical. Whether autonomous agents will truly need regulated banking services (rather than just API keys to Stripe) remains to be proven. The timeline for mainstream agent commerce is uncertain.
