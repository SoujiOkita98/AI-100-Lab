# Moore Threads

## Overview

Moore Threads is a Chinese GPU company that designs both AI accelerator chips and graphics processors, positioning itself as a domestic alternative to Nvidia across the full stack -- from data center AI training to consumer gaming. The company was founded in 2020 by Zhang Jianzhong, who previously served as Nvidia's Global Vice President and General Manager for China.

Moore Threads went public on Shanghai's STAR Market on December 5, 2025, raising over $1 billion in one of the year's largest Chinese tech IPOs. The company has yet to turn a profit due to heavy R&D spending, but its revenue reached 701.8 million RMB in H1 2025 with losses narrowing. [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2025/07/01/chinese-gpu-startups-moore-threads-metax-file-ipos-seeking-combined-1-65b-funding/)

## Founder: Zhang Jianzhong (James Zhang)

Zhang Jianzhong spent over a decade at Nvidia, rising to **Global Vice President and General Manager of Nvidia China** -- one of the most senior positions a Chinese national held at the company. His deep knowledge of Nvidia's technology, product strategy, and China market dynamics made him uniquely positioned to build a Chinese GPU competitor.

Zhang co-founded Moore Threads with Zhang Yubo in 2020, just as U.S.-China tech tensions were escalating. The company name is a play on "Moore's Law." [SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/tech/big-tech/article/3327614/meet-moore-threads-how-former-nvidia-vice-president-created-chinese-gpu-star)

## Key Investors

Pre-IPO investors include **ByteDance**, **Tencent**, **Sequoia Capital China (HongShan)**, **GGV Capital**, and **Shenzhen Capital Group**.

## Products

### Huashan AI Chip (December 2025)
Moore Threads unveiled the Huashan AI training and inference chip based on its 5th-generation GPU architecture ("Huagang"). Zhang claimed Huashan outperforms Nvidia's Hopper-generation chips (H100/H200) in compute power, memory bandwidth, and capacity, though full specifications remain under wraps. The chip targets AI model training and inference for large language models. [TrendForce](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2025/12/22/news-chinas-moore-threads-unveils-huashan-ai-chip-reportedly-takes-aim-at-nvidias-hopper/)

### Lushan Graphics Chip
A consumer/professional graphics GPU claiming 15x performance improvement and 50x ray tracing improvement over the previous generation. [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/pc-components/gpus/moore-threads-unveils-next-gen-gaming-gpu-with-15x-performance-and-50x-ray-tracing-improvement-ai-gpu-with-claimed-performance-between-hopper-and-blackwell-also-in-the-works)

## What Makes Moore Threads Interesting (GSB Research Perspective)

1. **Nvidia executive defection.** Zhang Jianzhong's departure from Nvidia to found a Chinese competitor is one of the most consequential talent moves in the semiconductor industry. It represents the risk that export controls accelerate, rather than prevent, the development of indigenous Chinese chip capabilities.

2. **Full-stack GPU ambition.** Unlike Cambricon (which focuses on AI-specific accelerators), Moore Threads aims to compete with Nvidia across the entire GPU stack -- from data center AI training to consumer gaming. This is a much more ambitious (and capital-intensive) strategy.

3. **ByteDance and Tencent as investors.** Having China's two largest internet companies as investors provides both capital and a built-in customer base for AI infrastructure.

4. **IPO frenzy.** The Moore Threads IPO energized the broader narrative around China's domestic GPU industry, similar to how Cambricon's stock rally did for AI chips.

## Key Risks

- Performance claims unverified by independent benchmarks
- Manufacturing dependent on SMIC (same yield/capacity constraints as Cambricon)
- Still unprofitable with heavy R&D spending
- Software ecosystem (CUDA alternative) is immature compared to Nvidia's
