# Genesis Therapeutics

## Overview

Genesis Therapeutics is a computational drug discovery company that uses proprietary molecular AI technology to discover and develop novel small-molecule drugs for severe and debilitating diseases. Spun out of Stanford University's Vijay Pande lab, Genesis has built a platform that applies deep learning to molecular interactions, enabling the identification of drug candidates with higher predicted efficacy and better safety profiles than traditional screening methods. The company has secured multi-target collaborations with major pharmaceutical companies including Gilead Sciences and Incyte.

## Founders & Team

### Evan Feinberg, Ph.D. -- Founder & CEO
- Co-invented key deep learning technologies for molecular science during his Ph.D. in the lab of Vijay Pande at Stanford University.
- Published peer-reviewed papers on the application of deep learning to molecular simulations and drug discovery.
- Leads both the scientific vision and commercial strategy.

### Academic Origins
- The company spun out of Vijay Pande's lab at Stanford, known for pioneering work in computational chemistry, molecular dynamics, and the Folding@home distributed computing project.
- Pande later became a general partner at Andreessen Horowitz (a16z), further connecting Genesis to the venture ecosystem.

## Technology

Genesis Therapeutics' platform combines several AI/ML approaches for small-molecule drug discovery:

- **Molecular AI:** Proprietary deep learning models trained on molecular interaction data to predict drug-target binding, selectivity, and pharmacological properties.
- **Physics-informed models:** Integrates physics-based molecular simulations with machine learning, combining the interpretability of physical models with the pattern-recognition power of neural networks.
- **Stanford lineage:** Built on foundational research from the Pande lab in molecular dynamics and protein folding, giving the company deep expertise in the physics of molecular interactions.

## Business Model

- **Pharma partnerships:** Multi-target collaborations with large pharmaceutical companies. Gilead paid $35M upfront for three targets, with milestone payments and potential for additional targets. Incyte collaboration announced in February 2025.
- **Internal pipeline:** Developing proprietary drug candidates for undisclosed therapeutic indications.
- **Hybrid approach:** Revenue from pharma partnerships funds operations while internal pipeline development captures long-term upside.

## Key Partnerships

- **Gilead Sciences (2024):** Strategic collaboration for AI-driven drug discovery. $35M upfront payment for three targets, with potential for additional targets and milestone payments.
- **Incyte (Feb 2025):** Strategic AI-focused research collaboration for novel therapeutic discovery.
- **Nvidia:** NVentures investment and likely GPU infrastructure partnership, reflecting the compute-intensive nature of molecular AI.

## What Makes Genesis Therapeutics Interesting

1. **Stanford pedigree + Pande lab lineage.** Spun out of one of the most influential computational chemistry labs in the world. Vijay Pande's dual role as Stanford professor and a16z GP creates a unique academic-venture bridge.

2. **Validated pharma partnerships.** Gilead and Incyte collaborations with meaningful upfront payments demonstrate that large pharma companies are willing to pay for Genesis's AI capabilities -- a stronger signal than fundraising alone.

3. **Nvidia backing.** Direct investment from NVentures validates the computational approach and likely provides infrastructure advantages for training molecular AI models.

4. **Diverse investor base.** The mix of tech VCs (a16z), life sciences specialists (Baker Brothers), strategic pharma (Eli Lilly), and public market crossovers (BlackRock, Fidelity) provides both capital and domain expertise.

5. **Hybrid revenue model.** Pharma partnership revenue provides near-term cash flow while internal pipeline preserves upside -- a pragmatic approach that reduces burn rate risk.

## Risks & Open Questions

- **Pipeline opacity.** Specific internal drug candidates and their therapeutic areas are not publicly disclosed, making it difficult to assess pipeline value.
- **Clinical milestones.** No disclosed clinical-stage programs from the internal pipeline. The company's AI-discovered molecules have not yet entered human trials (publicly).
- **Partnership dependency.** Revenue concentration in a small number of pharma partnerships creates risk if collaborations underperform or are terminated.
- **Competitive landscape.** Numerous well-funded AI drug discovery companies (Recursion, Xaira, Relay Therapeutics) are pursuing similar small-molecule discovery approaches.
- **Valuation uncertainty.** With $327M raised but no disclosed valuation, it is difficult to assess whether the company is fairly priced relative to its stage and milestones.
