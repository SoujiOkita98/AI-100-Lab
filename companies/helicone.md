# Helicone

## Overview

Helicone is an open-source LLM observability platform and AI gateway that enables developers to monitor, evaluate, and experiment with their LLM applications through a simple one-line integration. Before being acquired by Mintlify in March 2026, Helicone had processed over 14.2 trillion tokens and served 16,000 organizations. The platform supports 100+ models and provides cost tracking, prompt analytics, and performance monitoring across major LLM providers.

## Products & Technology

1. **LLM Observability:** End-to-end monitoring of LLM requests with latency, cost, and quality metrics
2. **AI Gateway:** Proxy layer that routes and logs LLM API calls across providers
3. **Prompt Analytics:** Tools for analyzing prompt performance and iterating on prompt engineering
4. **Evaluation:** Built-in evaluation frameworks for measuring LLM output quality
5. **Experimentation:** A/B testing and comparison tools for different models and prompts

The platform is open-source (Apache v2.0 license) and integrates with major LLM providers including OpenAI, Anthropic, and others.

## Business Model

- **Open-source with managed cloud:** Free self-hosted version; paid cloud SaaS for convenience
- **Revenue (2024):** ~$1M ARR with a 5-person team
- **Acquisition:** Acquired by Mintlify in March 2026; continuing in maintenance mode post-acquisition

## What Makes Helicone Interesting

1. **Capital-efficient execution.** Reaching $1M ARR with a 5-person team on $5.5M in total funding is remarkably efficient, demonstrating strong product-market fit.

2. **Massive scale achieved.** Processing 14.2 trillion tokens and serving 16,000 organizations from a tiny team shows the power of the open-source distribution model.

3. **Successful exit via acquisition.** Being acquired by Mintlify (a16z-backed documentation platform) validates the LLM observability space and creates a combined knowledge infrastructure platform.

4. **One-line integration philosophy.** The extreme simplicity of integration (one line of code) drove viral adoption among developers who wanted observability without complex setup.
