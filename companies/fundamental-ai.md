# Fundamental

## Overview

Fundamental is an AI research company founded in October 2024 by former DeepMind researchers that emerged from stealth in February 2026 with $255 million in funding at a $1.4 billion valuation. The company builds Nexus, which it calls a "large tabular model" (LTM) -- a purpose-built AI system for enterprise structured data analysis.

What makes Nexus technically distinctive is that it is deterministic (same input always produces same output), does not use the transformer architecture that underlies GPT, Claude, and other LLMs, and is specifically designed for tabular/structured data rather than natural language. This positions it as a fundamentally different tool from general-purpose LLMs.

## Funding story

**2024 -- Seed (~$30M).** Initial funding to build the core technology.

**2026 -- $255M Series A at $1.4B valuation.** Led by Oak HC/FT, Valor Equity Partners, Battery Ventures, and Salesforce Ventures. Hetz Ventures also participated. The angel investor list is notable: Perplexity CEO Aravind Srinivas, Brex co-founder Henrique Dubugras, and Datadog CEO Olivier Pomel -- all operators who understand enterprise data infrastructure. Emerged from stealth as a day-one unicorn.

## What makes them interesting

1. **A genuine architectural departure.** Most AI startups are building on transformers. Fundamental explicitly rejected this, building a deterministic model for structured data. If enterprises need reliable, repeatable analysis of their tabular data (and they do), this addresses a real gap that LLMs struggle with.

2. **Determinism is a killer feature for enterprise.** LLMs hallucinate. They give different answers to the same question. For financial analysis, compliance, and business intelligence, deterministic outputs are not a nice-to-have -- they are a requirement. This is a genuine product-market insight.

3. **The "large tabular model" category creation.** Fundamental is not competing with OpenAI or Anthropic. It is defining a new category (LTM vs. LLM), which is a smart positioning strategy that avoids direct competition with better-funded frontier labs.

4. **DeepMind pedigree + enterprise-focused angel investors.** The combination of deep research talent (DeepMind) with enterprise operator angels (Srinivas, Dubugras, Pomel) suggests a team that understands both the science and the go-to-market.

5. **Massive TAM.** Enterprise structured data -- spreadsheets, databases, data warehouses -- is arguably the largest untapped AI opportunity. LLMs are mediocre at this. A purpose-built solution could capture enormous value.

## Notes

- **Confidence is medium** because the company just emerged from stealth and there is limited information on product traction, customer adoption, or technical benchmarks.
- **Valuation note:** $1.4B for a stealth-stage company with no public revenue is aggressive, even by 2026 standards. The DeepMind pedigree and category creation thesis are carrying the valuation.
- **Competitive risk:** Databricks, Snowflake, and major cloud providers are all pursuing AI-powered analytics. Fundamental's differentiation depends on whether the LTM architecture delivers meaningfully better results than fine-tuned LLMs applied to structured data.
