# Biren Technology

## Overview

Biren Technology is a Shanghai-based AI chip company that designs general-purpose GPUs for AI training and inference. The company went public on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange on January 2, 2026, raising HK$5.58 billion (~$717 million) in an IPO priced at the top end of its marketed range -- a strong signal of investor demand for Chinese AI chip companies.

Biren is notable for being on the **U.S. Commerce Department's Entity List** (added in October 2022), which restricts its access to American semiconductor technology and equipment. Despite these sanctions, the company successfully completed its IPO and continues to develop chips manufactured on domestic processes.

## Founders

### Zhang Wen
Zhang Wen was formerly the **President of SenseTime**, one of China's most prominent AI companies. His experience at SenseTime gave him deep understanding of the AI workloads that drive chip demand, informing Biren's product strategy.

### Jiao Guofang
Jiao Guofang is a former **Huawei** employee with expertise in semiconductor design and AI computing architecture.

The founding team's combination of AI application expertise (SenseTime) and hardware engineering experience (Huawei) positioned Biren to design chips specifically optimized for real-world AI workloads. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biren_Technology)

## Funding

Biren raised approximately $904 million across 6 rounds before its IPO. Key rounds include:
- **Series C (June 2025)**: $209M, with backing from Guangdong and Shanghai government funds
- **Pre-IPO valuation**: ~$2.19B (November 2023 estimate)
- **IPO (January 2026)**: Raised HK$5.58B at HK$19.60/share (top of range)

## What Makes Biren Interesting (GSB Research Perspective)

1. **Sanctions-to-IPO pathway.** Biren's trajectory from being placed on the U.S. Entity List in 2022 to completing a $717M Hong Kong IPO in 2026 demonstrates that U.S. sanctions have not prevented Chinese AI chip companies from accessing capital markets and continuing development.

2. **SenseTime-to-chip-company pipeline.** Zhang Wen's move from running SenseTime (China's leading AI application company) to founding an AI chip company illustrates the vertical integration impulse in China's AI ecosystem -- the same companies that need AI chips are now producing the talent that designs them.

3. **Government-backed funding.** Guangdong and Shanghai provincial government funds participating in Biren's pre-IPO rounds reflects the strategic national importance of domestic AI chip development.

4. **Hong Kong as a listing venue.** Biren's choice of HKEX (rather than Shanghai STAR) reflects the utility of Hong Kong as a capital market for sanctioned Chinese tech companies seeking international investor access.

## Key Risks

- U.S. Entity List status limits access to EDA tools, IP, and advanced manufacturing equipment
- Manufacturing constraints (dependent on domestic foundries)
- Competition from Cambricon, Moore Threads, Huawei Ascend
- Profitability timeline uncertain
