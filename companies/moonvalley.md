# Moonvalley

**One-liner:** AI research company building production-grade generative video models trained exclusively on licensed content, targeting Hollywood studios and enterprise clients.

## Founders & Leadership

**Team composition:** ~123 employees drawn from DeepMind, Google, Meta, Microsoft, TikTok, DreamWorks, and film/VFX veterans. The scientific core is London-trained (DeepMind), while the product and business side is Toronto-based, with creative operations in Los Angeles.

## Product: Marey

Moonvalley's flagship model, **Marey**, was announced in March 2025 and reached general availability in July 2025. It is described as the world's first production-grade AI video model trained exclusively on licensed, high-definition content.

**Key capabilities:**
- Native 1080p video generation at 24 FPS, up to 30 seconds (breaking the industry norm of 5-10 second clips)
- **3D-aware camera control** -- converts any 2D scene into a 3D environment, enabling cinematic camera moves (handheld simulation, trajectory shifts) from a single image
- **Object motion guidance** -- draw trajectories for elements; physics-aware movement with realistic weight, momentum, and timing
- **Motion transfer** -- extract motion from reference video and apply to new subjects/scenes
- **Pose transfer and inpainting** for element-specific edits
- Upcoming features (announced): lighting controls, deep object trajectories, character libraries

**Platform:** Moonvalley also announced beta access to **Voyager**, a proprietary platform unlocking Marey's full capabilities for high-end production workflows.

## Business Model

Moonvalley operates a **dual-tier model**:

1. **Consumer/Creator (credit-based subscriptions):**
   - $14.99 / 100 credits
   - $34.99 / 250 credits
   - $149.99 / 1,000 credits

2. **Enterprise:** Direct partnerships with studios, production companies, and brands. Enterprise clients can use Moonvalley's models with their own IP via custom licensing arrangements. API access for developers and internal platform teams is in development.

## What Makes Moonvalley Interesting

### 1. The "Clean Data" Bet
While competitors (Runway, Pika, Sora) have faced legal scrutiny over training data provenance, Moonvalley made a deliberate strategic choice to train exclusively on licensed content. They work with content partners to package videos into datasets that Moonvalley purchases. This is a significant moat if Hollywood and enterprise clients demand legal indemnification -- which they increasingly do.

### 2. Hollywood-Native DNA
Bryn Mooser brings genuine Hollywood credibility (two Oscar nominations, Emmy wins, 200+ films). The co-founding of **Asteria Labs** with Natasha Lyonne signals ambition beyond tooling -- Moonvalley wants to participate in the content creation itself. Strategic investment from CAA (the talent agency) further cements this positioning.

### 3. DeepMind Research Pedigree
The scientific leadership (Malinowski and Binkowski) came directly from DeepMind's video model team, giving the company deep technical credibility in a field where model quality is the primary differentiator.

### 4. Massive Seed Stage
$154M raised while still technically at "seed" stage is extraordinary, reflecting investor conviction and the capital intensity of training large video models. The CoreWeave investment provides strategic compute access.

### 5. Enterprise Positioning vs. Consumer-First Competitors
While Runway and Pika built consumer-first products, Moonvalley is explicitly targeting professional filmmakers and enterprises first. This is a higher-margin, stickier market if they can deliver on quality.

### Open Questions / Risks

- **Quality gap:** Independent comparisons (as of mid-2025) suggest Moonvalley's output quality still trails Runway Gen-4 in some areas, particularly camera motion smoothness and parallax effects. The licensed-data-only constraint may limit training diversity.
- **Burn rate:** Training frontier video models is extremely capital-intensive. $154M is substantial but may require further rounds.
- **Competitive pressure:** OpenAI (Sora), Google (Veo), Runway, and Pika are all well-funded and iterating rapidly. The ethical/licensed positioning is a differentiator but not a permanent moat if competitors clean up their data practices.
- **Revenue trajectory:** Consumer pricing is modest; enterprise revenue traction is unconfirmed publicly.
