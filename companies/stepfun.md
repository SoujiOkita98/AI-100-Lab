# StepFun (阶跃星辰)

## Overview

StepFun is a Shanghai-based AI foundation model company founded in April 2023 by Jiang Daxin, a 16-year veteran of Microsoft. It is one of China's "Six Little Tigers" (六小虎) -- the cohort of leading Chinese AI startups that also includes Zhipu AI, MiniMax, Baichuan AI, Moonshot AI, and 01.AI. Among this group, StepFun is notable for its consistent focus on **multimodal AI** from inception and its aggressive strategy of embedding models directly into consumer hardware ("AI plus terminals").

## Founders and Leadership

### Jiang Daxin (姜大昕) -- Co-Founder and CEO

- **Education**: Ph.D. in Computer Science, University at Buffalo
- **Prior career**: Joined Microsoft in 2007. Rose to Global Vice President and Chief Scientist of Microsoft's Software Technology Center Asia over a 16-year career. Led projects on the Bing search engine, Cortana voice assistant, Azure cognitive services, and natural language systems for Microsoft 365.
- **Motivation**: Jiang has said he decided to start StepFun after OpenAI released ChatGPT in November 2022, telling Chinese media: "I thought, I can do it myself, maybe even better."

### Key Co-Founders (also ex-Microsoft)

- **Jiao Binxing** -- leads search-related work at StepFun
- **Zhu Yibo** -- head of systems at StepFun

Both were recruited by Jiang from Microsoft. The founding team's deep Microsoft Research DNA is a defining characteristic; the company draws heavily on expertise in search, NLP, and large-scale systems from MSRA and Microsoft's broader Asia engineering operations.

### Yin Qi (印奇) -- Chairman (appointed January 2026)

- Co-founder and former CEO of **Megvii Technology** (Face++), one of China's early computer vision unicorns
- Currently also chairman of Qianli Technology (千里科技), a Geely-backed intelligent driving company
- Appointed chairman to oversee strategic direction, particularly the "AI plus terminal devices" strategy and autonomous driving integration
- Has outlined three 2026 objectives: (1) surpass 1 million installations of the Qianli autonomous driving system, (2) restore StepFun's foundational models to the global top tier, and (3) launch AI-native hardware within 12-15 months

The Yin Qi appointment is strategically significant -- it signals StepFun's bet on hardware distribution and automotive AI, not just model research.

## Business Model and Go-to-Market

StepFun's business model has three pillars:

1. **API / Cloud platform**: Developer access to Step models via platform.stepfun.com
2. **On-device AI ("AI plus terminals")**: Embedding models directly into smartphones and vehicles. By end of 2025, StepFun models were deployed on **42+ million phones** through partnerships with Oppo, Honor, ZTE, and others (~60% of China's major phone brands).
3. **Automotive AI**: Voice models and "AgentOS" integrated into Geely vehicles, with deployment expected to surpass 1 million vehicles by end of 2026. The Yin Qi chairmanship directly reinforces this vertical.

This "model-to-device" distribution strategy differentiates StepFun from competitors who focus primarily on cloud APIs or consumer chatbot apps.

## What Makes StepFun Interesting

1. **Multimodal-first from day one**: Unlike peers that started with text LLMs and added modalities later, StepFun bet on multimodal AI from its founding. Jiang Daxin's stated roadmap: "AI must go from unimodal to multimodal, to embodied intelligence, and finally to AGI."

2. **Hardware distribution at scale**: 42M+ phone deployments and a vehicle integration pipeline give StepFun real-world distribution that most model labs lack. This is a meaningful moat if on-device AI becomes the primary interface.

3. **The Yin Qi factor**: Bringing in the Megvii founder as chairman is an unusual move for a 2-year-old startup. It signals a strategic pivot toward commercialization and distribution over pure research, and creates a potential "xAI + Tesla" parallel -- combining foundation model research with automotive/hardware deployment.

4. **Strong state backing**: Heavy involvement from Shanghai municipal investment vehicles (Shanghai Guotou, Fortera Capital, Pudong VC) provides both capital and political support in an environment where government backing matters.

5. **Scaling law conviction**: Jiang Daxin has publicly maintained that scaling laws remain valid and that bigger models with more data will continue to improve -- a contrarian position as some in the industry debate diminishing returns from scale.

6. **Open-source strategy**: Several models released under Apache 2.0, including Step 3.5 Flash and collaborations with Geely on video/audio models.

## Risks and Open Questions

- **Monetization timeline**: Despite large distribution numbers, revenue and path to profitability are not publicly disclosed. On-device AI licensing economics remain unproven at scale.
- **Competition**: Faces intense competition from both the other "Six Tigers" and from China's tech giants (Baidu, Alibaba, ByteDance) who have far larger resources.
- **Export controls**: U.S. chip restrictions constrain access to cutting-edge training hardware, though StepFun has not publicly detailed its compute strategy.
- **IPO execution**: Hong Kong IPO plans are reported but unconfirmed; peer companies Zhipu AI and MiniMax listed in Hong Kong in early 2026 with mixed results.
