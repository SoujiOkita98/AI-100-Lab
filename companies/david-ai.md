# David AI

## Overview

David AI is the world's first dedicated audio data research lab, building the data layer for next-generation audio and speech AI. Founded by two former Scale AI employees, the company pays people worldwide to have natural conversations that get recorded on separate microphone channels, cleaned, and annotated with rich metadata. This data is then supplied to AI companies building speech recognition, multilingual AI, and conversational AI systems.

David AI raised $80 million across three funding rounds in less than 10 months -- one of the fastest fundraising trajectories in the YC S24 cohort -- reaching a ~$500M valuation by October 2025.

## Founders and Team

### Tomer Cohen (CEO)
- Previously worked at Scale AI, where he gained deep expertise in data infrastructure for AI training.
- Identified a critical gap in the market: while text and image data for AI training were well-served by companies like Scale AI, audio data remained fragmented and low-quality.

### Ben Wiley (CTO)
- Also a Scale AI alumnus.
- Brings technical expertise in data pipelines and ML infrastructure.

## Business Model

### How It Works

1. **Data collection:** David AI operates a global network of contributors who are paid to have natural conversations. These are recorded on separate microphone channels to capture clean, multi-speaker audio.
2. **Processing and annotation:** Recordings are cleaned, segmented, and annotated with rich metadata (speaker identity, language, accent, emotion, topic, etc.).
3. **Data supply:** The processed audio datasets are sold to AI companies building speech recognition, text-to-speech, voice cloning, multilingual AI, and conversational AI models.
4. **Quality differentiation:** Unlike scraped web audio or synthetic data, David AI's datasets are purpose-built for AI training with controlled recording conditions and detailed annotations.

### Market Positioning

David AI positions itself as the "Scale AI for audio" -- bringing the same rigor and scale to audio data collection that Scale AI brought to image and text labeling. The company's thesis is that as AI models move beyond text to multimodal capabilities (especially voice interfaces like those in ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini), demand for high-quality audio training data will surge.

## What Makes David AI Interesting

1. **Fastest fundraising in YC S24:** Raising $80M across three rounds in under 10 months, with a 4x valuation jump between Series A and B, signals strong investor conviction.

2. **Audio data gap:** Text and image data for AI training are well-commoditized, but audio data remains underserved. As voice interfaces proliferate (Siri, Alexa, ChatGPT Voice, etc.), the demand for diverse, high-quality audio training data is likely to grow significantly.

3. **Scale AI pedigree:** Both founders came from Scale AI, the dominant player in AI training data. They understand the market dynamics and are applying Scale's playbook to an adjacent, underserved vertical.

4. **NVIDIA as strategic investor:** NVIDIA's participation in the Series B signals alignment with the broader AI hardware ecosystem -- NVIDIA benefits from more AI model training, which requires more data.

5. **Risks and open questions:**
   - **Synthetic audio competition:** As text-to-speech models improve, synthetic audio data could reduce demand for human-recorded data.
   - **Market size uncertainty:** The audio data market is nascent; it's unclear how large it will become relative to text/image data markets.
   - **Customer concentration:** The number of companies building frontier speech/audio AI models is relatively small.
