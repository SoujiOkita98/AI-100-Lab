# Clay

## One-liner

AI-powered data enrichment and workflow automation platform that lets go-to-market teams build personalized outbound pipelines by combining 130+ data providers in a spreadsheet-like interface.

## Founding Story

Amin and Rusan, friends from McGill who both worked at Microsoft, spent years iterating on the idea of making data more accessible and actionable. Clay went through roughly six years of foundational product work (2017-2023) before hitting explosive product-market fit. The company was originally Canadian-founded but is now headquartered in New York City.

## Product & Business Model

### What Clay Does

Clay is a **"GTM development environment"** -- essentially a powerful spreadsheet that can:

1. **Enrich data** by pulling from 130+ data providers (LinkedIn, Clearbit, Apollo, ZoomInfo, etc.) simultaneously, waterfall-style, to maximize coverage.
2. **Automate workflows** using AI agents that score leads, write personalized emails, research companies, and trigger outbound sequences.
3. **Integrate with CRMs and outbound tools** (Salesforce, HubSpot, Outreach, etc.) to operationalize enriched data.

### Pricing / Monetization

Clay uses a **credit-based SaaS subscription model**:

| Tier | Monthly Price | Credits/Month |
|------|--------------|---------------|
| Free | $0 | ~100 |
| Starter | ~$134 | 2,000 |
| Explorer | ~$314 | 10,000 |
| Pro | ~$720 | 25,000 |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom pool |

All plans include **unlimited users** -- cost scales with data volume (rows and credits), not seats. This is a key differentiator vs. per-seat competitors like ZoomInfo ($15K+/yr base) and Apollo ($49/user/mo).

## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Positioning |
|-----------|------------|
| **ZoomInfo** | Legacy market leader; expensive, full-stack sales intelligence |
| **Apollo.io** | Lower-cost alternative; strong on prospecting + sequences |
| **Clearbit** (acquired by HubSpot) | Data enrichment, now embedded in HubSpot |
| **Lusha, Cognism, Kaspr** | Point solutions for contact data |
| **6sense, Demandbase** | Intent-data and ABM platforms |

Clay's edge: it **aggregates** 130+ providers rather than competing as a single data source, and its spreadsheet-like workflow builder lets technical GTM teams create custom pipelines without engineering support.

## What Makes Clay Interesting

1. **The "GTM Engineering" category creation.** Clay is actively defining a new job title -- "GTM Engineer" -- someone who sits between sales ops and software engineering. This is a bet that the future of sales is programmable, not just tool-assisted.

2. **Aggregator model for data.** Rather than building a proprietary data asset (like ZoomInfo), Clay sits on top of everyone else's data, letting users waterfall across providers. This is capital-efficient and provider-agnostic.

3. **Credit-based, not seat-based pricing.** Unlimited users removes adoption friction inside organizations. Revenue scales with usage, not headcount.

4. **Extraordinary growth trajectory.** $1M to $100M ARR in ~2 years is among the fastest B2B SaaS ramps in recent history, comparable to companies like Wiz and Rippling.

5. **6 years of "wilderness" before PMF.** The company was founded in 2017 but only hit explosive growth around 2023. This is a compelling founder resilience story -- Amin and team iterated for years before the AI wave and product-market fit converged.

6. **$5B valuation at ~50x ARR.** The January 2026 tender offer at $5B on ~$100M ARR reflects enormous market confidence, though it also signals that expectations are very high.

7. **Community and ecosystem play.** Clay has built a strong community of "Clay experts" and agencies (Claygencies) that build workflows for other companies, creating a services ecosystem around the platform.

## Risks & Open Questions

- **Sustainability of growth rate.** Tripling revenue year-over-year is extraordinary; the question is whether Clay can maintain momentum as it enters the mid-market and enterprise segments.
- **Data provider dependency.** Clay's value relies on third-party data providers continuing to allow access. If key providers (LinkedIn, etc.) restrict APIs, Clay's coverage could degrade.
- **Competitive response.** ZoomInfo, Apollo, and HubSpot (with Clearbit) are all adding AI workflow features. The moat may narrow.
- **Profitability.** No public data on burn rate or path to profitability. At ~1,167 employees, the cost base is substantial.
