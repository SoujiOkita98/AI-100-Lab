# Granola -- AI Meeting Notes

## Overview

Granola is an AI-powered notepad that combines a user's typed notes with automatic meeting transcription to produce enhanced, structured meeting notes. Unlike competing tools that insert AI bots into video calls, Granola runs locally on the user's computer and works silently in the background, making it unobtrusive and privacy-friendly. The product has gained strong traction among tech executives, VC partners, and startup leaders, growing from launch in May 2024 to $22M ARR by the end of fiscal 2024. The company is reportedly raising at a near-unicorn valuation in early 2026.

## Founders & Team

| Founder | Role | Background |
|---------|------|------------|
| **Chris Pedregal** | Co-Founder & CEO | Computer Science at Stanford. Former Google Product Manager (Gmail, Search, Maps). In 2013, left Google to found **Socratic**, an AI-powered tutor for high school students that grew to 10M+ MAU and won Apple "App of the Year" (2017). Socratic was acquired by Google in 2018, where it now receives 4B+ questions per year. |
| **Sam Stephenson** | Co-Founder | Studied graphic design at Falmouth. Spent time in San Francisco at a design agency and education non-profit. Previously at Ideaflow (a note-taking startup). Built products for Swim Smooth, Carv, and various B2B companies. |

**Origin story:** Pedregal was inspired to build Granola after experimenting with GPT-3 and realizing that AI could augment -- rather than replace -- the human act of note-taking in meetings. He teamed up with Stephenson in March 2023, and they launched the first product in May 2024. The app grew 10% in weekly active users week-over-week from launch.

## Business Model

Granola operates a **freemium SaaS** model with seat-based pricing:

| Tier | Price | Key Features |
|------|-------|--------------|
| Basic | Free | Unlimited meetings, limited history retention |
| Business | $14/month per user | Centralized billing, team collaboration, full history |
| Enterprise | $35+/month per seat | SSO, admin controls, AI training opt-out, priority support |

**Key dynamics:**
- Over 57% of users are in leadership roles (executives, founders, VPs), giving the product a top-down adoption pattern
- Notable customers include leaders at Vercel, Ramp, Roblox, and partners at Benchmark, Sequoia, Accel, and Union Square Ventures
- Average user has ~6 meetings per week on the platform
- Weekly user base has grown sixfold since launch

## What Makes Granola Interesting

1. **"AI augmentation" philosophy:** Granola's core insight is that people do not want AI to fully automate their notes -- they want AI to enhance notes they are already taking. This human-in-the-loop design makes the output feel personalized rather than generic.

2. **No bot in the meeting:** Unlike Otter.ai, Fireflies, and other competitors, Granola does not add a visible AI bot to video calls. It processes audio locally, which removes the social friction of having an AI "participant" in sensitive meetings. This is a major reason it has been adopted by VCs and executives.

3. **Socratic exit pedigree:** CEO Chris Pedregal previously built and sold an AI education app to Google for an undisclosed amount, giving him direct experience scaling AI consumer products and navigating a Google acquisition.

4. **Elite investor network:** The combination of NFDG (Nat Friedman/Daniel Gross), Lightspeed, Spark, and angel investors who are themselves founders of major tech companies creates an unusually strong distribution channel among Silicon Valley decision-makers.

5. **Speed of growth:** Going from launch (May 2024) to $22M ARR in under a year, with a potential unicorn valuation by early 2026, represents one of the fastest growth trajectories in the AI productivity space.

6. **Team collaboration expansion:** The May 2025 Series B coincided with the launch of collaborative features for teams, signaling a shift from individual productivity tool to enterprise workspace -- a classic SaaS expansion playbook.

## Competitive Landscape

- **Otter.ai** -- Largest competitor; $100M ARR, 25M users. Uses in-meeting bot approach.
- **Fireflies.ai** -- AI meeting assistant with CRM integrations.
- **tl;dv** -- AI meeting recorder focused on sales teams.
- **Fathom** -- Free AI note-taker; strong in the SMB segment.
- **Microsoft Copilot / Google Gemini** -- Platform-level AI meeting features built into Teams and Meet.

## Key Risks

- Microsoft and Google are embedding AI meeting notes directly into Teams and Meet, potentially commoditizing the standalone market
- Otter.ai has a significant head start in users and enterprise penetration
- Privacy-sensitive positioning may limit data advantages for model improvement
- The product currently works best for video meetings; expanding to other contexts (in-person, phone) is an open challenge
