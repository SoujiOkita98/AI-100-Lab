# Observe.AI

## Overview

Observe.AI is an AI-powered platform for contact centers that embeds intelligence into 100% of customer conversations. Originally built around conversation intelligence and quality assurance automation, the company has evolved into a full agentic AI platform offering VoiceAI Agents, ChatAI Agents, and real-time AI copilots. The platform serves enterprises looking to automate customer interactions while maintaining quality and compliance oversight.

## Founders and Team

### Swapnil Jain -- Co-Founder & CEO

Swapnil Jain is from Vidisha, a small tier-4/tier-5 town in Madhya Pradesh, India. He studied Computer Science at IIT Delhi (B.Tech). After graduating, he joined Twitter in San Francisco as a software engineer and spent approximately three years there, rising to Technical Team Lead / Senior Software Engineer. Notably, he was Twitter's first employee based in India and helped open Twitter's India office, spearheading the company's first India-based engineering teams. He left Twitter to co-found Observe.AI in 2017.

### Jithendra Vepa -- Co-Founder & CTO / Chief Scientist

Jithendra Vepa brings deep speech technology expertise with 18+ years in the field. He holds a B.E. in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from Andhra University, an M.Sc (Engg.) in Signal Processing from the Indian Institute of Science (IISc, Bangalore), and a PhD in Speech Synthesis and Speech Signal Processing from the University of Edinburgh. His career includes research roles at Rhetorical Systems (UK), IDIAP Research Institute (Switzerland), Philips Research, and Samsung R&D India, where he built the speech technology team as Senior Principal Engineer. He is a Senior Member of IEEE. His deep domain expertise in ASR (automatic speech recognition) and speech processing is the technical backbone of Observe.AI.

### Akash Singh -- Co-Founder, Head of Engineering

Akash Singh is an IIT Delhi graduate (like Swapnil) with experience in high-tech and startup environments. He manages the engineering team from Bengaluru, India.

### Sharath Keshava Narayana -- Co-Founder & CRO

Sharath is a second-time entrepreneur with 14+ years of experience building and managing global sales teams. Prior experience includes roles at Unbxd, AWS, and Akamai. He leads go-to-market functions.

**Founder Pattern:** All four co-founders are of Indian origin. The team combines IIT Delhi CS talent (Swapnil, Akash), world-class speech science expertise (Jithendra via IISc + Edinburgh PhD), and enterprise sales experience (Sharath). The India-US axis is central to the company's DNA -- engineering in Bengaluru, GTM in the US.

## Business Model

Observe.AI operates as an **enterprise SaaS** platform with a subscription model. Key revenue characteristics:

- **Per-agent or per-interaction pricing** (typical for contact center software; exact pricing not publicly disclosed)
- **Land-and-expand motion**: starts with conversation intelligence / QA automation, expands into real-time copilots and VoiceAI agents
- **Target market**: Mid-market to large enterprise contact centers (350+ customers as of 2025)
- **Growth metrics**: The company reported ~150% ARR growth and 125% YoY increase in customers with $100K+ contracts (fiscal year not specified but likely FY2022 or FY2023). Interaction volume analyzed grew 3x YoY.
- **Revenue figures**: Absolute ARR/revenue not publicly disclosed. Estimated in the $50M-$100M ARR range based on funding stage and growth metrics (this is speculative).

## Product Evolution

1. **Phase 1 (2017-2022)**: Conversation Intelligence platform -- post-call analytics, automated QA scoring of 100% of interactions, agent coaching insights. Contact-center-specific LLMs.
2. **Phase 2 (2023-2024)**: Added generative AI capabilities, real-time agent assist copilots, after-call summarization.
3. **Phase 3 (2025+)**: Launched **VoiceAI Agents** (GA March 2025) and ChatAI Agents -- autonomous AI agents that handle customer calls end-to-end. Companies can go live in one week. Also acquired **Dubdub** to expand conversational AI offerings.

This evolution from analytics to automation is strategically significant. The company went from observing conversations to participating in them.

## What Makes Observe.AI Interesting

1. **Analytics-to-Automation Flywheel**: By starting with conversation intelligence (analyzing millions of interactions), Observe.AI built proprietary training data and domain expertise before launching autonomous VoiceAI agents. The data moat from the analytics phase directly improves agent quality.

2. **Contact-Center-Specific LLMs**: Rather than using generic foundation models, Observe.AI has built LLMs tuned for the contact center domain -- understanding compliance language, sentiment in service contexts, and industry-specific terminology.

3. **Hybrid Workforce Vision**: The platform is designed for gradual AI adoption -- enterprises can start with human agents assisted by AI copilots, then selectively automate specific call types with VoiceAI agents, all monitored through the same QA/compliance layer. This pragmatic approach reduces adoption friction in risk-averse industries like healthcare and finance.

4. **Indian Founder DNA in Enterprise AI**: All four co-founders are Indian-origin, combining IIT Delhi engineering talent, IISc/Edinburgh speech science, and enterprise GTM experience. The Bengaluru engineering center provides cost-efficient R&D. This is a pattern increasingly common in high-value enterprise AI companies.

5. **Y Combinator to SoftBank in 4 Years**: The trajectory from YC W18 to a $125M SoftBank-led Series C by 2022 demonstrates rapid validation.

6. **Market Timing**: The $400B+ contact center market is one of the largest enterprise software TAMs and is in early innings of AI transformation. The shift from post-call analytics to real-time and autonomous agents represents a massive expansion of addressable spend per customer.
