# Jump -- Company Profile

## Overview

Jump is an AI-powered operating system for financial advisors that automates administrative workflows, enabling advisors to spend more time with clients. Launched publicly in January 2024, the platform has scaled to 27,000 advisors in under two years -- nearly 1 in 10 US financial advisors now use Jump. The company continues to add over 2,000 new advisors each month, making it one of the fastest-growing AI platforms in the wealth management industry.

## Founders & Key People

### Parker Ence -- Co-Founder & CEO

- **Prior career:** Led data broker Veraset, gaining experience in data-driven business models at scale.
- **Role at Jump:** CEO, overall strategy and growth.

### Tim Chaves -- Co-Founder

- **Prior career:** Part of the founding team behind ZipBooks, an accounting software company acquired by Divvy in 2019.
- **Role at Jump:** Product and operations.

### Adam Kirk -- Co-Founder

- **Prior career:** Also part of the founding team at ZipBooks prior to its acquisition by Divvy.
- **Role at Jump:** Technology and engineering.

### Origin Story

The founding team had deep experience in fintech through ZipBooks (Chaves and Kirk) and data platforms (Ence). They recognized that financial advisors were drowning in administrative tasks -- CRM updates, meeting prep, compliance documentation, and follow-ups -- and that modern AI could automate the vast majority of this work. Jump launched publicly in January 2024, supported by a $4.6M seed round.

## Product & Technology

### Core Platform

Jump provides an AI operating system that automates the administrative workflow of financial advisors:

- **Meeting Preparation:** Automatically gathers client data, recent portfolio changes, and relevant market updates before advisor-client meetings.
- **Post-Meeting Documentation:** Transcribes and summarizes meeting notes, generates action items, and updates CRM records.
- **Compliance Documentation:** Automates the creation of compliance-ready records for regulatory requirements.
- **Client Communication:** Drafts personalized follow-up emails, account reviews, and client-facing content.
- **Workflow Orchestration:** Connects across the advisor's tech stack (CRM, portfolio management, custodian platforms) to reduce context-switching.

### Technical Approach

Jump is built as an AI agent that understands the specific workflows and compliance requirements of financial advisory. The platform integrates with major advisor tech stacks and custodian platforms to provide contextual automation.

## Business Model

- **Target customers:** Financial advisors at RIAs, independent broker-dealers, and wirehouses across the US.
- **Go-to-market:** Bottom-up adoption by individual advisors, with expansion to firm-wide deployments. The rapid growth (2,000+ new advisors per month) suggests a product-led growth motion.
- **Revenue model:** SaaS subscription (specific pricing not disclosed).
- **Market size:** There are approximately 300,000 financial advisors in the US, and Jump already serves ~9% of them.

## Customers & Traction

- **27,000 advisors** on the platform as of February 2026.
- Nearly **1 in 10 US financial advisors** now use Jump.
- Adding **2,000+ new advisors per month**, indicating strong organic growth.
- Scaled from zero to 27,000 users in less than 2 years since public launch.

## Competitive Landscape

- **Nevis** -- AI for wealth management advisors ($40M raised, Sequoia-backed).
- **Zocks** -- AI notetaker for financial advisors.
- **Pulse360** -- Workflow automation for advisors.
- **Salesforce Financial Services Cloud** -- Incumbent CRM with AI features.

Jump differentiates through its rapid adoption rate and purpose-built design for the financial advisor workflow, rather than being a general-purpose tool adapted for finance.

## What Makes Jump Interesting

1. **Extraordinary adoption velocity.** Going from launch to 27,000 advisors (~9% of the US market) in under 2 years is remarkable for enterprise-grade software in a regulated industry.

2. **Strategic investor validation.** Backing from Allianz Life Ventures, TIAA Ventures, and Citi Ventures represents endorsement from major incumbents who serve the same advisor base.

3. **Network effects potential.** As more advisors adopt Jump, the platform accumulates workflow data that can improve its AI and create switching costs.

4. **Clear path to market dominance.** With 2,000+ advisors joining monthly and a 300,000-advisor addressable market, Jump could reach a significant share of the US advisory market within a few years.

5. **Founded in Salt Lake City.** Operating outside Silicon Valley with lower burn rates while achieving top-tier growth metrics.

## Key Risks & Open Questions

- **Revenue per advisor.** With 27,000 advisors, the revenue per user and overall ARR remain undisclosed. Advisor willingness to pay for AI tools is still being proven at scale.
- **Retention rates unknown.** Fast sign-up does not guarantee sticky usage; churn rates are not public.
- **Compliance risk.** AI-generated documentation in regulated financial services requires high accuracy to avoid regulatory issues.
- **Competitive pressure from Nevis.** Sequoia-backed Nevis is targeting the same market with strong VC backing.
- **Valuation undisclosed.** Cannot assess whether the $80M Series B was raised at a reasonable multiple.
