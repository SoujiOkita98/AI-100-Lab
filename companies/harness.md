# Harness

## Overview

Harness is an AI-powered software delivery platform that automates the "after-code" phase of software development -- the testing, security scanning, deployment, verification, and cost optimization work that consumes roughly 70% of engineering time. Founded by serial entrepreneur Jyoti Bansal (who previously built and sold AppDynamics to Cisco for $3.7B), Harness uses AI agents and a proprietary software delivery knowledge graph to automate functions across the entire software delivery lifecycle. The company surpassed $250M ARR in 2025 and raised $240M at a $5.5B valuation.

## Founder and Leadership

### Jyoti Bansal -- Founder & CEO

- **Background**: Founded AppDynamics in 2008, grew it to a market leader in application performance management, and sold it to Cisco for $3.7 billion in 2017 (one day before its planned IPO). An IIT Delhi alumnus.
- **Significance**: Bansal is one of the most successful enterprise software founders of the last decade. His ability to build and scale enterprise products is Harness's strongest asset. He is a repeat founder who has navigated both the startup phase and the scale phase.

## Product and Technology

### Core Platform

Harness's platform spans six major modules:

1. **Continuous Integration (CI)**: AI-optimized build and test pipelines
2. **Continuous Delivery (CD)**: Automated deployment with canary, blue-green, and rolling strategies
3. **Feature Flags**: Granular feature rollout and experimentation
4. **Cloud Cost Management**: AI-driven cloud spend optimization
5. **Service Reliability Management**: Automated incident response and SLO management
6. **Security Testing Orchestration**: Automated security scanning integrated into CI/CD

### AIDA (AI Development Assistant)

Launched in 2023, AIDA integrates generative AI across the software delivery lifecycle. Capabilities include autocomplete for pipeline configurations, onboarding assistance, automated code reviews, chaos engineering experiment generation, and natural-language pipeline creation.

### Software Delivery Knowledge Graph

Harness's proprietary knowledge graph maps code changes, services, deployments, tests, environments, incidents, policies, and costs. This provides the AI agents with rich context for automation decisions.

## What Makes Harness Interesting

1. **The "after-code" thesis is strategically compelling.** AI coding assistants are accelerating code production, but the testing, security, deployment, and observability pipeline remains largely manual. Harness is the leading AI-native company addressing this bottleneck. As AI writes more code, the demand for automated delivery infrastructure grows proportionally.

2. **Founder pedigree is exceptional.** Jyoti Bansal built AppDynamics from zero to a $3.7B acquisition. His track record of building enterprise-grade infrastructure software is directly relevant to Harness's market.

3. **$250M+ ARR with a clear path to IPO.** The revenue scale, combined with the Goldman Sachs-led Series E, positions Harness as a strong IPO candidate in the 2026-2027 timeframe.

4. **Platform breadth creates cross-sell opportunities.** By covering CI, CD, feature flags, cloud cost management, security testing, and reliability -- all unified by a knowledge graph -- Harness can expand within accounts more effectively than point-solution competitors.

5. **Workday partnership validates enterprise credibility.** Workday selecting Harness to power agentic AI software delivery at enterprise scale is a significant reference customer.

## Competitive Landscape

- **GitHub Actions** (Microsoft) -- CI/CD incumbent with massive distribution
- **GitLab** -- integrated DevOps platform
- **CircleCI**, **Jenkins** -- traditional CI/CD tools
- **LaunchDarkly** -- feature flag competitor
- **Vantage**, **CloudHealth** -- cloud cost management

## Key Uncertainties

- Profitability and burn rate are not publicly disclosed
- How effectively the AI-powered features differentiate from increasingly capable competitors
- IPO timing and market conditions
- Whether the breadth of the platform creates complexity that slows enterprise adoption
