# Moveworks -- Agentic AI Copilot for Enterprise Employee Support

## Company Overview

Moveworks built an AI-powered employee support platform -- an "enterprise copilot" -- that automates IT, HR, finance, and facilities requests through natural language conversation. Deployed inside collaboration tools like Slack and Microsoft Teams, the platform uses a combination of proprietary and open-source LLMs to understand employee requests, resolve them autonomously where possible, and route complex issues to the right human agent.

The company's core thesis was that most internal employee support requests (password resets, software provisioning, policy questions, expense inquiries) are repetitive and automatable. By sitting as an AI layer across enterprise systems (ServiceNow, Workday, Salesforce, Jira, Coupa, etc.), Moveworks could resolve 40-60% of Tier-1 support tickets without human intervention, dramatically reducing mean time to resolution and support costs.

By late 2024, Moveworks had surpassed **$100M in ARR** (up from ~$55.6M earlier that year), served **350+ large enterprises** including 10% of the Fortune 500, and had over **5.5 million employee users**. On March 10, 2025, ServiceNow announced it would acquire Moveworks for **$2.85 billion** in cash and stock. The deal closed on **December 15, 2025**.

## Technology & Product

### Core Architecture

Moveworks' platform centers on its **Agentic Reasoning Engine** -- an LLM-powered architecture that:

1. **Plans** multiple steps to accomplish a goal.
2. **Selects and executes** the next action (API call, workflow trigger, knowledge retrieval).
3. **Observes results and adapts** the plan dynamically.

The engine employs a mix of proprietary and open-source models, each optimized for specific subtasks (intent classification, slot filling, policy validation, action orchestration).

### MoveLM -- Proprietary Enterprise LLM

Moveworks developed **MoveLM**, a 7B-parameter instruction-tuned LLM trained on:
- 6+ years of anonymized enterprise interaction data from hundreds of customers.
- Synthetic supplemental data and curated external datasets.
- Over **1.2 million high-quality, enterprise-specific training examples**.
- Fine-tuned with reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF).

MoveLM was purpose-built for enterprise function-calling and reasoning tasks -- understanding IT/HR/finance terminology, enforcing business policies, and generating API-compatible outputs.

### Key Platform Components

- **Enterprise Copilot:** Conversational AI interface in Slack/Teams for employee self-service.
- **Enterprise Search:** Unified search across 100+ enterprise applications with permission-aware retrieval.
- **Agent Studio:** Low-code tools for building custom AI agents and workflows.
- **Connector Ecosystem:** Pre-built integrations with ServiceNow, Workday, Salesforce, Jira, Coupa, SAP, and many more.
- **FedRAMP Authorization:** Approved for U.S. government deployments.

## Business Model

- **SaaS subscription model** with pricing based on number of employees/seats.
- Revenue primarily from annual recurring contracts with large enterprises (typically 10,000+ employees).
- Land-and-expand motion: initial deployment often in IT support, then expanding to HR, finance, facilities.
- Available on **AWS Marketplace** for simplified procurement.
- Pre-acquisition ARR growth: ~$55.6M (early 2024) to $100M+ (late 2024) -- nearly doubling in under a year.

## What Makes Moveworks Interesting (Research Notes)

1. **Early mover in enterprise AI agents (2016).** Founded well before the ChatGPT era, Moveworks was building conversational AI for enterprises when most competitors were still rule-based chatbots. This gave them years of proprietary training data -- a durable competitive advantage.

2. **ARR trajectory validates enterprise AI demand.** The jump from ~$55.6M to $100M+ ARR in a single year (2024) coincided with enterprise adoption of agentic AI accelerating post-GPT-4. This near-doubling suggests strong product-market fit and expansion within existing accounts.

3. **Acquisition economics tell a cautionary tale.** At $2.85B, the exit was solid in absolute terms but only ~1.36x the Series C valuation. Late-stage investors (Tiger Global, Alkeon) likely saw modest returns. This reflects the compression of AI startup valuations between the 2021 peak and 2025 M&A market -- a useful data point for understanding AI startup exit dynamics.

4. **Proprietary data flywheel.** MoveLM's training on 6+ years of real enterprise interactions across hundreds of customers is difficult to replicate. This data moat -- not the model architecture itself -- was arguably the most valuable asset ServiceNow acquired.

5. **Front-end vs. back-end positioning.** Moveworks positioned itself as the "front door" to enterprise services (the conversational layer employees interact with), while ServiceNow owns the "back end" (workflow orchestration, ITSM, HRSM). The acquisition unifies both layers under one company, which is strategically clean but also highlights the vulnerability of front-end-only AI companies when platform owners decide to integrate vertically.

6. **FedRAMP authorization** opened government markets -- a significant differentiator against competitors lacking this compliance milestone.
