# Nous Research

## Overview

Nous Research is an open-source AI research lab founded in 2022 by a small group of researchers who met on Discord and Twitter. Headquartered in New York, the company has grown from a grassroots community project into a funded startup that sits at the unusual intersection of open-source AI and decentralized crypto infrastructure. With roughly 20-30 employees and approximately $65-70M in total funding, Nous punches well above its weight in model quality -- its Hermes and Nomos model families routinely compete with outputs from labs that are orders of magnitude larger and better funded.

The company's core thesis is that AI development should not be monopolized by a handful of well-capitalized incumbents. It pursues this through two complementary strategies: (1) releasing high-quality open-source models under permissive licenses, and (2) building Psyche, a decentralized training network on the Solana blockchain that allows anyone with spare GPU capacity to contribute to model training.

## Founders and Team

Nous Research has an unconventional founding story. The team did not emerge from Stanford, Google, or Meta -- they coalesced organically from online AI communities.

- **Jeffrey Quesnelle (CEO):** The most conventionally credentialed founder. Holds an MS in Computer Science from the University of Michigan. Spent five years as Director of Software Development at Intrepid Control Systems (automotive networking / autonomous driving), then moved into crypto as an MEV engineer at Eden Network. This dual background in systems engineering and blockchain directly informs the Psyche network architecture. ([Source](https://usethebitcoin.com/crypto-personalities/all-you-need-to-know-about-jeffrey-quesnelle-the-co-founder-of-nous-research/))

- **Karan Malhotra (Head of Behavior):** Holds a BA in Philosophy and Religion from Emory University -- a notably non-technical background for an AI lab co-founder. Described as "crypto native." His work at Nous focuses on model alignment and behavioral steering, which is central to the Hermes model identity of being "neutrally aligned" and highly steerable by the user rather than the provider. ([Source](https://chainofthought.xyz/p/nous-research-all-you-need-to-know))

- **Teknium (Head of Post-Training):** A pseudonymous figure with an extensive open-source track record on GitHub. Teknium is widely credited in the open-source LLM community for pioneering data synthesis and fine-tuning methodologies. Sometimes referenced as "Ryan Teknium" but full identity remains undisclosed. The decision to maintain anonymity while co-founding a venture-backed company is itself notable and reflects the crypto/open-source cultural roots of the organization. ([Source](https://chainofthought.xyz/p/nous-research-all-you-need-to-know))

- **Shivani Mitra (Co-Founder):** Lower public profile than the other founders. Listed in corporate filings and press coverage but limited public information is available about her specific role and background. ([Source](https://theaiinsider.tech/2025/04/30/nous-research-lands-65m-to-champion-open-source-approach-to-ai-development/))

The team remains small (roughly 20-30 people as of early 2026). Key contributors beyond the founders include Roger Jin (lead trainer on the Nomos project), Dakota Mahan (infrastructure), and Chen Guang (advisory). The lean team size relative to output is a recurring theme in coverage of the company.

## Key Products and Models

### Hermes Model Family

The Hermes series is Nous Research's flagship general-purpose model line. Key characteristics:

- **Neutral alignment / high steerability:** Unlike models from OpenAI or Anthropic that embed provider-determined safety behaviors, Hermes models are designed to be "neutrally aligned" -- the user controls the model's behavior through system prompts rather than having restrictions baked in. This is philosophically deliberate, not an oversight. ([Source](https://venturebeat.com/ai/nous-research-drops-hermes-4-ai-models-that-outperform-chatgpt-without-content-restrictions))

- **Hermes 4 (2025):** Introduced "hybrid reasoning" -- users can toggle between fast responses and deeper chain-of-thought processes. Claimed to match or exceed ChatGPT-class performance on standard benchmarks. ([Source](https://hermes4.nousresearch.com/))

- **Hermes 4.3 (December 2025):** Based on ByteDance's Seed-OSS-36B-Base model. Notably, it was the first production model trained entirely on the Psyche decentralized network -- a proof of concept for the entire Psyche thesis. ([Source](https://venturebeat.com/ai/nous-research-just-released-nomos-1-an-open-source-ai-that-ranks-second-on))

- **Hermes Agent (February 2026):** An open-source autonomous agent system built on the Hermes-3 model family, featuring multi-level memory and remote terminal access to address memory decay and environmental isolation in agentic workflows. ([Source](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/02/26/nous-research-releases-hermes-agent-to-fix-ai-forgetfulness-with-multi-level-memory-and-dedicated-remote-terminal-access-support/))

### Nomos 1

Released in early 2026, Nomos 1 is a 30B-parameter reasoning model (only 3B parameters active at inference via mixture-of-experts) that scored 87/120 on the 2025 Putnam Mathematical Competition -- enough to rank 2nd out of ~3,988 human competitors. It runs on consumer-grade hardware. Released under Apache 2.0 on Hugging Face. This result is remarkable given the team and compute resources involved relative to competitors. ([Source](https://venturebeat.com/ai/nous-research-just-released-nomos-1-an-open-source-ai-that-ranks-second-on))

### Psyche Network

Psyche is Nous Research's decentralized AI training infrastructure, built on the Solana blockchain. Key technical elements:

- **DisTrO optimizer:** Compresses inter-GPU communication by 1,000-10,000x, making distributed training over the open internet practical rather than requiring co-located data center GPUs. ([Source](https://nousresearch.com/nous-psyche/))
- **Solana coordination layer:** Smart contracts store training metadata, participant lists, and random task assignments, providing fault tolerance and censorship resistance. ([Source](https://cointelegraph.com/news/nous-research-raises-50m-paradigm-decentralized-ai-solana))
- **Successful proof of concept:** Trained a 15B-parameter model in a distributed test run (December 2024), and Hermes 4.3 was the first full production model trained entirely on Psyche.

## Funding and Investors

Nous has raised approximately $65-70M in total funding across multiple rounds. The investor base skews heavily toward crypto-native firms:

- **Seed (~2023):** Led by Distributed Global, with North Island Ventures and Delphi Digital.
- **Pre-Series A (~2024):** ~$15M from Together AI, Distributed Global, North Island Ventures, Delphi Digital, and Raj Gokal (Solana co-founder).
- **Series A (April 2025):** $50M led almost entirely by Paradigm, the prominent crypto venture firm. Valued at $1B on a fully diluted token basis.

The $1B valuation is tied to the NOUS token on Solana, not traditional equity. This is a critical nuance -- it reflects crypto market dynamics as much as a traditional startup valuation methodology.

## Business Model

Nous Research's business model is still evolving and is not fully transparent. Based on available information:

1. **Token economics (NOUS on Solana):** The Psyche network appears designed around a token-incentive model where compute contributors are compensated via NOUS tokens. This is the primary monetization thesis tied to the Paradigm investment.

2. **Open-source model distribution:** Models are released freely on Hugging Face under permissive licenses (Apache 2.0). They are also available through third-party inference platforms like OpenRouter and Ollama. Direct revenue from model distribution appears minimal.

3. **API access:** Nous Portal (portal.nousresearch.com) offers model access, though pricing and scale are not well documented.

4. **Enterprise services (speculative):** No confirmed enterprise contracts are publicly known, but the infrastructure and model quality suggest potential for enterprise fine-tuning and deployment services.

The honest assessment is that Nous Research is currently more of a research organization with token-based funding than a revenue-generating business in the traditional sense. The crypto funding structure provides runway without requiring immediate product-market fit in the conventional SaaS sense.

## What Makes Nous Research Interesting

1. **Extraordinary output-to-resource ratio.** A team of ~20-30 people with ~$65M in funding is producing models (Hermes, Nomos) that compete with outputs from organizations spending $100B+ annually on capex. This is arguably the single most interesting thing about Nous.

2. **Decentralized training as a real product.** Many crypto-AI projects are vaporware. Nous actually trained a production model (Hermes 4.3) on Psyche. If DisTrO-based distributed training scales further, it could meaningfully change who can train frontier models.

3. **Cultural origins.** The team emerged from Discord communities and open-source contributions, not from elite AI labs. A pseudonymous co-founder, a philosophy major, and an automotive software engineer built a billion-dollar-valued AI lab. This is a genuinely different origin story from the Stanford/Google/Meta pipeline that dominates the space.

4. **Philosophical stance on alignment.** The "neutral alignment" position -- where the user controls model behavior rather than the provider -- is a deliberate ideological stance that differentiates Hermes from virtually every other major model family. Whether this is a feature or a liability depends on your perspective, but it has earned Nous a devoted community.

5. **Crypto-AI intersection done seriously.** Nous is one of very few projects that credibly bridges the crypto and AI worlds with actual technical substance rather than just narrative. The Paradigm investment at $1B validates this to some degree, though the token-based valuation structure means the market is pricing the vision, not proven revenue.

## Key Risks and Open Questions

- **Revenue model clarity.** It remains unclear how Nous generates sustainable revenue beyond token economics. If the NOUS token loses value, the economic model for Psyche contributors weakens.
- **Scaling the team.** At ~20-30 people, the team is impressively productive but may face bottlenecks as ambitions grow.
- **Regulatory risk.** The "no content restrictions" positioning of Hermes models may attract regulatory scrutiny, particularly in the EU.
- **Crypto market dependency.** The Paradigm-led funding and Solana-based infrastructure tie Nous's fortunes partly to crypto market conditions.
- **Competition from well-funded open-source efforts.** Meta (Llama), Mistral, and others are investing billions in open-source models. Nous's edge is cultural and architectural, not financial.
