# Safe Superintelligence Inc. (SSI)

## Mission

SSI exists to solve one problem: build a **safe superintelligence**. The company explicitly states it has "one goal and one product." Safety and capability are treated as intertwined technical challenges to be advanced in tandem, not as trade-offs. The business model is designed to insulate research from short-term commercial pressures -- there are no product launches, no API offerings, and no revenue as of early 2026.

## Origin Story

In May 2024, Ilya Sutskever departed OpenAI after a turbulent period that included his involvement in the brief ouster of CEO Sam Altman in November 2023 and growing internal disagreements over the priority given to AI safety. Jan Leike, who co-led OpenAI's Superalignment team with Sutskever, resigned the same day, publicly citing an erosion of safety culture. OpenAI subsequently dissolved the Superalignment team.

One month later, in June 2024, Sutskever announced the formation of Safe Superintelligence Inc. with co-founders Daniel Gross and Daniel Levy. The company set up offices in Palo Alto and Tel Aviv.

## Founders

### Ilya Sutskever -- CEO & Co-founder
- **Born:** 1986, Nizhny Novgorod, Russia. Emigrated to Israel at age 5; moved to Canada at 16.
- **Education:** BSc Mathematics (2005), MSc Computer Science (2007), PhD Computer Science (2013) -- all at the University of Toronto under **Geoffrey Hinton**.
- **Key contributions:** Co-invented AlexNet (2012) with Alex Krizhevsky and Geoffrey Hinton, a breakthrough that catalyzed the modern deep learning era. Major contributor to sequence-to-sequence learning, GPT model series, CLIP, DALL-E, and work related to AlphaGo.
- **Career:** Google Brain researcher; co-founded OpenAI in 2015 and served as Chief Scientist for nearly a decade, overseeing the research trajectory that led to ChatGPT.
- **Why he matters:** Sutskever is widely regarded as one of the most important technical figures in AI. His conviction that superintelligence is imminent -- and that safety must be solved before it arrives -- is the intellectual foundation of SSI.

### Daniel Gross -- Co-founder (departed July 2025)
- **Born:** 1991, Jerusalem, Israel. Raised Orthodox Jewish.
- **Education:** Applied to Y Combinator while attending a mechina (pre-military program) in Israel; was reportedly the youngest founder ever accepted at the time.
- **Career:** Led AI/ML efforts at Apple; partner at Y Combinator; prolific angel investor (early backer of Uber, GitHub, Perplexity.ai); co-managed the NFDG investment fund with Nat Friedman (former GitHub CEO).
- **Departure:** In July 2025, Gross left SSI to join Meta's newly formed Superintelligence Labs. Reports indicate Meta had first attempted to acquire SSI outright (at its $32B valuation) and to recruit Sutskever directly; both efforts were rebuffed before Gross departed independently.

### Daniel Levy -- President & Co-founder
- **Education:** BSc from Ecole Polytechnique (France); PhD in Computer Science from Stanford University.
- **Career:** Internships at Microsoft, Meta, and Google; subsequently a senior researcher at OpenAI where he led the Optimization Team. Assumed the title of President at SSI following Gross's departure.

## Technical Approach

- **"Straight shot" to superintelligence:** SSI is not building incremental products or commercial APIs. The entire organization is oriented toward a single long-horizon research goal.
- **Google TPUs over NVIDIA GPUs:** In April 2025, SSI announced a partnership with Google Cloud to use Tensor Processing Units (TPUs) for its training infrastructure -- a notable divergence from the NVIDIA-centric norm in frontier AI labs.
- **Focus areas (reported, not officially confirmed):** Synthetic reasoning, novel optimization techniques, and safety protocols developed in tandem with capability advances. Full-scale training of a large model (possibly 500B+ parameters) is expected in 2025-2026, though details remain scarce.
- **Deliberately lean team:** ~18 employees as of early 2026. The company has prioritized hiring a small number of elite researchers rather than scaling headcount.

## What Makes SSI Interesting (from a research perspective)

1. **Founder conviction bet:** SSI is arguably the purest expression of the thesis that safety and superintelligence must be co-developed from scratch, outside the commercial pressures of an existing product company. Sutskever walked away from one of the most influential positions in AI to pursue this.

2. **Unprecedented valuation-to-employee ratio:** At ~$32B valuation with ~18 employees and zero revenue, SSI may have the highest per-capita valuation of any private technology company in history. This reflects investor confidence in Sutskever personally and in the long-term value of solving superintelligence.

3. **Anti-product philosophy:** Unlike OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, and xAI -- all of which monetize through products and APIs -- SSI has explicitly refused to ship anything. This is either visionary discipline or an existential risk to the company, depending on one's priors.

4. **Talent magnet and talent vulnerability:** The Gross departure to Meta illustrates both the caliber of people SSI attracts and the competitive pressure from big tech. Meta's failed acquisition attempt suggests SSI is perceived as a genuine threat.

5. **Safety-capabilities unity thesis:** SSI rejects the framing that safety is a constraint on capability. Instead, the company treats them as "technical problems to be solved through revolutionary engineering and scientific breakthroughs" that advance together. This is a testable claim that will be scrutinized heavily once SSI publishes results.

6. **Geopolitical dimension:** Dual Palo Alto/Tel Aviv presence, with Israeli-origin founders (Sutskever, Gross), situates SSI at the intersection of US and Israeli AI ecosystems.

## Open Questions & Uncertainties

- **No public benchmarks or papers** have been released as of March 2026. It is unclear how far SSI's research has progressed.
- **Revenue model** is undefined. The company has stated it will eventually have a product, but no timeline or form has been disclosed.
- **Leadership stability** after Gross's departure is an open question. Sutskever is a researcher by background, not an operator.
- **Compute strategy:** The TPU bet is unconventional. Whether this is a strategic advantage or a constraint remains to be seen.
- **Burn rate:** With $3B raised and a small team, most capital is presumably going to compute. Runway should be substantial, but frontier training runs are expensive and getting more so.
- **Exact employee count** varies across sources (16-20 range); the company does not publicly disclose headcount.
