# Niv-AI

## Overview

Niv-AI emerged from stealth in March 2026 to solve one of the most overlooked bottlenecks in AI infrastructure: GPU power management. Conventional power meters cannot detect millisecond-level power spikes from GPUs, so data center operators maintain large safety buffers -- leaving up to 30% of power capacity completely unused.

Niv-AI is developing new sensors and management tools that measure GPU power at millisecond precision, enabling operators to safely run racks at higher utilization without risk of tripping breakers or damaging hardware.

## Funding Story

The $12M seed was led by Glilot Capital and Grove Ventures, two of Israel's top deep-tech VCs, with participation from Arc VC, Encoded VC, Leap Forward, and Aurora.

## What Makes Them Interesting

1. **30% stranded capacity.** If Niv-AI's claims hold, data centers could unlock nearly a third more compute without building new infrastructure -- a massive cost saving.
2. **Hardware-software integration.** Building custom sensors plus management software is a defensible moat that pure-software competitors cannot easily replicate.
3. **Timing.** With AI compute demand vastly exceeding supply and data center power becoming the binding constraint on AI scaling, any technology that increases GPU density per rack is extremely valuable.
4. **U.S. deployment timeline.** Expects operational systems in U.S. data centers within 6-8 months.
