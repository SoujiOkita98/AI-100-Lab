# Speak -- AI Language Learning

## Overview

Speak is an AI-powered language learning platform that uses speech recognition and conversational AI to help users practice speaking foreign languages. Unlike traditional language apps that rely on multiple-choice and text exercises, Speak is built around voice-first interaction -- users learn by talking out loud to an AI tutor. The company achieved unicorn status in December 2024 after raising a $78M Series C at a $1B valuation, and has reached approximately $100M in annualized revenue, positioning it as the most serious AI-native challenger to Duolingo.

## Founders & Team

| Founder | Role | Background |
|---------|------|------------|
| **Connor Zwick** | Co-Founder & CEO | Dropped out of Harvard to build Flashcards+, a flashcard app that was acquired by Chegg in 2013. Thiel Fellow (second cohort). Met co-founder Andrew Hsu through the Thiel Fellowship. |
| **Andrew Hsu** | Co-Founder & CTO | Child prodigy who enrolled at the University of Washington at age 12, graduating with degrees in neurobiology, biochemistry, and chemistry. Started a PhD in Neuroscience at Stanford at age 16, dropped out at 19 to join the Thiel Fellowship (first cohort). Previously founded Airy Labs (educational games). American-Taiwanese; partly motivated by regret at not learning his parents' language growing up. |

**Origin story:** Zwick and Hsu met through the Thiel Fellowship and spent a full year researching machine learning, initially using YouTube videos as training material to develop an accent-detecting algorithm. They became convinced that voice-based AI could transform language learning, founding Speak in 2016 -- years before the current generative AI wave.

## Business Model

Speak operates a **freemium consumer subscription** model:

- Free tier with limited daily usage
- Paid subscription plans unlock unlimited conversations, advanced AI feedback, and additional languages
- Core differentiation is voice-first: users spend the majority of their time speaking, not tapping

**Revenue trajectory:** The company has reached approximately $100M in annualized revenue, making it one of the largest AI-native consumer subscription businesses outside of ChatGPT, Midjourney, and Suno.

## Market Position & Traction

- Named **Google Play "Best App of 2024"** in Hong Kong, Korea, and Taiwan
- Over **230 million utterances** by Korean learners as of February 2026
- South Korea is the company's strongest market, where Speak has become a mainstream English learning tool
- Celebrity endorsement: launched a brand campaign with Korean actor Kim Woo-bin for 2026
- Supports 9+ languages including Spanish, English, French, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Portuguese, German, and Chinese
- Expanding aggressively into Japan, Latin America, and other Asian markets

## What Makes Speak Interesting

1. **Pre-GPT founding:** Speak was founded in 2016, long before the current AI hype cycle. The founders bet on voice AI for language learning years before LLMs made it feasible at scale, giving them deep domain expertise and proprietary data advantages.

2. **OpenAI's early bet:** Speak was one of the first companies to receive investment from the OpenAI Startup Fund (2022), signaling early conviction from the leading AI lab that voice-based AI tutoring was a high-potential application.

3. **Korea-first, global ambition:** Rather than launching in the US, Speak found product-market fit in South Korea's massive English-learning market. This Asia-first strategy is unusual for a San Francisco startup and has proven highly effective.

4. **Thiel Fellowship pedigree:** Both founders are Thiel Fellows who dropped out of elite institutions (Harvard, Stanford) -- a pattern that resonates with Silicon Valley's founder mythology and investor networks.

5. **Duolingo challenger:** Speak is the most credible AI-native threat to Duolingo (which generated $748M revenue in 2024). While Duolingo has added AI features, Speak was built voice-first from day one.

6. **$100M ARR milestone:** Reaching $100M ARR puts Speak in rare company among consumer AI startups and demonstrates that AI tutoring can be a large standalone business.

## Competitive Landscape

- **Duolingo** -- Dominant incumbent; $748M revenue (2024), 67% of all language learning app revenue. Adding AI features but fundamentally a gamified text-first app.
- **ELSA Speak** -- AI pronunciation coach; smaller scale, focused on English.
- **Babbel / Rosetta Stone** -- Traditional language learning; less AI-native.
- **ChatGPT / Claude** -- General-purpose LLMs used informally for language practice; not purpose-built.

## Key Risks

- Heavy concentration in South Korea; US and European markets remain unproven at scale
- Duolingo could accelerate its AI voice features with its massive user base and data
- General-purpose AI assistants (ChatGPT, Gemini) increasingly capable of conversational language tutoring
- Subscription fatigue in consumer apps; retention is critical for language learning products
