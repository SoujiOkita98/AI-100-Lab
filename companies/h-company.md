# H Company

> Paris-based AI startup building autonomous "action-oriented" agents for enterprise and consumer markets, backed by a record-breaking European seed round.

## Company Overview

H Company is a French AI startup that develops autonomous AI agents designed to take action on behalf of users — going beyond conversational AI to actually execute tasks across web interfaces and enterprise workflows. Founded in 2023 by a team of DeepMind veterans, the company raised $220M in the largest seed round in European AI history and launched its flagship product, Runner H, in November 2024.

The company experienced a notable leadership upheaval in August 2024 when three of its five co-founders (Wierstra, Tuyls, Perolat) departed over operational disagreements. Gautier Cloix serves as President and CEO. Despite the turbulence, the company grew to ~201 employees and reached $22.1M in revenue by 2025.

## Products

### Runner H (launched Nov 2024)

Runner H is an agentic API platform that combines a large language model with a 2-billion parameter vision-language model (Holo-1) to execute tasks autonomously across web interfaces. Key metrics:

- **92.2% success rate** on computer-use benchmarks (state-of-the-art at launch)
- **5.5x lower cost** than competing solutions from OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google
- Public beta launched June 2025

### Holo-1

A purpose-built 2B-parameter visual model designed for understanding and interacting with screen content — optimized for agent computer use rather than general image understanding.

### Tester H (launched Jun 2025)

An autonomous QA testing agent designed for developer workflows, leveraging the same action-oriented architecture as Runner H.

## Business Model

H Company operates a **usage-based API model** for its agent products:

- Enterprise customers pay for API access to Runner H and Tester H agents
- Pricing based on task execution volume
- **2025 revenue:** $22.1M (per GetLatka)

## What Makes H Company Interesting

1. **Largest European AI seed round:** $220M from a star-studded investor list including Eric Schmidt, Bernard Arnault, Amazon, and Samsung signaled that Europe could compete for frontier AI talent and capital.

2. **Action-oriented architecture:** Rather than building a general-purpose chatbot, H Company focused specifically on agents that can take action — clicking, navigating, filling forms — through computer interfaces. This "computer use" approach is now validated by Anthropic and OpenAI.

3. **Cost efficiency:** 5.5x lower cost than competing computer-use agents while maintaining state-of-the-art success rates — a meaningful competitive advantage for enterprise adoption.

4. **Founder drama:** The departure of three co-founders (including DeepMind founding member Daan Wierstra) just months after the seed round is a risk factor but the company appears to have navigated the transition.

5. **French AI ecosystem flagship:** H Company has become a symbol of France's ambition to compete in frontier AI, with backing from French billionaires (Arnault, Niel) and government investor Bpifrance.

## Uncertainties and Open Questions

- **Valuation:** No public valuation figure from the seed round, though $220M at seed implies a likely $1-2B+ post-money valuation.
- **Next funding round:** No announced Series A as of early 2026; whether the company will need additional capital.
- **Co-founder departures:** The loss of three of five co-founders (including two DeepMind founding-era researchers) raises questions about long-term research leadership.
- **Competitive pressure:** Anthropic's computer use API, OpenAI Operator, and Google's Project Mariner are all targeting the same computer-use agent market.
