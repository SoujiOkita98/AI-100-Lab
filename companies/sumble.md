# Sumble

## One-liner

AI-powered sales intelligence platform built by the founders of Kaggle that uses a knowledge graph and large language models to surface contextual, real-time signals about target companies -- helping enterprise sales teams understand what is actually happening inside their prospects.

## Founders & Leadership

### Anthony Goldbloom -- Co-founder & CEO

- Co-founder and former CEO of **Kaggle**, the world's largest data science and machine learning community, which was acquired by Google in 2017.
- Deep expertise in data science, machine learning, and building data-centric platforms.

### Ben Hamner -- Co-founder & CTO

- Co-founder and former CTO of **Kaggle**.
- Previously a software engineer at Google and technical leader in applied ML.
- Brings deep experience building large-scale data and ML systems.

## Founding Story

Founded in 2022 by the Kaggle co-founders, Sumble spent over two years in stealth mode building its knowledge graph and AI infrastructure before emerging publicly in October 2025 with $38.5M in funding. The thesis: existing sales intelligence tools (ZoomInfo, Apollo, etc.) provide static firmographic data (company size, industry, revenue), but sales teams actually need dynamic, contextual intelligence -- what is happening inside a company right now (leadership changes, product launches, regulatory filings, strategic shifts) -- to time their outreach effectively.

## Product & Business Model

### What Sumble Does

Sumble is a **contextual sales intelligence platform** that:

1. **Trawls the web** across social media, job boards, company websites, regulatory filings, news sources, and more to gather real-time signals about companies.
2. **Builds a knowledge graph** underpinned by LLMs that connects disparate data points into coherent narratives about what is happening inside target organizations.
3. **Surfaces actionable insights** for sales teams, such as: a company just hired a new CTO (potential technology re-evaluation), filed a patent in a new area (strategic shift), or posted job listings suggesting expansion into a new market.

### Pricing / Monetization

Enterprise SaaS model focused on large B2B sales teams. Specific pricing undisclosed, but the customer base (Snowflake, Figma, Wiz) suggests premium enterprise contracts.

## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Positioning |
|-----------|------------|
| **ZoomInfo** | Legacy sales intelligence; strong on firmographic data, weaker on real-time context |
| **Apollo.io** | Contact database + sequences; limited contextual intelligence |
| **Clay** | Data enrichment aggregator; complementary rather than directly competitive |
| **6sense / Demandbase** | Intent data and ABM; different signal sources |
| **LinkedIn Sales Navigator** | Relationship intelligence; manual research required |

Sumble differentiates by focusing on **contextual, narrative intelligence** rather than static data fields, leveraging its founders' deep ML expertise to build a knowledge graph that competitors cannot easily replicate.

## What Makes Sumble Interesting

1. **Kaggle founder pedigree.** Anthony Goldbloom and Ben Hamner built the world's most important data science community. Their technical credibility in ML/AI is unmatched in the sales intelligence space.
2. **Knowledge graph approach.** Rather than providing data tables, Sumble builds connected narratives about companies -- a fundamentally different (and harder to replicate) approach to sales intelligence.
3. **Enterprise customer quality.** Signing Snowflake, Figma, Wiz, Vercel, and Elastic within the first year of launch suggests genuine product-market fit with sophisticated buyers.
4. **Stealth-to-scale trajectory.** Two+ years in stealth building core infrastructure before launching suggests a technically deep product with defensible IP.

## Risks & Open Questions

- **Data freshness and accuracy.** Real-time web crawling and knowledge graph maintenance is technically demanding; accuracy at scale is the key challenge.
- **Market education.** The "contextual intelligence" category is new; Sumble needs to convince buyers to add another tool to their stack.
- **Competition from incumbents.** ZoomInfo and others are adding AI features; the question is whether Sumble's knowledge graph approach creates a durable moat.
- **Revenue scale unclear.** 17 enterprise customers is early; the path to $10M+ ARR will require significant sales team buildout.
