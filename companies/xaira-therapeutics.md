# Xaira Therapeutics

## Overview

Xaira Therapeutics is an AI-powered drug discovery company that launched in April 2024 with $1 billion in committed funding -- one of the largest launch rounds in biotech history. The company combines machine learning, large-scale biological data generation, and therapeutic product development to build a platform for designing novel medicines. Xaira's core technology originated from the University of Washington's Institute for Protein Design, where foundational models for protein and antibody design (RFdiffusion and RFantibody) were developed.

## Founders & Team

### Marc Tessier-Lavigne -- CEO
- Former chief scientific officer at Genentech and former president of Stanford University.
- Deep background in neuroscience and drug development.

### David Baker, Ph.D. -- Co-Founder
- Professor of Biochemistry and Director of the Institute for Protein Design at the University of Washington.
- 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry laureate for computational protein design.
- Creator of Rosetta software suite and RFdiffusion/RFantibody models that form the technical backbone of Xaira.

### Hetu Kamisetty, Ph.D. -- Co-Founder
- Previously at Meta (Facebook) and the Institute for Protein Design.
- Expertise in machine learning applied to protein structure prediction.

### Arvind Rajpal, Ph.D. -- Co-Founder
- Former head of large molecule drug discovery at Genentech (Roche).
- Deep experience in therapeutic antibody development.

### Notable Board Members
- Scott Gottlieb, M.D. -- Former FDA Commissioner
- Carolyn Bertozzi, Ph.D. -- Nobel laureate in Chemistry (2022)
- Alex Gorsky -- Former CEO of Johnson & Johnson

## Technology

Xaira's platform centers on generative AI models for protein and antibody engineering:

- **RFdiffusion:** A diffusion-based generative model for de novo protein design, capable of generating novel protein structures from scratch.
- **RFantibody:** An extension of RFdiffusion focused on antibody design, enabling the generation of novel therapeutic antibodies against specified targets.
- **Data generation:** The company operates large-scale wet labs to generate proprietary biological datasets that feed back into model training, creating a data flywheel.

The approach aims to move beyond traditional screening-based drug discovery toward computationally designed molecules with optimized properties from the start.

## Business Model

- **Internal pipeline:** Developing proprietary drug candidates using AI-designed proteins and antibodies.
- **Platform approach:** Building generalizable AI tools for connecting biological targets and engineered molecules to human diseases.
- **Therapeutic focus areas:** Undisclosed at launch, but the team's background suggests oncology, immunology, and infectious disease as likely areas.

## What Makes Xaira Therapeutics Interesting

1. **Scale of launch funding.** $1B at inception is nearly unprecedented in biotech and reflects deep investor conviction in the AI-for-drug-discovery thesis.

2. **Nobel-caliber science.** David Baker's protein design work (Nobel Prize 2024) is the scientific foundation. The company is essentially the commercial vehicle for some of the most important advances in computational biology.

3. **World-class board.** A former FDA commissioner, two Nobel laureates, and a former J&J CEO provide unmatched regulatory, scientific, and commercial expertise.

4. **Generative biology approach.** Rather than using AI to screen existing compound libraries, Xaira generates entirely new molecules computationally -- a fundamentally different and potentially more powerful paradigm.

5. **Investor quality.** ARCH Venture Partners made its largest-ever bet on Xaira. The syndicate reads like a who's who of both biotech and tech venture capital.

## Risks & Open Questions

- **Pre-clinical stage.** Despite $1B in funding, the company has no clinical-stage programs yet. The gap between computational protein design and approved drugs remains large.
- **Translation risk.** AI-designed molecules still need to survive the same clinical trial gauntlet as traditionally discovered drugs. Computational advantages in design do not eliminate manufacturing, toxicology, or efficacy risks.
- **Valuation pressure.** At $4B pre-revenue, the company must deliver clinical milestones to justify the price.
- **Competitive landscape.** Isomorphic Labs (Google/DeepMind), Recursion, and other well-funded AI drug discovery companies are pursuing similar goals.
