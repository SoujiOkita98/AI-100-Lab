# Adaption Labs

## Overview

Adaption Labs is a San Francisco-based AI startup founded by Sara Hooker (former VP of Research at Cohere, previously Google DeepMind) and Sudip Roy (former head of inference computing at Cohere). The company is building AI systems that adapt in real time to tasks without requiring fine-tuning, prompt engineering, or escalating compute costs -- a direct challenge to the industry's "bigger model = better model" assumption.

In February 2026, Adaption raised a $50M seed round led by Emergence Capital Partners at a $1B valuation, making it a born-unicorn. The company is backed by Mozilla Ventures, Fifty Years, Threshold Ventures, Alpha Intelligence Capital, and E14 Fund.

## Funding story

**February 2026 -- Seed ($50M at $1B valuation).** Emergence Capital Partners led. Participation from Mozilla Ventures, Fifty Years, Threshold Ventures, Alpha Intelligence Capital, E14 Fund, and Neo. Born-unicorn status reflects extreme investor conviction in the founding team and the adaptive AI thesis.

## What makes them interesting

1. **Counter-thesis to scaling laws.** While most AI companies chase larger models, Adaption bets that smaller models with adaptive inference can match or exceed larger ones on specific tasks. If correct, this could dramatically improve AI economics for enterprise deployment.

2. **Elite research-to-production team.** Hooker is a leading researcher in model efficiency and was VP of Research at Cohere. Roy ran inference computing at Cohere. Together they understand both the theory and the practical challenges of making AI efficient in production.

3. **Born unicorn.** A $1B valuation on a seed round for a pre-product company is remarkable, reflecting the caliber of the founders and the conviction that adaptive inference is the next major shift in AI infrastructure.
