# Luminance

> UK-based Legal-Grade AI platform used by 1,000+ organizations across 70 countries, including all Big Four and a quarter of the Global Top 100 law firms.

## Company Overview

Luminance is a London-headquartered AI company that builds what it calls "Legal-Grade AI" for contract review, negotiation, and legal workflow automation. Founded in 2015 by Cambridge-based researchers and originally seed-funded by the late Dr. Mike Lynch (founder of Autonomy), Luminance has grown to serve more than 1,000 organizations across 70 countries. Its client base includes all Big Four consultancy firms, over a quarter of the Global Top 100 law firms, and enterprises such as AMD, BBC Studios, Hitachi, Liberty Mutual, and Koch Industries.

## Founders

- **Adam Guthrie** -- Founder and Chief Technical Architect. Cambridge-based academic who developed Luminance's core AI technology.
- **Dr. Graham Sills** -- Founder and Director of AI. Also from Cambridge.
- **Dr. Mike Lynch** (deceased) -- Provided the original seed funding. Lynch was the founder of Autonomy (acquired by HP for $11.1 billion) and one of the UK's most prominent tech entrepreneurs. He died in a yacht accident off the coast of Sicily in August 2024.

## Business Model

Enterprise SaaS for law firms, in-house legal teams, and consultancies. Luminance's platform handles:
- **Contract review and analysis:** AI-powered review of large document sets for due diligence, M&A, and compliance
- **Contract negotiation:** Automated redlining and clause comparison
- **Legal workflow automation:** End-to-end contract lifecycle management

## What Makes Luminance Interesting

1. **Forbes AI 50 recognition:** Selected for the 2025 Forbes AI 50 list as a pioneer in legal AI.
2. **Deep enterprise adoption:** 1,000+ organizations across 70 countries, including all Big Four firms -- a scale of legal AI deployment few competitors match.
3. **UK AI champion:** One of the most prominent AI companies to emerge from the UK ecosystem, with Cambridge academic roots.
4. **Mike Lynch legacy:** The company's origin story is intertwined with one of the UK tech industry's most prominent (and tragic) figures.
5. **U.S. expansion:** Headcount grew 80% in 2024 to 250, with U.S. staff tripling, signaling aggressive North American expansion.
