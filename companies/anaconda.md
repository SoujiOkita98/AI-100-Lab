# Anaconda

## Overview

Anaconda is the world's most popular Python and R distribution platform for data science and AI, with over 50 million users and 21 billion downloads. Founded in 2012 by Travis Oliphant (creator of NumPy) and Peter Wang, the company -- originally named Continuum Analytics -- has become the default environment for Python-based data science and AI development. 95% of Fortune 500 companies rely on Anaconda.

In July 2025, Anaconda raised $150M in Series C funding led by Insight Partners, reaching a $1.5B valuation and unicorn status. The company operates profitably with $150M+ in ARR.

## Funding story

**2012 -- Seed.** Initial funding from UCSD to develop the Anaconda distribution as Continuum Analytics.

**July 2015 -- Series A ($24M).** Led by General Catalyst and BuildGroup. Funded growth of the open-source distribution and early commercial products.

**2018-2021 -- Series B (multiple tranches).** Citi Ventures invested in 2018, with Snowflake joining a 2021 extension. Funded enterprise product development.

**July 2025 -- Series C ($150M at $1.5B valuation).** Led by Insight Partners with Mubadala Capital (Abu Dhabi). Reached unicorn status. Capital earmarked for scaling the Anaconda AI Platform for enterprise use.

## What makes them interesting

1. **Foundational infrastructure for AI.** Anaconda is effectively the package manager and environment manager that the entire Python AI ecosystem runs on. This is deeply embedded infrastructure with enormous switching costs.

2. **Profitable at scale.** $150M+ ARR with profitability is rare among AI infrastructure companies. The freemium model with 50M+ free users creates a massive enterprise upsell funnel.

3. **Founder pedigree.** Travis Oliphant created NumPy, arguably the single most important library in scientific Python. This gives Anaconda unmatched credibility in the ecosystem.
