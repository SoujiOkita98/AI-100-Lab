# Gather AI

## Overview

Gather AI is a Pittsburgh-based physical AI company that uses autonomous drones and AI-powered cameras to monitor warehouse inventory in real time. Founded in 2017 by three Carnegie Mellon University Robotics Institute PhD researchers, the company has built what it calls the world's first Physical Intelligence Platform for intralogistics.

Rather than replacing warehouse workers, Gather AI's drones serve as a co-pilot for logistics teams -- autonomously flying through warehouse aisles to scan barcodes, read text and expiration dates, predict stockouts, and flag safety issues before they become problems.

## Technology

Gather AI deploys two types of sensing:
1. **Autonomous drones**: Off-the-shelf drones that fly through warehouse aisles scanning pallet labels on racks
2. **Camera systems on forklifts**: Off-the-shelf cameras mounted on existing equipment that passively capture inventory data during normal operations

The AI layer interprets visual data (barcodes, text, expiration dates), builds a real-time inventory map, and actively seeks out specific data points -- the company describes its drones as "curious" because they proactively investigate anomalies.

## Business Model

Gather AI operates a SaaS model, charging warehouse operators for ongoing access to its AI-powered inventory monitoring platform. The hardware (drones and cameras) uses off-the-shelf components, keeping capital costs low. Revenue is recurring and tied to the volume and frequency of inventory scans.

Key customers include Kwik Trip, Axon, GEODIS, and NFI Industries.

## What Makes Gather AI Interesting

1. **CMU Robotics Institute pedigree**: The founders built autonomous helicopters at CMU before applying that expertise to warehouse environments -- a more commercially tractable market.

2. **Off-the-shelf hardware strategy**: By using commodity drones and cameras rather than proprietary hardware, Gather AI keeps deployment costs low and focuses its differentiation on the AI software layer.

3. **"Curious" AI**: The system doesn't just passively scan -- it actively seeks out specific inventory data, predicts issues, and flags anomalies, creating more value than simple barcode reading.

4. **Keith Block backing**: The former Salesforce co-CEO leading the Series B brings enterprise sales expertise and credibility to the go-to-market motion.

5. **Proven ROI**: Warehouse inventory management is a pain point with clear, measurable returns -- reduced shrinkage, fewer stockouts, improved safety compliance -- making this an easier enterprise sale than many robotics applications.
