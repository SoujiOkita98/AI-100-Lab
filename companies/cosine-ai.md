# Cosine

## Overview

Cosine is a London-based AI company building autonomous software engineering agents, most notably its flagship product "Genie." Cosine's approach centers on training AI models to reason like human developers -- understanding codebases holistically, planning multi-step solutions, and executing complex engineering tasks autonomously. The company has gained attention for its strong performance on software engineering benchmarks, with its Genie agent consistently ranking at or near the top of SWE-Bench and SWE-Lancer leaderboards.

## Product and Technology

### Genie (Core Product)

- **Autonomous Software Engineer**: Genie can solve bugs, build features, refactor code, and handle complex engineering tasks either fully autonomously or collaboratively with developers
- **Human-Like Reasoning**: Cosine's proprietary training approach focuses on "codifying human reasoning" -- training models on high-quality data that captures how expert developers think through problems, not just the final code output
- **Benchmark Performance**:
  - **SWE-Bench**: Scored 30% when first released (August 2024), representing a 56% improvement over the next best score at the time (19%) and a 2,196% improvement over GPT-4's 1.31%
  - **Genie 2**: The second-generation model achieves 72% on SWE-Lancer, outperforming models from OpenAI and Anthropic

### Technical Approach

Cosine describes itself as a "Human Reasoning Lab." The core technical insight is that training AI on the reasoning process of expert developers (not just code samples) produces agents that can handle novel, complex engineering tasks more reliably. This involves:
- Curating high-quality reasoning traces from expert developers
- Fine-tuning foundation models (initially GPT-4o) on these reasoning traces
- Building agentic scaffolding that plans, executes, and verifies multi-step solutions

## What Makes Cosine Interesting (Research Notes)

1. **Benchmark dominance with minimal funding.** Achieving top-tier SWE-Bench and SWE-Lancer scores with only ~$2.5M in funding is remarkable. This suggests either exceptional engineering talent, a genuinely novel training approach, or both.

2. **The "human reasoning" training methodology** is a differentiated technical approach. Rather than training on code corpora alone, Cosine captures the reasoning process of expert developers -- the "why" behind code decisions. If this approach scales, it could produce more reliable and generalizable coding agents.

3. **Genie 2 outperforming OpenAI and Anthropic on SWE-Lancer** with a fraction of their resources is a strong signal. It suggests that domain-specific training data and agent architecture can compensate for raw model scale.

4. **The company appears underfunded relative to its technical achievements.** $2.5M is tiny for the AI coding agent space (compare: Cognition raised $175M, Factory raised $70M). This suggests a larger funding round is likely imminent.

5. **YC pedigree** provides network, credibility, and access to future funding rounds.

## Key Uncertainties

- Very limited funding relative to competitors -- sustainability and scaling are open questions
- Revenue and customer metrics are not publicly available
- Whether benchmark performance translates to reliable production use
- The team is small (32 people) and the company is still early-stage
- No disclosed enterprise customers or significant revenue
