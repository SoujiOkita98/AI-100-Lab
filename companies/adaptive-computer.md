# Adaptive Computer

## Overview

Adaptive Computer is a San Francisco-based startup founded in 2025 by Dennis Xu (co-founder of Mem, one of OpenAI's earliest investments) and Mike Soylu. The company builds an AI-native no-code platform that allows non-programmers to create full-featured applications by describing what they want in natural language -- part of the emerging "vibe coding" movement.

The company raised a $7M seed round in April 2025 led by Pebblebed (the fund of Keith Adams, former Chief Architect at Slack and Facebook), with participation from Conviction Partners, Jake Paul's Anti Fund, Radical Ventures, and Roblox CEO David Baszucki.

## Funding story

**April 2025 -- Seed ($7M).** Led by Pebblebed. Notable investors include David Baszucki (Roblox founder/CEO), Radical Ventures, Conviction Partners, and Jake Paul's Anti Fund. The platform was in alpha at time of announcement.

## What makes them interesting

1. **Non-programmer founder building for non-programmers.** Dennis Xu is self-described as not a programmer, which gives him authentic empathy for the target user. His previous company Mem was one of OpenAI's first investments, demonstrating AI product instinct.

2. **Interconnected app ecosystem.** Unlike tools that generate isolated apps, Adaptive Computer creates an ecosystem where apps share data and integrate, approaching a new kind of personal computing.

3. **Vibe coding market.** The "vibe coding" space (generating software from natural language) is rapidly growing, with significant VC interest. Adaptive competes for the non-developer segment alongside Lovable, Bolt.new, and others.

4. **Note on branding.** The company launched as "Adaptive Computer" but now operates under the brand "Emergent" at emergent.sh. This should not be confused with the separate company Emergent (founded by Mukund and Madhav Jha) that raised $23M from Lightspeed.
