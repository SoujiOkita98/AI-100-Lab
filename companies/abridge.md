# Abridge

## One-Liner

AI-powered ambient clinical documentation platform that listens to doctor-patient conversations and automatically generates structured medical notes, now expanding into revenue cycle intelligence.

## Founding Story & Team

**Shiv Rao, MD** is a practicing cardiologist at UPMC who earned his bachelor's degree in history from Carnegie Mellon University (2001) and later trained in cardiology. From 2013 to 2019, he led UPMC's provider-facing investment portfolio, investing in health-tech startups, and helped fund a Machine Learning in Health program at Carnegie Mellon. The problem he set out to solve was deeply personal: the nightly ritual of dictating notes for every patient seen that day — what physicians call "pajama time." He founded Abridge in late 2018.

**Sandeep Konam** (Co-Founder, CTO) holds a master's degree in robotics from Carnegie Mellon University (2017). He remains CTO and oversees the technical vision and product development.

**Florian Metze** (Co-Founder, departed) was an Associate Research Professor at CMU's Language Technologies Institute. He left Abridge in 2019 and is now a Research Scientist Manager at Meta.

**Zachary Lipton** serves as Chief Technology Officer (note: some sources list him as CTO alongside Konam; his exact current title may differ — possibly Chief Scientist). He was a machine learning professor at Carnegie Mellon and a former professional jazz saxophonist.

**Julia Chou** (COO) is a former Google engineer who left tech for culinary school and worked at Michelin-starred restaurant Octavia in San Francisco before joining Abridge.

**Brian Wilson** — Chief Commercial Officer. **Sagar Sanghvi** — Chief Financial Officer.

The company's intellectual roots trace to the **Pittsburgh Health Data Alliance**, a collaboration between the University of Pittsburgh, UPMC, and Carnegie Mellon University. The CMU connection runs deep across the founding team.

**Named to TIME's 100 Most Influential People in AI (2024):** Shiv Rao.

## Business Model

- **B2B enterprise SaaS.** Abridge sells directly to healthcare provider organizations (health systems, hospitals, large physician groups), not to individual clinicians.
- **Pricing:** approximately **$2,500 per clinician per year** via enterprise subscription licenses (per Sacra research; exact pricing may vary by contract).
- **Revenue:** estimated **~$100M ARR** as of May 2025, up from ~$60M at end of 2024. Contracted ARR reportedly reached **$117M** in Q1 2025.
- **Expansion into revenue cycle management (RCM):** Abridge is building a "Contextual Reasoning Engine" that integrates clinical documentation with coding (ICD), billing, prior authorization, and compliance workflows — moving beyond pure ambient scribing.

## Product & Technology

Abridge's core product listens to doctor-patient conversations (ambient AI scribe) and automatically generates structured clinical notes within the EHR. Key capabilities:

- **55+ medical specialties** supported
- **28 languages** for multilingual encounters
- **91% of medical note creation** automated (company claim)
- **Patient summaries:** converts clinician notes into patient-friendly versions at multiple reading levels
- **Revenue cycle intelligence:** AI-assisted coding suggestions, prior authorization support, care gap identification surfaced at the point of conversation
- **EHR integration:** deep native integration with Epic, the dominant EHR platform (used by ~38-42% of US hospitals)

The company uses a combination of proprietary models and foundation models. CEO Rao has discussed building specialized AI model pipelines rather than relying on a single large model.

## Customers & Distribution

As of mid-2025, Abridge is deployed across **~150-200 health systems**. Notable customers include:

- **Kaiser Permanente** — rolled out to 24,600 physicians across 40 hospitals and 600+ clinics
- **Mayo Clinic** — enterprise-wide deployment to 2,000+ physicians, plus nursing pilots

### The Epic Partnership (and Its Complications)

Abridge's primary go-to-market advantage was its deep integration with **Epic Systems**, the dominant EHR. Abridge gave Epic an equity stake (reportedly single-digit percentage) and an ongoing revenue share in exchange for preferential integration and distribution access. This secured Abridge a 3-6 month integration depth advantage over competitors.

**However, this relationship has shifted significantly:**
- In October 2025, Epic reportedly ended the partnership program under which it held an equity stake in Abridge and **sold its shares**.
- In February 2026, Epic launched **"AI Charting"** — its own native ambient documentation tool, powered by Microsoft's Dragon Ambient AI and Epic's Cosmos dataset of 300M+ patient records.
- This positions Epic as both a former partner and a direct competitor.

## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Notes |
|-----------|-------|
| **Epic AI Charting** (launched Feb 2026) | Native EHR integration, Microsoft-powered, massive distribution advantage |
| **Microsoft/Nuance DAX Copilot** | Powers Epic's tool; also sold independently; legacy Dragon dictation franchise |
| **Ambience Healthcare** | Well-funded ambient AI scribe competitor |
| **Nabla** | Another AI scribe startup |
| **Freed** | Smaller AI scribe player |

The ambient scribe market is increasingly crowded and the core product risks commoditization. Epic's entry is the most significant competitive threat, as it controls the EHR platform through which most of Abridge's customers access the product.

## What Makes Abridge Interesting

1. **Founder-market fit is exceptional.** Shiv Rao is a practicing cardiologist who personally experiences the documentation burden. The founding team's deep roots in Carnegie Mellon's ML/NLP research give the company genuine technical DNA, not just a wrapper on GPT.

2. **Speed of scaling is remarkable.** From ~$60M ARR (end 2024) to ~$100M+ ARR (mid-2025), deployed across 150+ health systems including marquee names like Kaiser and Mayo. Few health-tech companies achieve enterprise adoption at this pace.

3. **The "platform or perish" strategic bet.** Abridge is racing to become more than an AI scribe before scribing becomes a commodity feature of EHRs. The pivot toward revenue cycle intelligence, coding, prior authorization, and clinical decision support at the point of care represents an ambitious attempt to build a durable moat. CEO Rao frames Abridge as infrastructure linking clinical care with healthcare finance.

4. **The Epic relationship is a fascinating case study in platform risk.** Abridge gave Epic equity and revenue share to secure distribution — then Epic sold the shares, launched a competing product, and became the biggest competitive threat. This is a textbook example of the "your biggest partner becomes your biggest rival" dynamic in enterprise software.

5. **Pittsburgh as an AI hub.** Abridge is one of the most prominent examples of a company emerging from Pittsburgh's CMU-UPMC ecosystem rather than Silicon Valley, though it now has a San Francisco presence as well.

6. **Valuation trajectory carries risk.** At $5.3B on ~$100-117M ARR, Abridge trades at roughly 45-53x revenue — aggressive even by AI startup standards. The company needs to prove it can sustain growth and defend against Epic's native offering to justify this multiple.

## Key Risks

- **Epic platform risk:** Epic's launch of native AI Charting (Feb 2026) could erode Abridge's core value proposition. Health systems may prefer a bundled solution from their EHR vendor.
- **Commoditization of ambient scribing:** Multiple players offer similar core functionality; differentiation is limited at the scribe layer.
- **Valuation pressure:** 45-53x ARR multiple requires sustained hypergrowth and successful expansion beyond scribing.
- **Revenue share commitments** to Epic (and possibly others) may constrain margins, though the exact terms are not publicly disclosed.

## Key Uncertainties

- Exact current ARR and growth rate in 2026 are not publicly confirmed.
- Employee count is estimated; no authoritative public figure found.
- Whether Sandeep Konam or Zachary Lipton currently holds the CTO title (or if titles have been restructured) is unclear from available sources.
- The full financial terms of the (now-ended) Epic partnership are not public.
- Abridge's 2026 strategy post-Epic's competitive entry is still developing.
