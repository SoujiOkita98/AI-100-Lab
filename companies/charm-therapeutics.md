# Charm Therapeutics

## Overview

Charm Therapeutics (stylized as CHARM) is a London-based biotech company using deep learning to discover and develop drugs against hard-to-drug targets. The company's proprietary DragonFold technology applies end-to-end 3D deep learning to predict protein-ligand co-crystal structures, enabling structure-based drug design for targets that have historically resisted computational approaches. Charm's lead program is a menin inhibitor for acute myeloid leukemia (AML), discovered using DragonFold, with clinical entry planned for early 2026.

## Founders & Team

### David Baker, Ph.D. -- Co-Founder
- Director of the Institute for Protein Design at the University of Washington.
- 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry laureate.
- Also co-founder of Xaira Therapeutics; prolific in spawning AI-biology companies from his research.

### Laksh Aithani -- Co-Founder (former CEO)
- Led the company from founding through Series A.

- Background in AI/ML applied to structural biology.

### Gary Glick -- Executive Chairman (current operational leader)
- Experienced biotech executive leading the company through clinical entry.

## Technology: DragonFold

DragonFold is Charm's proprietary deep-learning platform for structure-based drug discovery:

- **Protein-ligand co-folding:** Predicts the 3D co-crystal structure of a protein-ligand complex using only a protein's amino acid sequence and a ligand's chemical structure as inputs.
- **End-to-end approach:** Unlike traditional methods that require experimentally determined protein structures, DragonFold can predict binding configurations from sequence alone.
- **Hard-to-drug targets:** The technology is specifically designed for targets where traditional structural biology methods (X-ray crystallography, cryo-EM) have failed to produce useful structures for drug design.
- **Validated in clinic-ready program:** The menin inhibitor candidate for AML was discovered using DragonFold and has shown robust preclinical activity.

## Business Model

- **Internal pipeline:** Developing proprietary drug candidates discovered using DragonFold, with the AML menin inhibitor as the lead program.
- **Platform partnerships:** Potential to license DragonFold capabilities or enter co-development agreements with pharma companies.
- **Oncology focus:** Current programs focus on cancer, leveraging the ability to drug difficult protein-protein interaction targets common in oncology.

## What Makes Charm Therapeutics Interesting

1. **Clinic-ready AI-designed drug.** The menin inhibitor heading to clinical trials in early 2026 would be among the first drugs designed primarily through deep learning to enter human testing. This is a concrete proof point for the entire AI drug discovery field.

2. **David Baker connection.** Co-founded by a Nobel laureate whose protein design work underlies multiple billion-dollar companies (Xaira, Charm). Baker's involvement provides scientific credibility and recruiting advantages.

3. **Nvidia investment.** Direct backing from Nvidia's venture arm validates the compute-intensive approach and likely provides GPU infrastructure advantages.

4. **DragonFold differentiation.** While AlphaFold predicts single protein structures, DragonFold predicts protein-ligand complexes -- a more directly drug-design-relevant capability.

5. **Capital efficiency.** $150M total funding with a clinical-stage program represents better capital efficiency than many AI drug discovery peers that have raised more without reaching the clinic.

## Risks & Open Questions

- **Leadership transition.** The departure of co-founder/CEO Laksh Aithani in 2025 introduces execution risk during a critical pre-clinical to clinical transition.
- **Single lead program.** The menin inhibitor is the primary disclosed program. Pipeline breadth and diversity are unclear.
- **Clinical translation.** Preclinical activity does not guarantee clinical success. The menin inhibitor faces competition from other menin inhibitors already in clinical trials (e.g., from Syndax, Zeno).
- **UK-based operations.** London headquarters may present challenges for US-centric pharma partnerships and clinical trial infrastructure, though it provides access to European talent.
