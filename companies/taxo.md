# Taxo

## Overview

Taxo is a healthcare AI company building what it describes as the world's fastest and most accurate AI reasoning engine for healthcare administration. The platform automates data extraction and decision-making in healthcare claims processing, automatically assessing claims against coverage rules, policies, and patient benefits. Taxo is currently piloting at Harvard and Stanford teaching hospitals.

Founded by Dr. Ahmed Kerwan and part of Y Combinator's S24 batch, Taxo raised a $5M seed round from Y Combinator, General Catalyst, and Character in March 2025.

## Founders and Team

### Dr. Ahmed Kerwan (CEO)
- Has a medical background, providing deep domain expertise in healthcare administration workflows.
- Affiliated with Harvard's innovation ecosystem.
- Identified the massive inefficiency in healthcare claims processing as an opportunity for AI automation.

## Business Model

### How It Works

1. **AI reasoning engine:** Taxo processes healthcare claims by automatically extracting relevant data from medical documents and assessing claims against coverage rules, policies, and patient benefits.
2. **Autonomous decision support:** Rather than simple data extraction, Taxo applies multi-step reasoning to determine claim validity, coverage applicability, and appropriate processing steps.
3. **Hospital integration:** The platform integrates with existing hospital and payer systems, operating as an autonomous layer on top of current workflows.
4. **Target market:** Initially focused on large academic medical centers (Harvard, Stanford teaching hospitals) with plans to expand to broader healthcare systems and payers.

### Market Context

Healthcare administration accounts for roughly $1 trillion in annual spending in the United States, with claims processing representing a significant portion. Manual claims review is slow, error-prone, and expensive. An AI system that can accurately automate this process represents a massive market opportunity.

## What Makes Taxo Interesting

1. **Founder-domain fit:** Having a physician-founder (Dr. Kerwan) provides credibility and deep understanding of healthcare workflows that purely technical founders would lack.

2. **Elite pilot customers:** Piloting at Harvard and Stanford teaching hospitals provides both validation and a beachhead into the most prestigious healthcare institutions.

3. **Investor quality for seed stage:** Having Y Combinator, General Catalyst, and Character all participate in a $5M seed round signals strong conviction from top-tier investors.

4. **Massive TAM:** Healthcare administration is a trillion-dollar problem in the US alone. Even capturing a small percentage of claims processing automation represents a large business.

5. **AI reasoning differentiation:** Taxo positions itself as a "reasoning engine" rather than a simple data extraction tool, suggesting more sophisticated AI capabilities that could create defensibility.

6. **Risks and open questions:**
   - **Healthcare sales cycles:** Selling to hospitals is notoriously slow; pilot-to-production conversion can take years.
   - **Regulatory and compliance:** Healthcare AI faces significant regulatory scrutiny (HIPAA, FDA, state regulations).
   - **Accuracy requirements:** Healthcare claims processing errors can have serious financial and patient-care consequences; the bar for accuracy is extremely high.
   - **Competition:** Companies like Olive AI, Waystar, and others are also targeting healthcare administration automation.
