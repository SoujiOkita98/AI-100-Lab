# Callosum

## Overview

Callosum is a London-based startup building software that orchestrates AI workloads across heterogeneous chip architectures -- NVIDIA, AMD, AWS Trainium/Inferentia, Cerebras, SambaNova, and others. Named after the corpus callosum (the brain structure connecting its two hemispheres), the company challenges the prevailing AI infrastructure model of running workloads on banks of identical NVIDIA GPUs. Their thesis: just as the brain achieves intelligence through diverse, specialized neural circuits working together, AI systems can be more efficient by intelligently distributing tasks across different chip types.

## Founding Story and Team Origins

Callosum was co-founded by Danyal Akarca and Jascha Achterberg, who met during their PhD studies at the University of Cambridge around 2019. Both are Cambridge-trained neuroscientists whose academic research sits at the intersection of neuroscience and computing. Their published research in Nature journals explored how the brain's heterogeneous architecture achieves computational efficiency -- the direct intellectual foundation for Callosum's technology.

Beyond academia, the founders have industry experience at Intel and have collaborated with Google DeepMind.

## Business Model

- **Multi-chip orchestration software:** Automatically routes AI inference and training tasks across the optimal mix of different chip architectures
- **Enterprise-focused:** Targets organizations running complex, multi-step AI workflows
- **Cost and performance optimization:** Claims ~2x accuracy improvement, 7x speed improvement, and 4x cost reduction compared to single-hardware (mono-GPU) approaches
- **Chip-agnostic:** Supports NVIDIA, AMD, AWS custom silicon, Cerebras, SambaNova, and others

The company addresses the growing "inference optimization" market, which Deloitte estimates will account for two-thirds of all AI compute by 2026.
