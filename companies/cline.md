# Cline

## Overview

Cline is a San Francisco-based open-source AI coding agent founded in 2024 by Saoud Rizwan. What started as a side project for Anthropic's "Build with Claude" hackathon in June 2024 has grown into one of the most widely adopted AI coding tools, with 2.7M+ developer installs and Fortune 500 customers including Samsung and SAP.

Cline raised $32M in combined seed and Series A funding, with the Series A led by Emergence Capital in July 2025. The company differentiates through radical transparency -- developers see exactly what the AI does, control their own API spend, and can use any model provider.

## Funding story

**November 2024 -- Seed ($5M).** Pace Capital invested. Rizwan moved from Indiana to San Francisco to build the company full-time after the open-source project gained significant organic traction.

**July 2025 -- Series A ($27M).** Emergence Capital led, with Pace Capital, 1984 Ventures, Essence VC, and Cox Exponential. Notable angels include YC partner Jared Friedman, Logan Kilpatrick, and Addy Osmani. Coincided with launch of Cline Teams enterprise product.

## What makes them interesting

1. **Hackathon reject to 2.7M+ installs.** Rizwan lost the Anthropic hackathon but the project resonated with developers. Organic, bottom-up adoption at this scale is the strongest possible product-market-fit signal.

2. **Transparency as a moat.** While competitors lock developers into proprietary systems, Cline lets developers choose any model, see every action, and control spend. This builds trust with enterprise security teams and individual developers alike.

3. **Open-source enterprise playbook.** Cline follows the proven GitLab/Elastic model: free open-source core drives massive adoption, then Cline Teams converts enterprises. With 2.7M+ installs, the top of the funnel is enormous.
