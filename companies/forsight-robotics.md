# ForSight Robotics

## Overview

ForSight Robotics is an Israeli surgical robotics company developing the world's first robotic surgery platform specifically designed for cataract and ophthalmic (eye) surgery. The company's ORYOM platform ("daylight" in Hebrew) combines AI-based algorithms, advanced computer vision, and precision micromechanics to enable robotic eye surgery -- a field where sub-millimeter accuracy is critical and the global demand for procedures is exploding.

ForSight was co-founded by Prof. Moshe Shoham, the legendary Israeli roboticist who previously co-founded Mazor Robotics (spine surgery robots, acquired by Medtronic for $1.64 billion in 2018).

## Technology

The ORYOM platform integrates:
- **AI-based surgical planning algorithms** for pre-operative and intra-operative guidance
- **Advanced computer vision** for real-time tracking of surgical instruments and eye anatomy
- **Precision micromechanics** enabling the sub-millimeter movements required for ophthalmic surgery

The system is designed to address two critical problems: a global shortage of trained ophthalmic surgeons and exploding demand for cataract surgery as populations age worldwide. Cataract surgery is the most commonly performed surgery globally, with over 20 million procedures annually.

## Business Model

ForSight targets the medical device market with a capital equipment + consumables model typical of surgical robotics:
1. **System sales/leases**: Hospitals and surgical centers purchase or lease the ORYOM platform
2. **Per-procedure consumables**: Recurring revenue from procedure-specific disposable components
3. **Software subscriptions**: AI-powered surgical planning and analytics

The company achieved ISO 13485:2016 certification and is preparing for clinical trials, with regulatory approval being the key milestone for commercial launch.

## What Makes ForSight Robotics Interesting

1. **Moshe Shoham's track record**: The co-founder previously built Mazor Robotics into a $1.64B exit to Medtronic. Having a proven surgical robotics entrepreneur at the helm dramatically reduces execution risk.

2. **Fred Moll endorsement**: The founder of Intuitive Surgical (da Vinci, $180B+ market cap) investing personally is an extraordinary signal of product credibility in surgical robotics.

3. **Massive unmet need**: Cataract surgery is the world's most common surgical procedure, yet demand far outstrips the supply of trained surgeons, particularly in developing countries. Robotic assistance could democratize access.

4. **Precision requirements as moat**: Ophthalmic surgery requires sub-millimeter precision that is extremely difficult to replicate, creating a high technical barrier to entry.

5. **Israel's medical robotics cluster**: ForSight benefits from Israel's deep bench of medical robotics talent, with institutions like the Technion and companies like Mazor providing a rich ecosystem.
