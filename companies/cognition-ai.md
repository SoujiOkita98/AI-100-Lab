# Cognition AI

> Makers of **Devin**, the world's first autonomous AI software engineer, and acquirers of **Windsurf**, the AI-powered IDE.

## Company Overview

Cognition AI builds autonomous AI agents for software engineering. Their flagship product, Devin, was unveiled in March 2024 and went viral as the first AI system capable of autonomously planning, writing, debugging, and deploying code end-to-end. The company has grown explosively — from $1M ARR in September 2024 to $155M combined ARR by July 2025 (post-Windsurf acquisition) — and reached a $10.2B valuation within roughly two years of founding.

## Founders and Team

All three co-founders are elite competitive programmers who won gold medals at the International Olympiad in Informatics (IOI). This shared background in competitive programming is central to the company's culture and technical approach.

### Scott Wu — CEO

- **Born:** 1997, Louisiana, to Chinese immigrant parents
- **Education:** Harvard University (attended ~2 years, dropped out)
- **Competitive programming:** Three-time IOI gold medalist; ranked #1 globally at IOI 2014; 2011 MATHCOUNTS national champion; Codeforces legend-rated
- **Prior experience:** Co-founder and CTO of Lunchclub (AI networking platform, backed by Lightspeed and Coatue, 2017-2022)
- **Family:** Elder brother Neal Wu is also a three-time IOI gold medalist and competitive programming legend (Harvard '14, parents are chemical engineers originally from Shanghai). Neal works at Cognition as an engineer.
- Sources: [Scott Wu — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scott_Wu), [Contrary Research](https://research.contrary.com/company/cognition)

### Steven Hao — CTO

- **Education:** MIT, BS in Computer Science and Mathematics (2014-2018)
- **Competitive programming:** IOI 2014 gold medalist (6th globally)
- **Prior experience:** Early engineer at Scale AI (sensor fusion for autonomous vehicles); internships at D.E. Shaw, Dropbox
- **Ethnicity note:** Likely Chinese American based on name and background, but not explicitly confirmed in sources
- Sources: [Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/person/steven-hao-250f), [Contrary Research](https://research.contrary.com/company/cognition)

### Walden Yan — CPO (Chief Product Officer)

- **Born:** ~2001-2002 (reported as 23 years old in 2025 articles); Chinese descent
- **Education:** Harvard University, BS in Computer Science and Economics; MIT PRIMES researcher (cryptography and ML)
- **Competitive programming:** IOI 2020 gold medalist (19th globally, after 1,000+ hours of training)
- **Prior experience:** Engineer at Anysphere (makers of Cursor); co-founder of DeepReason (web3 security); managing partner at Inverted (media consulting, later acquired)

## Windsurf Acquisition (July 2025)

In one of the most dramatic episodes in AI startup history, Cognition acquired Windsurf (formerly Codeium) in July 2025 for approximately $250M. The deal came together in under 72 hours after:

1. **OpenAI's $3B acquisition offer for Windsurf expired**
2. **Google executed a $2.4B reverse acqui-hire**, taking Windsurf's CEO Varun Mohan, co-founder Douglas Chen, and research leaders
3. **Cognition swooped in** for the remaining entity: IP, product, trademark, brand, ~210 employees, $82M ARR, 350+ enterprise customers

All Windsurf employees received accelerated vesting and financial participation in the deal. Post-acquisition, Cognition's combined enterprise ARR jumped to $155M, and headcount roughly tripled.

Sources: [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/07/14/cognition-maker-of-the-ai-coding-agent-devin-acquires-windsurf/), [Cognition blog](https://cognition.ai/blog/windsurf), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/09/08/cognition-valued-at-10point2-billion-two-months-after-windsurf-.html)

## Business Model and Pricing

Cognition operates a **usage-based + subscription hybrid model**:

| Plan | Price | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| Core (individual) | $20/month minimum | Pay-as-you-go at $2.25/ACU |
| Teams | $500/month | 250 ACUs included ($2.00/ACU), additional ACUs at $2/each |
| Enterprise | Custom pricing | Enhanced security, SSO, custom deployments |

**ACU (Agent Compute Unit)** = a normalized measure of compute (VM time, model inference, networking). Approximately 1 ACU = 15 minutes of active Devin work.

The pricing was dramatically lowered with Devin 2.0 (April 2025) — the original launch price was $500/month minimum, repositioned to $20/month to broaden adoption.

Revenue split is now between Devin (autonomous agent) and Windsurf (AI-powered IDE), serving both individual developers and enterprise teams.

Source: [VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/programming-development/devin-2-0-is-here-cognition-slashes-price-of-ai-software-engineer-to-20-per-month-from-500/), [Devin Pricing](https://devin.ai/pricing/)

## What Makes Cognition AI Interesting

1. **Fastest-growing AI company by ARR trajectory:** $1M to $73M ARR in 9 months organically, before the Windsurf acquisition pushed combined ARR to $155M. Sub-$20M total net burn is remarkable capital efficiency.

2. **Prodigy-driven founding team:** Three IOI gold medalists, all in their 20s, with deep competitive programming roots. The Wu brothers' family story — children of Shanghai-born chemical engineers who became world-class programmers in Louisiana — exemplifies the Chinese immigrant STEM talent pipeline.

3. **Category creation:** Devin was the first product to credibly demonstrate autonomous end-to-end software engineering by an AI agent, catalyzing the entire "AI coding agent" category and triggering competitive responses from every major AI lab.

4. **Windsurf acquisition was a masterclass in opportunism:** Cognition turned the chaotic collapse of Windsurf's OpenAI deal and Google acqui-hire into a transformative acquisition — gaining 350+ enterprise accounts, $82M ARR, and 210 engineers for ~$250M in under 72 hours.

5. **Two-product strategy post-acquisition:** Devin (autonomous agent that works independently) + Windsurf (AI-assisted IDE where humans remain in the loop) gives Cognition coverage across the full spectrum of AI-assisted development, from pair programming to fully autonomous task execution.

6. **Founders Fund as a consistent champion:** Peter Thiel's fund led the Series A, Series A extension, and Series C — an unusually deep conviction bet from one of Silicon Valley's most selective firms.

7. **Competitive moat uncertainty:** The AI coding space is intensely competitive (GitHub Copilot, Cursor/Anysphere, Amazon Q, Google Jules, Claude Code). Whether Devin's autonomous approach or the IDE-integrated approach wins long-term remains an open question. Cognition's bet is that both matter, hence the Windsurf acquisition.
