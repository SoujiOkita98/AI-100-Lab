# Dataminr

## Overview

Dataminr is a New York-based AI company that provides real-time detection of emerging events, threats, and critical information by analyzing publicly available data at massive scale. The platform ingests signals from over 100,000 public data sources — including social media, news feeds, sensors, the deep/dark web, and other digital channels — and uses proprietary AI/ML models to surface alerts faster than traditional intelligence methods. The company serves corporate security teams, government agencies, defense organizations, newsrooms, and NGOs in over 100 countries.

## Founders & Origins

**Ted Bailey (Founder, Chairman & CEO):**
Bailey attended Choate Rosemary Hall (1996-2000) and Yale University (graduated ~2004), where he designed his own major studying the societal impact of emerging information technologies (radio, telegraph, etc.). He has cited the September 11, 2001 attacks as a formative experience that exposed critical gaps in real-time information dissemination — a problem he set out to solve. In 2009, he founded Dataminr with two fellow Yale graduates. Bailey has been recognized as a World Economic Forum Technology Pioneer, Business Insider 40 Under 40, and Crain's New York 40 Under 40.

**Jeff Kinsey (Co-Founder & CTO):**
A Yale graduate who co-founded Dataminr with Bailey in 2009. Kinsey designed and built the company's original engineering and AI infrastructure and continues to lead technical innovation, including the firm's recent work in predictive AI, generative AI, and agentic AI for real-time event detection.

**Sam Hendel (Co-Founder):**
Yale B.A. (2003). Before Dataminr, Hendel worked as an Associate Director at UBS (2003-2006) in portfolio and proprietary trading, then as Co-Portfolio Manager for risk arbitrage and event-driven strategies at Satellite Asset Management (2006-2009). He is no longer in a day-to-day operating role at Dataminr. He went on to become President and Portfolio Manager at Easterly Investment Partners (Levin Easterly) and later co-led a $1.1B acquisition of a 62,000-title music copyright catalog (KMR Music Royalties II) in partnership with KKR, founding Chord Music Partners and later Jukebox, a music royalty investment platform.

**Origin thesis:** All three founders were Yale graduates who saw an opportunity to apply real-time computational analysis of public information streams — initially Twitter's firehose — to detect breaking events faster than any human analyst could. The company was founded in the early Twitter era and was one of the first to build a large-scale commercial product on top of social media data streams.

## Business Model

Dataminr operates a **SaaS subscription model** with annual or multi-year enterprise contracts, generating recurring revenue. Key product lines:

- **Dataminr Pulse** — For corporate security, risk, and crisis management teams. Provides real-time alerts on physical threats, supply chain disruptions, geopolitical events, and reputational risks.
- **Dataminr Pulse for Cyber Risk** — Focused on cybersecurity threat detection from public data signals (dark web, paste sites, social channels).
- **First Alert** — Designed for the public sector, defense, and NGO markets. Delivers real-time event alerts to government agencies and humanitarian organizations.
- **News product** — Serves ~1,500 newsrooms globally with breaking-event detection.

Revenue is driven by licensing fees, with premium tiers for customization, API access, and integration into client workflows. The company has been investing heavily in generative AI and agentic AI capabilities since 2024-2025.

### ThreatConnect Acquisition (October 2025)

Dataminr announced its intent to acquire **ThreatConnect** for **$290M** in October 2025. ThreatConnect operates a threat intelligence management and security operations platform used by 250+ enterprise and government clients. The combined entity aims to create "Client-Tailored Intelligence" — agentic AI that reasons across both public (Dataminr) and internal (ThreatConnect) data domains. This is a significant expansion into the cyber threat intelligence orchestration market.

## What Makes Dataminr Interesting

1. **First-mover in real-time public-data AI:** Dataminr was one of the earliest companies to build large-scale ML on social media firehoses (starting with Twitter in 2009). This head start created deep institutional relationships and proprietary training data advantages that are difficult to replicate.

2. **Defense & intelligence community entrenchment:** The $280M+ DoD contracts and NATO partnership give Dataminr a deep moat in government/defense — a market with long procurement cycles and high switching costs. Few commercial AI startups have this level of classified and unclassified government access.

3. **Expanding from detection to orchestration:** The ThreatConnect acquisition signals a strategic shift from being an "alert engine" to becoming a full-stack threat intelligence platform with agentic AI. If successful, this significantly expands TAM from real-time alerting (~niche) into the broader security operations and threat intelligence market.

4. **Agentic AI pivot:** Dataminr is investing aggressively in agentic AI — autonomous agents that can reason across data domains, triage threats, and recommend actions. This positions the company at the frontier of applied AI in security, beyond simple classification and alerting.

5. **Scale of public data ingestion:** Processing 100,000+ data sources in real-time with sub-minute latency is a genuine engineering moat. The infrastructure required for this kind of real-time multimodal AI at scale is expensive and complex to build.

6. **Pre-IPO positioning:** With ~$200M+ ARR, a $4.1B valuation, a new CFO from CrowdStrike, and "pre-IPO convertible" language in recent financing, Dataminr appears to be in active IPO preparation — making it one of the more notable upcoming tech IPO candidates.

## Risks & Open Questions

- **Twitter/X data access:** Dataminr's original business was built on privileged access to Twitter's firehose. Under Elon Musk's ownership, X has significantly restricted API access and raised prices. Dataminr has diversified to 100K+ sources, but the degree of remaining dependency on X data is unclear.
- **Profitability:** Revenue growth from $168M to ~$222M over three years is solid but not hypergrowth. Profitability status is not publicly disclosed. [Uncertain]
- **IPO timing:** The company has missed its own stated IPO timelines before (2023 target). Market conditions and the competitive landscape will determine if a 2026-2027 IPO materializes.
- **Competition:** Flashpoint, Recorded Future (acquired by Mastercard for $2.65B in 2024), Babel Street, and others compete in adjacent threat intelligence markets.
