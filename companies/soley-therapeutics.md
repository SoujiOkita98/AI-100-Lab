# Soley Therapeutics

## Overview

Soley Therapeutics is a science-first, tech-enabled drug discovery company that uses cells as biological sensors to uncover first-in-class medicines. Founded by UCSF scientists Dr. Yerem Yeghiazarians (cardiologist, stem cell pioneer) and Dr. Kurosh Ameri (cancer biologist who defined tumor anoxia), the company translates cellular stress biology into mechanistic drug discovery insights.

The company deploys full-stack AI infrastructure via a collaboration with Oracle, using OCI AI Infrastructure, NVIDIA AI Enterprise, and NVIDIA Blackwell GPUs to power its computational pipeline.

## Funding Story

The $200M Series C announced in January 2026 is earmarked for pushing two oncology programs into the clinic: a lead AML (acute myeloid leukemia) asset targeting IND filing in 2026, and a second solid tumor asset entering IND-enabling studies.

## What Makes Them Interesting

1. **Novel biology.** The "cell stress sensing" platform is a differentiated approach -- using cells as sensors rather than purely computational drug design.
2. **Near-clinical assets.** Two programs approaching IND filing gives concrete de-risking milestones for investors.
3. **Full-stack AI deployment.** Oracle/Nvidia infrastructure partnership shows serious computational investment alongside the biology.
4. **Founder depth.** A cardiologist and a cancer biologist who spent a decade at UCSF researching stress biology bring unusual scientific depth.
