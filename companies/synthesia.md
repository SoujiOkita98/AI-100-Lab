# Synthesia

## Overview

Synthesia is a London-based AI video generation platform that converts text scripts into professional videos featuring photorealistic AI avatars. The platform eliminates the need for cameras, actors, or studios, enabling enterprises to create training, communications, and marketing videos at scale. It supports 240+ avatars speaking in 160+ languages with automatic lip-sync.

Founded in 2017 by two Danish entrepreneurs (Riparbelli and Tjerrild) and two prominent computer vision academics (Niessner at TU Munich and Agapito at UCL), the company sits at the intersection of deep academic research and enterprise SaaS execution.

## Business Model

SaaS subscription platform with tiered pricing:

- **Starter:** From ~$30/month (self-serve, limited features)
- **Creator:** Mid-tier plan for teams
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing with SSO, API access, custom avatars, brand kits, and dedicated support

Revenue is heavily weighted toward enterprise (70%+). Key use cases: corporate training, L&D, internal communications, onboarding, sales enablement, and localization of video content across languages.

## Product & Technology

- **AI Avatars:** 240+ stock avatars; custom avatar creation from a short video recording of a real person (with consent verification)
- **Express-2 Engine:** Diffusion transformer model for full-body avatars with natural gestures (hand movements, pointing, waving), paired with state-of-the-art voice cloning
- **Multilingual:** 160+ languages with frame-accurate lip sync for translations
- **Document-to-Video:** Converts PDFs, slide decks, and web pages into branded videos
- **Synthesia 3.0 (2025-2026):** Major platform update introducing:
  - **Video Agents:** Interactive avatars that pause, ask questions, and respond based on user input -- aimed at enterprise training
  - **Copilot:** AI assistant that writes scripts, connects to knowledge bases, and suggests visuals (rolling out 2026)
- **Security:** SOC 2 Type 2 and GDPR compliant; dedicated Trust & Safety team

## Customers

Enterprise-heavy customer base including: Bosch, Merck, SAP, Xerox, Zoom, Reuters, and many Fortune 100 companies. Primary buyers are L&D teams, HR, and corporate communications departments.

## What Makes Synthesia Interesting

1. **Academic-to-Enterprise Pipeline:** Rare combination of world-class computer vision research (TU Munich + UCL) translated into a category-defining enterprise product. The founding team bridges deep science and business execution.

2. **European AI Champion:** One of the highest-valued AI startups in Europe ($4B). Headquartered in London, demonstrating that frontier AI companies can be built outside Silicon Valley.

3. **Enterprise Gravity:** 90% Fortune 100 penetration is extraordinary for a company founded in 2017. The shift from "cool demo" to mission-critical enterprise workflow (training, compliance, onboarding) creates deep switching costs.

4. **Revenue Velocity:** Growing from $88M to $150M+ ARR in roughly one year, with a path to $200M+ in 2026. This is strong, capital-efficient growth for a European AI startup.

5. **Interactive AI Agents (Video Agents):** The move from passive video generation to interactive avatar-driven experiences (where avatars ask questions and adapt) could redefine enterprise training and open a new product category.

6. **Deepfake Tension:** Synthesia sits at the center of the deepfake debate. Its avatars are realistic enough to fool banks and family members (per WSJ testing). The company has invested heavily in its "Three Cs" ethical framework (Consent, Collaboration, Control) and passed NIST content moderation testing, but the dual-use risk remains a strategic and reputational challenge.

7. **Adobe Failed Acquisition:** The reported ~$3B acquisition attempt by Adobe in 2025 -- and Synthesia's refusal -- signals the founders' conviction in building an independent, larger outcome.

## Key Risks

- **Commoditization:** Open-source video generation models are improving rapidly; avatar generation may become a feature rather than a product.
- **Deepfake Regulation:** Tighter government regulation of synthetic media could impose compliance costs or restrict use cases.
- **Platform Risk:** If Adobe, Google, or Microsoft embed similar capabilities natively into their productivity suites, Synthesia's distribution advantage narrows.
- **Competition from HeyGen** and others at lower price points for SMB/prosumer segments.
