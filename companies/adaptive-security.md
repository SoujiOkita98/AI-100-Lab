# Adaptive Security

## Overview

Adaptive Security is a New York-based cybersecurity startup founded in 2024 by Brian Long and Andrew Jones, the duo who previously built Attentive to $500M in annual revenue. The company builds AI-powered tools to defend enterprises against the rising threat of AI-generated social engineering attacks, including deepfake voice calls (vishing), AI-generated SMS phishing (smishing), and deepfake video.

Adaptive raised $146.5M across four rounds in roughly one year, backed by OpenAI (their first cybersecurity investment), a16z, Bain Capital Ventures, and NVIDIA. The company grew to 500+ enterprise customers within 12 months of its January 2025 public launch.

## Funding story

**Early 2024 -- Seed (~$12M).** Abstract Ventures led initial funding to develop the AI social engineering defense platform.

**April 2025 -- Series A ($43M).** Co-led by a16z and OpenAI Startup Fund. This was OpenAI's first and only cybersecurity investment, signaling that the creators of the AI threat also see the urgency of AI defense.

**September 2025 -- Series A Extension ($12M).** OpenAI doubled down, bringing total Series A to $55M.

**December 2025 -- Series B ($81M).** Bain Capital Ventures led. NVentures (NVIDIA), OpenAI, a16z, Capital One Ventures, and Citi Ventures all participated. Three financing rounds in one year is exceptional velocity.

## What makes them interesting

1. **OpenAI's only cybersecurity bet.** The company that created the deepfake threat is investing in defending against it. This is both a strong signal and a unique strategic advantage.

2. **Proven founders.** Long and Jones built Attentive to $500M ARR. They know how to scale enterprise software and are applying that playbook to AI security.

3. **Market timing.** AI-powered social engineering is an exploding threat category. Legacy security training tools were not designed for AI-generated attacks, creating a greenfield opportunity.
