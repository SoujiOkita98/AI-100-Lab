# Day AI

## One-liner

AI-native CRM platform built by HubSpot veterans that automatically captures, enriches, and organizes customer relationship data -- positioning itself as "the Cursor of CRM" by using AI to eliminate manual data entry and surface actionable relationship intelligence.

## Founders & Leadership

### Christopher O'Donnell -- Co-founder & CEO

- Former **VP of Product at HubSpot**, where he helped scale the company through its growth phase and IPO era.
- Deep domain expertise in CRM, marketing automation, and product-led growth.

### Michael Pici -- Co-founder

- Former **HubSpot** executive with extensive experience in sales and go-to-market operations.
- Brings hands-on understanding of the CRM pain points that Day AI aims to solve.

## Founding Story

O'Donnell and Pici spent years at HubSpot watching sales reps struggle with CRM data entry -- the bane of every salesperson's existence. Despite billions invested in CRM technology (Salesforce, HubSpot, etc.), the fundamental problem remained: CRMs are only as good as the data entered into them, and salespeople hate entering data. Day AI was founded in 2023 with the thesis that AI can finally solve this problem by automatically understanding and organizing customer relationships from emails, calls, meetings, and other signals -- no manual input required. Sequoia backed them from day one.

## Product & Business Model

### What Day AI Does

Day AI is an **AI-native CRM** that:

1. **Automatically captures relationship data** from emails, calendar events, calls, and meetings -- eliminating manual CRM data entry.
2. **Enriches contacts and accounts** using AI to pull in contextual information from public sources.
3. **Surfaces relationship intelligence** -- who knows whom, which deals are progressing, which relationships are going cold -- without requiring reps to log anything.
4. **Provides an AI assistant** that can answer questions about relationships, draft follow-ups, and recommend next actions.

### Pricing / Monetization

SaaS subscription model. Specific pricing undisclosed but positioned as a modern alternative to legacy CRM platforms (Salesforce, HubSpot) for growth-stage and mid-market companies.

## What Makes Day AI Interesting

1. **"Cursor of CRM" positioning.** Just as Cursor reimagined the code editor with AI-first design, Day AI aims to reimagine CRM from the ground up -- not bolting AI onto a legacy architecture but building natively around it.
2. **Sequoia conviction.** Sequoia led both the seed and Series A, with partner Pat Grady (who also led Clay's rounds) joining the board. This signals deep conviction in the AI CRM thesis.
3. **HubSpot founder expertise.** O'Donnell and Pici lived the CRM problem at scale; they know exactly where the existing tools fail and what users actually want.
4. **Zero-input CRM thesis.** If Day AI can truly eliminate manual data entry while maintaining data quality, it solves the single biggest pain point in CRM adoption.

## Risks & Open Questions

- **Early stage.** Revenue and customer traction are not yet publicly disclosed; the product is still in its growth phase.
- **Crowded AI CRM market.** Multiple well-funded competitors (Attio, Reevo, Clarify) are attacking the same problem.
- **Salesforce and HubSpot response.** Both incumbents are aggressively adding AI features; the window for disruption may be narrower than expected.
- **Data privacy concerns.** Automatically capturing data from emails and calls raises privacy and compliance questions, especially in regulated industries.
