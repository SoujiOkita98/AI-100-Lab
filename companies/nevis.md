# Nevis -- Company Profile

## Overview

Nevis is an AI platform purpose-built for Registered Investment Advisors (RIAs) that automates the administrative workflows consuming up to 80% of a financial advisor's time. Founded by three former Revolut executives, the company emerged from stealth in December 2025 with $40 million in total funding from Sequoia Capital, ICONIQ, and Ribbit Capital. Nevis already serves some of the fastest-growing US wealth management firms, supporting RIAs that collectively manage more than $50 billion in client assets.

## Founders & Key People

### Mark Swan -- Co-Founder & CEO

- **Background:** Grew up in Aberdeen, Scotland. His interest in sustainable finance began early -- he spent a year as an intern with Al Gore's Generation Investment Management before university.
- **Prior career at Revolut:** Held leadership roles at Revolut (specific role not publicly detailed at the same level as co-founders).
- **Role at Nevis:** CEO, overall strategy and fundraising.

### Philipp Burda -- Co-Founder & CPO

- **Background:** Entrepreneurial from age 15, when he started a short-lived web design agency. Spent a decade in academia before entering fintech.
- **Prior career at Revolut:** Rose through the ranks as Head of Fincrime, then Head of Data Science for the retail product, and finally Partner and Head of GenAI product initiatives.
- **Role at Nevis:** Chief Product Officer, product vision and AI strategy.

### Ivan Chalov -- Co-Founder & COO

- **Background:** Worked as a trader at an investment bank before joining fintech.
- **Prior career at Revolut:** Ascended from Data Scientist to General Manager of Retail Business.
- **Role at Nevis:** Chief Operating Officer, operations and business development.

### Origin Story

Swan, Burda, and Chalov met at Revolut, where they each held leadership positions spanning product, data science, GenAI, and business management. Their combined experience building AI-powered financial products at scale, paired with the observation that financial advisors spend most of their time on administrative tasks rather than advising clients, motivated them to found Nevis. The company operated in stealth for most of 2024-2025 before publicly launching in December 2025.

## Product & Technology

### Core Platform

Nevis builds AI tools that automate the end-to-end administrative workflow of wealth advisors:

- **Client Meeting Preparation:** AI-driven synthesis of client portfolio data, market updates, and relevant financial events.
- **Documentation and Note-Taking:** Automated transcription, summarization, and CRM updates.
- **Compliance Workflows:** Streamlined generation of compliance-ready documentation.
- **Client Communication:** Personalized outreach and follow-up automation.

### Technical Approach

The founding team's deep experience in data science and GenAI at Revolut (one of the world's largest digital banks) informs Nevis's AI architecture. The platform is designed specifically for the RIA workflow rather than as a general-purpose tool adapted for finance.

## Business Model

- **Target customers:** Registered Investment Advisors (RIAs) in the US, from fast-growing independent firms to larger advisory practices.
- **Current traction:** Serves RIAs collectively managing over $50 billion in client assets.
- **Revenue model:** SaaS subscription (specific pricing not disclosed).
- **Market thesis:** Advisors spend up to 80% of their time on administrative work. By reclaiming that time, Nevis enables firms to grow without proportional headcount increases, directly impacting firm economics.

## Competitive Landscape

- **Jump** -- AI operating system for financial advisors ($105M raised, 27,000 advisors).
- **Zocks** -- AI meeting assistant for financial advisors.
- **Brightwave** -- AI for investment research (adjacent, not direct competitor).
- **Salesforce Financial Services Cloud** -- Incumbent CRM with AI features.

Nevis differentiates through its founding team's experience building AI products at Revolut scale, its Sequoia/ICONIQ/Ribbit backing which provides deep wealth management industry connections, and its focus on RIAs specifically.

## What Makes Nevis Interesting

1. **Elite founding team.** Three former Revolut leaders who collectively built fintech products at massive scale, including heading GenAI initiatives, data science, and the retail business line.

2. **Sequoia double-down.** Sequoia leading both the seed and Series A signals strong conviction in the team and market opportunity.

3. **Strategic investor alignment.** ICONIQ (which manages wealth for tech billionaires) and Ribbit Capital (premier fintech investor) provide unmatched access to the wealth management ecosystem.

4. **Large and growing TAM.** The US RIA market manages over $8 trillion in assets and is the fastest-growing segment of wealth management. AI automation directly addresses the advisor capacity constraint.

5. **$200M valuation at stealth exit.** Achieving this valuation before a public launch suggests exceptional early traction and investor demand.

## Key Risks & Open Questions

- **Very early stage.** The company is less than two years old and just emerged from stealth; product-market fit at scale remains to be proven.
- **Competing with Jump's head start.** Jump already has 27,000 advisors and strong momentum; Nevis needs to differentiate effectively.
- **Revenue and ARR unknown.** No public financial metrics beyond AUM served.
- **Small team.** Likely still a small team given the early stage; scaling enterprise sales in wealth management requires relationship-driven go-to-market.
