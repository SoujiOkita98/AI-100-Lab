# Skydio

## Overview

Skydio is the largest drone manufacturer in the United States, building AI-powered autonomous drones for defense, public safety, and enterprise customers. Founded in 2014 by three MIT autonomous flight researchers, the company has delivered over 60,000 drones to 3,800 organizations worldwide.

Skydio's core technology is **Skydio Autonomy**, an AI software stack that enables fully autonomous flight -- including obstacle avoidance, path planning, and mission execution -- using onboard computer vision and deep learning. This eliminates the need for highly trained pilots and enables complex missions in GPS-denied and cluttered environments.

Key products include:

- **Skydio X10** -- Latest flagship drone with advanced sensors, extended range, and enhanced autonomy
- **Skydio X2** -- Enterprise/defense drone (Blue UAS-approved for US government)
- **Skydio Dock** -- Autonomous docking station enabling persistent, remote drone operations
- **Skydio 3D Scan** -- AI-powered automated inspection and mapping software

## Team

All three co-founders met at MIT, where they led award-winning autonomous flight research:

**Adam Bry** (CEO) earned his MS in Aeronautics and Astronautics at MIT and has testified before Congress on US drone policy and the threat posed by Chinese drone manufacturer DJI.

**Abraham Bachrach** (PhD, MIT) is a leading expert in autonomous navigation and SLAM, providing the core perception and mapping technology.

**Matt Donahoe** (MS, MIT) co-developed the autonomous flight software that became Skydio's foundational technology.

The MIT-to-startup pipeline gave Skydio a multi-year head start in autonomous flight AI that competitors have struggled to replicate.

## What makes them interesting

1. **American DJI alternative.** With Chinese drones facing increasing bans in US government and allied nation use, Skydio is the primary beneficiary as the largest US-made drone manufacturer with comparable autonomous capabilities.

2. **AI-first approach.** Unlike most drone companies that build flying cameras, Skydio builds autonomous robots that happen to fly. The AI software stack is the moat, not the hardware.

3. **Defense growth trajectory.** Over 50% of $1.2B in bookings comes from defense, and every US military branch uses Skydio drones. The company is positioned to benefit from the Pentagon's push for autonomous systems.

4. **$180M revenue at 80% growth.** Strong revenue traction with rapid growth indicates genuine product-market fit across multiple customer segments.

5. **Blue UAS certification.** Government approval for use by US federal agencies creates a significant competitive moat against non-certified competitors.

6. **Skydio Dock enables "drone-as-a-service."** Autonomous docking stations allow persistent, remote operations without on-site pilots, opening new use cases in infrastructure monitoring, border security, and military ISR.

## Risks and open questions

- **DJI competition.** Despite government bans, DJI's consumer and enterprise drones remain dominant globally. If bans are relaxed or enforcement is weak, Skydio loses its protected position.
- **IPO timing.** At $2.2B+ valuation, the company faces pressure to either IPO or continue growing rapidly to justify private market pricing.
- **Hardware margins.** Drone hardware is inherently lower-margin than software. Skydio's long-term profitability depends on software and services revenue growing as a share of total revenue.
- **International expansion.** Most revenue is US-based. Expanding to allied nations requires navigating export controls and competing with established local players.
