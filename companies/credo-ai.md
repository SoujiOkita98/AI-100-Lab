# Credo AI -- AI Governance & Responsible AI Platform

## Company Overview

Credo AI is an AI governance SaaS platform that helps enterprises minimize AI-related risk by ensuring their AI systems are fair, compliant, secure, auditable, and human-centered. The platform provides oversight and accountability for the procurement, development, and deployment of AI systems, incorporating context from standards like NIST RMF, emerging regulations like the EU AI Act, and NYC Local Law 144.

Credo AI sits at the intersection of AI technology and AI policy -- a rapidly growing category as governments worldwide introduce AI regulation.

## Founders and Team

### Navrina Singh -- Founder & CEO
- **Education:** Bachelor's in Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering from India; M.S. in ECE from University of Wisconsin-Madison (2003); MBA from USC Marshall.
- **Origin:** India. Bachelor's degree in engineering from India.
- **Prior experience:** Nearly 20 years building products at **Microsoft** and **Qualcomm**.
- **Policy roles:** Member of the U.S. Department of Commerce **National AI Advisory Committee (NAIAC)**, advising the President. AI expert at **OECD**. Advisor to **UN AI Advisory Board**. Young Global Leader at **World Economic Forum**. Former executive board member of **Mozilla Foundation**.
- **Recognition:** **TIME100 AI 2025** -- the 100 Most Influential People in AI. **Inc. 2025 Female Founders 500**. Fast Company #4 Most Innovative Company in AI (2024).

**Founder Pattern:** Navrina Singh represents a distinctive Indian-American AI founder archetype: the "policy-tech hybrid." Unlike most Indian AI founders from pure engineering backgrounds, Singh built her career at the intersection of products and governance. Her 20 years at Microsoft and Qualcomm gave technical credibility, while her policy engagement (NAIAC, OECD, WEF, UN) gave regulatory insight. This is a company where the Indian founder angle is not immediately obvious.

## What Makes Credo AI Interesting

1. **Indian founder where the Indian angle is not obvious:** Singh's public profile emphasizes policy and governance dimensions, making Credo AI easy to miss in Indian founder tracking.
2. **Regulatory tailwind:** As the EU AI Act and US AI executive orders take effect, the market for AI governance tools is expanding rapidly.
3. **TIME100 AI recognition:** Being named alongside Sam Altman and Jensen Huang is extraordinary for a governance startup founder.
4. **Government advisory access as competitive moat:** Singh advises the US President (via NAIAC), the UN, and the OECD on AI policy -- providing unparalleled insight into upcoming regulations.
5. **Female Indian-American AI founder:** One of very few female Indian-American founders leading a venture-backed AI company.
