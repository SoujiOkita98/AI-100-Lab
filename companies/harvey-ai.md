# Harvey AI -- Company Profile

## Overview

Harvey is a domain-specific AI platform built for legal and professional services. The company develops customized large language models that help lawyers with legal research, contract analysis, due diligence, compliance, litigation support, and document drafting. Founded in the summer of 2022 by a former BigLaw litigator and a former DeepMind/Meta ML researcher who were roommates in Los Angeles, Harvey has become one of the fastest-growing enterprise AI startups, reaching ~$195M ARR by end of 2025 (up ~3.9x from ~$50M at end of 2024).

## Founders & Key People

### Winston Weinberg -- CEO & Co-Founder

- **Education:** BA from Kenyon College; JD from USC Gould School of Law.
- **Prior career:** Securities and antitrust litigation associate at O'Melveny & Myers LLP. Left BigLaw after approximately one year to co-found Harvey.
- **Role at Harvey:** CEO, responsible for overall strategy, business development, and go-to-market.

Sources: [TechCrunch profile](https://techcrunch.com/2025/11/14/inside-harvey-how-a-first-year-legal-associate-built-one-of-silicon-valleys-hottest-startups/), [Founderspedia](https://founderspedia.com/winston-weinberg/)

### Gabriel Pereyra -- President & Co-Founder

- **Education:** BS in Computer Science from USC; pursued PhD in Neuroscience at the University of Oxford (did not complete).
- **Prior career:** Google Brain Resident (2016), Research Scientist at DeepMind (2017), founded a stealth startup (2018), joined founding team of XOKind (2020), Machine Learning Engineer at Meta (2022).
- **Role at Harvey:** President, leads technical and product strategy.

Sources: [Digidai profile](https://digidai.github.io/2025/11/22/gabriel-pereyra-harvey-ai-president-legal-revolution-deep-analysis/), [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/gabepereyra/)

### Origin Story

Weinberg and Pereyra were roommates in Los Angeles. In mid-2022, they experimented with chain-of-thought prompting over California landlord-tenant statutes, generated answers to 100 questions from r/legaladvice, and showed the outputs to three practicing landlord-tenant attorneys. On 86 of 100 samples, at least two of three attorneys said they would send the response with zero edits. On July 4, 2022, the founders met with OpenAI's C-suite; OpenAI became their seed investor and gave them early access to GPT-4.

Source: [LawNext podcast (Jan 2026)](https://www.lawnext.com/2026/01/lawnext-from-roommates-to-billionaires-harveys-founders-gabriel-pereyra-and-winston-weinberg-on-building-ai-infrastructure-for-law.html)

## Business Model

- **Subscription SaaS.** Harvey licenses its AI platform to law firms and corporate legal departments on a per-seat basis.
- **Pricing:** Base offering reportedly starts at ~$1,200 per lawyer per month, with 12-month commitments and ~20-seat minimums.
- **Expansion dynamics:** Median seat count reportedly doubles within 12 months of initial deployment. Sector-specific modules (insurance, financial services) and integrations into document-management systems lift average contract values over time.
- **Revenue trajectory:** ~$50M ARR (end 2024) --> ~$195M ARR (end 2025), roughly 3.9x year-over-year growth.

Sources: [Sacra](https://sacra.com/c/harvey/), [Contrary Research](https://research.contrary.com/company/harvey)

## Product

Harvey offers a platform with several core capabilities:

- **Legal Research:** Complex legal, regulatory, and tax research across jurisdictions.
- **Document Analysis:** Securely store, organize, and bulk-analyze legal documents; identify discrepancies across hundreds of contracts.
- **Drafting:** AI-assisted drafting of legal documents, briefs, and memos.
- **Workflow Builder:** Launched June 2025; over 18,000 custom workflows created by end of 2025. Users can run pre-built workflow agents or build custom ones tailored to their firm.
- **Shared Spaces:** Launched December 2025; allows firms to securely share documents and workflows with clients.
- **Integrations:** Harvey for Outlook Add-In; mobile app with Voice to Prompt and Magic Prompt features.
- **Custom Models:** Harvey partnered with OpenAI to build a custom-trained case law model on all of U.S. case law, injecting domain-specific knowledge and legal reasoning into base models.

Sources: [Harvey blog -- Top 5 Product Releases of 2025](https://www.harvey.ai/blog/top-5-product-releases-of-2025), [OpenAI case study](https://openai.com/index/harvey/)

## What Makes Harvey Interesting

1. **Fastest-scaling vertical AI company.** Went from $0 to ~$195M ARR in roughly three years, with valuation going from seed to ~$11B in under four years. Four funding rounds in 14 months (Feb 2025 -- Feb 2026).

2. **Deep OpenAI relationship.** One of the first companies seeded by the OpenAI Startup Fund, with early GPT-4 access. This gave Harvey a significant head start in building legal-specific fine-tuned models before competitors had access.

3. **Founder complementarity.** The pairing of a practicing BigLaw attorney (Weinberg) with a top-tier ML researcher from DeepMind/Meta (Pereyra) gave the company both domain credibility and technical depth from day one.

4. **Enterprise distribution moat.** Adoption by >50% of AmLaw 100 and 1,000+ total customers creates powerful network effects: more usage generates more legal-domain training signal, and switching costs rise as firms build custom workflows.

5. **Expansion beyond law.** Harvey is positioning itself as "Professional Class AI," signaling ambitions to extend into accounting, tax, compliance, and other professional services -- potentially via the PwC relationship and sector-specific modules.

6. **Revenue quality.** Seat-based SaaS with 12-month commitments and natural expansion (median seat count doubles in 12 months) means strong net revenue retention, which explains investor willingness to fund at rapidly escalating valuations.

## Risks & Open Questions

- **Valuation sustainability.** At ~$11B on ~$195M ARR, Harvey trades at ~56x ARR -- premium even by AI standards. Continued hypergrowth is priced in.
- **Model dependency.** Deep reliance on OpenAI models; unclear how defensible the product is if competitors (e.g., Clio, Thomson Reuters CoCounsel, LexisNexis) build comparable fine-tuned legal models on open-weight alternatives.
- **Regulatory risk.** AI-generated legal advice carries liability risk; unauthorized practice of law concerns remain unresolved.
- **Profitability.** With $1.2B+ raised and high inference costs, path to profitability is unclear.
