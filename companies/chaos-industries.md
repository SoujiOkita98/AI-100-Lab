# Chaos Industries

## Overview

Chaos Industries is a Los Angeles-based defense technology company that builds counter-drone radar systems and AI-powered communication platforms. Founded in 2022, the company has become one of the fastest-growing defense startups, raising over $1 billion in funding and reaching a $4.5 billion valuation within just three years.

The company's flagship product is the **Vanquish** radar system, purpose-built to detect small unmanned aerial vehicles (drones) at ranges of hundreds of kilometers. As drones have become the dominant asymmetric threat on modern battlefields (as demonstrated in Ukraine), the demand for effective counter-drone detection and defeat systems has surged.

## Funding story

Chaos Industries has raised over $1 billion across three disclosed funding rounds, with an extraordinarily compressed timeline between rounds.

| Round | Date | Amount | Post-Money Valuation | Lead Investor(s) |
|-------|------|--------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Series B | Jun 2024 | $145M | Undisclosed | -- |
| Series C | May 2025 | $275M | $2B | Accel / NEA |
| Series D | Nov 2025 | $510M | $4.5B | Valor Equity Partners |

The Series D came just four months after the Series C, reflecting intense investor demand and rapid business growth. Key investors include Valor Equity Partners, Accel, NEA, and 8VC.

## Team

**John Tenet** (CEO) and **Bo Marr** are serial defense tech co-founders who previously co-founded Epirus, a directed-energy weapons company. Their experience building Epirus -- which developed microwave-based counter-drone systems -- directly informed Chaos Industries' focus on the counter-drone detection problem. The founding team combines deep expertise in radar systems, electronic warfare, and defense contracting.

## What makes them interesting

1. **Counter-drone is the hottest defense market.** The Ukraine war has demonstrated that cheap drones are reshaping warfare. The ability to detect and track drone swarms is a critical capability gap that every military in the world is urgently trying to fill.

2. **Fastest billion-dollar raise in defense tech.** Going from founding to $1B+ raised in three years, with three rounds in 18 months, indicates either extraordinary product-market fit or extraordinary investor conviction (likely both).

3. **Founding team pedigree.** Having previously built Epirus (a defense unicorn focused on counter-drone defeat via directed energy), the founders bring deep domain expertise and existing relationships with defense procurement offices.

4. **Detection + AI.** The Vanquish radar's ability to detect small drones at long ranges is enhanced by AI-powered signal processing, which helps distinguish drones from birds and other false positives -- a critical challenge in counter-UAS operations.

5. **Growing global market.** Counter-drone systems are needed not just by military forces but also for critical infrastructure protection (airports, power plants, government buildings), expanding the addressable market.

## Risks and open questions

- **Competitive landscape.** The counter-drone market is attracting significant investment from both startups (Epirus, Dedrone, DroneShield) and defense primes (Raytheon, Lockheed Martin, Northrop Grumman).
- **Technology validation.** Public details on operational testing and deployment of the Vanquish system are limited.
- **Rapid valuation growth.** A $4.5B valuation for a 3-year-old company carries execution risk. The valuation may price in years of future growth.
- **Founder overlap with Epirus.** The relationship between Chaos Industries and Epirus (which some founders also co-founded) could raise questions about IP, non-competes, or strategic overlap.
