# Emergent

## Overview

Emergent is an AI-powered "vibe coding" platform that allows anyone -- including non-technical users -- to build full-stack, production-ready web applications by describing what they want in plain English. Founded by twin brothers Mukund and Madhav Jha, the company has achieved extraordinary growth: $100M ARR within eight months of its public launch, making it one of the fastest SaaS companies to reach that milestone. The platform uses autonomous coding agents to generate, test, and deploy applications directly from natural-language intent.

Emergent was part of Y Combinator's S24 batch and has since raised approximately $100M across seed, Series A, and Series B rounds.

## Founders and Team

### Mukund Jha (CEO)
- Twin brother of Madhav. Born and raised in Bihar, India.
- Columbia University Engineering graduate with experience at Google.
- Previously co-founded Dunzo, India's first quick-commerce platform (backed by Google and Reliance), where he served as CTO.
- The Dunzo experience, which ultimately did not succeed, informed his approach to building Emergent with leaner operations.

### Madhav Jha (CTO)
- PhD in Theoretical Computer Science from Penn State University.
- Served as a von Neumann postdoctoral fellow at Sandia National Labs.
- Founding member of the research team that built and shipped Amazon SageMaker.
- Brings deep expertise in distributed systems and ML infrastructure.

The company has ~75 employees, with 70 based in Bengaluru and the remainder in San Francisco.

## Business Model

### How It Works

1. **Natural-language app building:** Users describe their desired application in plain English; Emergent's AI agents generate the full-stack code, UI, and backend.
2. **Autonomous deployment:** The platform handles testing and deployment, producing production-ready applications.
3. **Subscription pricing:** Plans range from $20 to $200 per month, with additional fees for app deployment and hosting.
4. **Marketplace potential:** Emergent positions itself as enabling "everyday entrepreneurs" to build and sell software.

### Traction (as of early 2026)
- $100M ARR achieved in approximately 8 months from public launch.
- $15M ARR within 3 months of launch; $50M ARR within 7 months.
- Over 1 million builders on the platform within 3 months of the Series A announcement.
- The platform tripled its valuation from ~$100M to $300M between September 2025 and January 2026.

## What Makes Emergent Interesting

1. **Unprecedented SaaS growth trajectory:** Reaching $100M ARR in 8 months places Emergent among the fastest-growing SaaS companies ever, rivaling the trajectories of ChatGPT and Cursor.

2. **Democratization of software development:** By enabling non-technical users to build real applications, Emergent dramatically expands the addressable market for software creation tools.

3. **India-origin, global ambition:** The twin founders from Bihar building a $300M company with 70 employees in Bengaluru represents a powerful signal for India's AI startup ecosystem.

4. **Capital efficiency:** Achieving $100M ARR with 75 people implies exceptionally high revenue per employee (~$1.3M), suggesting strong unit economics.

5. **Competitive landscape:** Emergent competes with Lovable, Bolt, Replit, and other AI app builders. The vibe-coding space is crowded and fast-moving.

6. **Risks and open questions:**
   - **Retention and quality:** Can AI-generated apps maintain quality at scale? Customer churn in low-code/no-code platforms has historically been high.
   - **Competition:** The vibe-coding market is becoming intensely competitive, with well-funded players like Replit and Lovable.
   - **Revenue durability:** Subscription revenue from consumer/SMB users can be volatile.
