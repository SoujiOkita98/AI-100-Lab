# NEURA Robotics

## Overview

NEURA Robotics is a German cognitive and humanoid robotics company building robots designed to work seamlessly alongside humans in manufacturing, logistics, and healthcare environments. Founded in 2019 in Metzingen, Germany, NEURA has rapidly become one of Europe's leading robotics companies, distinguishing itself with "cognitive" robots that combine advanced perception, learning, and natural human interaction.

## Products

NEURA produces a range of robotic systems:

- **MAiRA**: A cognitive robot described as the world's first cognitive cobot, featuring integrated sensors for touch, sight, and hearing that enable it to perceive and respond to its environment in real time.
- **4NE-1 (Humanoid)**: NEURA's humanoid robot platform designed for general-purpose tasks in industrial and service environments.

## Business Model

NEURA sells robotic hardware and associated software to enterprise customers in manufacturing, logistics, and healthcare. The company's cognitive approach -- embedding perception and learning directly into the robot -- differentiates it from traditional industrial robot arms that rely on pre-programmed routines. Revenue comes from hardware sales, software licensing, and integration services.

## What Makes NEURA Robotics Interesting

1. **European robotics champion**: One of very few European companies competing at the frontier of cognitive and humanoid robotics, an arena dominated by US and Chinese startups.

2. **Cognitive-first approach**: Unlike many competitors focused purely on humanoid form factors, NEURA emphasizes the "cognitive" layer -- perception, learning, and natural interaction -- as the core differentiator across its product line.

3. **Strategic investors**: Volvo Cars Tech Fund and Delta Electronics provide automotive and electronics manufacturing expertise and potential go-to-market channels.

4. **Speed of growth**: From founding in 2019 to raising EUR 120M in Series B by January 2025, NEURA has scaled rapidly by European deeptech standards.

5. **Industrial pedigree**: Germany's strength in precision manufacturing and industrial automation provides a natural customer base and talent pool.
