# Scribe

## One-liner

AI workflow documentation platform that automatically captures and optimizes how work gets done, used by 5M+ employees at 600K+ companies including 94% of Fortune 500.

## Founders & Leadership

### Jennifer Smith -- Co-Founder & CEO

- Former McKinsey management consultant who experienced the pain of documenting enterprise workflows firsthand.
- Previously at Greylock Partners.
- Founded Scribe in 2019 to build the "operating system for know-how."

### Aaron Podolny -- Co-Founder & CTO

- Former Google engineer, joined via acquisition of his software automation startup.
- Has been working with machine learning technology since 2016.

## Founding Story

Jennifer Smith spent years at McKinsey watching consultants and enterprise teams struggle to capture and share institutional knowledge. The process of creating step-by-step documentation was tedious and manual. In 2019, she teamed up with Aaron Podolny to build Scribe, a platform that automatically records and generates workflow documentation as employees work.

## Product & Business Model

### What Scribe Does

1. **Automatic documentation** -- A browser extension and desktop app that records user actions and auto-generates step-by-step guides with screenshots and annotations.
2. **Scribe Optimize** -- Maps workflows across the enterprise to reveal where automation and AI will yield the highest returns.
3. **Enterprise knowledge base** -- Centralizes and organizes how-to knowledge across 40,000+ software applications.

### Pricing / Monetization

Freemium model with paid tiers for teams and enterprises. Individual users can create guides for free; enterprise plans add collaboration, branding, analytics, and the Optimize product.

## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Positioning |
|-----------|------------|
| **Tango** | Similar auto-documentation for workflows |
| **Whatfix** | Digital adoption platform with in-app guidance |
| **WalkMe** | Digital adoption platform (acquired by SAP) |
| **Loom** | Video-based workflow documentation |
| **Notion / Confluence** | General knowledge management |

Scribe differentiates with automatic capture (no manual writing), massive adoption across Fortune 500, and the new Optimize product that identifies AI/automation opportunities.

## What Makes Scribe Interesting

1. **Product-led growth engine.** 5M users across 600K companies, with 94% Fortune 500 penetration -- a massive distribution advantage.
2. **AI workflow intelligence.** Scribe Optimize moves the company from documentation to strategic workflow optimization.
3. **Efficient path to unicorn.** Reached $1.3B valuation with $135M total raised, indicating strong capital efficiency.

## Risks & Open Questions

- Can Scribe Optimize justify enterprise pricing beyond the free documentation tool?
- Competition from digital adoption platforms (WalkMe, Whatfix) moving into AI documentation.
- Reliance on browser extension model may face enterprise security concerns.
