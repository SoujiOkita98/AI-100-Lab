# Isomorphic Labs

## Overview

Isomorphic Labs is an Alphabet subsidiary spun out of Google DeepMind to apply frontier AI to drug discovery. The company's name reflects its founding thesis: that biology and information science share deep structural parallels ("isomorphisms") that AI can exploit to redesign the pharmaceutical R&D process from first principles. Founded and led by Sir Demis Hassabis -- 2024 Nobel Laureate in Chemistry for AlphaFold 2 -- Isomorphic Labs is arguably the highest-pedigree AI-for-drug-discovery venture in the world.

The company is headquartered in London and had approximately 348 employees as of February 2026.

## Corporate Backing and Funding

Isomorphic Labs was incorporated in February 2021 and publicly announced in November 2021 as a new Alphabet company separate from, but closely connected to, DeepMind. It operated for its first several years using internal Alphabet capital before raising its first external round.

**$600M funding round (March 2025):** Led by Thrive Capital, with participation from GV (Google Ventures) and follow-on capital from Alphabet. The valuation was not officially disclosed but was widely reported in the $3.5B--$5B range. This was one of the largest single rounds in AI-biotech history at the time.

The Alphabet parentage provides advantages beyond capital: deep integration with DeepMind's research talent and compute infrastructure (including access to Google's TPU clusters), which would be prohibitively expensive for an independent startup to replicate.

## Core Technology: The IsoDDE Platform

In February 2026, Isomorphic Labs publicly unveiled the **Isomorphic Drug Design Engine (IsoDDE)**, which multiple commentators described as effectively "AlphaFold 4." IsoDDE is a unified AI system for computational drug design that extends well beyond AlphaFold 3's protein-structure prediction into end-to-end drug-design tasks:

- **Protein-ligand structure prediction:** More than doubles AlphaFold 3's accuracy on the hardest generalization targets.
- **Binding affinity prediction:** Outperforms gold-standard physics-based methods (e.g., free energy perturbation) at a fraction of the cost and time.
- **Antibody-antigen modeling:** On a 334-complex test set, achieved 39% accuracy in the high-fidelity regime (DockQ > 0.8) vs. 17% for AlphaFold 3 and 2% for Boltz-2.
- **Cryptic pocket discovery:** Identifies novel binding sites from amino acid sequence alone, without specifying ligand identity.

IsoDDE is not publicly available; it is proprietary to Isomorphic Labs and deployed within its pharma collaborations.

## Pharma Partnerships

### Eli Lilly (announced January 2024)

- **Upfront payment:** $45 million
- **Milestone potential:** >$1.7 billion (excluding royalties)
- **Scope:** Multi-target, small-molecule drug discovery for undisclosed disease areas

### Novartis (announced January 2024)

- **Upfront payment:** $37.5 million
- **Milestone potential:** $1.2 billion (excluding royalties)
- **Scope:** Multi-target, small-molecule drug discovery

**Combined Lilly + Novartis potential: ~$3 billion** (upfront + milestones, excluding royalties).

### Johnson & Johnson / Janssen Biotech (announced January 2026)

- **Financial terms:** Not disclosed
- **Scope:** Cross-modality partnership spanning small molecules, biologics, peptides, and molecular glues -- notably broader than the earlier Lilly/Novartis deals, which focused on small molecules

In all three collaborations, the operating model is the same: Isomorphic Labs performs in silico design and prediction using IsoDDE; the pharma partner handles experimental validation, IND-enabling studies, and clinical development.

**Note on the "$3B" figure:** The ~$3B number widely cited in press refers to the *maximum* milestone-based payouts for the Lilly and Novartis deals combined. Actual realized revenue depends on program success and milestone achievement. The guaranteed upfront payments total $82.5M. This distinction matters.

## Business Model

Isomorphic Labs operates a **hybrid platform + pipeline** model:

1. **Partnered programs (platform):** Pharma companies bring disease targets; Isomorphic applies IsoDDE for in silico drug design; revenue comes from upfront payments, milestone payments tied to preclinical/clinical/commercial success, and potential royalties on approved drugs. This is the revenue-generating side today.

2. **Proprietary pipeline:** The company is also building its own internal pipeline, with lead candidates primarily in oncology and immune-mediated disorders currently in IND-enabling stages. First clinical trials are targeted for late 2026 (pushed back from an earlier 2025 goal). If successful, this would transform Isomorphic from a technology partner into a drug company with direct product revenue.

The model is capital-efficient in theory: AI-driven design compresses the discovery phase from years to months, reducing the cost of generating clinical candidates. However, experimental validation and clinical trials remain expensive and time-consuming regardless of how the molecule was designed.

## What Makes Isomorphic Labs Interesting (for GSB research purposes)

1. **Unmatched technical moat.** AlphaFold is arguably the most important scientific AI system ever built. Isomorphic Labs has exclusive commercial rights to its drug-discovery applications, plus a ~350-person team that includes many of the original AlphaFold researchers. The IsoDDE results suggest they are widening their lead, not resting on AlphaFold 3.

2. **Credibility of leadership.** Demis Hassabis is a Nobel laureate running a drug discovery company -- an unprecedented combination of AI research credibility and scientific legitimacy. This matters enormously for recruiting talent and signing pharma deals.

3. **Validation from sophisticated buyers.** Lilly, Novartis, and J&J are not naive about AI hype. Their willingness to sign multi-billion-dollar milestone deals signals genuine confidence in the technology's utility, not just optionality.

4. **The Alphabet structural advantage.** Access to DeepMind's research pipeline, Google's compute infrastructure (TPUs), and Alphabet's balance sheet creates a resource asymmetry that pure-play AI biotech startups cannot match. The $600M raise brought in external governance and validation while Alphabet retains majority ownership.

5. **The platform-to-pipeline transition.** The most interesting strategic question is whether Isomorphic can evolve from a "picks and shovels" AI vendor into a fully integrated drug company. The proprietary oncology pipeline is the test case. Success would fundamentally change the company's value and competitive position.

6. **Timing risk and the "valley of death."** Despite the AI sophistication, Isomorphic's proprietary candidates still face the same clinical trial attrition that plagues all drug development (~90% failure rate in clinical trials industry-wide). The first clinical data, expected in 2027 at the earliest, will be the real proof point. Until then, the company's value is largely based on platform credibility and partnership economics.

## Key Uncertainties

- **Valuation:** The $3.5B--$5B range is based on press reports at the time of the 2025 round, not confirmed by the company.
- **Proprietary pipeline details:** Specific targets, indications beyond "oncology," and molecule identities have not been publicly disclosed.
- **J&J deal financials:** Terms of the January 2026 Johnson & Johnson collaboration have not been disclosed.
- **Clinical timeline:** First-in-human trials were originally targeted for 2025, then pushed to late 2026. Further delays are possible.
- **Revenue recognition:** It is unclear how much of the $82.5M in upfront payments has been recognized, or what additional milestone payments have been triggered.
