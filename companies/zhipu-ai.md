# Zhipu AI (Z.ai) -- Company Profile

## Overview

Zhipu AI -- marketed internationally as **Z.ai** and formally incorporated as **Knowledge Atlas Technology** -- is one of China's most prominent foundation-model companies and the first of the so-called "Six AI Tigers" (alongside Moonshot AI, Baichuan, MiniMax, 01.AI, and StepFun) to complete an IPO. The company was spun out of **Tsinghua University's Department of Computer Science and Technology** in 2019 and builds the **GLM (General Language Model)** family of large language models.

On **January 8, 2026**, Zhipu listed on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange (ticker 2513), raising approximately **$558 million** at a valuation of roughly **$6.5 billion**. The stock opened at HK$120 per share and surged as high as HK$130 on its first trading day.

### Executive Leadership

| Person | Role | Background |
|---|---|---|
| **Zhang Peng (张鹏)** | CEO | PhD from Tsinghua's KEG under the co-founders. Leads commercial strategy while maintaining the company's research-first ethos. Has stated the ambition to build "China's OpenAI." |
| **Liu Debing (刘德兵)** | Chairman | Former executive at Technicolor (China). Apprentice of Academician Gao Wen (Chinese Academy of Engineering). Oversees government relations and corporate governance. Together with Tang Jie, controls ~37% of voting rights through a founder consortium. |

### Team DNA

The company's intellectual roots run deep into Tsinghua's KEG lab. Much of the early engineering and research team comprised Tang Jie's and Li Juanzi's graduate students and post-docs. This academic pedigree gives Zhipu unusually strong ties to China's government-backed AI research ecosystem (BAAI, National Key Labs) and a pipeline of top-tier ML talent.

## Products and Model Portfolio

### GLM Model Family

The GLM (General Language Model) series is Zhipu's flagship. Evolution:

- **GLM-4.5 / GLM-4.5 Air** (Jul 2025) -- Next-gen language models; 355B total parameters (MoE), 32B active.
- **GLM-4.5V** (Aug 2025) -- Vision-language model, 106B parameters.
- **GLM-4.6** (Sep 2025) -- Notable for being trained on **Chinese-made chips** (Cambricon Technologies), reducing NVIDIA dependency.
- **GLM-4.7** (Dec 2025) -- Open-source coding model. Matches premium competitors on key benchmarks at dramatically lower cost (~$3/month vs. $200/month for comparable US products). Free for local use.
- **GLM-5** (Feb 2026) -- 744B-parameter MoE model, 40B active parameters per token. **Trained entirely on Huawei Ascend chips** using the MindSpore framework with zero NVIDIA dependency. Priced at ~$1.00/M input tokens, ~$3.20/M output tokens -- roughly 5-8x cheaper than frontier US models.
- **GLM-5-Turbo** (2026) -- Optimized variant for agentic workloads.

### Other Products

- **AutoGLM** -- AI agent that executes tasks on smartphones via voice commands. Represents Zhipu's push into consumer AI.
- **Ying (映)** -- Text-to-video generation model (produces ~6-second clips).
- **Zhipu MaaS Platform** -- Enterprise API/SDK platform with 2.9 million users (15% paying) and 12,000+ enterprise clients, including 50% of China's top 10 internet firms.
- **Edge deployment** -- GLM-4.7-Flash targets consumer hardware; Samsung Galaxy S25 (China edition) ships with Zhipu's on-device model.

## Business Model

Zhipu operates a **Model-as-a-Service (MaaS)** business with three primary revenue channels:

1. **API and SDK licensing** -- Per-call or annual enterprise API access. This is the largest revenue driver (MaaS accounted for 84.5% of 2024 revenue).
2. **On-premises / private cloud deployments** -- Custom installations for state-owned enterprises, government agencies, and financial institutions. Higher-margin, relationship-driven.
3. **Consumer products** -- AutoGLM and edge-model licensing (e.g., Samsung partnership).

### Financial Snapshot

- **2024 revenue:** 312.4M RMB (~$43M USD)
- **H1 2025 revenue:** 190.9M RMB (~$27M), up 325% YoY
- **H1 2025 net loss:** 2.36B RMB (~$330M) -- R&D spending of 1.59B RMB was ~8x revenue
- **Post-IPO allocation:** 70% of IPO proceeds earmarked for frontier AGI research

The company is deeply unprofitable by design, prioritizing research scale over near-term margins -- a pattern consistent with OpenAI, Anthropic, and other frontier labs.

## What Makes Zhipu AI Interesting

1. **First Chinese LLM pure-play to IPO.** By going public before any of the other "Six Tigers," Zhipu set a market benchmark and gained access to public capital markets for its capital-intensive research.

2. **Deep Tsinghua academic lineage.** Unlike many Chinese AI companies that are led by industry veterans, Zhipu's DNA is fundamentally academic. The co-founders are active Tsinghua professors, and the company was literally incubated in a university lab. This gives them privileged access to China's top CS talent pipeline and government research funding.

3. **NVIDIA-independent training at scale.** GLM-4.6 was trained on Cambricon chips; GLM-5 was trained entirely on Huawei Ascend hardware. In the context of US export controls on advanced semiconductors, Zhipu is arguably the most advanced demonstration that China can train frontier models without NVIDIA GPUs.

4. **Aggressive open-source strategy.** GLM-4.7 (coding) was released open-source and competes with $200/month US tools at $3/month. This positions Zhipu as a credible alternative for cost-sensitive global developers and enterprises.

5. **Government alignment.** Tang Jie's BAAI connections, Liu Debing's state-enterprise background, and the Tsinghua affiliation make Zhipu a natural partner for Chinese government AI initiatives. The company reportedly serves government customers both domestically and internationally.

6. **"China's OpenAI" positioning.** CEO Zhang Peng has explicitly framed this ambition. With 70% of IPO funds going to AGI research, the company is betting on the same "research-first, monetize-later" playbook that has defined OpenAI and Anthropic.

---

## Key Risks and Open Questions

- **Profitability timeline unclear.** R&D spending at 8x revenue is extreme even by frontier AI standards. Public-market patience may be limited.
- **Competitive pressure from DeepSeek, Moonshot AI, Baichuan, and the hyperscalers** (Alibaba Qwen, Baidu ERNIE). The Chinese AI model market is intensely competitive with aggressive price wars.
- **US chip export controls** are a double-edged sword: they motivate domestic chip independence but also constrain available compute.
- **Valuation sustainability** post-IPO remains to be seen as more Chinese AI companies pursue listings.
