# Vannevar Labs

## Overview

Vannevar Labs is a San Francisco-based defense AI company that builds intelligence analysis platforms for the US military and intelligence community. Named after Vannevar Bush, the WWII-era science advisor who helped establish the modern US research-defense complex, the company specializes in AI-powered multi-source intelligence fusion to support real-time military decision-making.

The company's core platform ingests data from diverse intelligence sources -- signals intelligence (SIGINT), geospatial intelligence (GEOINT), open-source intelligence (OSINT), and human intelligence (HUMINT) -- and uses AI/ML to fuse, analyze, and present actionable insights to military commanders and intelligence analysts. The platform is designed for the specific operational needs of combatant commands, with particular focus on the Indo-Pacific theater.

## Funding story

Vannevar Labs has raised $91.5 million across three rounds, with a notably capital-efficient growth trajectory (achieving $80M revenue and profitability on relatively modest venture funding).

## Team

**Brett Granberg** (CEO) is uniquely positioned at the intersection of Silicon Valley and the intelligence community. His time at In-Q-Tel (the CIA's venture capital arm) gave him deep understanding of what the IC actually needs (versus what technology companies think it needs), while his McKinsey background provides operational and strategic rigor.

The founding team's IC and defense community relationships have been critical to Vannevar's land-and-expand strategy, starting with specific combatant commands and growing across the DoD.

## What makes them interesting

1. **Extraordinary capital efficiency.** Growing from $3.5M to $80M in revenue (23x) while raising only $91.5M total, and achieving profitability within three years, is exceptional in defense tech. Most competitors have raised 5-10x more capital for comparable or lower revenue.

2. **In-Q-Tel pedigree.** The CEO's background at the CIA's venture arm provides unmatched credibility and relationships with intelligence community customers. Few founders understand the IC's needs as intimately.

3. **INDOPACOM focus.** Vannevar's specialization in the Indo-Pacific theater aligns directly with the US military's #1 strategic priority: deterring China. This makes the company's work among the most strategically important in defense tech.

4. **DIU production contract.** The $99M ceiling DIU contract validates the technology at a production level, not just as a prototype or pilot. This is a critical milestone for any defense startup.

5. **Software-only, high-margin model.** Unlike hardware-focused defense startups, Vannevar builds pure software, enabling higher margins and faster iteration without manufacturing complexity.

6. **Profitable defense startup.** Achieving profitability is rare among defense tech startups, especially at this stage. This suggests strong unit economics and genuine product-market fit, not just hype-driven growth.

## Risks and open questions

- **Customer concentration.** Revenue is heavily concentrated in US military/IC customers. Budget shifts or changing priorities could impact growth.
- **Competition from Palantir.** Palantir's Gotham and Maven platforms directly compete in the military intelligence analysis space, with a much larger installed base and deeper government relationships.
- **Scale limitations.** At $91.5M raised and ~200 employees, Vannevar may face challenges scaling to compete with much larger, better-funded competitors.
- **Classification constraints.** Much of the company's work is likely classified, limiting its ability to publicly demonstrate capabilities, attract talent, or pursue commercial revenue.
- **Valuation opacity.** The company has not disclosed its valuation in any funding round, making it difficult to assess investor expectations and potential returns.
