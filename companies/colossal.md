# Colossal Biosciences

## Overview

Colossal Biosciences is a Dallas-based de-extinction and synthetic biology company founded in 2021 by serial entrepreneur Ben Lamm and Harvard geneticist George Church. The company uses AI-driven genomics and CRISPR gene-editing technology to pursue species restoration, with active projects to bring back the woolly mammoth, Tasmanian tiger (thylacine), and dodo bird.

Colossal has raised over $555M in total funding, reaching a $10.32B valuation as of September 2025 -- making it Texas' first decacorn. The company's investor base is heavily concentrated around Thomas Tull and TWG Global.

## Funding story

**September 2021 -- Seed (~$15M).** Thomas Tull led the initial round to launch the company with de-extinction as its core mission.

**March 2022 -- Series A ($60M).** Led by Thomas Tull with a diverse investor base including Breyer Capital, Animoca Brands, Paris Hilton, Draper Associates, and Charles Hoskinson.

**January 2023 -- Series B ($150M at ~$1B valuation).** Led by Tull's US Innovative Technology Fund. Colossal reached unicorn status. In-Q-Tel (CIA venture arm) participated.

**January 2025 -- Series C ($200M at $10.2B valuation).** TWG Global (Mark Walter and Thomas Tull's investment company) led. A 10x valuation jump from Series B, reaching decacorn status.

**September 2025 -- Series C Extension ($120M at $10.32B valuation).** Additional capital from TWG Global, Bob Nelsen, and filmmaker Peter Jackson. Brought total Series C to $320M.

## What makes them interesting

1. **Audacious scientific mission.** De-extinction is among the most ambitious applications of CRISPR and AI-driven genomics. If successful, the technology platform has applications far beyond bringing back mammoths.

2. **Decacorn status with concentrated backing.** The $10B+ valuation is largely driven by Thomas Tull and TWG Global's conviction. This concentrated investor base is both a strength (deep commitment) and a risk (limited price discovery).

3. **Near-term revenue strategy.** The ViaGen Pets acquisition provides commercial revenue while long-term de-extinction research continues, addressing the classic deep-tech monetization challenge.
