# FieldAI

## Overview

FieldAI builds embodiment-agnostic autonomy software -- what the company calls "Field Foundation Models" (FFMs) -- that serve as a universal AI brain for mobile robots. Unlike companies that build specific robot hardware, FieldAI's software can power legged, wheeled, flying, and tracked platforms, enabling them to navigate dynamic, unstructured environments without GPS, pre-defined maps, or pre-programmed trajectories.

The company was founded by Ali Agha, a former NASA JPL roboticist, and the team includes veterans from DeepMind, Google Brain, Tesla Autopilot, SpaceX, Zoox, Cruise, Amazon, and DARPA.

## Technology

FieldAI's Field Foundation Models provide risk-aware autonomy that allows robots to:
- Navigate unknown environments without prior mapping
- Adapt to dynamic and unpredictable conditions in real time
- Operate across diverse form factors (quadrupeds, humanoids, drones, tracked vehicles)

The technology is proven across industries including construction, energy, mining, logistics, and federal/defense applications.

## Business Model

FieldAI operates as a software platform provider, licensing its autonomy stack to robot manufacturers and operators across industrial verticals. The company targets the $25+ trillion physical labor economy, focusing on sectors where human access is dangerous, difficult, or expensive: construction sites, oil and gas facilities, mines, power utilities, and defense operations.

## What Makes FieldAI Interesting

1. **Embodiment-agnostic approach**: Rather than building its own robots, FieldAI provides the "brain" that works across any mobile platform. This positions it as a potential horizontal layer in the robotics stack.

2. **NASA JPL heritage**: The founding team's experience building autonomous systems for extraplanetary exploration translates directly to operating in GPS-denied, unmapped environments on Earth.

3. **Investor caliber**: Backing from Bezos, Gates, NVIDIA, Temasek, and Khosla at a $2B valuation signals strong conviction from sophisticated deep-tech investors.

4. **Industrial focus**: While many robotics AI companies chase humanoid consumer markets, FieldAI targets near-term revenue in heavy industry where autonomous navigation has immediate ROI.
