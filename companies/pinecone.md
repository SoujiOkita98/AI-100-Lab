# Pinecone

## Overview

Pinecone is a serverless vector database company that provides the infrastructure layer for AI applications requiring fast, accurate similarity search across high-dimensional data. Founded in 2019 by Edo Liberty, an Israeli-born researcher who previously led Amazon AI Labs at AWS and Yahoo's New York Research Lab, Pinecone essentially created the "vector database" product category as a standalone commercial offering. The company is headquartered in New York with engineering operations in Tel Aviv, Israel.

As of March 2026, Pinecone has raised $138 million in total funding and was last valued at $750 million (Series B, April 2023). The company serves over 20,000 organizations and employs approximately 129 people. In September 2025, founder Edo Liberty transitioned from CEO to Chief Scientist, handing operational leadership to veteran tech executive Ash Ashutosh (ex-Google, three-time founder). The company has been the subject of acquisition rumors, with potential suitors reportedly including Oracle, IBM, MongoDB, and Snowflake.

## Funding story

**2021 -- Seed ($10M).** Wing Venture Capital backed Pinecone's initial commercialization of vector search technology. The product was still in early access.

**2021 -- Series A ($28M at $170M valuation).** Led by Menlo Ventures. This round came as the concept of "vector databases" was still relatively obscure outside of ML engineering circles. (Note: some sources cite $17M for this round; the $28M figure comes from Calcalist/Ctech reporting.)

**2023 -- Series B ($100M at $750M valuation).** Led by Andreessen Horowitz, with Iconiq Growth, Menlo Ventures, and Wing Venture Capital participating. This round was timed perfectly with the generative AI explosion following ChatGPT's launch, as enterprises scrambled to build RAG (retrieval-augmented generation) pipelines and needed vector search infrastructure. The 4.4x valuation jump in roughly 16 months reflected the market's enthusiasm for AI infrastructure.

No subsequent funding rounds have been publicly announced as of March 2026. The $750M valuation remains the most recent publicly confirmed figure, though acquisition discussions reportedly target a price above $2 billion (per The Information).

## Team

**Edo Liberty (Founder, Chief Scientist):** The intellectual engine of Pinecone. Israeli-born, with a B.Sc. in Physics and Computer Science from Tel Aviv University and a Ph.D. in Computer Science from Yale. His career arc is the origin story of the company: as Director of Research at AWS, he built parts of SageMaker, Rekognition, Personalize, Forecast, and other services, gaining deep expertise in deploying vector search at massive scale. Before AWS, he ran Yahoo's New York Research Lab. Liberty saw that while large tech companies could build custom vector search systems, no turnkey solution existed for everyone else. That gap became Pinecone. He stepped down as CEO in September 2025 to focus entirely on technology as Chief Scientist.

**Ash Ashutosh (CEO, appointed September 2025):** A seasoned enterprise technology operator. Three-time founder of storage/data infrastructure companies (Serano Systems, AppIQ, Actifio), each leading to hundreds of millions in revenue and eventual acquisition. Former CTO of HP's Storage division, partner at Greylock Partners, and most recently Global Director of Solution Sales at Google (managing a $1B+ portfolio). His appointment signals Pinecone's pivot toward enterprise go-to-market execution. Penn State EE and CS degrees.

**Ram Sriharsha (CTO):** Previously VP of Engineering at Splunk and Principal Research Scientist at Yahoo. Brings experience scaling data infrastructure products.

**Lauren Nemeth (COO, joined 2024):** Leads Sales, Field Engineering, Marketing, Growth, and Partnerships globally.

**Bob Muglia (Board member, 2024):** Former President of Juniper Networks and EVP at Microsoft. Adds enterprise credibility.

The company has a notable Israeli research DNA -- founded by an Israeli entrepreneur, with engineering operations in Tel Aviv. The ~129-person headcount is lean for a company at this stage, suggesting a research-heavy, efficient organization.

## Business model

Pinecone operates a consumption-based SaaS model. The core product is a fully managed, serverless vector database accessed via API. Customers pay based on:

- **Read units** (queries and fetches)
- **Write units** (upserts, updates, deletes)
- **Storage** (per GB)

Pricing tiers range from a free Starter plan to Enterprise ($500/month minimum commitment) and Dedicated BYOC (Bring Your Own Cloud). The serverless architecture, launched in January 2024 and GA in May 2024, was a significant shift -- it reduced costs by up to 50x compared to the prior pod-based model and eliminated the need for customers to provision infrastructure.

Beyond the core database, Pinecone has expanded into adjacent services: **Pinecone Inference** (hosted embedding and reranking models) and **Pinecone Assistant** (an agent/chat API for building RAG applications). These represent a strategic move up the stack from pure infrastructure toward application-layer revenue.

The product is available on AWS Marketplace, Azure Marketplace, and GCP, enabling procurement through existing cloud commitments.

## Customers

Notable enterprise customers include Shopify, HubSpot, Gong, Zapier, Workday, BambooHR, and Vanguard. The loss of Notion as a customer in 2025 was widely reported and seen as a significant blow, both commercially and as a signal of competitive pressure.

Use cases span semantic search, RAG pipelines, recommendation systems, anomaly detection, and increasingly agentic AI workflows.

## What makes them interesting

1. **Category creator.** Pinecone essentially defined "vector database" as a standalone product category. Before Pinecone, vector search was a feature inside broader systems or a custom-built capability at large tech companies. Liberty's insight -- that this needed to be a managed, standalone service -- proved prescient as generative AI exploded.

2. **Serverless pivot was bold and well-timed.** The January 2024 serverless relaunch (up to 50x cost reduction) was a major architectural bet that aligned with customer demand for lower costs and simpler operations. Over 30,000 organizations indexed 25+ billion vectors on serverless within the first year.

3. **Founder-to-operator CEO transition.** The Liberty-to-Ashutosh handoff is a classic "founder steps back, operator scales" move. Ashutosh's track record of building enterprise revenue at three prior companies (all acquired) suggests Pinecone is optimizing for go-to-market at scale.

4. **Lean operation.** At ~129 employees and $138M raised, Pinecone is remarkably capital-efficient compared to AI infrastructure peers. This is a double-edged sword: it keeps burn low but may constrain R&D investment as competition intensifies.

5. **Strategic acquisition target.** The reported $2B+ sale discussions with potential suitors (Oracle, IBM, MongoDB, Snowflake) reflect the strategic value of vector search in the AI stack. Every major database and cloud vendor now needs vector capabilities, and acquiring Pinecone would be faster than building.

6. **Moving up the stack.** Pinecone Inference and Pinecone Assistant show ambition beyond pure infrastructure. If successful, this transforms Pinecone from a database vendor into an AI application platform -- a much larger addressable market.

## Risks and challenges

- **Competition is fierce and intensifying.** Every major cloud provider (AWS, Google Cloud, Azure) and database vendor (MongoDB, PostgreSQL/pgvector, Elasticsearch) now offers vector search capabilities. Open-source alternatives (Milvus, Chroma, Qdrant, Weaviate) compete on cost and flexibility. Pinecone's early-mover advantage is eroding.

- **Customer retention concerns.** The loss of Notion -- a high-profile, logo-quality customer -- suggests that vector search may be commoditizing faster than expected. Customers can increasingly get "good enough" vector search from their existing database providers.

- **Revenue trajectory is unclear.** If the 2025 revenue decline is real, it raises questions about whether the serverless pricing model cannibalized revenue and whether growth has stalled. At $750M valuation on ~$27M revenue (2024), the company trades at roughly 28x revenue -- demanding strong growth to justify.

- **Sale uncertainty.** CEO Ash Ashutosh has stated that acquisition is "not the goal," but the reported banker discussions and competitive pressure create strategic ambiguity. An acquisition could provide a strong exit for investors but would end Pinecone's independent trajectory.

- **Small team in a capital-intensive market.** 129 employees is lean. As AI infrastructure becomes a platform-level competition between hyperscalers with billions in R&D budgets, Pinecone's ability to innovate fast enough as a standalone company is an open question.

## Notes

- **Confidence level: Medium.** Revenue data for 2025 is inconsistent across sources. The Series A amount varies between $17M and $28M depending on the source. Acquisition rumors are based on reporting from The Information and Calcalist but are unconfirmed. Employee count comes from Tracxn/ZoomInfo estimates.
- **Israel connection:** Pinecone was founded by an Israeli entrepreneur and maintains engineering operations in Tel Aviv, making it part of Israel's significant AI infrastructure ecosystem.
- **The "vector database" category may be transient.** As vector search becomes a feature of general-purpose databases (PostgreSQL, MongoDB, Elasticsearch), the standalone vector database market may shrink. Pinecone's move up the stack toward Inference and Assistant products may be an acknowledgment of this risk.
