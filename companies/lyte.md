# Lyte

## Overview

Lyte emerged from stealth on January 5, 2026 with $107 million in aggregate funding to build what it calls the perception foundation for physical AI. The company's core product, LyteVision, integrates advanced 4D sensing, RGB imaging, and motion awareness into a single platform, delivering unified spatial and visual data through one connection.

The technology is designed to serve a wide range of physical AI platforms: autonomous mobile robots, robotic arms, quadrupeds, robotaxis, and humanoids.

## Founding Team

The three founders are the key architects behind Apple's depth-sensing technology stack, including Face ID. CEO Alexander Shpunt previously co-founded PrimeSense, the 3D sensing company that powered Microsoft Kinect and was acquired by Apple in 2013 for approximately $350 million. After the acquisition, the team spent roughly a decade at Apple building the depth-sensing platform.

## What Makes Them Interesting

1. **PrimeSense pedigree.** The founding team built the depth sensing technology that powers Face ID on a billion+ Apple devices. That is an unusually strong hardware track record.
2. **$107M before launch.** Raising nine figures while still in stealth indicates extraordinary investor confidence in the team and technology.
3. **CES 2026 recognition.** Won Best of Innovation in Robotics and Honoree in Vehicle Tech at CES 2026.
4. **Horizontal perception platform.** Rather than building a robot, Lyte is building the perception layer that all robots need -- a potentially larger market than any single form factor.
