# RunSybil

## Overview

RunSybil is an AI-native offensive security platform whose autonomous agent, Sybil, conducts continuous penetration tests against live applications -- finding, exploiting, and documenting real security vulnerabilities without humans in the loop. Customers have reported finding critical vulnerabilities that had gone undetected using traditional methods.

Co-founded by Ari Herbert-Voss (OpenAI's first security researcher) and Vlad Ionescu (former Meta red team lead), the company represents a rare intersection: people who understand how to build frontier AI systems and how to hack into complex software.

## Funding Story

The $40M round led by Khosla Ventures features notable strategic backers: Anthropic's Anthology Fund (the AI safety company investing in AI security), Menlo Ventures, and an angel list that includes Jeff Dean (Google) and Nikesh Arora (Palo Alto Networks CEO).

## What Makes Them Interesting

1. **Founder combination.** OpenAI's first security hire + Meta's red team lead is arguably the strongest founding team possible for AI-powered offensive security.
2. **Anthropic's Anthology Fund investing.** The AI safety company backing an AI security startup signals a new category of strategic investment.
3. **Already finding critical vulnerabilities.** Customers report Sybil finding real bugs that traditional pentesting missed, validating the technology.
4. **Continuous vs. periodic testing.** Traditional pentesting happens annually or quarterly; RunSybil runs continuously, matching the pace of modern software deployment.
5. **Customer quality.** Cursor, Notion, and major financial institutions as early customers demonstrates enterprise readiness.
