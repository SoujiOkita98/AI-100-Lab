# Truewind

## Overview

Truewind is an AI-powered digital accountant that automates bookkeeping, transaction categorization, documentation collection, and month-end close processes for startups and accounting firms. The platform acts as a "digital staff accountant that never sleeps," handling the repetitive financial tasks that typically consume significant time for finance teams. Trusted by over 100 customers including accounting firms EisnerAmper and Frank Rimerman, as well as fast-growing companies like Wefunder and Mozart Data.

## Founders & Key People

### Alex Lee -- Co-Founder & CEO
- **Background:** Previously worked at Plaid (fintech infrastructure) and JP Morgan; deep expertise in financial systems and infrastructure
- **Origin story:** Recognized that the accounting industry was ripe for AI automation, with massive amounts of repetitive, rules-based work that LLMs could handle effectively

### Tennison Chan -- Co-Founder
- **Background:** Software engineering; Bay Area tech experience

## Products & Technology

Truewind's platform automates core accounting workflows:

1. **Transaction Categorization:** AI-powered automatic categorization of bank and credit card transactions
2. **Documentation Collection:** Automated gathering and organization of invoices, receipts, and supporting documents
3. **Month-End Close:** Streamlined reconciliation and close processes
4. **Financial Reporting:** Automated generation of financial statements and reports

## Business Model

- **SaaS subscription:** Monthly fee for AI-powered accounting services
- **Target customers:** Startups, SMBs, and accounting firms
- **Strategic partnership:** Thomson Reuters Ventures is both an investor and a strategic partner, providing access to their network of accounting professionals

## What Makes Truewind Interesting

1. **Strategic investor alignment.** Thomson Reuters Ventures investing in both the seed extension and co-leading the Series A signals deep conviction and provides distribution into their massive accounting customer base.

2. **Large, underserved market.** The accounting and bookkeeping industry is enormous ($150B+ in the US alone) and has historically been slow to adopt technology, making it ripe for AI disruption.

3. **Strong B2B2B model.** By serving both direct customers and accounting firms, Truewind can scale through the accounting firms' existing client relationships.

4. **AI timing advantage.** LLMs are particularly well-suited for accounting tasks that require understanding financial documents, categorizing transactions, and applying rules -- tasks that were previously too nuanced for traditional automation.
