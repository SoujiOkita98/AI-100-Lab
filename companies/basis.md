# Basis -- Company Profile

## Overview

Basis is an AI-powered accounting platform that deploys autonomous agents to automate core accounting workflows, including financial statement preparation, tax return filing, expense tracking, and audit support. The company reached unicorn status in February 2026 with a $1.15 billion valuation, making it one of the fastest-growing AI startups in the accounting vertical. Basis counts approximately 30% of the Top 25 US accounting firms as customers, with firms reporting 30-50% efficiency gains from agent-based workflows.

## Founders & Key People

### Matthew Harpe -- Co-Founder & CEO

- **Prior career:** Worked at Boston Consulting Group on healthcare and data science projects, where he became acutely aware of how rarely organizations leverage granular accounting data in decision-making.
- **Role at Basis:** CEO, strategy, fundraising, and go-to-market.

### Mitchell Troyanovsky -- Co-Founder

- **Education:** Babson College.
- **Prior career:** First product hire at Puzzle Financial, an accounting ledger startup, where he helped build the company's platform.
- **Role at Basis:** Product and engineering leadership.

### Origin Story

Harpe and Troyanovsky are long-time friends who believe the impact of cutting-edge AI on accounting will rival the shift from paper to digital. After observing inefficiencies firsthand, they left their careers to found Basis in 2023. They began by deeply engaging with accountants across a wide range of firms, which led to a prototype that drew immediate interest from some of the largest accounting firms in the US.

## Product & Technology

### Core Platform

Basis provides end-to-end AI agents that handle complex accounting workflows:

- **Financial Statement Preparation:** Agents that automate the creation and review of financial statements from raw accounting data.
- **Tax Return Filing:** AI-driven tax preparation across multiple jurisdictions.
- **Expense Tracking:** Automated categorization, reconciliation, and tracking of expenses.
- **Audit Support:** AI agents that assist with audit preparation and documentation workflows.

### Technical Approach

Basis builds domain-specific AI agents purpose-built for accounting, rather than generic LLM wrappers. The agents are designed to handle the precision-critical nature of accounting work, where errors have regulatory and financial consequences. The platform integrates with existing accounting software stacks used by major firms.

## Business Model

- **Target customers:** Accounting firms, from mid-market to Big Four; approximately 30% of the Top 25 US accounting firms are already customers.
- **Go-to-market:** Enterprise sales to accounting firms, with AI agents deployed across accounting, tax, and audit workflows.
- **Value proposition:** 30-50% efficiency gains for CPA firms, enabling them to handle more clients without proportional headcount increases.
- **Revenue model:** SaaS subscription (specific pricing not disclosed).

## Competitive Landscape

- **Numeric** -- AI-powered close management and accounting automation ($89M raised).
- **Accrual** -- AI for accounting preparation and review (raised $75M from General Catalyst in Feb 2026).
- **BlackLine** -- Incumbent cloud accounting automation platform (public company).
- **Puzzle Financial** -- AI accounting ledger for startups.

Basis differentiates through its agent-based architecture that handles end-to-end workflows rather than point solutions, and its early traction with the largest accounting firms.

## What Makes Basis Interesting

1. **Fastest path to unicorn in accounting tech.** Reaching $1.15B valuation in ~2.5 years signals exceptional product-market fit in a traditionally slow-moving industry.

2. **Blue-chip investor syndicate.** Khosla, Accel, GV, plus individual backing from Jeff Dean, Nat Friedman, Daniel Gross, and Lloyd Blankfein represents an unusual concentration of AI and finance expertise.

3. **Massive TAM.** The global accounting services market exceeds $600 billion, with persistent labor shortages creating strong demand for automation.

4. **Rapid enterprise adoption.** 30% of Top 25 US accounting firms already using the product provides strong social proof and a foundation for expansion.

5. **Regulatory moat potential.** Accounting is highly regulated, making it difficult for generic AI tools to compete with purpose-built, compliance-aware agents.

## Key Risks & Open Questions

- **Accuracy requirements are extreme.** Accounting errors have legal and financial consequences; any AI hallucination risk could slow adoption.
- **Incumbent response.** BlackLine, Intuit, and other established players are adding AI capabilities.
- **Revenue scale unclear.** No public ARR figures despite the high valuation.
- **Concentration risk.** Heavy reliance on large accounting firms could create customer concentration issues.
