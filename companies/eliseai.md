# EliseAI -- Company Profile

## Overview

EliseAI is a vertical AI company that automates communications and operational workflows for the housing and healthcare industries. The company's AI platform manages leasing conversations, resident communications, and property operations for approximately 10% of the U.S. apartment market. More recently, EliseAI has expanded into healthcare automation, targeting the two sectors that together represent roughly 40% of household spending in America. Founded in 2017 -- years before the current generative AI wave -- EliseAI was an early mover in applying conversational AI to real estate operations.

## Founders & Key People

### Minna Song -- CEO & Co-Founder

- **Education:** B.S. in Computer Science and Engineering from MIT; also studied at the University of Cambridge.
- **Prior career:** Software development roles at Microsoft and MIT Lincoln Laboratory. Co-founded Influencers Required prior to EliseAI.
- **Role at EliseAI:** CEO, responsible for strategy, fundraising, and go-to-market.

### Stoyan (Tony) Stoyanov -- CTO & Co-Founder

- **Education:** Computer Science at the University of Cambridge, where he met Minna Song.
- **Role at EliseAI:** CTO, leads technology strategy and AI platform architecture. Built the platform that supports over one million conversations monthly.

### Origin Story

Minna Song took a job as an office manager at a real estate company after graduating to understand the market firsthand. Within her first week, the most obvious pain point became clear: communication. Tenants constantly complained about slow or nonexistent responses from agents. This observation led Song and Stoyanov to build an AI-powered communication layer for property management, founding EliseAI in 2017.

## Business Model

- **Subscription SaaS.** EliseAI licenses its AI platform to property management companies and healthcare organizations on a recurring basis.
- **Revenue:** ARR reportedly exceeds $100M as of 2025.
- **Market penetration:** The platform automates communications for approximately 1 in 10 U.S. apartments, giving it significant market share in multifamily housing.
- **Expansion:** The Series E funding is earmarked for expanding the healthcare vertical, where EliseAI is applying its conversational AI capabilities to patient communications, scheduling, and administrative workflows.

## Product

- **Leasing AI:** Automates prospect communications across email, SMS, chat, and phone, handling inquiries, scheduling tours, and following up.
- **Resident AI:** Manages maintenance requests, lease renewals, and resident communications.
- **Healthcare AI:** New vertical applying conversational automation to patient engagement and operational workflows.
- **Analytics:** Provides property managers with insights on conversion rates, response times, and operational efficiency.

## What Makes EliseAI Interesting

1. **Early mover advantage.** Founded in 2017, EliseAI built domain-specific conversational AI for real estate well before the ChatGPT-driven hype cycle, giving it years of proprietary training data and customer relationships.

2. **Massive market penetration.** Covering ~10% of U.S. apartments provides a strong data moat and network effects that are difficult for new entrants to replicate.

3. **Vertical expansion playbook.** The move from real estate to healthcare demonstrates a repeatable pattern of applying conversational AI to operationally complex, communication-heavy industries.

4. **Capital efficiency.** Reaching $100M+ ARR and $2.2B valuation on ~$392M total funding suggests relatively strong capital efficiency compared to many AI peers.

## Risks & Open Questions

- **Valuation premium.** At ~22x ARR, the valuation assumes continued rapid growth and successful healthcare expansion.
- **Healthcare execution risk.** Real estate and healthcare are fundamentally different markets with distinct regulatory requirements; success in one does not guarantee success in the other.
- **Competitive landscape.** Property management AI is increasingly crowded, with incumbents like Yardi and RealPage building their own AI capabilities.
