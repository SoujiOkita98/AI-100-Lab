# Epirus

## Overview

Epirus is a Torrance, California-based defense technology company that develops directed-energy weapon systems, specifically high-power microwave (HPM) technology designed to counter drones, drone swarms, and other electronic threats. Founded in 2018, the company has raised over $550 million and is one of the leading startups in the rapidly growing counter-drone market.

The company's flagship product is **Leonidas**, a scalable, solid-state high-power microwave system that defeats unmanned aerial systems by disrupting or destroying their electronics. Leonidas can either "stun" a drone (causing a hard reset) or permanently disable it, and is effective against both individual drones and coordinated swarms. The system has been validated in multiple Department of Defense testing programs.

## Funding story

Epirus has raised over $550 million across five rounds, with 8VC (led by co-founder Joe Lonsdale) as the most consistent backer.

## Team

The founding team includes **Joe Lonsdale**, who co-founded Palantir Technologies and runs 8VC, one of the most influential defense tech venture firms. Lonsdale's involvement provides Epirus with deep ties to the defense establishment, government procurement networks, and Silicon Valley capital.

**Bo Marr** and **John Tenet** later went on to also co-found Chaos Industries, a related counter-drone company focused on radar detection (complementary to Epirus's directed-energy defeat capability).

## What makes them interesting

1. **Directed energy is the future of counter-drone.** Unlike kinetic interceptors (which cost thousands of dollars per shot), directed-energy weapons have near-zero marginal cost per engagement. Against swarms of cheap drones, this economic advantage is decisive.

2. **DoD-validated technology.** Leonidas has been tested and proven effective in multiple DoD evaluation programs, giving Epirus credibility that many defense startups lack.

3. **Palantir founder pedigree.** Joe Lonsdale's involvement brings unmatched access to defense procurement networks, government relationships, and venture capital.

4. **Scaling from R&D to production.** The Series D explicitly funds the transition from prototype/testing to scaled manufacturing, which is the critical inflection point for defense hardware companies.

5. **Complementary ecosystem.** The Epirus-Chaos Industries relationship (shared founders) creates a natural detection-and-defeat counter-drone ecosystem: Chaos detects drones with Vanquish radar, Epirus defeats them with Leonidas HPM.

## Risks and open questions

- **Production scaling.** Transitioning from successful testing to reliable mass production of directed-energy systems is technically challenging.
- **Competition from primes.** Raytheon, Lockheed Martin, and other defense primes are also developing directed-energy counter-drone systems with larger engineering teams and existing production infrastructure.
- **Founder departures.** Key co-founders (Marr, Tenet) also co-founded Chaos Industries, raising questions about management focus and potential IP/strategic overlap.
- **Valuation uncertainty.** The Series D did not disclose a specific valuation, and the last confirmed valuation ($1.35B) was from 2022.
