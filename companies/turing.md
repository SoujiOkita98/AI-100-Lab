# Turing

## Overview

Turing began as an AI-powered platform for vetting and matching remote software developers with companies. Since 2022, it has undergone a significant strategic pivot: OpenAI engaged Turing to generate high-quality training code to improve LLM reasoning capabilities. This transformed the company into a critical infrastructure provider for frontier AI labs. Turing now straddles two businesses -- supplying human-generated code and data for LLM training (serving foundational model companies), and deploying AI-powered solutions for Fortune 500 enterprises.

The company reached approximately $300 million in annualized revenue run rate by early 2025, nearly tripling year-over-year, and has been profitable since around 2024.

## Founders

### Jonathan Siddharth (CEO)

Jonathan Siddharth grew up in Chennai, India. He earned his B.E. in Computer Science from Sri Venkateswara College of Engineering (SVCE) under Anna University, graduating with First Class with Distinction in 2005. He then completed his M.S. in Computer Science at Stanford University in 2007, where he was a Core Group Member of the Stanford India Association.

At Stanford, Siddharth met Vijay Krishnan. His early career included work at Yahoo's Search Relevance group and at Powerset (later acquired by Microsoft), focused on page ranking and applied machine learning. He and Krishnan co-founded Infoaxe (a personal web memory search engine) and later Rover, an AI-powered content discovery engine, in 2008.

After Revcontent acquired Rover for approximately $30 million in 2017, Siddharth served as SVP of Technology at Revcontent before co-founding Turing in 2018. He was also an Entrepreneur in Residence at Foundation Capital.

According to a Fortune profile (November 2025), Siddharth has taken only two weeks of vacation in seven years of running Turing and relies heavily on ChatGPT in his daily workflow.

### Vijay Krishnan (CTO)

Vijay Krishnan attended IIT Bombay before completing his M.S. in Computer Science at Stanford. He has over a decade of experience leading large-scale commercial machine learning efforts, including text categorization, personalized search, CTR prediction, and eCPM maximization. He co-founded Rover with Siddharth in 2008 and served as its CTO until the Revcontent acquisition. He then served as SVP of Data Science at Revcontent before co-founding Turing.

Both founders are of Indian origin, met at Stanford, and have been building companies together for nearly two decades.

## Business Model

Turing operates two interconnected business lines:

### 1. AGI Advancement / LLM Training Services
Turing provides high-quality, human-generated code and data to frontier AI labs (confirmed: OpenAI; others undisclosed). This includes:
- Supervised fine-tuning (SFT) training data
- Reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF)
- Direct preference optimization (DPO) data
- Synthetic data generation
- Multimodal training (text, image, audio)

The company mobilizes large teams of vetted engineers to generate training code that improves model reasoning and coding capabilities. This became the growth engine starting in 2022.

### 2. Enterprise AI Solutions ("Intelligence Pods")
Turing deploys production-grade AI agents and systems for Fortune 500 companies and government institutions, embedding directly into client workflows. Use cases include:
- Underwriting automation
- Audit preparation (reported 45% reduction in cycle times)
- Mortgage processing (reported 60% faster approvals)
- Compliance-driven audit assistants
- Customer support agents

### 3. Developer Talent Marketplace (Legacy/Ongoing)
The original business -- AI-powered matching of vetted remote software developers (pool of 2+ million globally) to companies needing engineering talent. This uses proprietary AI for vetting technical skills, coding ability, and communication. Matching is reported at 3-5 days.

## What Makes Turing Interesting

1. **Pivot timing and execution.** Turing's shift from a developer marketplace to an LLM training infrastructure provider was extraordinarily well-timed. The 2022 OpenAI engagement transformed the company's trajectory and tripled revenue.

2. **Critical position in the AI supply chain.** Turing sits at a chokepoint -- frontier AI labs need high-quality human-generated code to train models, and Turing has built the infrastructure (vetting, management, quality control) to deliver this at scale. This is a defensible position that is hard to replicate quickly.

3. **Profitable at scale.** Unlike many AI startups burning cash, Turing reached profitability at ~$300M ARR. This is notable in a sector characterized by heavy losses.

4. **Founder cohesion.** Siddharth and Krishnan have co-founded three companies together over ~18 years (Infoaxe, Rover, Turing). This level of founder continuity is rare and reduces co-founder risk.

5. **Indian founder advantage.** Both founders' Indian origins and deep ties to the Indian engineering talent ecosystem give Turing access to a vast, cost-effective developer pool. The company has grown to 5,000-6,000+ employees across six continents.

6. **Dual flywheel.** Working with AI labs on training data gives Turing deep expertise in what makes AI systems work, which in turn makes their enterprise AI deployments more credible. Enterprise revenue funds the talent infrastructure that serves the AI labs.

7. **Sovereign wealth backing.** The Series E was led by Khazanah Nasional Berhad (Malaysia's sovereign wealth fund), signaling institutional confidence and potentially opening Southeast Asian market opportunities.

## Competitive Landscape

- **Scale AI** -- Closest competitor in AI training data; Scale focuses more on data labeling/annotation while Turing emphasizes code generation and developer talent
- **Andela** -- Competes on developer talent marketplace; Andela invests more in developer training while Turing focuses on AI-driven vetting
- **Toptal / Arc.dev / BairesDev** -- Compete on the talent marketplace side but lack the LLM training infrastructure business
- **Surge AI / Invisible Technologies** -- Compete on human-in-the-loop data services

## Key Uncertainties

- Exact revenue split between LLM training services vs. enterprise AI vs. talent marketplace is not publicly disclosed
- Whether the LLM training data business is durable long-term (if models become self-improving, demand for human-generated training data could decline)
- Specific named enterprise customers beyond OpenAI are not publicly confirmed
- Details on Series A and Series C rounds are unclear from public sources
- Employee count varies significantly across sources (4,700 to 6,300), likely reflecting rapid growth and contractor vs. full-time distinctions
