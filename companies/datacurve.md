# Datacurve

## Overview

Datacurve provides premium, curated coding data for training and evaluating large language models. The company operates a "bounty hunter" platform called Shipd, where skilled software engineers complete challenging coding tasks that generate high-quality training datasets. Datacurve positions itself as a challenger to Scale AI in the AI training data market, specifically targeting the coding data vertical where quality and difficulty of data sourcing are critical differentiators.

Founded in 2024 by two ~20-year-old University of Waterloo CS students, Datacurve was part of Y Combinator's W24 batch and achieved approximately $9M ARR within 13 months of operations.

## Business Model

### How It Works

1. **Bounty platform (Shipd):** Datacurve operates a platform where 1,400+ skilled software engineers take on coding challenges in areas such as algorithms, testing, and UI/UX.
2. **Per-task compensation:** For each task solved, engineers receive fees ranging from $5 to $50, with over $1 million in bounties distributed to date.
3. **Consumer product approach:** As co-founder Serena Ge explained, "We treat this as a consumer product, not a data labeling operation" -- the platform is designed to attract and retain top engineering talent through engaging challenges.
4. **Data sales:** The curated coding datasets are sold to AI labs and companies training LLMs on code, where data quality directly impacts model performance.

### Traction (as of late 2025)
- ~$9M ARR achieved in 13 months.
- 1,400+ active software engineers on the Shipd platform.
- Over $1 million in bounties paid out.

## What Makes Datacurve Interesting

1. **Taking on Scale AI in a focused vertical:** Rather than competing with Scale AI across all data types, Datacurve focuses specifically on coding data -- a vertical where quality is paramount and generic crowd-workers cannot produce useful output.

2. **Gamified data collection:** The bounty-hunter model is a clever approach to sourcing high-quality data. By making the work engaging and compensating fairly, Datacurve attracts better engineers than traditional data-labeling operations.

3. **Young founders, rapid scaling:** Two ~20-year-old founders reaching $9M ARR in 13 months with a $150M valuation mirrors the Mercor playbook of young, ambitious founders scaling AI infrastructure businesses.

4. **Strategic investor base:** Having employees from DeepMind, Anthropic, OpenAI, and Vercel as investors provides both validation and potential customer relationships.

5. **Risks and open questions:**
   - **Scale AI competition:** Scale AI has vastly more resources and could enter the coding data vertical aggressively.
   - **Synthetic data threat:** As LLMs improve at generating code, synthetic coding data could reduce demand for human-generated datasets.
   - **Engineer retention:** Maintaining a pipeline of 1,400+ skilled engineers willing to do bounty work requires ongoing engagement and competitive pay.
