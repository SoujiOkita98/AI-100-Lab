# Birch Biosciences

## Overview

Birch Biosciences is developing novel plastic recycling technologies using generative AI and advanced enzyme engineering to create a circular economy for plastics. The company engineers enzymes with enhanced capabilities to break down plastics that are currently difficult or impossible to recycle through conventional methods. Their current focus is on PET plastics (beverage bottles, polyester clothing) and polyurethane plastics (foam for furniture, bedding, insulation). The company's technology development is supported by the U.S. Department of Energy and the National Science Foundation.

## Products & Technology

1. **AI-Powered Enzyme Discovery:** Generative AI models design novel enzymes optimized for breaking down specific types of plastic
2. **High-Throughput Screening:** Rapid testing of AI-designed enzyme variants to identify highest performers
3. **PET Recycling:** Enzymes that break down PET plastic (bottles, polyester) into reusable monomers
4. **Polyurethane Recycling:** Enzymes targeting polyurethane foam (furniture, insulation) -- a plastic type with virtually no current recycling solution
5. **Pilot Plant:** Facility planned for 2025 to demonstrate commercial-scale enzymatic recycling

## Business Model

- **B2B licensing + partnerships:** Works with major plastic manufacturers and producers
- **Technology licensing:** Licenses enzymatic recycling technology to existing recycling infrastructure
- **Government-supported R&D:** DOE and NSF grants fund core research

## What Makes Birch Biosciences Interesting

1. **AI + biotech convergence.** Using generative AI to design enzymes represents a powerful application of AI in the physical world -- the same approach being used by companies like Profluent Bio and Cradle for protein engineering.

2. **Climate tech with clear demand.** Plastic pollution is one of the world's most pressing environmental challenges, and enzymatic recycling offers a fundamentally new approach. Major regulatory pressure (EU plastic regulations, US sustainability mandates) creates growing demand.

3. **Government validation.** DOE and NSF support provides both non-dilutive funding and scientific credibility for the technology.

4. **Addressable plastics with no current solution.** Polyurethane foam recycling is virtually non-existent today, creating a greenfield opportunity if the enzyme technology proves scalable.

5. **Industry partnerships.** Working with several of the world's largest plastic manufacturers provides both revenue potential and a path to commercial deployment.
