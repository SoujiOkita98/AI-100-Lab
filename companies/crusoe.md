# Crusoe

## Overview

Crusoe is an AI infrastructure company that designs, builds, and operates data centers purpose-built for AI workloads. The company originally pioneered the use of waste flare gas from oil fields to power cryptocurrency mining rigs, and has since pivoted to become a major GPU cloud provider for AI training and inference. Crusoe operates large-scale data center campuses powered by a mix of wind, solar, batteries, and natural gas, positioning itself as a sustainability-focused alternative to hyperscalers.

## Founding Story and Team Origins

Crusoe was founded in 2018 by childhood friends Chase Lochmiller and Cully Cavness, who reconnected while hiking Colorado's 14,000-foot peaks. Lochmiller brought a quantitative computing background -- he holds undergraduate degrees in math and physics from MIT and a master's in computer science (AI specialization) from Stanford, and previously worked as a quantitative researcher and trader at Jump Trading and GETCO. Cavness brought deep energy sector expertise, with a background in geophysics, renewables, and energy investment banking, having grown up in an oil and gas family. They identified an opportunity to turn stranded natural gas (otherwise flared and wasted) into power for compute workloads.

The executive team also includes Chris Dolan (Chief Data Center Officer) and former Google AI/infrastructure leaders Erwan Menard and Nadav Eiron.

## Business Model

Crusoe operates as a vertically integrated AI cloud infrastructure provider. The company:
- Builds and operates its own data centers with on-site power generation
- Offers GPU-as-a-Service (NVIDIA GPU clusters) for AI training and inference
- Differentiates on sustainability (renewable and waste-energy powered) and cost efficiency
- Has secured major contracts, including building a large data center campus for OpenAI in Texas ($11.6B in total financing for that project)

Revenue is estimated at ~$276M in 2024, with projections approaching $1B in 2025.
