# Cambricon Technologies

## Overview

Cambricon Technologies is China's most valuable AI chip company and one of the most important semiconductor firms in the country's drive to reduce dependence on Nvidia. Founded in 2016 by brothers Chen Tianshi and Chen Yunji -- both former researchers at the Chinese Academy of Sciences -- Cambricon designs AI training and inference chips that are increasingly being adopted as Nvidia alternatives under U.S. export controls.

By September 2025, Cambricon's market capitalization reached 579 billion RMB (~$81 billion), briefly surpassing Kweichow Moutai as the most expensive stock on China's A-share market. The company posted 4.6 billion RMB in revenue in the first nine quarters of 2025 -- nearly 10x that of other Chinese AI chip startups. CEO Chen Tianshi's net worth surged to over $25 billion, making him one of the 100 richest people in the world. [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2025/10/24/cambricon-china-nvidia-revenue-stock-frenzy-ceo-chen-tianshi-worlds-richest-people/)

## Founders: The Chen Brothers

**Chen Tianshi** (born ~1985) and **Chen Yunji** (born ~1980) are brothers from Jiangxi Province. Their father was an electrical engineer and their mother a history teacher. Both were accepted as teenagers into the Special Class for the Gifted Young at the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC), one of China's most selective programs for prodigies.

- **Chen Yunji** was admitted to USTC at age 14 and received his PhD from the Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS) at 24. He specialized in semiconductor architecture and co-designed the neural processing unit that was integrated into Huawei's Kirin 970 chip.
- **Chen Tianshi** earned a bachelor's in mathematics from USTC in 2005 and a PhD in computer science in 2010, also from CAS. He focused on AI acceleration hardware.

The brothers co-founded Cambricon in 2016 to commercialize their research on neural network processors. The company name references the Cambrian geological period -- when complex life first exploded in diversity. ## Key Products and 2026 Production Targets

- **Siyuan 590**: Current-generation AI training/inference chip, competing with Nvidia's H100 in China's domestic market.
- **Siyuan 690**: Next-generation chip targeting higher performance.
- **2026 target**: Deliver approximately 500,000 AI accelerators, including up to 300,000 units of Siyuan 590/690 processors -- roughly triple 2025 output.

### Manufacturing Challenges

Cambricon's chips are manufactured by SMIC using the N+2 process node. Yield rates for the 590 and 690 chips are reportedly only ~20%, meaning four out of five chips produced are unusable. Shortages of HBM (high-bandwidth memory) and LPDDR components further constrain production. [TrendForce](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2025/12/15/insights-cambricon-remains-chinas-top-ai-chip-startup-rumored-2026-triple-output-faces-smic-limits/); [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/semiconductors/cambricon-targets-500000-ai-chips-in-2026-as-china-accelerates-domestic-hardware-push)

## What Makes Cambricon Interesting (GSB Research Perspective)

1. **"China's Nvidia" narrative.** As U.S. export controls force Chinese AI companies to find domestic chip alternatives, Cambricon is the primary beneficiary. Its revenue spiked 14x in Q3 2025 alone as orders shifted from Nvidia.

2. **Prodigy founders.** The Chen brothers' trajectory -- from teenage USTC gifted program to CAS researchers to chip company founders worth billions -- is a distinctly Chinese AI talent story.

3. **Valuation disconnect.** At $81B market cap, Cambricon trades at extraordinary multiples relative to revenue, reflecting a national strategic premium rather than pure financial fundamentals.

4. **Yield challenges as a constraint on China's AI ambitions.** The 20% yield rate illustrates the gap between chip design capability and manufacturing capability in China. Even if Cambricon designs competitive chips, SMIC's process limitations constrain actual supply.

5. **Zhipu AI's GLM-4.6 was trained on Cambricon chips**, making Cambricon a critical enabler of China's frontier model development.

## Key Risks

- SMIC manufacturing yield and capacity constraints
- Competition from Huawei's Ascend chips (which have stronger government backing)
- U.S. sanctions risk (potential tightening of equipment/EDA tool restrictions)
- Extreme valuation relative to current revenue
