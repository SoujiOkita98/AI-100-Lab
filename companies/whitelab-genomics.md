# WhiteLab Genomics

## Overview

WhiteLab Genomics provides gene and cell therapy companies with an AI-powered predictive simulation platform to expedite the design of genomic therapies. The platform uses a combination of machine learning and deep learning techniques to process multiple scientific hypotheses simultaneously, evaluating different genetic variants to predict the best molecular design for therapeutic candidates. Founded in Paris in 2019, the company went through Y Combinator's W22 batch to accelerate its US market entry.

## Founders & Key People

### David Del Bourgo -- Co-Founder & CEO
- **Background:** Biotech and life sciences executive with deep expertise in genomic medicine and computational biology
- **Origin:** French, based in Paris

## Products & Technology

1. **Predictive Simulation Platform:** AI-driven platform for designing gene and cell therapies
2. **Multi-Hypothesis Testing:** Simultaneously processes multiple genetic variants and molecular designs
3. **ML/DL Approach:** Combines machine learning and deep learning with biological data to predict optimal therapeutic candidates
4. **Scientific Data Integration:** Integrates genomic, biological, and experimental datasets for comprehensive analysis

## Business Model

- **B2B SaaS / Platform licensing:** Sells to gene and cell therapy companies, both pharma and biotech
- **Target customers:** Companies developing gene therapies, cell therapies, and RNA-based therapeutics
- **Revenue (~2024):** ~$7.4M (per Extruct AI estimates)

## What Makes WhiteLab Genomics Interesting

1. **AI applied to genomic medicine.** Gene and cell therapy is one of the most promising frontiers in medicine, and AI can dramatically accelerate the design process -- potentially cutting years off development timelines.

2. **European biotech with YC backing.** A Paris-based biotech going through YC signals ambition for the US market while leveraging Europe's strong genomics research ecosystem.

3. **Strategic pharma investors.** Having Debiopharm (Swiss pharma) as an investor provides both capital and potential customer relationships in the pharmaceutical industry.

4. **Growing market.** The gene therapy market is projected to reach $30B+ by 2030, driven by FDA approvals and expanding therapeutic applications.
