# Fourier Intelligence

## Overview

Fourier Intelligence is a Shanghai-based robotics company that has transitioned from rehabilitation robotics to building general-purpose humanoid robots. Founded in 2015 by Alex Gu, the company spent its first several years developing exoskeletons and rehabilitation devices before unveiling its GR-1 humanoid robot at the World Artificial Intelligence Conference in Shanghai in July 2023.

Fourier is notable as one of the first companies globally to pursue mass production of humanoid robots, and it has been described as a frontrunner in China's rapidly growing humanoid robotics ecosystem.

## Products

### GR-1 (2023-)
- **Form factor**: Full-size bipedal humanoid, 1.65 meters tall
- **Key specs**: Walks at 5 km/h, carries up to 50 kg, 40+ degrees of freedom
- **Status**: Production version launched; deployed in research institutions and pilot industrial settings

### GR-3 "Care-bot" (2026-)
- **Form factor**: Next-generation humanoid with improved dexterity and AI capabilities
- **Use case**: Healthcare and rehabilitation care, showcased at CES 2026
- **AI**: Trained using NVIDIA Isaac Gym for sim-to-real transfer of locomotion and manipulation skills

### Rehabilitation Robotics (Legacy)
- Fourier's original business line includes upper- and lower-limb rehabilitation robots deployed in hospitals and clinics globally

## Business Model

Fourier operates a dual business model:
1. **Humanoid robot sales**: Selling GR-series robots to research institutions, industrial partners, and eventually commercial deployers
2. **Rehabilitation robotics**: Continuing revenue from medical rehabilitation devices sold to hospitals and clinics worldwide

The rehabilitation business provides a revenue base while the humanoid program scales toward commercial viability.

## What Makes Fourier Intelligence Interesting

1. **Rehabilitation-to-humanoid pipeline**: Fourier's years of building rehabilitation exoskeletons gave it deep expertise in human biomechanics, actuator design, and safe human-robot interaction -- all directly applicable to humanoid development.

2. **Mass production ambitions**: Fourier is among the first companies globally to pursue volume manufacturing of humanoid robots, with production facilities in Shanghai.

3. **China's humanoid ecosystem**: Operating at the center of China's government-backed push to lead in humanoid robotics, with access to favorable policy, subsidies, and a massive domestic market.

4. **SoftBank and Saudi backing**: Investment from SoftBank Vision Fund II and Prosperity7 (Saudi Aramco) provides global capital and distribution networks.

5. **NVIDIA partnership**: Using NVIDIA Isaac Gym for sim-to-real training of humanoid locomotion and manipulation, placing Fourier in NVIDIA's robotics ecosystem.
