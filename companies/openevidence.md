# OpenEvidence

> The "ChatGPT for doctors" -- an AI-powered medical search engine used by over 40% of U.S. physicians, valued at $12 billion.

## Company Overview

OpenEvidence is an AI medical search engine that provides clinical decision support for physicians. Founded in 2022 by Daniel Nadler (who previously founded Kensho, acquired by S&P Global for $550M), the platform has achieved extraordinary adoption among U.S. clinicians. As of mid-2025, more than 430,000 registered U.S. physicians (approximately 40% of all U.S. physicians) use the platform, with over 8.5 million consultations per month. The company reached $100 million in annual revenue in January 2026.

## Founder

### Daniel Nadler -- CEO

Daniel Nadler is a serial entrepreneur best known for founding Kensho Technologies, an AI analytics company acquired by S&P Global for approximately $550 million in 2018. He founded OpenEvidence in 2022 to bring AI-powered evidence-based clinical decision support to physicians.

## Business Model

OpenEvidence operates as a clinical decision support platform for physicians. The platform launched "DeepConsult," an AI agent specifically designed for advanced medical research queries. Revenue model includes enterprise licensing to health systems and direct physician subscriptions.

## What Makes OpenEvidence Interesting

1. **Extraordinary physician adoption:** 40% of U.S. physicians adopted the platform in roughly two years -- one of the fastest organic adoption curves in healthcare tech history.
2. **Valuation trajectory:** From $1B to $12B in under a year, reflecting the perceived market size of AI-powered clinical decision support.
3. **Founder pedigree:** Daniel Nadler's track record with Kensho ($550M exit to S&P Global) gives credibility to scaling an AI platform in a regulated industry.
4. **AI agent expansion:** The launch of DeepConsult signals a move from search/retrieval toward autonomous medical reasoning agents.
5. **Revenue velocity:** Reached $100M ARR while still in rapid growth phase.
