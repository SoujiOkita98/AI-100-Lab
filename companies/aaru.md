# Aaru

## Overview

Aaru is an AI-powered synthetic research company that replaces traditional market research methods (surveys, focus groups, polling) with thousands of AI agents that simulate human behavior. The company generates near-instant consumer and voter insights at a fraction of the cost and time of conventional approaches.

## Founders & Team

Aaru was founded in March 2024 by three remarkably young founders:

- **Cameron Fink (CEO, ~20)** -- Dropped out of Dartmouth College to build Aaru. Previously had experience at Kleiner Perkins and at the Research Science Institute (RSI). He and Koh were 18-19 at founding.
- **Ned Koh (President, ~20)** -- Former Harvard student who dropped out to co-found Aaru. Had prior research experience at Northwestern University and had co-founded earlier startups.
- **John Kessler (CTO, ~16)** -- Was approximately 15 years old at the time of founding, making him a notable teen tech prodigy. Leads the technical architecture of Aaru's simulation models.

**Advisors / mentors:** Dave Burwick (former CEO of Boston Beer Company) and Frank Luntz (veteran pollster) have been cited as early mentors who helped the team transition from political polling to commercial applications.

## Technology & Product

Aaru's core product is a **synthetic audience simulation platform**:

1. **AI Agent Construction:** The system ingests census data, demographic profiles, and psychographic attributes to create AI agents. Each agent is assigned hundreds of personality traits -- from aspirations to family relationships to media consumption habits.
2. **Real-time Information:** Agents continuously ingest internet content meant to mimic the media diets of the real humans they replicate, keeping simulations current.
3. **Multi-step Reasoning:** Agents go through multiple reasoning steps before arriving at their final answer, a technique shown to improve prediction accuracy.
4. **Scale & Speed:** A typical simulation draws on ~5,000 AI respondents and completes in 30 seconds to 1.5 minutes. Aaru claims to cost less than 1/10th the price of surveying actual humans.

## Business Model

Aaru sells its synthetic research platform to enterprises, consulting firms, political campaigns, and agencies. Known clients and partners include:

- **Consulting / Professional Services:** Accenture, EY (Ernst & Young)
- **Agencies:** Interpublic Group (IPG)
- **Brands:** McDonald's, Boston Beer, Bayer, A24 (film studio)
- **Political:** Various campaigns, think tanks, and super PACs

The company appears to operate on a B2B services/SaaS model, though exact pricing and contract structures are not publicly disclosed. ARR was reportedly still below $10M as of late 2025.

## Track Record & Validation

### Successes
- **NY-16 Democratic Primary (June 2024):** Aaru's AI poll predicted the outcome of the Bowman vs. Latimer race within 371 votes -- without surveying a single human. The centrist George Latimer defeated incumbent Jamaal Bowman 58.7% to 41.3%.
- Across races tested, Aaru claims an average deviation of 0.49% from actual results, compared to 1.31% for traditional polling averages.

### Setbacks
- **2024 Presidential Election:** Aaru predicted Kamala Harris would narrowly win the Electoral College (53-47 probability). Trump won the election. CEO Cameron Fink defended the result as within the margin of error, describing it as a "coin flip." This miss is a legitimate concern for the methodology's reliability on high-stakes, large-scale predictions.

## What Makes Them Interesting

1. **Founder age:** A billion-dollar-valued company led by founders aged 16-20 is extraordinarily rare and signals either exceptional talent or exceptional hype (or both).
2. **Speed/cost disruption:** If the methodology works reliably, replacing weeks-long, six-figure research projects with 90-second, low-cost AI simulations is a genuine paradigm shift for the ~$80B+ global market research industry.
3. **Unusual valuation structure:** The multi-tier Series A is a window into how AI startup valuations are being engineered in the current market -- headline numbers may not reflect economic reality.
4. **Methodology risk:** The 2024 presidential election miss raises real questions about whether simulated agents can capture the complexity of human decision-making at scale. The approach is promising but unproven in high-stakes, high-variance scenarios.
5. **Blue-chip client roster:** Securing Accenture, EY, IPG, McDonald's, and A24 within ~18 months of founding -- despite the founders' youth -- suggests the product delivers real value in at least some use cases.

## Open Questions

- How does accuracy scale across different domains (consumer behavior vs. political polling vs. financial decisions)?
- What is the defensibility of the technology? Could incumbents (e.g., Nielsen, Qualtrics) or foundation model providers replicate this?
- What are the ethical implications of synthetic audience simulation, particularly in political contexts?
- How will the company grow ARR past the <$10M mark to justify the valuation?
