# Applied Compute

## Overview

Applied Compute is a San Francisco-based AI startup founded in May 2025 by three former OpenAI researchers: Yash Patil (Codex), Rhythm Garg (o1 reasoning model), and Linden Li (training infrastructure). The company builds custom AI models and autonomous agents for enterprises, rejecting the "one-size-fits-all" approach to AI in favor of intelligence specifically trained on each client's proprietary data and workflows.

Applied Compute has raised $100M in total funding ($20M pre-seed + $80M Series A), both rounds led by Benchmark. The company reached a $700M valuation just five months after founding, and as of January 2026, was reportedly in talks to raise at a $1.3B valuation.

## Funding story

**June 2025 -- Pre-Seed ($20M at $100M valuation).** Benchmark led. Initial capital to launch the company and begin working with enterprise clients.

**October 2025 -- Series A ($80M at ~$700M valuation).** Benchmark led again, with Sequoia Capital, Lux Capital, Hanabi Ventures, Neo, and Definition Capital participating. Notable angels include Elad Gil, Victor Lazarte, and Omri Casspi.

**January 2026 (reported) -- Potential new round at $1.3B valuation.** The Information reported the company is in talks for a new fundraise, suggesting continued rapid growth.

## What makes them interesting

1. **Elite OpenAI pedigree.** The founders built some of OpenAI's most important products -- Codex and the o1 reasoning model. This signals both technical depth and an understanding of what enterprise AI actually needs.

2. **Counter-thesis to generic AI.** While most AI companies offer general-purpose models, Applied Compute argues that enterprises need models specifically trained on their own data. This embedded, high-touch approach may prove more defensible than API access.

3. **Valuation velocity.** $100M post-money to potentially $1.3B in seven months. Even by AI startup standards, this is exceptional -- reflecting investor confidence in the team and the enterprise AI opportunity.
