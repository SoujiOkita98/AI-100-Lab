# Arize AI -- AI/ML Observability & Evaluation Platform

## Company Overview

Arize AI is an AI engineering platform focused on evaluation and observability for AI models and agents. The platform helps engineers develop, evaluate, troubleshoot, and monitor AI systems in production, providing visibility into model performance, data quality, and drift detection. As AI agents become more prevalent, Arize has expanded to include agent observability.

## Founders and Team

### Aparna Dhinakaran -- Co-Founder & Chief Product Officer
- **Education:** B.S. in EECS, UC Berkeley (2016). Published research with Berkeley AI Research (BAIR). On leave from Cornell University Computer Vision PhD.
- **Origin:** Born in Chennai, India. Parents immigrated to the US when she was 6 months old, eventually settling in the Bay Area.
- **Prior experience:** ML engineer at **Uber** (built core ML infrastructure including Michelangelo), **Apple**, and **TubeMogul** (acquired by Adobe).
- **Recognition:** Forbes 30 Under 30.

### Jason Lopatecki -- Co-Founder & CEO
- **Origin:** United States. Co-founded TubeMogul (acquired by Adobe for ~$580M).

**Founder Pattern:** Aparna Dhinakaran is an Indian-American (born in Chennai, raised in the US) who built critical ML infrastructure at Uber before co-founding Arize. She represents the "1.5 generation" Indian-American founder -- born in India but raised entirely in the US. She is a rare female Indian-American technical co-founder in the AI infrastructure space.

## What Makes Arize AI Interesting

1. **Uber ML infrastructure insider:** Dhinakaran's experience building Michelangelo at Uber gave unique insight into the observability gaps Arize addresses.
2. **Born in Chennai, raised in Bay Area:** Represents the "1.5 generation" Indian-American founder -- distinct from IIT-to-Silicon-Valley founders.
3. **Female Indian-American AI infrastructure founder:** One of very few women in AI infrastructure founding teams.
4. **ML observability as essential AI category:** Positioned in a "picks and shovels" role for the AI economy.
5. **Open-source on-ramp:** Phoenix (open-source observability library) serves as developer on-ramp to commercial platform.
