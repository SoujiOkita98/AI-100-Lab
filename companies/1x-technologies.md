# 1X Technologies

## Overview

1X Technologies (formerly Halodi Robotics) is a Norwegian-American humanoid robotics company building general-purpose androids for home and enterprise use. Founded in 2014 in Moss, Norway, the company spent nearly a decade in relative obscurity developing core actuation and control technology before OpenAI's 2023 investment catapulted it into the spotlight. It is now one of the leading contenders in the humanoid robotics race alongside Figure AI, Tesla Optimus, Agility Robotics, and others.

## Products

### EVE (2018-)
- **Form factor**: Wheeled humanoid (upper body + mobile base)
- **Use case**: Enterprise -- logistics, security patrols, healthcare facility assistance
- **Status**: Deployed with enterprise customers; served as the real-world testing platform for 1X's proprietary actuation, perception, and manipulation stack

### NEO (2024-)
- **Form factor**: Fully bipedal humanoid, human-sized
- **Key specs**: Can lift up to 70 kg (154 lbs); natural human gait with arm swings; can squat, sit in chairs
- **AI**: Built-in LLM for conversational interaction; 1X World Model for learning new tasks from video/voice prompts
- **Use case**: Consumer home assistant (opening doors, fetching items, household chores) + enterprise (manufacturing, warehousing, logistics via EQT deal)
- **Pricing**: $20,000 early access (priority delivery 2026) or $499/month subscription
- **NEO Gamma** (Feb 2025): Updated design with "Emotive Ear Rings" for communication cues, minimalist home-friendly aesthetic, improved visual manipulation model

## Business Model

1. **Hardware sales**: One-time purchase at $20,000 for early access units
2. **Subscription/RaaS**: $499/month recurring model (likely the long-term anchor for recurring revenue)
3. **Enterprise partnerships**: Large-volume deals such as the EQT arrangement (up to 10,000 units across 300+ portfolio companies, 2026-2030)
4. **Software updates**: NEO's capabilities grow over time via OTA software updates, creating a Tesla-like flywheel of hardware + improving software

The long-term model appears to follow a **Tesla-like trajectory**: combine hardware margins with recurring software revenue while driving down unit costs through vertical integration and manufacturing scale.

**Production roadmap** (per company statements): thousands of units in 2025, tens of thousands in 2026, hundreds of thousands in 2027, millions in 2028. These are aspirational targets and should be treated with skepticism.

## Team & Origins

The founding DNA is deeply Norwegian -- the company was born in the small coastal city of Moss, south of Oslo. Bernt Bornich is a University of Oslo-educated roboticist who spent nearly a decade building foundational robotics technology before the recent AI boom created a massive market opening.

The 2025 acquisition of **Kind Humanoid** brought in **Christoph Kohstall** (former Stanford scientist and Google robotics team member), strengthening the Bay Area engineering team. The company now has ~965 employees (as of Feb 2026) split between Palo Alto/Hayward (California) and Moss (Norway).

Key investor relationships also shape the team's direction:
- **OpenAI** (Series A2 lead) -- provides AI/LLM alignment and access to frontier models
- **EQT Ventures** (Series B lead) -- provides enterprise distribution through its massive portfolio
- **Samsung NEXT** -- strategic hardware/electronics expertise

## What Makes 1X Interesting

1. **Longest track record in the current humanoid wave**: Founded in 2014, 1X has been building humanoid robots for over a decade -- far longer than most competitors (Figure AI: 2022, Tesla Optimus program: ~2021). The EVE platform provided years of real-world deployment data before NEO.

2. **OpenAI's first robotics bet**: The OpenAI Startup Fund leading the Series A2 was a significant endorsement. It positioned 1X as OpenAI's preferred hardware partner for embodied AI at a time when the "LLMs meet physical world" thesis was gaining momentum.

3. **Consumer-first strategy in an enterprise-dominated field**: While most humanoid competitors (Figure, Agility/Digit, Apptronik) target warehouses and factories, 1X is explicitly building for the home. The $499/month subscription and $20,000 purchase price are consumer price points. The EQT enterprise deal provides revenue while the consumer market develops.

4. **1X World Model (Jan 2026)**: A physics-grounded video generation model that lets NEO watch a demonstration (video or voice prompt), simulate future actions, and translate them into motor commands via inverse dynamics. This is a compelling approach to the "generalization problem" in robotics -- rather than programming every task, the robot learns from observation.

5. **European origin, American scaling**: Rare among top AI/robotics startups, 1X bridges Norwegian engineering heritage with Silicon Valley speed and capital. Manufacturing remains partly in Norway, giving geographic diversification.

6. **EQT partnership as distribution moat**: The deal to deploy up to 10,000 NEOs across 300+ EQT portfolio companies (2026-2030) is a built-in go-to-market channel that most competitors lack. EQT is both investor and customer aggregator.

7. **Kind Humanoid acqui-hire**: Consolidation play that brought Stanford/Google robotics talent in-house early in the humanoid land grab.

## Key Risks & Open Questions

- The $1B Series C at $10B valuation was reported in Sep 2025 but its closure is unconfirmed as of this writing. If it did not close, the company may face capital constraints against better-funded rivals (Figure AI raised $1B+ at $39B).
- Production roadmap (millions of units by 2028) is extremely aggressive and unproven.
- Consumer home robotics has a long history of commercial failures (Jibo, Kuri, etc.). NEO is far more capable, but consumer adoption at scale remains unproven.
- Competition is intensifying rapidly: Tesla, Figure, Agility, Unitree, and Chinese entrants are all racing toward similar form factors.
