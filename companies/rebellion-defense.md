# Rebellion Defense

## Overview

Rebellion Defense is a defense technology startup that builds AI-powered software for the U.S. Department of Defense and allied nations. The company brands itself around the concept of "SensorOS" -- a software layer that integrates sensor data for military decision-making. Founded in 2019 by former government technology leaders, Rebellion reached unicorn status in 2021 but has since undergone significant downsizing and leadership changes.

## Founders and Key People

### Chris Lynch (Co-Founder, Former CEO)
- Serial entrepreneur and venture capitalist from Seattle, Washington.
- Founding director of the **Defense Digital Service (DDS)** at the Pentagon, recruited by Secretary of Defense Ash Carter in 2015.
- At DDS, launched high-profile programs including **JEDI Cloud**, **Hack the Pentagon**, and provided technical expertise on the F-35 Joint Strike Fighter and Next Generation GPS OCX.
- Served under Presidents Obama and Trump at DDS before departing in April 2019 to start Rebellion Defense.
- Fellow at Harvard Kennedy School's Belfer Center; lecturer at Stanford University's computer science department.
- Officially departed Rebellion Defense in September 2023.

### Nicole Camarillo (Co-Founder)
- Attorney and consultant with a national security technology background.
- Former chief strategist for **U.S. Army Cyber Command**, which works closely with DDS on defense cybersecurity.

### Oliver Lewis (Co-Founder)
- British policy director with experience in the UK Ministry of Defence and the **UK Government Digital Service**.
- Previously worked at Improbable, a UK-based simulation technology startup.
- Brought understanding of UK government security priorities and transatlantic defense cooperation.

### Chase Kimball (Co-Founder)
- Listed as a co-founder in corporate filings. Limited public background information available. [Uncertainty: specific role and background details not well documented in public sources.]

### Ben FitzGerald (Current CEO, appointed January 1, 2024)
- Early investor in Rebellion Defense since 2019; served as Executive Chair from May 2023.
- Former **Executive Director of Strategy, Data, and Design** in the Pentagon's Office of the Undersecretary of Defense for Acquisition and Sustainment.
- Led what has been described as the most significant reorganization of defense acquisition since the Goldwater-Nichols Act of 1986.

### Barry Sowerwine (Former Interim CEO, now Board Member)
- Served as interim CEO during the 2023 transition period after Chris Lynch's departure.
- Credited with steering the company through a strategic restructuring that led to a reported 50% increase in annual revenue.
- Transitioned to the Board of Directors upon FitzGerald's appointment.

### Eric Schmidt (Board Member)
- Former Google CEO and chairman; prominent investor in defense technology startups.
- Board member and early financial backer of Rebellion Defense.
- His involvement has drawn scrutiny given his dual role advising the DoD on technology matters.

## Products and Technology

### SensorOS
Rebellion's overarching platform concept -- described as "the world's first Sensor OS." A hardware-to-software integration layer designed to be interoperable across defense sensor systems.

### Iris
The flagship product. A suite of modular AI capabilities for warfighters:
- **Tracking and classification** of objects of interest across land, sea, and air domains.
- **Multi-modal, multi-domain data fusion** for high-fidelity entity identification.
- **Anomaly detection** and predictive analytics.
- **Real-time integration** -- embeds into third-party platforms to display insights and alerts without disrupting existing workflows.
- Deployed to the U.S. Navy for target recognition and tracking.

### Other Products (Historical)
- **SECURE** -- vulnerability assessment for cyber resilience.
- **Dispatch** -- mission planning tool.
- **Nova** -- defense analysis.

[Uncertainty: it is unclear which of these secondary products remain actively developed following the 2023 restructuring and workforce reductions.]

## Business Model

- **Government contracts**: Primary revenue comes from U.S. Department of Defense contracts, including direct awards and subcontracts.
- **Commercial adaptation model**: Rebellion builds commercial-grade software adapted for defense use cases, rather than traditional cost-plus defense contracting.
- **Mission Engineering services**: Following the 2023 restructuring, the company increased emphasis on deploying engineering teams directly to support specific military programs.
- **Allied nations**: The company has a UK presence (Oliver Lewis's background), though the extent of non-U.S. government work is not well documented publicly.

## Military Customers and Contracts

### U.S. Navy
- **Project Overmatch** (February 2024): Awarded a subcontract (via Fuse) to deploy Mission Engineering teams building custom software for the Navy's Project Overmatch -- a key element of the Pentagon's **Combined Joint All-Domain Command and Control (CJADC2)** strategy.
- **Target Recognition Contract** (May 2024): Won a competitive contract to provide Iris software to enhance target recognition and tracking for Sailors. This was Rebellion's second Navy contract win in 2024.
- **Expanded Navy contract** (April 2025): Further expansion of target recognition work.

## Turbulent History and Current Status

### 2019-2021: Rapid Growth
- Founded in 2019 with strong government connections and blue-chip investors.
- Quickly raised $224M and reached $1B unicorn valuation by September 2021.
- Grew to over 200 employees across Washington, D.C. and Seattle offices.

### 2022-2023: Restructuring and Layoffs
- **Late 2022**: First round of layoffs due to "reprioritization of product lines."
- **Spring 2023**: Laid off approximately 35% of workforce (~90 employees). The company cited the need to rebalance teams toward successful product areas and extend financial runway.
- Relocated to a more modest UK office; increased budget rigor.
- Internal communications around layoffs were widely criticized -- reportedly, even directors were unaware of cuts months later.
- **September 2023**: Co-founder Chris Lynch officially departed.
- **November 2023**: Barry Sowerwine named interim CEO.
- **December 2023**: Ben FitzGerald appointed CEO effective January 1, 2024.

### 2024-2026: Stabilization Under New Leadership
- Won multiple Navy contracts in 2024, signaling continued DoD relevance.
- FitzGerald focused on Mission Engineering and the JADC2 opportunity.
- As of February 2026, headcount is approximately **40 employees** (per Tracxn), a dramatic reduction from peak levels.
- No new funding rounds announced publicly.
- The company remains active with government contracts but operates at a fraction of its former scale.

**Assessment:** Rebellion Defense appears to be in a "survive and focus" mode -- dramatically smaller than its unicorn-era peak, but still winning contracts and maintaining relevance in the defense AI market. The JADC2 opportunity and Navy contracts provide a path forward, but the company faces intense competition from better-funded defense tech firms like Anduril, Shield AI, and Palantir. Whether the $1B valuation remains defensible given the ~40-person headcount is an open question.
